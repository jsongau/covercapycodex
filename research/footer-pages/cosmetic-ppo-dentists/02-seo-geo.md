## FAQ (mirrors FAQPage schema, 6 Q&A)
1. Do PPO dental plans cover cosmetic dentistry? — No, as a rule. Whitening, veneers for appearance, and cosmetic reshaping are standard PPO exclusions. A PPO pays only when a procedure is restorative or medically necessary, such as a crown on a cracked tooth.
2. How much do veneers cost in 2026? — Porcelain veneers run about $900 to $2,500 per tooth, with a national average near $1,765. A full set of 6 to 8 teeth commonly costs $12,000 to $24,000. Composite veneers are cheaper at about $250 to $1,500 per tooth.
3. How much does professional teeth whitening cost? — In-office professional whitening typically costs $400 to $800 per session, averaging around $508. Laser whitening can cost slightly more. Take-home dentist kits cost less than in-office sessions.
4. When does PPO insurance ever pay toward a veneer or crown? — When the work is restorative, not cosmetic. If a veneer or crown is the clinically appropriate way to restore a tooth damaged by trauma or significant decay, some PPO plans may cover part of it with prior authorization, often 30 to 50 percent, subject to the annual maximum. Request a predetermination first.
5. What is the difference between cosmetic and restorative dentistry? — Restorative dentistry repairs damaged or decayed teeth and is usually covered by PPO plans. Cosmetic dentistry improves the look of healthy teeth and is usually not covered. The same procedure can fall on either side depending on why it is done.
6. How do I find a cosmetic dentist who takes my PPO? — Search by your location and PPO carrier so you only see in-network offices, then confirm cosmetic services and the negotiated rate before booking. CoverCapy lets you find and verify a PPO dentist in minutes.
