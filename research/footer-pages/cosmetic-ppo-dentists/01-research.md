## What PPO dental insurance does and does NOT cover (2026)

- **General rule:** Most PPO dental plans do NOT cover purely cosmetic work, including teeth whitening, veneers placed for appearance only, cosmetic contouring/reshaping, and gum contouring. These are standard plan exclusions.
- **The restorative vs cosmetic line (this is the key concept):** The same procedure can be covered or excluded depending on WHY it is done.
  - Covered (restorative / medically necessary): a crown or filling on a decayed, cracked, or root-canaled tooth; a veneer that is the most clinically appropriate way to restore tooth structure lost to trauma or significant decay (rare, needs documentation + prior authorization).
  - Not covered (cosmetic): whitening a healthy tooth, veneers to change shape/color of healthy teeth, reshaping for looks.
- **Restorative-veneer exception:** Delta Dental PPO may give partial coverage when a veneer is part of a restorative plan with prior authorization, typically 30% to 50% of the approved fee, subject to the annual maximum (usually $1,000 to $2,000). Cigna PPO follows similar logic and requires detailed clinical documentation. Treat as exception, not the norm. Always request a predetermination first.
- **Whitening exception:** Most plans exclude whitening, but a few carriers are starting to add whitening perks. Guardian has been noted as including a teeth-whitening benefit on some plans. Always read the specific plan.
- **Practical takeaway for patients:** Use the PPO for the restorative half of a smile makeover (the crowns, the root canal, the build-up that a cosmetic case often needs) and budget cash/financing for the purely cosmetic layer.

## Competitor / GEO angle
Most cosmetic cost pages stop at the price table and the flat line "insurance does not cover it." The CoverCapy angle: explain the restorative-vs-cosmetic line clearly (the part patients actually need), then funnel to a verified in-network PPO dentist so the restorative portion is billed at the negotiated rate, and pair with financing for the cosmetic portion. Concierge framing, not a generic listicle.

## Do-not list
- Do NOT claim PPO plans cover cosmetic veneers or whitening as a rule.
- Do NOT quote a specific "percentage covered" as if it applies to cosmetic work generally; the 30 to 50 percent figure is an exception for restorative veneers with prior authorization only.
- Do NOT invent plan premiums or maximums; cosmetic work is excluded, so no plan number is presented as cosmetic coverage.
- No em-dashes, no roman numerals, sameAs arrays.
