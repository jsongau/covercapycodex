## 1. THE ORIGIN / THE WHY

### The core insight

Nobody wakes up wanting a Guardian plan. Nobody lies awake hoping to understand annual
maximums. People wake up with a cracked tooth on a Sunday night, a new baby and a fresh
set of benefits they have never read, a wedding photo six weeks out, a move to a city
where they know no one, or a job change that quietly swapped one dental plan for another.

The thing people actually shop for is a moment, not a brand. Dental care arrives attached
to a life event, and the insurance question only shows up afterward, as friction. CoverCapy
is built around that truth. We organize dental care around the moments people are actually
living through, not around the carrier logos the industry is organized by.

### The gap that creates the need

At the moment a life event sends someone looking for care, three questions land at once:

- Am I actually covered.
- What will this really cost me.
- Who near me takes my exact plan.

The honest answer is usually a shrug. There is no shortage of dentists and no shortage of
insurance. The shortage is an answer at the exact moment a person needs one. Every practice
online describes itself with the same words, gentle, modern, advanced, caring, and those
words are easy to write and impossible for a patient to verify from a website. Meanwhile
offices feel the same gap from the other side: reaching the right patient is expensive, and
people walk away when the experience is confusing rather than clear.

(Supporting external context, already sourced in 01-research.md: patient acquisition in
healthcare runs roughly $286 per lead, and about 36% of patients left a provider in the
last two years over dissatisfaction. Source: Patientdesk.ai, "Complete 2026 Guide to Dental
Patient Acquisition Strategies," accessed 2026-06-26. Use lightly on About; the friction,
not the figure, is the point.)

### The mission narrative (the why, in full)

CoverCapy exists to close the gap between a life event and a good dental decision. We sit
beside the patient the way a boutique concierge would, turning the messiest moment in dental
care into the calmest. We translate PPO coverage into plain language, give an honest sense of
cost before the chair instead of after the bill, confirm that a specific office takes a
person's exact PPO network, and hand them off cleanly to a real, modern dentist. The promise
is captured in one line the brand already lives by: get cover today, see a dentist tomorrow.

This is built on first-hand knowledge of how dental offices and PPO networks actually work,
the eligibility checks, the treatment estimates, the claims, and the money conversation at the
front desk. That insider view is what lets the guidance reflect the real chair-side experience
rather than a marketing version of it. CoverCapy is a guidance and discovery platform, not an
insurance carrier and not a dental practice, and it is free for patients.

### Why it compounds (the quiet flywheel)

Every verified office and every prepared patient makes the next match a little better. A
patient who arrives knowing their network and their likely cost is a better patient for the
office, and an office that has been reviewed and confirmed is a safer choice for the next
patient. That is why CoverCapy is built as a network rather than a list. It is a quiet
flywheel, and it is the moat: clarity accumulates.

## 2. THE PROMISE

CoverCapy is a concierge for PPO dental care. The promise rests on three commitments that the
About page should treat as load-bearing pillars.

- **Coverage clarity.** We translate PPO plans into plain language so a person knows what their
  plan does and does not cover before they sit in the chair, not after.
- **Cost honesty.** We help estimate what someone will actually owe, in ranges rather than false
  precision, so the bill is never the surprise. Illustrative, never a quote.
- **Verified discovery.** We confirm a dentist takes a person's exact PPO network and surface
  modern, in-network offices. Think of it as a coverage check for dental, run before the visit,
  with the member ID never stored.

The shape of the promise is a clean handoff: clarify, estimate, confirm, then step back and let
a real dentist take care of the person.

## 4. VALUES AND PRINCIPLES (framed as real commitments)

These are written as commitments the company is accountable to, not slogans. Each maps to
something already true on the site (about.html trust grid, footer fineprint, CLAUDE.md rules).

1. **Transparency over persuasion.** Sponsored or featured placements are always labeled.
   Patient tools are free; revenue comes from practice programs, not from the patient. Guidance
   is never sold to the highest bidder.

2. **Patient-first by structure, not slogan.** The patient pays nothing and the experience is
   designed around the patient's three questions (covered, cost, in-network), because the
   business model lets it be. The member ID is never stored; we record only that verification
   was provided.

3. **Verification over claims.** Anyone can write "modern" and "caring." We replace easy words
   with reviewed signals. Capy Accreditation is selective by design: membership begins a review,
   it does not grant the credential, and an office can be approved, conditionally approved, asked
   to improve, or declined. It is a private CoverCapy credential, not a government certification
   or a dental license.

4. **Honesty about limits.** We help people prepare; we do not replace their own check. Benefits,
   pricing, network participation, and credentials should always be confirmed directly with the
   carrier and the office. CoverCapy is not an insurance carrier, broker of record, or dental
   practice.

5. **Modern standards.** We favor technology-forward, patient-first practices and a calm, clear
   experience over jargon and pressure. No countdown timers, no false urgency, no surprise bills.

6. **Built by insiders.** The guidance reflects a working knowledge of eligibility, estimates,
   claims, and the front-desk money conversation, so it holds up against the real chair-side
   experience.

## 5. TONE GUIDE

### Voice in one line
Boutique-hotel concierge meets editorial magazine: calm, confident, warm, precise. The feel of
luxury handbag shopping, never a healthcare portal, never insurance comparison, never generic
SaaS.

### Register
- First-person plural ("we") for mission and stance.
- Confident but never hyped. No exclamation marks, no growth-hacking language.
- Warm and human: name the real moment (a cracked tooth on a Sunday night) before naming the
  mechanism (PPO network confirmation).
- Editorial rhythm: short declarative sentences, occasional one-line emphasis, prose that reads
  like a well-set magazine paragraph rather than a feature list.

### Hard rules (non-negotiable)
- No em-dashes. Use commas, colons, or rewrite.
- No roman numerals in any list or heading.
- No invented founders, dates, headcount, funding, user counts, dentist counts (beyond the
  existing "6,400+ pages" fact), or testimonials.
- Always restate, at least once per page: CoverCapy is not an insurance carrier or a dental
  practice.
- Cost language is illustrative, never a quote. Use ranges, not false precision.
- Member ID is never stored.
- Use italics for the practice name or for a single emphasized word in the editorial voice
  (mirrors the existing H1 treatment of "calmest"), sparingly.

### Words and phrases that are on-brand
concierge, prepared, calm, clarity, honest, verified, in-network, your exact plan, hand off,
the moment, life event, modern, plain language, get cover today see a dentist tomorrow.

### Words and phrases to avoid
best-in-class, revolutionary, disrupt, seamless solution, unlock, leverage, game-changing,
synergy, anything that reads like a SaaS landing page or an insurance-comparison grid, urgency
or scarcity framing, clinical-outcome guarantees.

### Tagline (canonical)
Get cover today, see a dentist tomorrow.
