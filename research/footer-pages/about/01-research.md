# 01 — Research: about.html

About CoverCapy is mostly **internal brand truth**, not externally sourced claims. Per the
agent brief: do NOT fabricate company history, team names, funding, or metrics. Keep claims
truthful and mission-driven. The only external facts used below are market-context figures
that support the "people shop around life events, not insurance brands" thesis, each cited.

## Internal brand truth (source: CLAUDE.md + index.html + capy-accreditation.html)
- Positioning: luxury concierge for PPO dental. Tagline: "Get cover today, see a dentist tomorrow."
- Feel: boutique-hotel concierge / editorial magazine. NOT a healthcare portal, NOT generic SaaS,
  NOT an insurance-comparison site.
- What CoverCapy does: compare PPO dental plans, estimate treatment costs, and find in-network
  dentists across the US. Built by dental-office insiders. Free to use for patients.
- Scale of the static discovery layer: 6,400+ pages across dentists, cities, metros, and states.
- Capy Accreditation: a private CoverCapy credential (not a government certification or dental
  board license) reviewing modern, technology-forward, patient-first practices. Selective.
  Membership begins a review; payment does not grant the credential. Outcomes: approved,
  conditionally approved, asked to improve, or declined.
- Membership tiers: free, capy_accredited, platinum_elite.
- CoverCapy is a dental guidance and discovery platform, NOT an insurance carrier or dental
  practice. (Footer fineprint — must be echoed for trust/legal accuracy.)
- Eligibility verification concept ("Plaid for dental eligibility"): verify a patient's PPO
  coverage at a specific office before the visit. Member ID is never stored (only
  member_id_provided: boolean).

## External market context (cited — supports the thesis, not company claims)

1. Network-effect / marketplace framing (used lightly on About; deeper on the network page):
   Network effects are a demand-side phenomenon: each new user raises utility for every other
   user. A marketplace flywheel compounds: more supply improves the experience for demand,
   which attracts more demand, which attracts more supply.
   - Source: Stripe Atlas, "Andrew Chen on marketplaces" — https://stripe.com/guides/atlas/andrew-chen-marketplaces (accessed 2026-06-26)
   - Source: Cobbleweb, "Grow the value of your marketplace with powerful network effects" — https://www.cobbleweb.co.uk/grow-marketplace-value-with-network-effects/ (accessed 2026-06-26)

2. Patient behavior / acquisition context (supports "people shop around life events; offices
   struggle to reach them; verification reduces friction"):
   - Patient acquisition costs in healthcare average roughly $286 per lead; ~36% of patients
     left a provider in the last two years due to dissatisfaction.
   - Source: Patientdesk.ai, "Complete 2026 Guide to Dental Patient Acquisition Strategies" —
     https://www.patientdesk.ai/blog/complete-2026-guide-to-dental-patient-acquisition-strategies (accessed 2026-06-26)

3. PPO market context (supports "PPO is where coverage and choice meet"):
   - DPPO plans offer a wide network and let patients see in- and out-of-network providers,
     with best rates in-network.
   - Source: Cigna, "Dental Preferred Provider Organization (DPPO) Plans" —
     https://www.cigna.com/individuals-families/shop-plans/plans-through-employer/dental-ppo (accessed 2026-06-26)
   - Source: Humana, "PPO Dental Insurance" — https://www.humana.com/dental-insurance/ppo (accessed 2026-06-26)

## Guardrails for About copy
- No invented founders, headcount, funding rounds, dates founded, or city of HQ.
- No invented user counts, dentist counts beyond the page-count fact already in CLAUDE.md
  ("6,400+ pages"), or testimonials.
- Frame ambition and mission in present/aspirational voice without claiming achieved metrics.
- Always restate: CoverCapy is not an insurance carrier or a dental practice.
