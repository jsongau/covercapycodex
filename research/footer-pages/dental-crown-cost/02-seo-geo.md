## Extractable quick answer (GEO / AI Overviews)
A dental crown typically costs about $800 to $2,500 per tooth in 2026, with a national average near $1,000 to $1,500. Porcelain-fused-to-metal is usually cheapest; zirconia, all-ceramic, and gold cost more. Most PPO plans cover crowns as a major service at 50% to 60% after the deductible and a 6-to-12-month waiting period.

## FAQ Q&A (these go verbatim into FAQPage schema)
1. How much does a dental crown cost in 2026? — about $800 to $2,500 per tooth; average $1,000 to $1,500.
2. Does dental insurance cover crowns? — most PPO plans cover crowns as a major service at 50% to 60% after deductible and a 6-to-12-month wait, when medically necessary.
3. What is the cheapest type of crown? — porcelain-fused-to-metal, roughly $800 to $2,000.
4. How much is a crown with insurance? — often $400 to $900 out of pocket after a 50% major benefit, until the annual maximum is used.
5. Is there a waiting period for crown coverage? — frequently 6 to 12 months; some plans pay from day one.
6. Can I finance a dental crown? — yes: 0% healthcare cards, in-house plans, dental savings plans, FSA/HSA, personal loans.
