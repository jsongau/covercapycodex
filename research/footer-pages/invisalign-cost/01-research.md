## A. Invisalign cost ranges (2026, US, without insurance)

| Package | Typical range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Invisalign Express | ~$1,800 to $4,000 (avg ~$3,500) | Minor correction, fewer aligners |
| Invisalign Lite | ~$3,000 to $4,500 | Mild to moderate cases |
| Invisalign Comprehensive (Full) | ~$5,000 to $8,000 (most adults avg ~$5,700) | Full bite/alignment correction |

Headline: **Invisalign runs roughly $3,000 to $8,000**, with express/lite cases at the low end and full comprehensive treatment $5,000 to $8,000.

## B. Invisalign vs traditional braces (2026)
- Braces average ~$3,500 to $5,500; Invisalign ~$4,500 to $8,500.
- For mild to moderate cases, Invisalign and metal braces often cost about the same.
Source: dentistdecoded.com (above).

## C. How PPO plans cover orthodontics (SSOT + general norm)
Orthodontics is a **separate benefit** with its own **lifetime orthodontic maximum**, NOT the annual maximum, and **adults are frequently excluded**.

From `/data/plans/`:
- **Delta Dental PPO Premium** (`delta-dental-ppo-premium.md`): the broker-sold Premium tier covers **adults AND children at 50% after a 12-month wait, $1,500 lifetime orthodontic maximum**, separate $50 ortho deductible; **Invisalign/clear aligners covered on the same terms as braces**. (ACA-exchange certificates are pediatric/medically-necessary only; adult ortho on an individual plan is unusual.)
- **Guardian Premier 2.0** (`guardian-premier-ppo.md`): **child only (dependents under 19) at 60% in-network (50% out) after a 12-month wait, $750/benefit-year and $1,500 lifetime**; **adult orthodontics NOT covered on any Guardian individual plan**.
- **NCD Complete by MetLife** (`metlife-ncd-complete.md`): **orthodontics not covered.**

General norm to present (SSOT + external): when ortho is covered, plans pay about **25% to 50% of the cost up to a lifetime maximum, commonly $1,000 to $2,500 (some premium plans to ~$3,500)**, and **most plans limit full ortho benefits to dependents under 18 (about 86% of plans)**; adult coverage is growing but less common.

External corroboration (general norms only, not used for any specific plan number):
- hotalinginsurance.com — Does Insurance Cover Invisalign: https://hotalinginsurance.com/gap-insurance/does-insurance-cover-invisalign (accessed 2026-06-26) — lifetime ortho max $1,000 to $3,000; ~86% limit full benefits to under-18.
- invisalign.com — How to Pay for Invisalign / Insurance and Payment Options: https://www.invisalign.com/invisalign-cost/insurance-and-payment-options (accessed 2026-06-26)
- cobbdefense.com — How Much Insurance Will Cover for Invisalign in 2026: https://cobbdefense.com/invisalign-cost-with-insurance/ (accessed 2026-06-26)

## D. HSA / FSA
Invisalign is HSA/FSA/HRA/LPFSA eligible. FSA/HSA dollars can cover copays, deductibles, or the balance above a plan's lifetime ortho maximum.
Source: HSA Store — HSA Eligible Invisalign Orthodontics: https://hsastore.com/hsa-eligibility-list/i/invisalign-orthodontics (accessed 2026-06-26)

## E. Financing
Most orthodontic offices offer in-house monthly plans with a down payment. CareCredit (accepted by many Invisalign dentists) offers deferred-interest promos (6, 12, 18, 24 months) or fixed-APR terms up to 60 months for larger cases; monthly payments commonly land around $58 to $233 depending on length and any insurance offset.
Sources:
- CareCredit — Clear Aligners (Invisalign) Cost and Financing: https://www.carecredit.com/well-u/health-wellness/invisalign-cost-invisalign-financing/ (accessed 2026-06-26)
- withcherry.com — Invisalign Payment Plan guide: https://withcherry.com/blog/invisalign-payment-plan (accessed 2026-06-26)
