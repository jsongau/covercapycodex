## Extractable quick answer (GEO / AI Overviews)
Invisalign typically costs about $3,000 to $8,000 in 2026, with express cases at the low end and full comprehensive treatment around $5,000 to $8,000. Some PPO plans cover orthodontics at about 25% to 50% up to a lifetime maximum, often $1,000 to $2,500, but most limit full benefits to dependents under 18, so adult coverage is less common.

## FAQ Q&A (verbatim into FAQPage schema)
1. How much does Invisalign cost in 2026? — about $3,000 to $8,000; express/lite cheaper, full $5,000 to $8,000.
2. Is Invisalign covered by insurance? — sometimes, as orthodontics, about 25% to 50% up to a lifetime ortho maximum; many plans exclude adults.
3. Does Invisalign cost more than braces? — for mild to moderate cases they cost about the same; braces average $3,500 to $5,500.
4. Is there a lifetime maximum for orthodontics? — yes, ortho usually has its own lifetime max, often $1,000 to $2,500, separate from the annual maximum.
5. Can I use HSA or FSA for Invisalign? — yes, Invisalign is HSA/FSA eligible; funds can cover copays or the balance above your ortho max.
6. How can I finance Invisalign? — in-house monthly plans, CareCredit promos over 6 to 60 months, or a PPO ortho benefit that pays a share.
