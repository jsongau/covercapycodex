# 03 — Content Outline: Insurance Carrier Intelligence

Voice: editorial concierge. Teach plainly, like a knowledgeable friend who has read the brochures. No em-dashes, no roman numerals. Scope CSS `.carrier-intelligence-page`. WebPage/Article + Organization + BreadcrumbList + FAQPage JSON-LD.

## Section order

1. **Hero**
   - Eyebrow: "CoverCapy / Carrier Intelligence"
   - H1: "How to Read a Dental Insurance Plan"
   - Subhead: six numbers and one clause decide what a dental plan is actually worth to you. Here is how to read them, and how to match a plan to your life.
   - Primary CTA: "Compare PPO dental plans" -> /compare-ppo-dental-plans. Secondary: "See the carrier watch" -> /carrier-watch.html.
   - Trust chips: "Plain English", "Facts from carrier brochures", "No paid placement".

2. **TL;DR quick-answer** (the 02 extractable answer; the six-things checklist in a tidy card).

3. **Sticky sub-nav**: The six numbers / Missing-tooth clause / Networks / How CoverCapy verifies / Choose by life event / FAQ.

4. **"The six things to read first"** (H2) — a numbered explainer (use words, not roman numerals), one subsection each:
   a. **Annual maximum** — what it is; typical $1,000-$2,000; 73% of PPO members now $1,500+ (Money/NADP); shelf range $1,000 (UHC) to $10,000 (MetLife). Note rollover + preventive-credit trend (ADA).
   b. **Deductible** — what it is; ~$50 typical, PPO avg ~$60 (MoneyGeek); the lifetime-deductible wrinkle (MetLife $100 once, ever) as a "read carefully" example.
   c. **Coverage tiers (100 / 80 / 50)** — coinsurance = plan's share; the graduated-percentage nuance (MetLife major 10->50->60; UHC basic 50->65->80; Ameritas/Mutual major 20->50); low first-year rate is the price of no/short waits.
   d. **Waiting periods** — the three buckets; waiver rules (Aetna 90-day, Delta/Humana prior comparable coverage; Humana implant never waivable; Guardian WA/MN caps); NY regulatory change (cite, state-specific).
   e. **(grouped) the fine-print caps** — annual vs lifetime caps (Guardian implant $1,250 lifetime; Mutual $3,000 lifetime; Humana $2,000/yr + $4,000 lifetime; MetLife implant $3,000/calendar-year within max). Teach the annual-vs-lifetime distinction.
   f. lead into the clause section.

5. **The missing-tooth clause** (H2) — the trap most buyers miss. Plain definition, why it exists, who carries it on the shelf (Guardian; Delta initial-placement, CA nuance; Aetna in-force-only; UHC higher tiers). Action: if you already have a gap, ask before enrolling. Compact callout box.

6. **Networks: in-network vs out-of-network** (H2) — PPO definition; >80% of market is PPO (ADA); why in-network saves (negotiated fee + direct billing); shelf network sizes (Aetna 442k+ locations, Mutual 400k+, Guardian 360k+, Delta 112k dentists/278k locations, Humana 143k+ dentists, UHC 80k+ dentists); caveat that networks change and must be verified at the office. Bridge to next section.

7. **How CoverCapy verifies your coverage** (H2) — the product mechanic, framed as trust:
   - We never invent which carriers an office accepts; acceptance is sourced and dated.
   - The free flow: choose your carrier, confirm contact, send a verification request to the office; CoverCapy never stores your member ID, only that one was provided.
   - Wording matches confidence ("in-network with" vs "reported to accept").
   - CTA: "Verify my coverage, free" -> /find-my-dentist.html.

8. **Choose by life event** (H2) — a scannable mapping (the 02/01 list). Each item links to the relevant plan page and to /compare-ppo-dental-plans. Keep it situational and human.

9. **A quick worked read** (H2, optional short) — take one plan (e.g. Ameritas PrimeStar Complete) and read it line by line using the six numbers, so the reader sees the method applied once. Use only data/plans facts; label the premium estimate.

10. **FAQ accordion** (mirrors the 8 FAQPage entries).

11. **Internal links block** + closing CTA. Link out to carrier-watch, compare, find-my-dentist, dental-insurance-market.

## Copy guardrails
- Every named plan figure traces to data/plans; every "what's normal" figure cites a market source.
- Premiums always labeled estimates.
- Member ID never stored. Verification never fabricated.
- Honor each plan's do_not when named (e.g. MetLife deductible = "lifetime," not "vanishing"; Guardian major = 60% in-network; Mutual no major wait; Humana basic = 90 days; Ameritas year-one major 20% and activation "next day," not "instant").
