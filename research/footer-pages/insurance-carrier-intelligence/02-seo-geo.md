## FAQ Q&A (FAQPage schema; 40-60 words each, standalone)

1. **What is a dental insurance annual maximum?**
The annual maximum is the most a dental plan will pay for covered care in a benefit year. Anything above it, you pay. Individual maximums usually run $1,000 to $2,000, though some plans reach $5,000 or $10,000. About 73% of PPO members now carry a $1,500 or higher maximum.

2. **What does a dental insurance deductible cover?**
The deductible is the amount you pay out of pocket before the plan's coinsurance begins, usually around $50 a year, and it normally applies to basic and major work only, not preventive cleanings. Read the fine print: some plans use a one-time lifetime deductible rather than a yearly one.

3. **What do the 100, 80 and 50 percentages mean?**
They are coinsurance tiers, the share the plan pays. Most plans pay about 100% for preventive care, 80% for basic work like fillings, and 50% for major work like crowns. Some plans graduate these percentages upward over the first few years, so a low first-year rate often pairs with no waiting period.

4. **What is a dental insurance waiting period?**
A waiting period is the time after coverage starts before a category of work is covered. Preventive usually has none, basic often waits up to six months, and major work often waits six to twelve months. Many carriers waive basic and major waits if you had recent prior dental coverage.

5. **What is the missing-tooth clause?**
The missing-tooth clause means many dental plans will not pay to replace a tooth that was already missing before your policy began, so an implant, bridge or denture for an existing gap can be excluded. If you already have a gap, confirm how a plan treats it before enrolling.

6. **Does in-network matter for dental insurance?**
Yes. In-network dentists accept the carrier's negotiated fee, which is usually well below the cash price, and they typically file the claim for you. Out-of-network care often costs more and may require you to pay upfront and seek reimbursement. Networks change, so confirm acceptance at the specific office.

7. **How do I choose dental insurance for my situation?**
Match the plan to your near-term need. Want cleanings and fillings cheaply, pick a low-premium entry plan. Coming off employer coverage, choose one that waives waits. Need work now, pick a no-waiting plan. Planning an implant, choose a high annual maximum. Want adult braces, that is rare and narrows the field.

8. **How does CoverCapy verify dental coverage?**

It never invents which carriers an office takes, and it never stores your member ID, only that one was provided. Acceptance is sourced and dated, and you confirm it at the office.
