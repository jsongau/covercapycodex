# 01 — Research: Sponsored Dentist Listings

Audience: dentists and practice owners. Page sells transparent sponsored placement
inside the CoverCapy directory. All external facts below carry a source URL + 2026 access date.

## What "sponsored listing" means at CoverCapy (internal facts, from repo)
- CoverCapy is a luxury concierge PPO dental directory + SEO network (6,400+ pages).
  Source: repo `CLAUDE.md`, accessed 2026-06-26.
- Membership tiers control directory visibility and search radius:
  - `free` — $0, 8 mile search radius
  - `capy_accredited` — ~$285/mo founder rate, 10 mile radius
  - `platinum_elite` — $1,499/mo, 18 mile radius
  Source: repo `CLAUDE.md` (Membership Tiers table), accessed 2026-06-26.
- Ranking tiebreaker is the Capy Crowns score. Placement is influenced by accreditation status,
  profile completeness, local availability, patient trust indicators, and participation.
  CoverCapy does not promise a specific position or patient count.
  Source: repo `capy-accreditation.html` FAQ + disclosure block, accessed 2026-06-26.
- Sponsored / Featured Member placements are labeled as such and tie to the Advertising Disclosure.
  Source: repo `footer-preview.html` legal strip ("Some listings or links may be sponsored"),
  accessed 2026-06-26.

## External benchmarks used for "what sponsored visibility is worth" framing
These contextualize directory placement against the cost of buying the same patient elsewhere.

1. Average dental patient acquisition cost (PAC): $150–$350 per new patient; general dentistry
   $150–$300; cosmetic $250–$500; competitive metros $300–$500+.
   Source: Dentplicity, "Dental Patient Acquisition Cost Benchmarks (2026 Data)",
   https://dentplicity.com/blog/dental-patient-acquisition-cost-benchmarks — accessed 2026-06-26.
   Corroborated by Incept Health, "What's the Average CPA for New Dental Patients?",
   https://www.incept-health.com/insights/whats-the-average-cost-per-acquisition-cpa-for-new-dental-patients
   — accessed 2026-06-26.

2. Pay-per-booking marketplace fees (Zocdoc): $35–$110 per new-patient booking, charged at booking
   regardless of attendance; no upfront or subscription fee.
   Source: Zocdoc Help Center, "Understanding Zocdoc pricing and billing",
   https://www.zocdoc.com/provider-help/en/articles/10859404-understanding-zocdoc-pricing-and-billing
   — accessed 2026-06-26. Range corroborated by Zocdoc "Pay-Per-Booking Fees Explained",
   https://www.zocdoc.com/blog/pay-per-booking-fees-explained/ — accessed 2026-06-26.

3. Marketing budget norm: 4–7% of annual revenue for established practices, 8–10% for growth-stage.
   Source: VIZISITES, "Dental Marketing Budget Blueprint 2025",
   https://vizisites.com/how-much-do-dentists-spend-on-marketing/ — accessed 2026-06-26.

4. PAC has risen 25–40% since 2020 driven by Google Ads cost and keyword competition.
   Source: Dentplicity (same URL as #1) — accessed 2026-06-26.

## Competitor / positioning angle
- Generic directories (Zocdoc, Google LSAs, Yelp) sell clicks or bookings without context about
  whether the patient already holds active PPO coverage. CoverCapy's sponsored placement sits in
  front of patients who arrive through the PPO funnel, so the audience is pre-qualified.
- Transparency is the differentiator: every sponsored slot is labeled and tied to the public
  Advertising Disclosure, which matches FTC native-advertising guidance that paid placement be
  clearly and conspicuously identified.
  Source: FTC, "Native Advertising: A Guide for Businesses",
  https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/native-advertising-guide-businesses
  — accessed 2026-06-26 (cited as the disclosure principle, not a CoverCapy claim).

## Ranges only — no false precision
Present all dollar figures as ranges. Sponsored placement does not guarantee a position or a
number of patients; copy must say so (mirrors capy-accreditation.html disclosure language).
