## Section 2 — Where coverage comes from (horizontal bar breakdown, pure CSS)
Population coverage mix from NADP: 51% employer, 28% Medicaid/CHIP, 8% Medicare, 1% other public,
3% individual, 13% none. Render as a stacked/segmented CSS bar + legend. Note the 2024 decline of
2.3% and that it is the first reversal after a record.

## Section 3 — PPO vs DHMO (why PPO wins)
PPO = 89% of commercial enrollment. Explain why: choice of dentist, out-of-network flexibility,
in-network negotiated rates. DHMO smaller but some analysts project faster growth from a low base.
Tie to CoverCapy: we focus on PPO because that is where patients and dentists actually meet.

## Section 4 — The coverage gap, and why it exists
Four drivers (from research D): dental decoupled from medical; Medicare excludes routine dental;
life events end employer plans; voluntary share now >50% so cost shifts to the individual. Present
as a clean 4-item grid. This is the emotional/strategic core: gaps are life-event-driven, not lazy.

## Section 5 — Life-event-driven demand (timeline / list)
The moments that send people looking for cover: new job / job loss, turning 26, retirement and
Medicare, going 1099 / starting a business, a sudden treatment need (implant, crown, emergency).
This is exactly when "get cover today, see a dentist tomorrow" matters. Link to financing + estimator.

## Section 6 — Where the market is heading (trends)
No-waiting-period and direct-to-consumer individual plans; NY 2025 waiting-period removal; voluntary
and self-insured rising. Frame as the market moving toward the individual, which is the lane CoverCapy serves.

## Section 7 — Where CoverCapy fits
Plain statement: CoverCapy does not sell or underwrite insurance. It is a concierge layer: compare
PPO plans, find a PPO/Capy Accredited dentist, verify coverage, use the in-network rate. Three-step
"how it works" mini-flow. CTAs to compare + find-a-dentist + capy-accreditation.
