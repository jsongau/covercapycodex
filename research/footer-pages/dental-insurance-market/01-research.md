# 01 — Research evidence: US Dental Insurance Market

Every external figure below carries a source URL and an access date. Access date for all
items: 2026-06-26. Where market-research houses disagree, a range is given. Prefer the
primary sources (NADP, ADA HPI) for coverage counts and the analyst houses only for the
dollar-size of the market, clearly labeled as analyst estimates.

---

## A. Coverage counts (primary sources)

### NADP 2025 Dental Benefits Report (published Dec 9, 2025; 2024 data year)
- Nearly **284 million Americans** are covered by some form of dental benefit, about **83% of the population**.
- This is a **decline of 2.3%** from the prior year. Enrollment fell in BOTH commercial and public plans.
  - Commercial enrollment down 2.0% vs 2023; publicly funded benefits down 3.0%.
- Sources of coverage across the population surveyed:
  - **51% employer-sponsored**
  - **28% Medicaid / CHIP**
  - **8% Medicare**
  - **1% other public assistance**
  - **3% individual plans**
  - **13% no dental coverage**
- Commercial market structure:
  - **DPPO (PPO) products = 89% of commercial dental enrollment**, unchanged year over year; DPPO enrollment itself dipped 1.9% in 2024.
  - About **49% of commercial group dental is employer-sponsored vs 51% voluntary** (employee-paid).
  - Stand-alone dental plans remain the typical vehicle; only ~1.2% of commercial dental is integrated into medical.
  - ~46% of group dental is self-insured, up from 44%.
- Source: NADP via GlobeNewswire, "NADP Report Shows Continued Decline in Dental Benefits Enrollment," Dec 9, 2025.
  https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2025/12/09/3202727/0/en/NADP-Report-Shows-Continued-Decline-in-Dental-Benefits-Enrollment.html
  (Full report: https://www.nadp.org/nadp-report-shows-continued-decline-in-dental-benefits-enrollment/ )

### NADP earlier (2023 data year, record high)
- At year-end 2023, NADP reported a record ~290 million Americans (about 87%) covered. The 2024
  data shows the first reversal of that record, hence the "284M / 83%" current figure.
- Source: NADP, "NADP Research Reveals Record in Dental Coverage for Americans," Feb 1, 2024.
  https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2024/02/01/2822498/0/en/NADP-Research-Reveals-Record-in-Dental-Coverage-for-Americans.html

### ADA Health Policy Institute — the uninsured adult view
- About **27% of adults (~72 million people)** do not have dental insurance.
- Of US adults who lack general health insurance (~26 million), **more than four in five (83%) also lack dental coverage**.
- ~31% of Medicare and ~33% of Medicaid recipients lack dental coverage.
- Uninsured for dental is highest among adults 18–29 and 60+, and among lower-income and lower-education adults.
- By race/ethnicity, Hispanic adults most likely uninsured (~30%), Asian/Pacific Islander least (~19%).
- Sources:
  - ADA HPI "Coverage, Access & Outcomes": https://www.ada.org/resources/research/health-policy-institute/coverage-access-outcomes
  - Dental Tribune, "One in four Americans lacks dental insurance," 2025:
    https://us.dental-tribune.com/news/one-in-four-americans-lacks-dental-insurance-ada-urges-wider-preventive-coverage/
  - Oral Health Group, "27% of Americans don't have dental insurance," 2025:
    https://www.oralhealthgroup.com/dental-industry/27-of-americans-dont-have-dental-insurance-ada-urges-coverage-for-preventive-services-1003986963/

> Note on the two coverage numbers: NADP counts ALL ages and ALL benefit sources (commercial +
> public), giving ~83% covered / ~17% uncovered population-wide. ADA HPI's "27% / 72M" is an
> ADULTS-only figure. Both are correct for their denominator; the page presents both and labels
> the denominator each time. Do not blend them into a single contradictory stat.

## C. No-waiting-period / direct-to-consumer trend

- Individual / direct-to-consumer dental plans are a small but distinct slice (~3% of the
  population per NADP; ~4.8% of commercial-covered lives per NADP commentary).
- A clear product trend is **no-waiting-period and direct-to-consumer individual plans** from major
  carriers (Spirit, Humana Complete, Guardian Direct, Cigna Dental 1500, Aflac, Anthem Essential Choice).
  Higher premium typically buys shorter or no waiting period.
- Regulatory tailwind: **New York eliminated waiting periods for most adult services on
  individual stand-alone dental marketplace plans effective Jan 1, 2025.**
- Sources:
  - NY State of Health press release on stand-alone dental plan improvements:
    https://info.nystateofhealth.ny.gov/news/press-release-improvements-to-stand-alone-dental-plans
  - Humana Complete Dental (no waiting period PPO): https://www.humana.com/dental-insurance/complete-dental-plan
  - Guardian full coverage no waiting period: https://www.guardianlife.com/dental-insurance/full-coverage-no-waiting-period
  - Cigna Dental 1500: https://www.cigna.com/individuals-families/shop-plans/dental-insurance-plans/cigna-dental-1500

## D. Why coverage gaps exist (synthesis, sourced)

1. Dental is mostly **decoupled from medical** (only ~1.2% integrated per NADP), so losing a job,
   aging into Medicare, or buying a marketplace medical plan often leaves a person with NO dental.
2. **Medicare does not include routine dental** in Original Medicare; ~31% of Medicare recipients
   lack dental (ADA HPI). Medicare Advantage dental enrollment actually FELL 11.4% in 2024 (NADP).
3. **Life events** create coverage gaps: job change, retirement, turning 26 and aging off a parent's
   plan, divorce, starting a business, gig/1099 work. The voluntary/individual share is now over
   half of commercial group dental (51% voluntary per NADP), showing the burden is shifting to the individual.
4. The 2024 reversal (284M, down 2.3%) is the **first decline after a record**, so the gap is
   currently widening, not shrinking.
