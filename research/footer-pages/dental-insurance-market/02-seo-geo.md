## FAQ Q&A (these go verbatim into FAQPage schema; 40–60 words each, standalone-factual)
1. How many Americans have dental insurance?
   ~284 million Americans, about 83% of the population, had some form of dental benefit at the end of
   2024, according to the National Association of Dental Plans. That was a 2.3% decline from the prior
   year, the first drop after a record high near 290 million in 2023.

2. What percent of Americans have no dental insurance?
   Population-wide, about 13% have no dental coverage (NADP, 2024 data). Among adults specifically,
   the ADA Health Policy Institute estimates about 27%, roughly 72 million people, lack dental
   insurance. The two figures differ because one counts all ages and the other counts only adults.

3. How big is the US dental insurance market?
   Analysts estimate the US dental insurance market at roughly $95 billion to $100 billion in 2025
   and 2026, with mid to high single-digit annual growth projected into the next decade. These are
   analyst estimates and vary by methodology, so CoverCapy presents them as a range.

4. Do most people have PPO or DHMO dental plans?
   PPO plans dominate. Dental PPO products make up about 89% of commercial dental enrollment, a share
   unchanged year over year (NADP, 2024 data). DHMO plans are a smaller segment, though some analysts
   project DHMO to grow faster from a lower base.

5. Why do so many people lack dental insurance?
   Dental is usually separate from medical coverage, so it is easy to lose. Original Medicare excludes
   routine dental, job changes and retirement end employer plans, and adults age off a parent's plan at
   26. Over half of commercial group dental is now voluntary, meaning the cost increasingly falls on the individual.

6. How is the dental insurance market changing in 2026?
   Coverage dipped in 2024 for the first time after a record, individual and voluntary plans are taking a
   larger share, and carriers are expanding no-waiting-period, direct-to-consumer plans. New York removed
   waiting periods on most adult marketplace dental plans in 2025, a signal of where the market is heading.

7. Does CoverCapy sell dental insurance?
   No. CoverCapy is a concierge layer over the PPO dental market. It helps you compare PPO plans, then
   find a PPO or Capy Accredited dentist who treatment-plans around your in-network rates, so your
   coverage is used effectively. CoverCapy does not underwrite or issue insurance policies.
