## 1. All-in single-tooth implant cost, US, 2026

A complete single-tooth implant (surgical fixture plus abutment plus final crown)
typically costs **$3,000 to $6,000** in the United States in 2026. The commonly cited
average lands near **$4,500**. Several sources quote a tighter without-insurance band of
$3,000 to $5,000 for a standard case by a general dentist; the upper figures ($5,000 to
$6,000+) reflect specialists, premium implant systems, and high-cost metros.

**Important pricing trap:** advertised "$399" or "$1,000 implant" offers almost always
refer to the titanium fixture ONLY, not the finished tooth. Adding the required abutment
and crown brings the real total up by roughly $1,300 to $2,500. Treat any sub-$2,000
all-in quote as incomplete until the components are itemized.

## 2. Component-level cost table, single tooth, 2026

This is the core deliverable. "Typical" is the most commonly cited mid-point band, not a
single number. The implant fixture is only a fraction of the all-in price; the remainder
is surgical chair time, prosthetic fabrication, lab fees, and overhead.

| Component | Low | Typical | High | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Consultation + imaging (CBCT cone-beam scan) | $100 | $200 to $400 | $750 | Small field-of-view single-tooth scan $150 to $300; large full-head scan up to $750. Some all-in quotes fold this in. |
| Tooth extraction (only if a failing tooth is present) | $150 | $300 to $650 | $800+ | Simple extraction $150 to $300; surgical extraction higher. Not needed if the gap already exists. |
| Implant fixture (titanium post) | $1,000 | $1,500 to $2,500 | $3,000 | The surgically placed root. Premium systems (Straumann, Nobel Biocare) sit at the top of the band. |
| Surgical placement (often bundled into the fixture fee) | — | included to $1,000 | — | Many quotes bundle placement with the fixture; specialists itemize it separately. |
| Abutment (connector) | $400 | $500 to $800 | $1,000 | Stock vs custom; metal vs ceramic. Custom abutments cost more. |
| Crown — acrylic / PMMA | $800 | $1,000 to $1,300 | $1,500 | Lowest-cost material; shorter lifespan. |
| Crown — porcelain-fused-to-metal (PFM) | $1,200 | $1,400 to $1,800 | $2,000 | Mid-tier durability and aesthetics. |
| Crown — monolithic zirconia | $1,500 | $2,000 to $2,500 | $3,000 | Highest cost, best longevity (20 to 25+ years), stain-resistant. Usual best long-term value. |
| **All-in total (fixture + abutment + crown), standard case** | **$3,000** | **$4,000 to $4,500** | **$6,000** | Excludes extraction, bone graft, and sinus lift add-ons. |

**Add-ons that push a case above the standard band** (covered in depth by the existing
`01-research.md` pack; summarized here for completeness):
- Bone graft (socket preservation): roughly $300 to $1,200; complex grafts higher.
- Sinus lift (upper back teeth): roughly $1,500 to $2,500 per side, up to $5,000 complex.
- IV sedation / anesthesia: several hundred dollars where used.

## 3. Crown material comparison (the single biggest swing inside the restoration)

The crown is where a patient has the most direct control over cost. Material choice moves
the crown line by more than $1,500 on its own.

| Crown material | Cost band | Trade-off |
|---|---|---|
| Acrylic / PMMA | $800 to $1,500 | Cheapest, lowest durability, often used as a temporary or interim crown. |
| Porcelain-fused-to-metal | $1,200 to $2,000 | Good middle ground on cost, strength, and look. |
| Monolithic zirconia | $1,500 to $3,000 | Most expensive, longest lasting (20 to 25+ years), stain-resistant; usually the better long-term value especially on back (molar) teeth. |

Sources:

## 4. Regional variation across the US, 2026

City and state pricing varies more than the national average suggests. The spread is
roughly 20 to 50 percent between the cheapest and most expensive markets for the same
standard single-tooth case.

**Higher-cost states / metros (national average plus 10 to 50 percent):**
- High-cost states named across sources: Maine, New York, Connecticut, Oregon, Rhode
  Island, Maryland, District of Columbia, California, Massachusetts, Alaska, Hawaii.
- New Jersey, New York, and California run roughly 10 to 20 percent above the national
  average.
- Major metros (New York City, Los Angeles, Chicago) run roughly 20 to 50 percent higher.
  A Manhattan specialist can charge 30 to 50 percent more than a general dentist in a
  rural market for the same procedure.

**Lower-cost regions (national average minus 10 to 25 percent):**
- The Deep South generally runs below average. Louisiana is repeatedly cited as a
  below-average market, with a single implant around $3,000 to $5,000 in 2026, roughly 10
  to 25 percent under national averages.
- Rural areas across all regions tend to be lower than nearby metros.

**Illustrative metro band:** in Dallas, a single implant (fixture, abutment, crown) runs
roughly $3,500 to $6,500 in 2026, showing how a single major metro can span the entire
national range depending on provider and materials.

## 5. What drives the price spread

Five layered factors explain why the same procedure ranges from $3,000 to $6,000+:

1. **Geography and overhead.** Rent, staff salaries, and lab fees vary sharply by market.
   Metro overhead (NYC, LA, Chicago) pushes prices 20 to 50 percent above rural areas.
2. **Provider type.** General dentists charge less; specialists (oral surgeons,
   periodontists, prosthodontists) charge more, reflecting advanced training and complex-
   case capability. The same provider tier difference can move the fee 30 to 50 percent.
3. **Implant system.** Standard titanium fixtures sit at the low end; premium branded
   systems (Straumann, Nobel Biocare) and advanced digital planning sit at the high end.
4. **Restoration material.** Acrylic is cheapest; zirconia is the most expensive and
   longest-lasting. This alone swings the crown line by $1,500+.
5. **Procedural complexity.** CBCT imaging, extraction of a failing tooth, bone grafting,
   sinus lifts, and sedation each add hundreds to several thousand dollars. A simple case
   with an existing healthy gap sits at the bottom of the range; a case needing extraction
   plus graft plus sinus lift sits well above it.

The low end of the national range = lower-cost region, general dentist, standard titanium,
outsourced lab crown, no add-ons. The high end = metro specialist, premium system,
zirconia crown, plus one or more surgical add-ons.

## 6. Premium vs budget clinic pricing, 2026

The same single-tooth implant carries very different list prices depending on the clinic
model. The premium tier costs more largely because quotes are all-inclusive (imaging,
surgery, anesthesia, restoration bundled), which reduces billing surprises.

| Provider model | Single implant price band | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| ClearChoice (premium, specialist-led, all-inclusive) | $5,000 to $7,500 | Highest end; quotes bundle imaging, surgery, anesthesia, and final restoration. |
| Aspen Dental (large chain, budget-friendly) | $3,158 to $6,533 (avg ~$4,259) | Wide range; positioned as a more affordable national option. |
| General dentist, lower-cost region | $3,000 to $5,000 | Bottom of the national band for a standard case. |
| Metro specialist, premium system + zirconia | $5,000 to $6,000+ | Top of the national band before surgical add-ons. |

Takeaway for CoverCapy copy: an all-inclusive quote that looks more expensive is not
necessarily a worse deal; itemized "budget" quotes can climb once the abutment, crown,
imaging, and add-ons are added back. Always compare on an all-in basis.

## 7. Overseas and dental-tourism context (note risks, do NOT recommend)

Single-tooth implants are markedly cheaper abroad, which is why patients ask about it.
This section exists to inform, not to recommend overseas care. CoverCapy positioning is
PPO coverage with a US in-network dentist, so frame overseas pricing as context plus a
clear list of trade-offs.

**Price comparison (2026):**
- Mexico: roughly $650 to $1,000 per single implant, versus $3,000 to $5,000 in the US.
- Costa Rica: typically 50 to 70 percent below US pricing (some procedures up to 80
  percent lower).

**Risks and trade-offs to surface (do not bury these):**
- **Follow-up gap.** Overseas implants usually require two trips months apart. If a
  complication appears after returning home, the original clinic is not nearby, and US
  dentists are often reluctant to revise another provider's work.
- **Variable quality and oversight.** Top clinics use the same implant systems as US
  practices, but quality varies widely between clinics; verifying credentials is on the
  patient.
- **Limited legal recourse** if something goes wrong abroad.
- **No insurance offset.** Most US dental plans exclude care performed outside the
  network, especially outside the country, so the patient typically pays 100 percent of
  overseas treatment and any later corrective work at home.

CoverCapy angle: the overseas discount is real, but it trades away follow-up access,
recourse, and any insurance offset. A US PPO plan plus an in-network dentist keeps care,
warranty, and benefits in one place.

## 8. Quick figures for page copy (all ranges, GEO-extractable)

- Single-tooth implant, all-in, US 2026: **$3,000 to $6,000** (average near $4,500).
- Fixture: $1,000 to $3,000. Abutment: $400 to $1,000. Crown: $800 to $3,000 by material.

  insurance trade-offs.
