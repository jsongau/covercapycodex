## TL;DR / quick answer block (GEO)
"A single dental implant typically costs $3,000 to $6,000 all in. Most PPO plans pay 20 to
60 percent after a waiting period, capped by the annual maximum and sometimes blocked by a
missing-tooth clause, so plan on $1,500 to $3,000 out of pocket even with coverage."

## Section: What you pay for (the components)
Prose intro that an implant is staged, each stage billed separately. Cost table (from
01-research):  extraction, bone graft, post, abutment, crown, single-tooth total, full
arch. Note metro premium of 20 to 40 percent.

## Section: What drives the price
Bulleted: geography/metro, provider type (oral surgeon vs general dentist), materials
(titanium vs zirconia, porcelain crown grade), preparatory surgery (graft, sinus lift,
extraction), number of implants / full-arch design.

## Section: Full mouth and All-on-4
Short prose + mini table: per arch acrylic, per arch zirconia, both arches. Note more
implants per arch adds cost.

## Section: With vs without PPO insurance
Explain implants are a "major" service. Coverage table from the SSOT (Ameritas, Humana,
Guardian, Delta) with exact rates, waits, caps. Call out the two plans that exclude
implants (Aetna Direct, UHC Primary). Two binding constraints: annual maximum and
missing-tooth clause. Worked example: $4,500 implant, 50% coverage, $1,500 annual max → plan
pays $1,500, you owe ~$3,000. Honor every do_not trap.

## Section: The missing-tooth clause (callout)
Dedicated callout box. If the tooth was already missing before coverage, many plans pay
nothing toward replacing it. Check before enrolling. Link to /compare-ppo-dental-plans.

## Section: How to pay less (savings)
Bulleted strategies: stay in-network, stage across two benefit years for two annual
maximums, get a predetermination of benefits, choose a no-missing-tooth-clause plan, buy
coverage before the gap exists. Link estimator + compare.

## Section: Financing
CareCredit promo (note deferred interest at ~32.99% if not paid in full), in-house plans,
third-party lenders (Cherry et al., some no hard credit check), HSA/FSA. Link to
/dental-financing-monthly-payments.
