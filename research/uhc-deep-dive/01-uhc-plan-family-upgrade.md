# UHC / UnitedHealthOne (Golden Rule) Individual Dental — Full Plan Family + Upgrade Question

Research file for CoverCapy. Carrier: UnitedHealthcare individual dental, underwritten by **Golden Rule Insurance Company** (AM Best A+), administered by **Dental Benefit Providers, Inc.** Sold at uhone.com / shop.uhone.com. Network: **UnitedHealthcare Dental** ("National Options PPO 30").

Researched 2026-06-26. Where a fact comes from an agent-built brochure vs a third-party review, the source is named inline. Treat brochure (uhone.com PDF) facts as primary; treat third-party reviews (Money, ilhealthagents) as secondary/corroborating and note any conflicts.

---

## 0. SCOPE NOTE — naming has changed over time

There are TWO product "series" of UHC individual dental plans for adults under 65, plus a senior line:

1. **Dental Primary series** (4 plans): Primary, Primary Plus, Primary Preferred, Primary Preferred Plus.
2. **Dental Premier series** (4 plans): Premier Choice, Premier Elite, Premier Plus, Premier Max.
3. **Senior Dental (Dental Gen / "65+") line** (4 plans): Saver, Plus, Basic, Deluxe — for ages 64+, no waiting periods. (Out of scope for the under-65 shelf but noted for completeness.)

So Money's statement "UHC offers 12 dental insurance plans: eight policies without age restrictions and four plans designed for adults aged 64 and over" matches: 8 under-65 (4 Primary + 4 Premier) + 4 senior. (Source: Money review, money.com/unitedhealthcare-dental-insurance-review, pub Apr 2023.)

**IMPORTANT naming caveat:** an older agent page (ilhealthagents.com, modified 2025-05) also shows products literally named **"Plan 1000"** and **"Plan 2000"** ($100 deductible; Plan 2000 covers implants 50% after deductit with 12-mo wait, $1,500 lifetime implant max). These appear to be a different/legacy or state-variant packaging. The current uhone.com brochures and the current official plan pages use the Primary / Premier naming, NOT "Plan 1000/2000". Do not surface "Plan 1000/2000" as current national products without re-verifying; flag as possibly legacy/state-specific.

## 1. THE FULL UNDER-65 PLAN FAMILY (8 plans)

All plans, every series:
- Preventive: **100% day one, no deductible, no waiting period** (2 routine exams + cleanings per calendar year; bitewing X-rays 1 series/cal year; panoramic 1 per 36 months).
- Deductible: **$50 per person** (family max 3 deductibles). On Primary/Primary Plus the $50 applies to basic only; on Preferred and all Premier plans it is combined basic + major.
- Eligibility: primary insured **age 18–64 at application**; spouse any age; children 0–25 (drop at 26).
- "Year one / year two" coinsurance steps are by **POLICY year** (plan anniversary). Annual maximum and deductible reset by **CALENDAR year** (Jan 1).

## 2. WHICH PLANS COVER IMPLANTS / MAJOR WORK — vs PRIMARY

- **Major work (crowns, root canals, bridges, dentures, oral surgery):** covered by **6 of 8** plans — Primary Preferred, Primary Preferred Plus, and all four Premier plans. NOT covered by Primary or Primary Plus.
- **DENTAL IMPLANTS:** covered by **exactly ONE** plan — **Premier Plus**. Brochure, "Premier Plus Plans only: Implants — covered under Major Services and subject to annual maximum — 1 time per tooth per 60 months." Money review: Premier Plus pays 50% of implants after year two, up to the $2,000 annual max. (Surgical implant example in brochure: retail $2,131.63; you pay $972 yr1 / $648 yr2.) **Implants are an explicit EXCLUSION on every other UHC individual dental plan**, including the $3,000 Premier Max.
- **ORTHODONTICS:** covered by **only Premier Plus**, and only for **dependents under 19**: 50% after 12-month wait, separate $150 lifetime ortho deductible, **$1,000 lifetime ortho max**. No adult ortho on any UHC individual plan.

**Compared to Primary Dental** (the plan in our SSOT): Primary = preventive 100% + basic only, $1,000 max, NO major, NO implants, NO ortho. To get **major work**, the cheapest step up is **Primary Preferred** (still $1,000 max, major at 60% by yr2 after a 6-mo wait). To get a **higher annual max** with major, go Premier Choice ($1,500), Premier Elite ($2,000), or Premier Max ($3,000). To get **implants or child braces**, the ONLY option is **Premier Plus** ($2,000 max; implants 50% by yr2; ortho $1,000 lifetime for kids).

Quick "best of" map (from Money review):
- Shortest basic wait: **Premier Max** (4 months).
- 6-month major wait (vs industry 12): Premier Elite, Primary Preferred, Primary Preferred Plus.
- 60% major coinsurance (vs typical 50%): Primary Preferred, Primary Preferred Plus, Premier Max.
- Only implants + braces: **Premier Plus**.
- Highest max ($3,000): **Premier Max** (but no implants/braces).

## 3. THE UPGRADE QUESTION — can a Primary member move up to a higher plan?

**What IS documented (official + corroborated):**
- **No open enrollment window.** UHC individual dental is sold and can be applied for **year-round** — "Both dental insurance plans and non-insurance dental discount products are available all year round. You don't have to wait to apply or subscribe." (Official: uhone.com/health-insurance/supplemental/dental-insurance, FAQ, fetched 2026-06-26.) There is no annual lock-in; a member is not restricted to a once-a-year change window.
- **Each plan is a separate policy / separate application.** UHONE's site-wide disclosure states: "All products require separate applications. Separate policies or certificates are issued." (Official footer, same page.) The brochure's "Conditions Prior To Coverage" section describes coverage beginning only when **a new application is completed, accepted/approved by Golden Rule, and first premium paid** — i.e., the mechanism for getting a different plan is a **new application for that plan**, issued as a new policy.
- **Renewability is one-directional / guaranteed renewable on the SAME form.** The policy "is renewable until" death/non-payment/member-requested termination/carrier non-renewal of that form/fraud. There is no documented "in-policy tier upgrade" rider — the contract renews the plan you bought; it does not contain a clause to swap your Primary policy into a Premier policy mid-term.

**What is NOT documented (state this plainly — do NOT guess):**
- **There is NO official UHONE/Golden Rule page that describes a formal "upgrade from Primary to Premier" process, an in-plan upgrade button, or whether switching resets waiting periods.** The brochures, the official dental landing page, and the official plan pages are all silent on member-initiated plan-to-plan changes. WebSearch across uhone.com surfaced nothing on existing-member plan changes; the search assistant likewise found "no specific details about whether existing members can switch... or whether changing plans would reset waiting periods" and recommended calling Golden Rule (1-800-657-8205 member / 1-800-445-9090) or an agent.

**Best-supported INFERENCE (label as inference, not a quoted fact):**
Because each plan is a separate Golden Rule policy issued on its own application with its own "Conditions Prior To Coverage," moving from Primary to a higher plan almost certainly means **applying for the new plan as a new policy (and cancelling the old one), NOT an in-place upgrade.** A new policy has its own effective date, so the new plan's **waiting periods and policy-year coinsurance steps would run from that new effective date** — i.e., a member who already sat out, say, a major-services wait on a Preferred plan would generally **start the new plan's clocks over**, and the policy-year coinsurance graduation (the 65%/80% / 50%/60% steps) would restart at "year one." This is the standard behavior of separately-issued Golden Rule dental policies and is consistent with the contract language, but **it is not stated verbatim on any official page**, so on a CoverCapy page it must be framed as "generally" / "typically" with a "confirm with the carrier" caveat — never as a quoted guarantee.

**Bottom line for the page:** "You can change plans any time of year (no open enrollment), but moving up from Primary is done by applying for the higher plan as a new Golden Rule policy, not an in-plan toggle. Because it's a new policy, expect the new plan's waiting periods and coinsurance year-counts to start fresh. UHC does not publish a formal upgrade process; confirm specifics with the carrier before switching." Everything after "no open enrollment" past the new-application point is supported by contract structure but is NOT an official upgrade-policy statement — keep it hedged.

## 4. BROCHURE DEEP-DIVE — facts worth surfacing on a page

**Frequency / quantity limits (all plans):**
- Routine exams + cleanings: **2 per calendar year.**
- Bitewing X-rays: **1 series per calendar year.** Full-mouth/panoramic X-ray: **1 per 36 months.**
- Child preventive (under 16; under 19 in IL): fluoride 2x/cal year; sealants once per 1st/2nd permanent molar per 36 months; space maintainers once per 60 months.
- Root canals: 1 per tooth per **lifetime**. Crowns: 1 per tooth per **60 months**. Full dentures: 1 per **60 months**. Bridges: 1 per **60 months**. Surgical extraction of erupted tooth/roots: 1 per tooth per lifetime. Implants (Premier Plus): 1 per tooth per **60 months**.

**Missing-tooth / pre-existing clause (Preferred + Premier major plans):** No benefit for "Initial placement of full or partial dentures or bridges... to replace functional natural teeth that are **congenitally missing or lost before insurance under the policy is in effect**." This is the classic **missing-tooth exclusion** — teeth lost before the policy started are not covered for replacement. Worth flagging for anyone shopping for bridge/denture/implant coverage to replace an already-missing tooth.

**Network:** "National Options PPO 30" (UnitedHealthcare Dental). Money review cites **100,000+ dental providers**; plans pay non-network benefits **based on the network negotiated rate** (Primary Plus / Preferred Plus / non-Plus variants differ on whether non-network pays at negotiated vs reasonable-and-customary). Non-network dentists may **balance-bill** the patient up to billed charges. Available in 45+ states incl. AK & HI; **NOT available in New York** or US territories (Money). Some plans not sold in certain states (e.g., in CO/MN only Preferred + Preferred Plus; in ME only Primary + Primary Plus, or Premier Choice + Premier Plus).

**Notable exclusions (all plans):** surgical extraction of **wisdom teeth**; cosmetic dentistry; **TMJ / jaw surgery / orthognathic**; nitrous oxide / conscious sedation; bone grafts/guided-tissue regeneration in edentulous areas; orthodontics or implants "unless included in your plan"; out-of-US except emergency; occlusal/night guards.

**Pricing (secondary):** Money review (Miami ZIP sample) found UHC dental premiums ranging **~$30 to ~$59/mo** depending on plan, age, ZIP, tobacco. Consistent with our SSOT's "~$30/mo entry estimate" for Primary as the floor and the higher Premier tiers at the top. uhone.com does NOT print premiums on plan pages (quote tool only) — keep all premium numbers labeled as estimates/ranges.

**CONFLICT to flag (basic-tier coinsurance):** The official brochure states basic on Primary as "policy pays 50% day one / 65% yr1 / 80% yr2" (member pays 50% → 35% → 20%). The Money review states Primary basic as "policyholder pays 35% year one, 20% after" with no day-one 50% step, and gives different numbers for Primary Preferred basic ("pays 65% yr1"). These don't fully reconcile — the brochure is the primary source and matches our SSOT (50/65/80 plan-pays graduation). Use brochure numbers; treat Money's per-plan coinsurance as approximate.
