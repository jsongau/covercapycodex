# Delta Dental Individual PPO Plans (Basic and Premium)

**One-line summary:** Delta Dental sells two individual/family PPO products, Basic (~$33/mo, $1,000 max, preventive plus basic only) and Premium (~$73/mo, $2,000 max, full coverage including major work and orthodontics), across 16 states plus DC, with calendar-year maximums and deductibles, state-varying waiting periods, and benefit details that differ meaningfully by underwriting entity and state.

Researched June 2026. All figures verified against the sources listed at the bottom. Where sources disagree, the conflict is flagged inline.

---

## 1. Delta Dental PPO Individual Premium

Verified against the DentalPlans.com Premium plan page and the official deltadentalins.com PPO comparison page.

### Cost and activation
- Premium starting at **$73.11/month** ($877.32/year) per the DentalPlans.com listing. The task brief's "~$73-75/mo" range is consistent with this. (Estimate: actual premium varies by age and ZIP; DentalPlans.com states "Prices may vary by age and ZIP code.")
- **Earliest activation in 9 days** per DentalPlans.com. Delta's own shop flow lets you "select from multiple start dates"; common practice is choosing the 1st or 15th of a month as the effective date. (The specific "1st/15th" date options are stated in the brief and are consistent with Delta's multi-start-date enrollment, but the exact selectable dates were not enumerated on the public marketing pages reviewed.)
- Enroll year-round (no open-enrollment restriction).

### Deductibles and maximums (per calendar year, resets January 1)
- **Calendar-year deductible:** $50 per enrollee / $150 per family.
- **Annual maximum:** $2,000 per enrollee, per calendar year. Both DentalPlans.com and deltadentalins.com confirm "$2,000/person" and "per calendar year."
- **Separate orthodontic deductible:** $50 per enrollee (distinct from the medical/dental deductible).
- **Orthodontic lifetime maximum:** $1,500 per enrollee.

### Coverage tiers (in-network, after deductible where applicable)
Source: DentalPlans.com Premium benefits table.

| Service category | Coverage | Waiting period |
|---|---|---|
| Diagnostic & Preventive (exams, cleanings, x-rays, sealants) | 100% | None |
| Basic (fillings, emergency pain treatment) | 80% | 6 months |
| Endodontics (root canals) | 50% | 12 months |
| Periodontics (gum treatments) | 50% | 12 months |
| Oral surgery (extractions) | 50% | 12 months |
| Prosthodontics (bridges, dentures, implants) | 50% | 12 months |
| Major (crowns, inlays, onlays, cast restorations) | 50% | 12 months |
| Orthodontics (adults and dependent children) | 50% | 12 months |
| Cosmetic (teeth whitening, night/mouth guards) | 80% (see Section 4 caveat) | listed under basic |

Notes:
- In-network and out-of-network coinsurance percentages are listed as identical on the DentalPlans.com table (e.g. 80% / 80%), but out-of-network reimbursement is calculated against Delta's lower maximum contract allowance, so real out-of-pocket cost is higher out of network. The official Virginia PDF shows preventive at 100% in-network but **80% out-of-network** for adults, confirming network does matter even where the headline percentage looks equal.
- Orthodontics covers **both adults and dependent children** at 50% on the Premium plan.

### Missing-tooth clause
- No missing-tooth exclusion is listed in the Premium benefit summaries reviewed, and several Delta Dental affiliates (e.g. Delta Dental of Iowa) state they do **not** apply a missing-tooth exclusion on individual plans.
- **Flag / confirm by state:** this is not universal. Industry sources note Delta "often includes a missing tooth clause" depending on group/contract, and language varies by underwriting entity. For a national hub, present "no missing-tooth clause" as **state-dependent, verify locally**, not as a guaranteed feature.

## 2. Delta Dental PPO Individual Basic (how it differs)

Verified against the DentalPlans.com Basic plan page and the deltadentalins.com comparison table.

### Cost
- Basic starting at **$32.75/month** ($393.00/year) per DentalPlans.com. (Estimate: varies by age and ZIP.)
- Same 9-day earliest activation.

### Deductibles and maximums (per calendar year)
- **Calendar-year deductible:** $50 per enrollee / $150 per family (same as Premium).
- **Annual maximum:** **$1,000 per enrollee** (half of Premium's $2,000).
- **No orthodontic deductible or lifetime maximum** (N/A).

### Coverage tiers (in-network)
Source: DentalPlans.com Basic benefits table and deltadentalins.com comparison.

| Service category | Basic plan coverage | Waiting period |
|---|---|---|
| Diagnostic & Preventive | 100% | None |
| Basic (fillings, emergency pain treatment) | 50% (Premium is 80%) | 6 months |
| Oral surgery (simple extractions only) | 50% | 6 months (basic tier) |
| Periodontics | Not a benefit | n/a |
| Endodontics / root canals (major) | Not a benefit | n/a |
| Prosthodontics (bridges, dentures, implants) | Not a benefit | n/a |
| Major (crowns, inlays, onlays) | Not a benefit | n/a |
| Orthodontics | Not a benefit | n/a |
| Teeth whitening / cosmetic | Not a benefit | n/a |

### Key differences vs Premium, summarized
1. Annual max is $1,000 vs $2,000.
2. Basic services pay 50% vs 80%.
3. No major services at all (no crowns, root canals, perio, prostho, implants).
4. No orthodontics (no ortho deductible, no $1,500 lifetime max).
5. No cosmetic/whitening benefit.
6. Roughly 45% of the Premium monthly cost ($32.75 vs $73.11).

The Basic plan is positioned by Delta as "wellness plus emergencies": exams, cleanings, fillings, and non-surgical/simple extractions.

## 3. State availability

Individual Delta Dental PPO plans (both Basic and Premium) are offered in **16 states plus DC**, per the official deltadentalins.com PPO page:

**AL, CA, DE, FL, GA, LA, MD, MS, MT, NV, NY, PA, TX, UT, WV, and DC.** This exactly matches the brief.

Underwriting entity varies by state (from the official disclaimer):
- Underwritten by **Delta Dental Insurance Company** in: AL, DC (Policy IENT-P-CORE-DC-R26 on the current page; the DentalPlans.com page still cites the older R22 policy form, see conflict note), FL, GA, LA, MS, MT, NV, UT.
- Underwritten by not-for-profit dental service companies in: **CA** (Delta Dental of California); **PA, MD** (Delta Dental of Pennsylvania); **NY** (Delta Dental of New York, Inc.); **DE** (Delta Dental of Delaware, Inc.); **WV** (Delta Dental of West Virginia, Inc.).
- In **Texas**, it is a Dental Provider Organization (DPO) plan, not a true PPO, though marketed under the PPO product line.

Residents of non-listed states are routed to deltadental.com for other affiliate products. Specific benefit details "may vary by state."

## 4. Whitening / cosmetic coverage (do NOT present as guaranteed)

This is a genuine source conflict that the hub must handle carefully.

- **DentalPlans.com Premium page** lists **Cosmetic Services (teeth whitening and mouth/night guards) at 80%**, in network and out.
- **deltadentalins.com comparison table** lists **Teeth whitening: 80%** on Premium, "not covered" on Basic.
- **BUT the state policy PDFs explicitly exclude cosmetics.** The Delta Dental of Virginia Basic policy exclusions state: "Expenses for services or supplies that are cosmetic in nature, including charges for personalization or characterization of dentures" are **not covered**.

**Conclusion / instruction for the hub:** Whitening/cosmetic coverage is **state-specific and product-specific**. The national marketing pages show 80% on Premium, but individual state policy documents carve cosmetics out as an exclusion. Do not present "80% whitening" as a guaranteed, universal Delta benefit. Frame it as: "Some Delta Premium plans list cosmetic/whitening at 80%, but coverage varies by state and is excluded under some state policies. Verify against the specific state policy."

## 5. Waiting periods and waiver rules

### Standard waiting periods (Premium)
- Diagnostic & Preventive: none.
- Basic: 6 months.
- Major / endo / perio / oral surgery / prostho / implants: 12 months.
- Orthodontics: 12 months.

The deltadentalins.com comparison page repeatedly labels basic and major waiting periods as "(varies by state)," so the 6/12-month figures from DentalPlans.com are the typical structure but not guaranteed identical in every state.

### Waiver of waiting periods
- Waiting periods can be **waived with proof of prior comparable (creditable) coverage**.
- Reported rule: the waiting period is waived if you had comparable dental coverage (e.g. an employer-sponsored plan with equivalent benefits) immediately before enrolling, generally with **no more than a 60- to 63-day gap** between the prior plan ending and the new plan starting. (The orthodontia-specific waiver example cited a 63-day maximum gap and a 12-month continuous prior-coverage requirement.)
- For orthodontics specifically: the 12-month ortho waiting period is waived only if all family members were covered under another insured dental plan **with orthodontic coverage** for at least 12 continuous months before enrolling, with no more than a 63-day gap.
- **Pro-ration:** credit toward the waiting period is pro-rated against the length of prior comparable coverage, so partial prior coverage yields partial waiver rather than full waiver. (Estimate / general rule: exact pro-ration mechanics vary by state policy; verify per policy document.)

## 6. Conflicting figures flagged

1. **Basic plan structure conflict (significant).** The Virginia "Delta Dental PPO Plus Premier Individual and Family Basic Plan" PDF is materially different from the DentalPlans.com national "PPO Basic":
   - The VA PDF's headline (PPO Plus Premier) tier shows **$75/person deductible, $225/family, $425 per-person out-of-pocket limit, NO annual maximum, no waiting periods, basic at 80%, and major services (root canals, perio, crowns, prostho) covered at 50%.**
   - A second embedded VA schedule (adult/standard PPO) shows **$50/$150 deductible, $1,000 annual max, basic at 50% with a 6-month wait, and NO major services** which matches the DentalPlans.com national Basic.
   - Takeaway: "Basic" is not one fixed product. Some state filings bundle a richer PPO Plus Premier dual-network Basic that DOES include major work at 50% and has no annual max but an OOP limit instead. The national DentalPlans.com summary (no major coverage, $1,000 max) is the conservative/typical version. **The hub must not state a single universal Basic benefit grid.**
2. **DC policy form number conflict (minor).** DentalPlans.com cites DC policy **IENT-P-CORE-DC-R22**; the current deltadentalins.com page cites **IENT-P-CORE-DC-R26**. The R26 (deltadentalins.com) form is the more recent/authoritative version; DentalPlans.com is showing a stale form number.
3. **Cosmetic/whitening conflict** documented in Section 4 (marketing pages say 80%, state policy PDFs exclude cosmetics).
4. **In-network vs out-of-network percentages** appear identical on DentalPlans.com tables (e.g. 100%/100%, 80%/80%) but the VA state PDF shows preventive at 100% in-network and 80% out-of-network, confirming the DentalPlans.com equal-percentage display is misleading on reimbursement basis.

## Implications for the hub

- **Use Premium as the flagship/anchor product**: $2,000 calendar-year max, 100% preventive with no wait, 80% basic (6-mo wait), 50% major and orthodontics (12-mo wait), $1,500 ortho lifetime, separate $50 ortho deductible, ~$73/mo. These are the cleanest, best-verified figures and map directly to the CoverCapy "verify my PPO" value proposition.
- **Position Basic as the budget entry point** (~$33/mo, $1,000 max, preventive plus fillings only) but do NOT promise major work or orthodontics on Basic, and explicitly note that some state versions (PPO Plus Premier Basic) differ.
- **Geo-gate everything to the 16 states plus DC.** For CoverCapy SEO pages outside AL/CA/DE/FL/GA/LA/MD/MS/MT/NV/NY/PA/TX/UT/WV/DC, do not claim Delta PPO individual availability; route to a generic "check your state" path.
- **Never hardcode whitening at 80% or "no missing-tooth clause" as guaranteed.** Both are state/policy dependent. Add a "varies by state, verify on policy" qualifier wherever these appear, to avoid the overconfident-claim problem flagged elsewhere in this project.
- **Surface the waiting-period waiver** (proof of prior comparable coverage, 60-63 day max gap, pro-rated) as a conversion lever: it lets switchers skip the 6/12-month waits, which is a strong reason to verify and enroll now.
- **Treat DentalPlans.com figures as the typical national grid but defer to state policy PDFs for legal accuracy.** Flag in build notes that Basic is not a single fixed product across states.
