# Delta Dental: Practical Usage (Finding a Dentist, Claims, Costs)

**One-line summary:** This file explains, from official Delta Dental sources, how a member actually uses a Delta Dental PPO plan day to day: choosing a PPO vs Premier vs out-of-network dentist, getting pre-treatment estimates, how claims and reimbursement work, when coverage starts, the digital tools members use, and how cancellation and calendar-year resets behave.

---

## 1. Finding a dentist: PPO vs Premier vs out-of-network

A Delta Dental PPO plan gives a member access to two contracted networks, plus the option of any non-contracted dentist:

- **Delta Dental PPO network.** Dentists agree to set fees in their Delta Dental contract and cannot charge PPO members more than that fee. Members generally pay the least out of pocket here and their annual maximum stretches furthest.
- **Delta Dental Premier network.** A larger, "safety net" network. Premier dentists also agree to set contracted fees, so PPO members still get cost protection, but the contracted Premier fees are typically higher than PPO fees, so the member usually pays somewhat more out of pocket than at a PPO dentist.
- **Non-Delta Dental (out-of-network) dentists.** These dentists have no contract with Delta Dental and can set their prices freely, so this is generally the most expensive option.

Delta Dental frames this as a tier system: members typically save the most at a PPO dentist, a little less at a Premier dentist, and the least at a non-Delta Dental dentist.

**Balance billing, by network.** Balance billing is when a dentist bills the patient for the difference between the dentist's usual fee and the contracted amount. Because both PPO and Premier dentists are contractually capped at their agreed fee, a PPO member is protected from balance billing at both PPO and Premier dentists. At a non-Delta Dental dentist there is no such cap: the member is responsible for the difference between what Delta Dental allows and what the dentist charges (see Section 3).

**Confirming a dentist is contracted for the EXACT product (PPO, not just Premier).** This is the single most important verification step, because many dentists participate in Premier but not PPO. Most PPO dentists are also in Premier, but the reverse is not true. To confirm the specific product:

1. Use the **Find a Dentist** tool, enter the location, and use the **Select a network** drop-down to choose the specific network (for example, Delta Dental PPO). Each listing shows the exact networks that dentist participates in.
2. Or log in to the member account and search by dentist or practice name within the member's own plan network, which filters to the networks that apply to that member's specific plan.

A dentist appearing under a Premier search has not been confirmed for PPO. The member should verify the PPO product specifically, ideally both through the directory and by confirming with the dental office, because a "Delta Dental dentist" who is Premier-only will be reimbursed at Premier (or out-of-network) terms, not PPO terms.

## 2. Pre-treatment estimates (predeterminations)

A pre-treatment estimate (also called a predetermination or pre-authorization) gives the member an approximate total cost for a proposed treatment and shows how that cost is expected to split between the plan and the member.

**How to get one:**

1. The member discusses the proposed treatment with the dentist and confirms what is being recommended, why, and what alternatives exist.
2. The dentist submits the proposed treatment plan plus any supporting x-rays to Delta Dental (dentists can submit through their Online Services account using the Submit Claim or My Patients tool and selecting "Pre-Treatment Estimate," or by mailing a paper claim with documentation).
3. Delta Dental reviews and returns the estimate, usually within a few days; complex plans can take longer.
4. Delta Dental responds to both the member and the dentist with the estimated cost.

**Why it matters, especially for big cases.** Estimates are not binding. Things can change between the estimate and the actual treatment that change the final cost, for example a change in coverage, hitting the annual maximum, or aging out of a procedure that has an age limit. For large or expensive cases (crowns, bridges, implants, orthodontics, full treatment plans), getting a predetermination first lets the member see the likely out-of-pocket amount, confirm the procedure is covered, and check that enough annual maximum remains before committing.

## 3. Claims, reimbursement, and what the patient pays

**At a contracted (PPO or Premier) dentist:**

- The dentist files the claim directly. There are no claim forms for the member to fill out.
- Delta Dental pays the dentist directly, so the member does not pay the full cost up front and then wait for reimbursement.
- The member pays only their share (deductible plus coinsurance, up to the annual maximum), based on the contracted fee.

**At a non-Delta Dental (out-of-network) dentist:**

- The member is typically charged the full price up front, even if their coinsurance is less than the full amount, because the dentist is not always paid directly by Delta Dental.
- The member then submits a claim to Delta Dental and receives a reimbursement check by mail for the plan's share.
- The plan's payment is based on the **maximum contract allowance** (also called the maximum plan allowance or reimbursement amount), not the dentist's actual fee. For some plans, out-of-network reimbursement is based on a percentile of area fees (for example, a plan that reimburses at the 90th percentile would calculate its payment from that figure, then apply the coinsurance percentage).
- The member is responsible for the **difference between the maximum contract allowance and the dentist's submitted fee**, on top of any deductible and coinsurance. Because a non-contracted dentist can charge whatever they want, this gap can be significant.

**About the allowances themselves.** Allowances differ by network (Delta Dental PPO allowances differ from Premier allowances in most cases) and vary by 5-digit ZIP code. Delta Dental reviews and updates these allowances on an ongoing basis, usually every 6 to 24 months, and updates often must first be filed for approval with state regulators. The plan's payment percentage (coinsurance) is a separate factor layered on top of the allowance.

## 4. Effective dates and enrollment

Delta Dental PPO individual and family plans offer **four effective-date choices** at purchase, built around the 1st and the 15th of the month. The earliest available start is roughly 9 days out (the 15th of the current month when buying early in the month).

- **Purchased the 1st through the 14th of the month:** the member can choose an effective date of the 15th of the current month, the 1st of next month, the 15th of next month, or the 1st of the month after next.
- **Purchased on or after the 15th of the month:** the member can choose the 1st of next month, the 15th of next month, the 1st of the month after next, or the 15th of the month after next.

This lets a member pick a future start date (for example, to align with the end of other coverage). PPO individual plans can be enrolled year-round. (Note: DeltaCare USA, a separate DHMO product, works differently, with a fixed effective date driven by a 21st-of-the-month cutoff; that is not the PPO experience.)

## 5. ID cards, member portal, mobile app, customer service

Members manage the plan through a Delta Dental online account, where they can:

- Check benefits and coverage
- Find a dentist within their own plan's network
- Track claims
- Pay premiums
- View and print their ID card

Because contracted dentists handle identification and claims directly with Delta Dental, members generally do not need to carry a paper ID card, but a printable card is available from the portal for anyone who wants a physical copy. Delta Dental also offers a mobile app for managing benefits from a phone, and customer service is available by phone for questions the portal does not answer.

## 6. Cancellation, changing plans, and calendar-year resets

**Making changes after purchase.** After buying an individual plan, a member generally has up to 60 days from the effective date to make changes, though some changes can affect the plan rate.

**Cancellation.** To cancel, the member contacts Delta Dental. The specific cancellation terms are in the member's plan Policy and Benefit Details or Evidence of Coverage, which govern the exact rules.

**Calendar-year reset behavior.** Two figures reset on a benefit-period (typically calendar-year) basis:

- **Deductible** (the amount paid toward services each year before the plan pays; preventive services are usually exempt). Example PPO deductibles are $50 per person and $150 per family per calendar year.
- **Annual maximum** (the cap on what the plan pays in a benefit period, commonly in the $1,000 to $2,000 range), which resets at the end of each benefit period, typically every 12 months.

**Switching plans and the reset.** A member switching to a different Delta Dental plan, or from another carrier, should expect the new plan's deductible and annual maximum to start fresh, because those accumulators belong to the specific plan and benefit period. Money already spent toward a deductible or maximum on a prior plan does not generally carry over. The exact behavior is governed by the new plan's Evidence of Coverage, so the member should confirm the new plan's benefit period and any waiting periods before switching, particularly mid-year, to avoid resetting accumulators they had already worked down.

## Implications for the hub find-a-dentist / verify modules

- **Verify the EXACT product, not just "Delta Dental."** The verify module should treat "Delta Dental PPO" and "Delta Dental Premier" as distinct, because most PPO dentists are in Premier but many Premier dentists are not in PPO. A dentist confirmed only for Premier should not be presented as PPO-verified. Mirror Delta's own pattern: a network selector plus a per-dentist list of participating networks, and prompt the member to confirm the PPO product specifically with the office.
- **Surface the balance-billing protection as the core PPO value.** The find-a-dentist and verify copy can educate that PPO and Premier dentists cannot balance-bill the member, while out-of-network dentists can charge the difference above the maximum contract allowance. This maps directly to CoverCapy's "insurance verified" positioning.
- **Out-of-network economics differ structurally.** Any "is my dentist covered" flow should distinguish three states (PPO, Premier, out-of-network) with different cost and claims consequences: direct claim filing and direct payment in-network, versus pay-up-front-and-get-reimbursed plus a possible balance owed out-of-network.
- **Pre-treatment estimate as a feature hook.** For high-cost treatments, the verify module could prompt members toward requesting a predetermination before committing, which fits big-case use (crowns, implants, ortho) and reinforces a concierge experience.
- **Effective-date logic is concrete and modelable.** The 1st/15th four-option scheme (earliest ~9 days out) is specific enough to drive an accurate "when can I be seen" expectation in coverage-start messaging, supporting the "cover today, see a dentist tomorrow" framing without overstating immediacy.
- **Reset behavior matters for switch timing.** If the hub ever advises on plan switching, deductibles and annual maximums reset by benefit period and generally do not carry over, so mid-year switches can reset accumulators. This is educational context, not advice, and should point members to their Evidence of Coverage.
