# Delta Dental: Implants and Major Restorative Coverage

**One-line summary:** On the Delta Dental PPO Premium individual plan, implants and all major restorative work (crowns, bridges, dentures, inlays/onlays, root canals, periodontics, extractions) are covered at 50% after a 12-month wait and a $50 deductible, all sharing a single $2,000 calendar-year annual maximum that is usually the real constraint on large cases.

---

## Plan baseline (Delta Dental PPO Individual Premium)

Per the official Premium plan benefit summary (data verified June 2026):

| Item | Value |
|------|-------|
| Annual maximum | $2,000 per enrollee, per calendar year |
| Annual deductible | $50 per enrollee / $150 per family |
| Diagnostic and preventive | 100%, no waiting period |
| Basic services (fillings, emergency pain relief) | 80%, 6-month wait |
| Major services and prosthodontics | 50%, 12-month wait |
| Orthodontics | 50%, 12-month wait, $1,500 lifetime max (separate $50 ortho deductible) |

Reimbursement is calculated on the maximum contract allowance. Coverage percentages are identical at Delta Dental PPO and non-PPO dentists on this plan, but PPO dentists accept reduced contract fees so the patient's out-of-pocket dollar amount is lower in network. (Source: DentalPlans.com Premium summary.)

## Implants (prosthodontics) at 50%

- Implants fall under **Prosthodontics** and are covered at **50%** on the Premium plan.
- Waiting period: **12 months** for major services, which includes implants. (Source: DentalPlans.com Premium summary.)
- The 50% benefit is paid against the maximum contract allowance and is subject to the **$2,000 calendar-year annual maximum** and the $50 deductible.
- Industry-wide, Delta PPO plans cover preventive at 100%, basic at 80%, and major (bridges, implants) at 50%. (Source: GetGlobalCare; Delta Dental PPO product page.)

### Missing-tooth clause status

- The Premium plan summary lists no missing-tooth exclusion, and Delta Dental does not apply a blanket missing-tooth clause across its plans the way some carriers do. Delta describes a "Missing Tooth Inclusion" on plans that cover replacement of teeth lost before coverage began for members aged 16 and over. (Source: Moores Chapel Dentistry; DayDream.)
- **State and group variation applies.** Missing-tooth provisions are set per policy and per state-licensed Delta company. Delta Dental of California underwrites California PPO plans, and group/employer contracts can layer on a missing-tooth limitation even when the individual product does not. Treat "no missing-tooth clause" as the Premium individual default and confirm against the member's actual Evidence of Coverage, especially in California. (Source: DentalPlans.com disclaimers; Delta Dental NJ missing-tooth resource.)

## Major services at 50%

All of the following are covered at **50%** on the Premium plan, after the 12-month major-services wait and subject to the shared $2,000 max (root canals/extractions also sit at 50% on this plan despite often being "basic" elsewhere):

| Category | Examples | Premium coverage |
|----------|----------|------------------|
| Major restorative | Crowns, inlays, onlays, cast restorations | 50% |
| Prosthodontics | Bridges, dentures, implants | 50% |
| Endodontics | Root canals | 50% |
| Periodontics | Gum treatments | 50% |
| Oral surgery | Tooth extractions | 50% |

(Source: DentalPlans.com Premium summary.)

## The $2,000 annual maximum on big cases (worked estimate)

The annual maximum is the total the plan will pay toward all dental care in one calendar year, and it is usually the binding limit on implant and full-arch work, not the 50% coinsurance.

**Worked estimate (illustrative, single $5,000 implant case):**

1. Allowed amount used for the example: $5,000.
2. Plan share at 50%: $2,500.
3. But the annual maximum caps total plan payment at $2,000.
4. **Plan actually pays about $2,000; the remaining roughly $3,000 is the patient's responsibility** (the $500 of coinsurance above the cap plus the patient's own 50% share), before deductible effects.

Because the cap resets each calendar year, **timing matters**: a case staged so that part of the treatment (for example, extraction and implant placement) lands in December and the restoration (abutment and crown) lands in January can draw on **two separate $2,000 maximums**, materially raising total plan dollars available. This is a scheduling decision to make with the dentist and confirmed via a pre-treatment estimate. Annual maximums commonly run $1,000 to $2,000 across PPO-style plans, and the Premium plan's $2,000 is at the higher end. (Sources: DentalPlans.com Premium summary and FAQ; GetGlobalCare.)

## Pre-treatment estimates and alternate-benefit clauses

**Pre-treatment estimate (predetermination):** Delta Dental PPO and Premier members can request a written pre-treatment estimate at no cost before work begins. Pairing the dentist's itemized estimate (with CDT codes) and Delta's written predetermination surfaces waiting periods, frequency limits, annual-maximum exposure, and balance-billing risk before any drilling starts. This is the only reliable way to get a real out-of-pocket number. (Source: GetGlobalCare; Delta Dental KS knowledge base.)

**Alternate-benefit and LEAT clauses (implant vs bridge downgrade):**

- Many Delta contracts include an alternate-benefit or **Least Expensive Alternative Treatment (LEAT)** provision. When more than one professionally acceptable treatment exists for the same problem, the plan pays its benefit based on the **least expensive** option, even if the dentist and patient choose a more expensive one.
- For a missing tooth, this can mean the plan calculates its 50% benefit against the allowance for a **fixed bridge or partial denture rather than the implant**. The patient may still choose the implant, but pays the difference between the implant fee and the lower allowed amount. (Sources: ADA LEAT clause guidance; eAssist alternate-benefits clause; Capline Dental Services.)
- Per the ADA, alternate-benefit and LEAT provisions are payment rules only and are not intended to dictate treatment; the treatment decision stays with the dentist and patient. (Source: ADA.)

## Frequency limits on crowns and dentures

- **Crowns:** Delta applies a frequency limit of roughly **once every 5 years (60 months)** on a crown or replacement crown on the same tooth. A replacement inside that window is typically denied unless the dentist submits a narrative showing medical necessity, such as fracture or catastrophic decay. (Source: RedentKlinik; Vivid Dental Raleigh.)
- **Dentures:** One complete upper, one complete lower denture, and any implant used to support a denture are typically payable **once in any 5-year period**. (Source: Delta Dental of North Carolina exceptions and reductions.)

## Common AI questions, plain answers

- **"Does Delta cover implants?"** Yes on the PPO Premium plan, at 50% as prosthodontics, after a 12-month wait, subject to the $50 deductible and the $2,000 annual maximum. Coverage can be reduced by an alternate-benefit or LEAT clause that pays based on a bridge or denture. Always confirm with a pre-treatment estimate.
- **"Delta implant waiting period?"** 12 months for major services on the Premium plan; some plans waive waiting periods if you had comparable prior coverage shortly before the effective date.
- **"Delta crown coverage?"** Crowns are a major service at 50% on the Premium plan, after the 12-month wait, with a frequency limit of about once every 5 years per tooth.
