# Inside Delta Dental: Why Seniors End Up With Delta Dental Through SCAN at 65

## Summary

When people turn 65, many discover that Original Medicare (Parts A and B) pays for almost no routine dental care. To get cleanings, fillings, crowns, and dentures covered, seniors typically enroll in a Medicare Advantage (Part C) plan that bundles dental as a supplemental benefit. A large nonprofit Medicare Advantage carrier in the West, SCAN Health Plan, delivers that dental benefit through Delta Dental in California and Washington (with DentaQuest used in its other states). This is the single most common path by which a 65-year-old in California "ends up with Delta Dental": not by shopping for a dental policy directly, but by choosing a Medicare Advantage plan whose embedded dental administrator happens to be Delta Dental.

This page explains the mechanics: the Medicare coverage gap, SCAN's structure and six-state footprint, the DeltaCare USA DHMO base versus the optional Delta Dental Medicare Advantage PPO buy-up, why an Advantage carrier contracts with Delta in the first place, and the important fact that Medi-Cal Dental (formerly Denti-Cal) is a completely separate government program even though Delta Dental of California ran it for decades. It closes with educational guidance on when a 65-plus person might or might not also want a separate individual Delta PPO policy.

This is educational background, not personalized advice. Benefit details, premiums, and service areas change every plan year; verify current specifics with the plan and with Medicare.

---

## 1. Original Medicare does not cover routine dental

Original Medicare (Part A hospital insurance and Part B medical insurance) does not pay for most routine dental care. Cleanings, exams, fillings, crowns, bridges, dentures, and tooth extractions are generally not covered. Medicare may pay only in narrow situations where dental work is tightly bound to a covered medical procedure, such as an oral exam required before a kidney transplant or heart valve surgery.

Because of this gap, a senior who wants predictable coverage for everyday dental care has three broad options:

1. Enroll in a Medicare Advantage (Part C) plan that includes dental as a supplemental benefit.
2. Buy a standalone individual dental policy.
3. Pay out of pocket.

Medicare Advantage is the most common route, because many of those plans fold dental, vision, and hearing benefits into a single plan, often at no extra premium beyond what the member already pays. (Sources: CMS Medicare dental coverage page; NCOA; Cigna Medicare dental explainer.)

## 2. How seniors "land" on Delta Dental: the Medicare Advantage carrier, not a dental shopping trip

A key point for understanding the CoverCapy audience: most 65-plus members did not choose Delta Dental directly. They chose a Medicare Advantage health plan. The dental benefit inside that plan is administered by whichever dental company the health plan contracted with. For SCAN Health Plan members in California and Washington, that company is Delta Dental.

So the path is:

Turn 65 -> Original Medicare leaves a dental gap -> enroll in a Medicare Advantage plan (e.g., SCAN) -> the plan's embedded dental benefit is run by Delta Dental.

The member experiences this as "I have Delta Dental now," but the relationship was created by the Advantage plan choice.

---

## 3. SCAN Health Plan: structure and six-state footprint

SCAN Health Plan is a large nonprofit Medicare Advantage organization based in California. For the 2026 plan year, SCAN operates in six states: California, Arizona, Nevada, Texas, New Mexico, and Washington. SCAN expanded into Washington (Pierce, Spokane, and Thurston counties) and grew within California and Texas for 2026, serving Medicare beneficiaries across roughly 33 counties. (Sources: SCAN press releases on 2026 expansion; NerdWallet SCAN review; Wikipedia SCAN overview.)

### Which dental administrator SCAN uses, by state

SCAN does not use one dental company across all six states. It splits the dental benefit by geography:

| Dental administrator | SCAN states served |
|---|---|
| Delta Dental | California, Washington |
| DentaQuest | Arizona, California, Nevada, New Mexico, Texas |

(Note: DentaQuest is listed as the administrator for several states including California in some SCAN plans, while Delta Dental serves California and Washington. The administrator a specific member has depends on their specific plan, not just their state. Members should confirm which network their plan uses.) (Source: SCAN FAQ "What's the difference between the Delta Dental and the DentaQuest dental plans?")

This is why a California SCAN member typically ends up with Delta Dental, while an Arizona or Texas SCAN member typically ends up with DentaQuest.

## 4. The two-tier dental design: DeltaCare USA DHMO base, optional Delta PPO buy-up

For SCAN members whose dental benefit runs through Delta Dental, the benefit is structured in two layers:

### Base layer: DeltaCare USA DHMO (no extra premium for most members)

Most SCAN members receive dental at no additional cost and select a dentist from the DeltaCare USA network. DeltaCare USA is a DHMO-style (dental HMO) plan. Like a medical HMO, it generally asks the member to use a contracted network dentist and to coordinate care through that assigned office. The trade-off is lower or no premium in exchange for less flexibility on which dentist to see.

### Upgrade layer: optional Delta Dental Medicare Advantage PPO buy-up (extra premium)

For members who want more freedom, SCAN offers an optional PPO buy-up for an additional premium. With the buy-up, the member can choose a dentist from the Delta Dental Medicare Advantage PPO network, and the PPO design allows the member to see a preferred dentist even outside the network (typically at a higher out-of-pocket share). SCAN gives members about two months after their plan membership begins to enroll in the optional dental PPO buy-up, with coverage starting the first of the following month after the application is received. (Sources: SCAN "Delta Dental Benefit" page; SCAN "Dental Buy-up" enrollment page; Delta Dental 2026 Medicare Advantage DHMO and PPO partner announcements.)

So the practical mental model is: DHMO is the included floor, and the Delta Dental Medicare Advantage PPO is the paid upgrade for choice.

---

## 5. The Delta Dental Medicare Advantage PPO network (the upgrade tier)

The Delta Dental Medicare Advantage PPO network is one of Delta Dental's national network-based programs built specifically for the growing population of Medicare Advantage members. Delta Dental's broader PPO network is among the largest in the country, with more than 112,000 dentists across over 278,000 locations nationwide. That scale is precisely what makes the PPO buy-up attractive: a senior is far more likely to find that their existing dentist already participates. (Sources: Delta Dental "Welcome to PPO" member page; Delta Dental 2026 Medicare Advantage PPO partner announcement; Delta Dental Medicare Advantage PPO network page for SCAN.)

## 6. Why a Medicare Advantage carrier contracts with Delta Dental

Health plans like SCAN are in the business of medical coverage, not running a nationwide dental network. Building and maintaining a dental network means credentialing dentists, processing dental claims, handling member and provider service, and managing eligibility and rosters. Rather than build that infrastructure, an Advantage carrier contracts it out to a specialized dental administrator. Delta Dental is an attractive partner for several reasons:

- **Network size.** Delta Dental's network is one of the largest in the United States, which raises the chance that a member's current dentist is already in-network.
- **Administration.** Delta Dental handles credentialing and recredentialing of dentists, eligibility, benefit details, claims processing, and provider tools. For some Medicare Advantage groups, Delta Dental has also partnered with SKYGEN, a Medicare benefits administration specialist, to streamline patient and benefit administration. (Source: Delta Dental "Medicare administration" page.)
- **Brand familiarity.** Delta Dental is one of the most recognized dental brands among consumers and dentists, which reduces friction for both members and providers.

In short, the Advantage carrier rents Delta Dental's network and back-office machinery instead of building its own.

## 7. Medi-Cal Dental (Denti-Cal) is a SEPARATE program, even though Delta Dental of California ran it

A frequent and important point of confusion: Delta Dental of California administered California's Medicaid dental program, known as Medi-Cal Dental (historically "Denti-Cal"), for decades. This is a state government program for low-income Californians, established in 1974, and at one point covered more than 13 million people, making it the largest state public dental insurance program in the country. Delta Dental of California acted as the administrator under contract with the California Department of Health Care Services (DHCS). (Sources: PRWeb / California Healthline reporting on the Denti-Cal contract awards; DrBicuspid coverage of the $7.3B contract; DHCS Medi-Cal Dental pages.)

Two things matter for a 65-plus reader:

1. **Different program entirely.** Medi-Cal Dental is a state Medicaid benefit, funded by state and federal dollars and governed by DHCS rules. The Delta Dental coverage a senior receives through a SCAN Medicare Advantage plan is a private commercial benefit. Having had Denti-Cal does not mean a person automatically has, or keeps, a private Delta Dental plan, and a private SCAN Delta Dental benefit is not Denti-Cal. Benefits, networks, covered procedures, and rules do not transfer between the two.

2. **Delta Dental no longer runs Medi-Cal Dental.** On May 13, 2024, DHCS completed the transition of Medi-Cal Dental fee-for-service administration from Delta Dental of California to Gainwell Technologies LLC. So even the historical link between the Delta Dental brand and California's Medicaid dental program has ended. A senior who hears "Delta Dental" in a Medicare Advantage context should not assume any connection to Medi-Cal. (Source: DHCS Stakeholder Update, May 13, 2024.)

The takeaway: same brand name, completely different programs, no benefit portability between them.

## 8. Educational guidance: when a 65-plus person might, or might not, also buy an individual Delta PPO

This section is educational framing, not personalized recommendations. Whether to add an individual Delta Dental PPO policy on top of a Medicare Advantage dental benefit depends on the person's situation.

### Situations where an additional individual Delta PPO is more likely worth considering

- **The member only has the DHMO base and dislikes the assigned-dentist model.** If the included DeltaCare USA DHMO does not include the member's preferred dentist, and the SCAN PPO buy-up is unavailable or already missed its enrollment window, a separate individual PPO can restore dentist choice.
- **The member expects major work** (crowns, bridges, dentures, implants where applicable) that may exceed the Advantage plan's annual dental maximum. Medicare Advantage dental benefits are commonly capped, with annual maximums often in the low four figures. A supplemental PPO can extend coverage past that cap.
- **The member splits time across states or travels** and wants the broadest national PPO network rather than a localized DHMO.

### Situations where an additional individual Delta PPO is usually unnecessary

- **The Advantage dental benefit already meets the member's needs**, especially if the member's dentist is in the DeltaCare USA network or the member added the SCAN Delta PPO buy-up. Buying a second policy can mean paying twice for overlapping coverage.
- **The member's dental needs are limited to routine preventive care** that the included benefit already covers in full.
- **The member would simply be duplicating a buy-up they could get inside SCAN.** Where the SCAN optional PPO buy-up is available and the member is still within its roughly two-month enrollment window, that route is usually simpler than purchasing a separate standalone policy, because it sits inside the same plan and the same Delta Dental network.

The general principle: first understand exactly which Delta benefit the member already has through their Medicare Advantage plan (DHMO base only, or DHMO plus PPO buy-up), then evaluate whether a separate policy fills a real gap or just duplicates existing coverage.
