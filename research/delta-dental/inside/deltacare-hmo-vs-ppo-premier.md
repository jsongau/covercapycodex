# Inside Delta Dental: DeltaCare USA (DHMO) vs Delta Dental PPO vs Delta Dental Premier

*Educational reference for the CoverCapy "Inside Delta Dental" hub. This page explains how Delta's main product families work. It is informational and not benefits advice. Always confirm specifics against your own plan documents.*

## Summary

Delta Dental sells dental coverage through several distinct product families, and the differences matter for how much you pay and which dentists you can see.

- **DeltaCare USA** is a dental HMO (a DHMO, sometimes called prepaid or managed care). You pick or are assigned a primary care dentist, pay fixed copays from a published schedule, have no deductible and no annual maximum, need a referral to see a specialist, and get no benefit if you go outside the network.
- **Delta Dental PPO** is a preferred-provider plan. Dentists in the network agree to set, contracted fees. You typically have a deductible and an annual maximum, you can see any dentist you want, and you save the most by staying in the PPO network. In-network dentists cannot balance bill you.
- **Delta Dental Premier** is Delta's larger, older fee-for-service network. Premier dentists also agree to set fees (generally higher than PPO fees) and also cannot balance bill plan members, but your out-of-pocket savings are smaller than at a PPO dentist.

Many Delta PPO plans are **dual network** plans that give members access to both the PPO and Premier networks at once. And some people who currently hold a DeltaCare USA (HMO) plan, especially through a group or Medicare Advantage carrier such as SCAN, can pay extra to **upgrade to a PPO option**.

## DeltaCare USA: the DHMO

DeltaCare USA is Delta Dental's dental HMO product. Like other DHMO plans, it provides lower-cost coverage with a focus on preventive care and predictable spending.

Key characteristics, per Delta Dental:

- **Set copays.** Members pay fixed copayments for covered services from a published copay schedule, so the cost for preventive, basic, and major services is known up front.
- **No deductible and no annual maximum.** When you use network dentists, there is no deductible and no annual dollar cap on covered benefits, and typically no waiting periods.
- **Assigned primary dentist.** You select a primary care dentist (or dental facility), and in most states one can be assigned to you. That dentist coordinates your care. In a handful of states (for example AK, CT, LA, ME, MS, MT, NC, ND, NH, OK, SD, VT, and WY), members are not assigned to a single general dentist but still must use a DeltaCare USA network dentist in the state.
- **Referral for specialists.** If you need specialist care, your primary dental facility or dentist submits a referral first.
- **No out-of-network benefits.** Coverage applies only when you use in-network DeltaCare USA dentists. Care outside the network is generally not covered.
- **Smaller network.** DHMO networks are smaller than PPO networks, and some plans cap the number of visits, treatments, or diagnostic tests.

### How DeltaCare USA differs from a PPO

The core trade is cost predictability versus flexibility. A DHMO like DeltaCare USA keeps costs low and fixed but locks you to a primary dentist, a referral process, and an in-network-only rule. A PPO costs more in premium and adds a deductible and annual maximum, but lets you see any dentist, skip referrals, and often get partial reimbursement out of network.

## Delta Dental PPO: the preferred-provider plan

Delta Dental PPO is a preferred-provider plan. Dentists who join the PPO network sign a contract agreeing to charge a set fee schedule.

Key characteristics:

- **Contracted fees.** PPO dentists agree to charge set fees as part of their Delta Dental contract and cannot charge PPO members more than those fees. The lower fees reduce out-of-pocket cost and stretch the plan's annual maximum further.
- **Deductible and annual maximum.** Most PPO plans include a deductible you meet before coverage kicks in and an annual limit on what the plan pays.
- **See any dentist, save most in network.** You are not required to designate a primary dentist and can switch dentists or see a specialist without a referral. You may receive partial reimbursement for out-of-network care, but you save the most at a PPO network dentist.
- **Balance-billing protection in network.** Because in-network dentists agree to set fees, they cannot bill you for the difference between their usual charge and the contracted amount. Out-of-network dentists have no contract with Delta and may balance bill.

## Delta Dental Premier: the fee-for-service safety net

Delta Dental Premier is Delta's larger, fee-for-service network. Together, the PPO and Premier networks make up what Delta describes as the largest dentist network in the nation.

Key characteristics:

- **Larger network, higher allowed fees.** Premier dentists also agree to set fees, but those fees are typically higher than PPO fees. Members see the same ease and quality as the PPO network with more limited out-of-pocket savings.
- **Also balance-billing protected.** Premier dentists are contracted with Delta and cannot charge PPO members more than the agreed fees, so members are protected from balance billing here too.
- **Often a safety net paired with PPO.** Premier works as a second-best option for PPO plan members who cannot find a convenient PPO dentist. Think of it as a tier system: members usually save the most at a PPO dentist, a little less at a Premier dentist, and the least at a non-Delta dentist.

## Dual-network plans: PPO plus Premier

Many Delta Dental PPO plans are "dual network," giving members access to both the Delta Dental PPO and Delta Dental Premier networks at the same time. Delta also lists a distinct **Delta Dental PPO Plus Premier** product in its find-a-dentist network selector.

How claims pay at each network under a PPO plan:

- **At a PPO dentist:** the dentist is reimbursed at PPO fees. Members pay the least out of pocket.
- **At a Premier dentist:** the dentist is reimbursed at either Premier fees or PPO fees, depending on how the plan or benefits administrator configures it.
  - Reimbursing Premier dentists at **Premier fees** gives members the lowest out-of-pocket cost at Premier dentists, but the plan pays more and may cost more overall.
  - Reimbursing Premier dentists at **PPO fees** raises members' out-of-pocket cost at Premier dentists but can lower overall plan cost and pushes members toward PPO dentists.
- **At a non-Delta dentist:** there is no fee contract, the dentist can set any price, and the member can be balance billed.

The reimbursement amount (the maximum plan allowance) is separate from the coverage percentage for a given service. Group plans can also add a network differential, for example covering diagnostic and preventive care at 100 percent at PPO dentists, 90 percent at Premier dentists, and 80 percent at non-Delta dentists, to steer members in network.

## Upgrading from DeltaCare USA (HMO) to a PPO option

Someone enrolled in a DeltaCare USA HMO is not necessarily stuck with HMO rules. Depending on the sponsor, a PPO upgrade may be available.

- **Medicare Advantage example (SCAN Health Plan).** SCAN members in Southern California and Washington can use the DeltaCare USA network at no additional cost or **upgrade to an optional PPO buy-up** for an additional monthly premium. With the optional PPO dental plan, using a dentist in the Delta Dental Medicare Advantage PPO network gives discounted rates on covered services, covered services at pre-set copays, no annual limit, and no claim forms to submit. Availability is plan-specific and enrollment is limited to certain times or qualifying events, so members are told to check their Evidence of Coverage or call member services.
- **Group plans.** Employers that offer Delta coverage can structure plan designs that encourage in-network use, and many group menus let an employee choose a PPO option instead of, or as a buy-up from, a DHMO option during open enrollment. Specifics depend on what the employer's plan offers.

The practical point: if a DeltaCare USA member wants any-dentist flexibility, no referrals, and out-of-network reimbursement, the path is usually a PPO plan or a PPO buy-up offered by the same sponsor, at a higher premium.

## Hub implications

- **Position three products, not one.** When a CoverCapy page mentions "Delta Dental," disambiguate which product is meant: DeltaCare USA (DHMO), Delta Dental PPO, or Delta Dental Premier. They behave very differently at the chair.
- **Verification logic should branch by product.** A DeltaCare USA patient must use an assigned in-network dentist with referrals, so "verify my coverage" for a DHMO is really "is this dentist my assigned DeltaCare USA provider." A PPO or Premier patient can use any dentist, so verification is about in-network savings tier, not eligibility to be seen.
- **Carrier tiles already separate Delta Dental PPO and Premier.** This is correct and worth reinforcing in copy: a patient can be "Delta" yet land in PPO, Premier, or DHMO with different cost outcomes. Consider a DeltaCare USA tile distinct from the two PPO/Premier tiles.
- **Upgrade angle is a content opportunity.** A short explainer on "I have DeltaCare USA but want to see any dentist" maps cleanly to the PPO buy-up story (SCAN, group plans) and supports CoverCapy's "see a dentist tomorrow" positioning.
- **Balance-billing protection is a trust message.** Both PPO and Premier protect members from balance billing in network, which is a reassuring, accurate point to surface on dentist profiles that accept Delta.
