# Is Delta Dental Good? Ratings, Awards, Reviews, and Complaints

*Inside Delta Dental hub: trust and authority page*

**Last reviewed: June 2026**

---

## Summary

Delta Dental is the most established and largest dental benefits carrier in the United States, serving roughly 80 million members through a network of about 39 independent member companies. On the two trust dimensions that matter most, the picture splits cleanly:

- **Financial strength is genuinely strong.** AM Best rates the major Delta Dental entities "A" (Excellent), with at least one entity (Delta Dental of California) carried at "A-" (Excellent) in a more recent action. These are creditworthy, well-capitalized insurers.
- **Member-experience reputation is mixed to weak.** Independent review platforms (Trustpilot, BBB) show low consumer scores driven by customer-service wait times, claim friction, and surprise waiting periods.

The honest read: Delta Dental is a financially solid, widely accepted carrier with the deepest dentist network in the country, but service quality and ease of claims vary by state company, and waiting periods on major and orthodontic work are a recurring frustration. It earns trust on stability and network, less so on customer service.

### Better Business Bureau

BBB results are inconsistent across Delta's many entities, which is itself a finding:

- TrustedChoice's profile cites an **A+ business profile** rating for one Delta Dental entity.
- Money's review reports the consumer-facing Delta Dental entity it examined as **not BBB accredited, with an F rating**, 128 complaints (10 unresolved, 5 unanswered), and a **customer review average of 1.05 out of 5**.

The gap is real and reflects that "Delta Dental" is dozens of separate BBB-profiled companies, not one. Cite the specific state entity when making a BBB claim.

Sources: [Money review (F rating, 1.05 stars, complaint counts)](https://money.com/delta-dental-insurance-review/), [TrustedChoice (A+ business profile)](https://www.trustedchoice.com/insurance-articles/c/delta-dental-company-review/), [BBB profiles](https://www.bbb.org/us/ca/san-francisco/profile/insurance-companies/delta-dental-of-california-enterprises-1116-41771/customer-reviews).

## Praise vs Gripes

### What members and reviewers praise

- **Largest network in the country.** About 156,000 in-network dentists nationally (estimate per industry comparisons; Delta states tens of thousands more across its plan tiers), the deepest acceptance of any U.S. dental carrier. "Never turned away from any dentist" is a recurring positive theme.
- **Brand trust and longevity.** Founded 1954; among the most recognized names in dental benefits; widely accepted at front desks.
- **Strong payouts when claims go through.** Many long-tenure members report generous coverage on cleanings, fillings, extractions, and major work, with no-form claims when using in-network dentists (the dentist files).
- **Plan flexibility.** Multiple product types (PPO, PPO Plus Premier, Premier, DeltaCare USA HMO, Patient Direct discount), customizable add-ons, and competitive annual maximums ($1,000 to $2,000, some plans no maximum).

### Common gripes

- **Waiting periods.** Roughly 6 months on basic care and 12 months on major and orthodontic services on many plans. A frequent source of "surprise" complaints when members expect immediate major coverage. (AARP-branded Delta PPO plans restart the ortho/restorative waiting period on plan switches.)
- **Customer-service variance by state company.** Long hold times, difficulty reaching a person, and slow claim handling are the dominant negative themes on Trustpilot and BBB. Because each state company runs its own service operation, experience is inconsistent.
- **Claim denials and appeals friction.** Reported denials tied to waiting periods and timely-filing rules (claims over 12 months old may not be paid).
- **Orthodontic and annual caps.** Ortho coverage is typically subject to a separate lifetime maximum and percentage limits, which can underwhelm members expecting fuller braces coverage even though Delta is rated "best for braces" relative to peers.
- **Minor friction points.** A $10 enrollment fee on some plans where competitors charge none.

Sources: [Money review (waiting periods, $10 fee, Trustpilot 1.9, BBB F)](https://money.com/delta-dental-insurance-review/), [Money best-list (Best for Braces)](https://money.com/best-dental-insurance/), [BBB complaints](https://www.bbb.org/us/ca/san-francisco/profile/insurance-companies/delta-dental-of-california-enterprises-1116-41771/complaints), [Trustpilot](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/www.deltadentalins.com), [ConsumerAffairs](https://www.consumeraffairs.com/health/delta-dental-plans-association.html).

## How Delta Compares on Trust

All figures below are best-available industry estimates from comparison roundups and should be labeled as such; network counts in particular vary by how each carrier defines "access points."

| Carrier | Network scale (estimate) | Financial strength | Trust profile (balanced) |
|---------|--------------------------|--------------------|--------------------------|
| **Delta Dental** | ~156,000+ dentists; ~80M members; largest in U.S. | AM Best A / A- (Excellent) | Strongest network and brand recognition; service and waiting-period complaints are the main drag. |
| **UnitedHealthcare** | Large national network via parent UnitedHealth Group | Backed by a top-tier, highly rated parent | Strong financial backing; dental often seen as a bolt-on to medical, service reputation mixed. |
| **Cigna** | ~149,000+ dentists, very large location count | Strong, well-rated multinational | Competitive premiums and wide network; frequently a top-overall pick in roundups. |
| **Guardian** | Large national PPO network | Well-rated mutual insurer | Strong on add-ons (named "best for whitening" in some lists); solid reputation, smaller footprint than Delta. |

Net: Delta wins on **network depth and brand trust**. Competitors differentiate on the exact things Delta is weakest at: Ameritas on no waiting periods, Cigna on price/value, Guardian on specific perks. UnitedHealthcare leans on parent-company financial heft.

## Verdict: Is Delta Dental Good Insurance?

**Yes, with honest caveats.** Delta Dental is a good choice if your priorities are (1) the widest possible dentist acceptance, (2) a financially rock-solid carrier (AM Best "A"/"A-" Excellent), and (3) flexible plan tiers including strong orthodontic value relative to peers. It is the default "safe" pick that most dentists already accept.

It is a **weaker** choice if you need **major or orthodontic work soon** (waiting periods of 6 to 12 months apply on many plans) or if **responsive customer service** is a deciding factor, since independent review scores (Trustpilot ~1.9, BBB as low as F / 1.05 stars on the entity Money reviewed) point to real service friction that varies by state company.

Balanced bottom line: trustworthy and stable on the things insurers must get right (paying claims, staying solvent, broad access), more uneven on the experience of dealing with them. A reasonable default, but compare against Ameritas (no waiting periods) and Cigna (value) before committing if those gaps matter to you. Independent reviewers land it around **3 out of 5** overall, which is a fair summary: solid, not flawless.

Sources: [TrustedChoice final rating 3/5](https://www.trustedchoice.com/insurance-articles/c/delta-dental-company-review/), [Money review and summary](https://money.com/delta-dental-insurance-review/).

## Hub implications

- **Lead with financial strength, not service.** The defensible, citable trust signal is AM Best "A"/"A-" (Excellent). Headline that. Avoid implying a uniform "Delta Dental rating" since the federation is ~39 separately rated companies.
- **Be specific with the award.** Money names Delta **Best for Braces / orthodontics**, not best overall (that was Denali). Using "Money's Best Dental Insurance" without the category would be misleading. Always pair the claim with the category and year.
- **Separate the employer award from product trust.** Do not present Forbes "Best Employers" as a consumer endorsement of the insurance.
- **Get ahead of the waiting-period gripe.** The single most common complaint is the 6/12-month waiting period on basic/major/ortho care. CoverCapy's "see a dentist tomorrow" positioning should explicitly address this (e.g., highlight no-waiting-period alternatives, or set expectations clearly) rather than ignore it.
- **Localize BBB/service claims.** Service quality and BBB grades vary by state company. Any BBB stat in copy should name the specific entity and date, or be framed as "varies by state company."
- **Use the honest 3/5 framing for E-E-A-T credibility.** A balanced verdict (strong network and stability, weaker service and waiting periods) reads as trustworthy and differentiates from the carrier's own marketing, which strengthens the page's authority signal.
- **Label all unverified items.** Forbes Advisor "best for seniors," CNN Underscored, and any J.D. Power rank for Delta are currently **unverified** and must be confirmed at the source or omitted.
