# Inside Delta Dental: Employer and Group Plans

A guide for employers who want to offer great dental benefits through Delta Dental.

---

## Summary

Delta Dental is the most widely offered dental carrier in the United States. The company reports serving more than 148,000 groups nationwide, says nearly half of U.S. Fortune 1000 companies offer Delta Dental, and notes that 1 in 3 Americans with dental insurance are covered by Delta Dental ([Delta Dental, Large corporation](https://www.deltadental.com/group/corporation/)). For employers, the appeal is the network reach: over 75% of U.S. dentists participate, with the Premier network reaching over 75% of oral health providers and the PPO network including over half of all U.S. dental professionals ([Delta Dental, Large corporation](https://www.deltadental.com/group/corporation/)).

Employers can choose among three core group designs: Delta Dental PPO (lowest member out of pocket, in network), Delta Dental PPO Plus Premier (PPO savings plus the much larger Premier network), and DeltaCare USA, a dental HMO (DHMO) built around low copays and preventive care with an assigned network dentist ([Delta Dental, Large corporation](https://www.deltadental.com/group/corporation/); [Delta Dental, Midsize to large group plans](https://www1.deltadentalins.com/employers/mid-large-groups.html)). A typical group plan covers preventive care at or near 100%, basic services around 80%, major services around 50%, with an annual maximum and an optional orthodontia rider.

A rich employer plan looks like a high annual maximum (toward the top of the $1,500 to $5,000 range) plus an orthodontia rider, the D&P Maximum Waiver so preventive visits do not draw down the annual maximum, and SmileWay Wellness Benefits for members with qualifying chronic conditions. Cost estimates below are labeled and approximate; actual pricing requires a quote tied to group size, location, and census.

## How Delta group and employer dental works

Delta Dental sells employee dental coverage to businesses of all sizes through a national network of 39 independent Delta Dental companies ([Delta Dental, Large corporation](https://www.deltadental.com/group/corporation/)). The product families an employer can offer:

- **Delta Dental PPO.** The preferred provider option. Employees pay the lowest out of pocket when they see a PPO network dentist who accepts reduced contracted fees ([Delta Dental, Large corporation](https://www.deltadental.com/group/corporation/)).
- **Delta Dental Premier.** The original fee for service plan with the largest dentist network. Premier dentists agree to contracted fees, so members pay copay plus deductible for covered services ([Delta Dental, Large corporation](https://www.deltadental.com/group/corporation/)).
- **Delta Dental PPO Plus Premier.** Combines both networks. Even when a dentist is in Premier but not in the employee's PPO network, the member still gets that dentist's contracted fee, which softens out of network exposure ([Delta Dental, Large corporation](https://www.deltadental.com/group/corporation/)).
- **DeltaCare USA (DHMO).** A dental HMO with low cost coverage focused on preventive care. Employees pick from a network and use minimal or no copay for preventive care ([Delta Dental, Large corporation](https://www.deltadental.com/group/corporation/)). DHMO designs typically have no annual maximum but require using the assigned network dentist.

### Typical group plan design

Most employer PPO designs follow a coverage ladder: preventive at or near 100%, basic services (fillings, extractions) around 80% after deductible, and major services (crowns, prosthetics) around 50%, all subject to an annual maximum and a deductible. Two real reference points:

- **University of California (UC) employer PPO.** Preventive exams and cleanings are free with no deductible; basic services such as fillings and extractions are covered at 80% after the deductible; a $50 deductible applies for some services. The total annual benefit per person is $1,700 in network (PPO dentist) and $1,500 for Premier or out of network ([UCnet, Delta Dental PPO highlights 2026](https://ucnet.universityofcalifornia.edu/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/2026-Delta-PPO-Highlights.pdf); [UCnet, Dental](https://ucnet.universityofcalifornia.edu/benefits/health-welfare/dental/)).

- **Annual maximum context.** Delta Dental notes annual maximums commonly fall in the $1,000 to $2,000 range, and industry data shows about 65% of dental PPOs carry a maximum of $1,500 or more ([Delta Dental of Washington, Annual maximum explained](https://www.deltadentalwa.com/dental-benefits-guide/annual-maximum); [mPloyer Advisor, dental and vision guide](https://mployeradvisor.com/blog/the-boring-yet-obligatory-guide-to-dental-vision-insurance-for-employers)). Plans toward $5,000 are available but sit well above the standard market and price accordingly.

- **Preventive carve out (D&P Maximum Waiver).** Delta Dental offers a D&P Maximum Waiver feature that keeps diagnostic and preventive services from counting toward the member's annual maximum, so the full maximum is preserved for fillings, crowns, and other work ([Delta Dental, Midsize to large group plans](https://www1.deltadentalins.com/employers/mid-large-groups.html)).

- **Orthodontia rider.** Employers can add an orthodontia rider. Ortho is typically covered at 50% with a separate lifetime maximum (often around $1,500 nationally) rather than counting against the annual maximum, which limits the employer's exposure per covered individual ([Delta Dental of Washington, Plus Ortho](https://www.deltadentalwa.com/our-plans/dental/plus-ortho); [mPloyer Advisor, dental and vision guide](https://mployeradvisor.com/blog/the-boring-yet-obligatory-guide-to-dental-vision-insurance-for-employers)).

## Plan design table

| Element | Standard group PPO | Rich employer PPO |
|---|---|---|
| Preventive (exams, cleanings, x-rays) | ~100%, often no deductible | ~100%, no deductible |
| Basic (fillings, extractions) | ~80% after deductible | ~80%, lower or no deductible |
| Major (crowns, prosthetics) | ~50% | ~50% to 60% |
| Annual maximum per person | $1,000 to $2,000 | $3,000 to $5,000 |
| Deductible | ~$50 individual | $0 to $50 |
| Orthodontia | Not included or rider | Rider included, ~50% to a separate lifetime max (~$1,500+) |
| Preventive vs maximum | Counts toward max unless waived | D&P Maximum Waiver so preventive does not draw down the max |
| SmileWay Wellness Benefits | Sometimes available | Added on for chronic conditions |
| Network | PPO only | PPO Plus Premier for wider access |

Sources: [Delta Dental, Large corporation](https://www.deltadental.com/group/corporation/); [Delta Dental, Midsize to large group plans](https://www1.deltadentalins.com/employers/mid-large-groups.html); [UCnet, Delta Dental PPO highlights 2026](https://ucnet.universityofcalifornia.edu/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/2026-Delta-PPO-Highlights.pdf); [Delta Dental of Washington, Plus Ortho](https://www.deltadentalwa.com/our-plans/dental/plus-ortho). Coverage percentages are typical industry structures; exact tiers vary by group and state.

## Employer cost estimates

All figures below are labeled estimates, not quotes. Actual premium depends on group size, location, plan richness, and the employee census. Delta Dental does not publish group rate cards; pricing comes from a quote ([Delta Dental, Midsize to large group plans](https://www1.deltadentalins.com/employers/mid-large-groups.html)).

**Market benchmark (estimate):** Average employer sponsored dental PPO premiums run about $62.75 per employee per month, while dental DHMO averages about $22.75 per member per month ([insurancecostguides.com, 2025 guide](https://insurancecostguides.com/pages/dental-insurance-guide); [MoneyGeek, 2026 guide](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/health/dental-insurance-costs/)).

**Cost sharing (estimate):** Employers commonly pay 70% to 80% of the dental premium, leaving employees responsible for the remaining 20% to 30% ([HealthPartners, employer dental cost](https://www.healthpartners.com/plan/blog/dental-insurance-employer-cost/)).

| Plan tier | Estimated total premium per employee per month | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| DHMO (DeltaCare USA) | ~$20 to $30 (estimate) | Lowest cost, network dentist, low copays, typically no annual max |
| Standard PPO ($1,500 to $2,000 max) | ~$45 to $65 (estimate) | Most common employer design |
| Rich PPO ($3,000 to $5,000 max + ortho rider) | ~$70 to $110+ (estimate) | High max, ortho rider, PPO Plus Premier, D&P waiver |

The rich PPO range is a directional estimate. A $5,000 annual maximum is well above the standard market, and adding an orthodontia rider raises premium further; precise pricing requires a quote ([mPloyer Advisor, dental and vision guide](https://mployeradvisor.com/blog/the-boring-yet-obligatory-guide-to-dental-vision-insurance-for-employers); [Delta Dental, Midsize to large group plans](https://www1.deltadentalins.com/employers/mid-large-groups.html)). Orthodontia exposure is moderated because the rider carries a separate lifetime cap rather than drawing on the annual maximum.

## SmileWay wellness and member perks as group selling points

These features help an employer's plan feel premium and support utilization and retention.

- **SmileWay Wellness Benefits.** Many Delta Dental PPO employer sponsored plans can add SmileWay Wellness Benefits, which give expanded dental coverage at no added cost to members diagnosed with certain chronic medical conditions. Qualifying enrollees can receive up to four total cleanings per year at 100%, plus 100% coverage each year for one scaling and root planing per quadrant ([Delta Dental, SmileWay Wellness Benefits](https://www1.deltadentalins.com/members/smileway-wellness-benefits.html); [Delta Dental, Midsize to large group plans](https://www1.deltadentalins.com/employers/mid-large-groups.html)). Members must opt in. In Texas this is branded SmileWay Enhanced Benefits ([Delta Dental, Midsize to large group plans](https://www1.deltadentalins.com/employers/mid-large-groups.html)).

- **Member perks (LifePerks and BrushSmart).** Delta Dental members can access LifePerks discounts on fitness, groceries and meal delivery, travel, entertainment, pet insurance, and childcare, plus BrushSmart discounts on oral health products and a LASIK surgery discount ([Delta Dental, Member perks](https://www1.deltadentalins.com/memberperks.html); [Delta Dental, SmileWay Wellness Benefits](https://www1.deltadentalins.com/members/smileway-wellness-benefits.html)).

- **D&P Maximum Waiver.** Keeping preventive care out of the annual maximum is a tangible richness signal: employees can use a cleaning schedule and still keep their full maximum for restorative work ([Delta Dental, Midsize to large group plans](https://www1.deltadentalins.com/employers/mid-large-groups.html)).

- **Service and reporting.** Midsize to large groups get designated onboarding and account management teams, real time online invoice and eligibility tools, and ongoing reporting and consulting to optimize plan performance ([Delta Dental, Midsize to large group plans](https://www1.deltadentalins.com/employers/mid-large-groups.html)).

## Setup steps for a small or mid-size employer

1. **Decide group size lane.** Delta Dental runs a Small Business Program for groups of roughly 2 to 50 enrolled employees, and a midsize to large track aimed at businesses with 100+ employees. Pick the lane that matches your headcount ([Delta Dental of Washington, small business plans](https://www.deltadentalwa.com/producer/tool-kit/small-business-plans); [Delta Dental, Midsize to large group plans](https://www1.deltadentalins.com/employers/mid-large-groups.html)).

2. **Engage a broker or benefits consultant, or contact Delta Dental sales directly.** Delta Dental's stated path is to contact your broker, benefits consultant, or the Delta Dental sales team. Brokers can pull instant quotes for 2 to 50 employee groups ([Delta Dental, Midsize to large group plans](https://www1.deltadentalins.com/employers/mid-large-groups.html); [Delta Dental of Connecticut, small group for brokers](https://www.deltadentalct.com/broker/our-plans/small-group)).

3. **Submit a request for proposal (RFP).** For larger or custom designs, use Delta Dental's RFP Assistant, entering the state where the company is headquartered or where benefits buying decisions are made, which routes the request to the correct local Delta Dental company ([Delta Dental, Large corporation](https://www.deltadental.com/group/corporation/); [Delta Dental, RFP tool](https://www.deltadental.com/group/rfp-assistant/)).

4. **Choose the plan family and richness.** Select PPO, PPO Plus Premier, or DeltaCare USA, then set the annual maximum, deductible, and coverage tiers, and decide whether to add the orthodontia rider, the D&P Maximum Waiver, and SmileWay Wellness Benefits ([Delta Dental, Large corporation](https://www.deltadental.com/group/corporation/); [Delta Dental, Midsize to large group plans](https://www1.deltadentalins.com/employers/mid-large-groups.html)).

5. **Set cost sharing.** Decide the employer contribution. The common market split is the employer paying 70% to 80% of premium (estimate) ([HealthPartners, employer dental cost](https://www.healthpartners.com/plan/blog/dental-insurance-employer-cost/)).

6. **Onboard.** Small business groups get a dedicated implementation consultant, and many small business dental and vision plans have no waiting periods for most services ([Delta Dental of Connecticut, small group for brokers](https://www.deltadentalct.com/broker/our-plans/small-group)). Midsize to large groups get designated onboarding and account management teams ([Delta Dental, Midsize to large group plans](https://www1.deltadentalins.com/employers/mid-large-groups.html)).

## Hub implications

- CoverCapy can position itself as the concierge layer on top of an employer's Delta Dental plan: help employees confirm which Delta network their plan uses (PPO, PPO Plus Premier, or DeltaCare USA), then verify acceptance at a specific office. The three network families are the key fork to explain on hub content.
- The "rich plan" story (high annual maximum, ortho rider, D&P Maximum Waiver, SmileWay) is a strong hub explainer that maps cleanly to CoverCapy's benefit maxing angle. The D&P Maximum Waiver and SmileWay extra cleanings are concrete, citable richness signals.
- Cost framing should always be labeled as an estimate. Use the benchmark of about $62.75 per employee per month for PPO and the 70% to 80% employer contribution split, and never present a fixed group price as fact.
- For verified employer examples, only UC employees and federal FEDVIP enrollees via Delta Dental are confirmed by primary sources. Anduril and other specific tech or defense names are unverified and must not be presented as Delta Dental clients without direct confirmation.
