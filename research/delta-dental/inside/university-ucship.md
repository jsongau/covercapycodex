# Inside Delta Dental: Universities, UC SHIP, and Student Dental

## Summary

A large share of US college students get their dental coverage through Delta Dental, often without realizing it. At the University of California, the UC Student Health Insurance Plan (UC SHIP) bundles dental into a single student plan, and that dental benefit is a Delta Dental of California PPO plan. Students are automatically enrolled unless they waive the plan, and the coverage follows the familiar Delta Dental PPO structure: preventive care at no cost, a small deductible on other services, and an annual benefit maximum that commonly sits around $1,000 but varies by campus and plan year (UCLA, for example, moved to a $1,200 maximum for 2026-27). This page explains why UC and many other universities choose Delta Dental, how the student coverage works, and what happens to that coverage at graduation. It is educational background, not personal benefits or purchasing advice.

## What UC SHIP is

UC SHIP is the University of California Student Health Insurance Plan. It is a comprehensive student plan that bundles medical, pharmacy, dental, vision, and mental health benefits together rather than selling dental separately. Students enrolled at participating UC campuses are automatically enrolled in UC SHIP, and the cost is billed as part of registration fees, with the annual cost typically split across payments. Dental is an included benefit within that bundle for both undergraduate and graduate students. A student who can show comparable outside coverage that meets UC's minimum criteria may request a waiver, which is handled through the plan's waiver administrator (Academic Health Plans). When a student waives, the dental benefit goes away with the rest of the plan, because dental is part of the bundle rather than a standalone add-on.

Sources: [UCLA Dental Plan](https://www.studenthealth.ucla.edu/insurance/benefits/dental-plan), [UC Davis UC SHIP Dental Benefits](https://shcs.ucdavis.edu/insurance/uc-ship-benefits-information/uc-ship-dental-benefits)

## The dental benefit is Delta Dental PPO

For UC SHIP, the dental benefit is provided through a Delta Dental of California PPO plan. Everyone enrolled in UC SHIP is automatically enrolled in the dental coverage. Dental services are not delivered at the campus student health center; students choose their own licensed dentist, and they pay the least by seeing a Delta Dental PPO dentist. The plan layers three cost tiers familiar to Delta Dental members:

1. Delta Dental PPO dentists, who accept the discounted PPO fee schedule (lowest out-of-pocket).
2. Delta Dental Premier dentists, a broad nationwide network whose fees are set by Delta Dental (usually lower out-of-pocket than a non-Delta dentist).
3. Any other licensed dentist, with no fee restrictions and typically the highest out-of-pocket cost.

A representative UC SHIP cost structure looks like this, though exact figures vary by campus and plan year:

- Deductible: about $25 per person per plan year on non-preventive services (some campuses show $0 on preventive and diagnostic care).
- Annual maximum: commonly around $1,000 per person per plan year, combining in-network and out-of-network benefits. UCLA published a 2026-27 increase to a $1,200 annual maximum.
- No waiting period.
- Preventive and diagnostic (exams, cleanings twice a year, x-rays): covered at 100% in-network, no cost.
- Basic services (fillings, extractions, root canals): covered around 80% in-network.
- Major services (crowns, inlays, onlays, cast restorations) and prosthodontics (bridges, dentures, implants): covered around 70% in-network.
- Orthodontics: typically not covered.

Where it varies: the exact deductible, the annual maximum, the coinsurance percentages, and the list of excluded services differ from campus to campus and from one plan year to the next. The numbers above are the common UC SHIP pattern, not a guarantee for any specific campus or year. Students confirm their own benefits through the UC SHIP Delta Dental member site.

Sources: [UC Davis UC SHIP Dental Benefits](https://shcs.ucdavis.edu/insurance/uc-ship-benefits-information/uc-ship-dental-benefits), [UCLA Dental Plan](https://www.studenthealth.ucla.edu/insurance/benefits/dental-plan), [UC SHIP Delta Dental member site](https://www.deltadentalins.com/ucship/)

## Why UC and other large universities pick Delta Dental

A few practical reasons explain why Delta Dental is so common in higher education:

- Network size near campuses. Delta Dental Premier is the largest dentist network in the country by unique participating dentists (more than 156,000), and Delta Dental PPO is also one of the largest. A big network means students are likely to find an in-network dentist close to campus, which matters when students do not have a car or a long-standing local dentist.
- Nationwide coverage that travels with students. Because the network is national, a student attending school in one state can still see an in-network Delta Dental dentist when they go home on breaks or move for an internship. For a population that splits time between a campus city and a home town, this portability is valuable.
- Student-friendly, simple design. The benefit is built around no-cost preventive care (two cleanings and checkups a year), which fits the goal of keeping young adults in routine care. About 28% of adults aged 20 to 34 have untreated tooth decay, the highest of any adult age group, so preventive-first design serves the demographic.
- Administrative fit for a bundle. Universities can fold a single Delta Dental group plan into a larger student health package, with eligibility driven off the registrar's enrollment data, online ID cards, a cost estimator tool, and self-service member portals. That reduces administrative load for the school's health center.

Sources: [Delta Dental of North Carolina Network Facts](https://deltadentalnc.com/about-us/network-facts), [Delta Dental of Minnesota Networks](https://www.deltadentalmn.org/delta-dental-networks), [Delta Dental Grin: dental coverage for college graduates](https://grin.deltadentalins.com/archive/2019/summer/dental-coverage-for-college-graduates/)

## How student coverage works in practice

- Automatic enrollment. UC SHIP students are enrolled in dental automatically; there is no separate dental sign-up.
- No referral. Dental does not require a referral from the student health center. A student picks a dentist and books directly.
- Eligibility by name and birthday. Registration on the Delta Dental member site uses the student's legal first and last name on file with the registrar, the campus student ID number, and date of birth. There is no separate dental application; eligibility flows from enrollment.
- What is covered. Preventive and diagnostic care first (no cost in-network), then basic, major, and prosthodontic services at decreasing coverage levels, all capped by the annual maximum.
- What is usually excluded. Orthodontics (braces) is typically not covered under UC SHIP dental, and some campuses also exclude items like maxillofacial prosthetics. Students who want orthodontic coverage generally look outside the student plan.
- Confirming details. Because specifics vary by campus and year, students verify their own coverage, find network dentists, and get cost estimates through the UC SHIP Delta Dental portal.

Sources: [UCLA Dental Plan](https://www.studenthealth.ucla.edu/insurance/benefits/dental-plan), [UC Davis UC SHIP Dental Benefits](https://shcs.ucdavis.edu/insurance/uc-ship-benefits-information/uc-ship-dental-benefits)

## Other universities and systems that use Delta Dental

Delta Dental is a widely used provider for student and scholar dental across many schools, often through the student health plan or through a voluntary buy-up plan. Verifiable examples include:

- University of Southern California (USC) offers a low-cost Delta Dental PPO option tied to its Student Health Insurance Plan.
- University of Denver offers Delta Dental coverage to students, structured as separate coverage from the medical student health plan.
- University of Iowa offers a student dental plan, with student dental administered in coordination with Delta Dental.
- Iowa State University runs a Delta Dental option within its Student and Scholar Health Insurance Program.
- Johns Hopkins University lists a Delta Dental plan among its student health benefits.
- University of Minnesota has a Delta Dental of Minnesota student dental plan.
- University Health Plans, a benefits administrator for many colleges, offers Delta Dental student plan options across multiple states (examples include Massachusetts, New Jersey, and Rhode Island school groups).

These examples show two common models: dental bundled into the student health plan (as with UC SHIP), and dental sold as an optional voluntary plan alongside the medical coverage. Schools and exact plan terms change over time, so any specific school's current offering should be confirmed on that school's benefits pages.

Sources: [USC SHIP Dental Insurance](https://sites.usc.edu/ship/dental-insurance/), [University of Denver Dental and Vision Coverage](https://studentaffairs.du.edu/health-counseling-center/insurance-plans-fees/dental-coverage), [University of Iowa Student Dental Insurance](https://hr.uiowa.edu/benefits/ui-student-insurance/student-insurance-overview/student-dental-insurance), [Iowa State Delta Dental](https://sship.hr.iastate.edu/delta-dental), [Johns Hopkins Delta Dental Plan](https://hr.jhu.edu/benefits-worklife/health-life/student-health-benefits/delta-dental-plan/), [Delta Dental of Minnesota: U of M Student Members](https://www.deltadentalmn.org/uofm-student-members), [University Health Plans Delta Dental options](https://www.universityhealthplans.com/letters/letter.cgi?group_id=161)

## The graduation gap: keeping Delta after SHIP ends

UC SHIP dental ends when a student is no longer enrolled, so graduation (or waiving the plan) creates a coverage gap. Delta Dental describes three common ways a recent graduate keeps dental coverage:

1. Stay on a parent's plan. Many dental plans cover adult children up to age 26, which lets a graduate keep the same plan and often the same dentist.
2. Get coverage through a new employer. Employer-sponsored dental is among the most common job benefits, and the employer usually pays part of the premium.
3. Buy an individual plan. For graduates who are self-employed or between jobs, Delta Dental offers individual and family plans, sold online and on state and federal exchanges. Availability differs by state: Delta Dental PPO individual plans are offered in a set of states (including California and others), and DeltaCare USA individual plans are offered in another set.

For a student who liked their UC SHIP Delta Dental dentist, an individual Delta Dental plan is one way to keep seeing an in-network Delta Dental provider after the student plan ends, since the dentist may participate in both the student group plan and individual Delta Dental networks. State availability and plan terms vary, so a graduate confirms what is offered where they live.

Sources: [Delta Dental Grin: how to keep your dental coverage after college](https://grin.deltadentalins.com/archive/2019/summer/dental-coverage-for-college-graduates/), [Delta Dental individuals and families](https://www1.deltadentalins.com/individuals-and-families.html)

## Hub implications

- UC SHIP is a high-volume, recurring Delta Dental touchpoint: every UC student is auto-enrolled in a Delta Dental PPO dental benefit, which makes "is my campus Delta Dental dentist nearby" a natural search intent CoverCapy can serve by surfacing Delta Dental PPO dentists near major UC campuses.
- The graduation gap is the clearest conversion moment for this audience. A student losing UC SHIP at graduation needs to either stay with a parent, find employer dental, or buy an individual plan, and CoverCapy can speak directly to "keep your Delta Dental dentist after graduation" without giving advice, framing it as continuity of care.
- Lean on the "varies by campus and plan year" reality as a trust signal rather than quoting hard numbers. The annual maximum alone ranges (about $1,000 commonly, $1,200 at UCLA for 2026-27), so content should teach the structure and point students to their own Delta Dental UC SHIP portal to confirm specifics.
- Orthodontics being excluded is a recurring student pain point and a useful educational angle: students wanting braces coverage are pushed outside the student plan, which is honest, non-advisory content that builds authority.
