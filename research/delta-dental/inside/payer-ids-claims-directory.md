# Inside Delta Dental: Payer IDs, Claims Addresses, and Phones Directory

Last reviewed: June 2026

## Summary

Delta Dental is not one insurer. It is the Delta Dental Plans Association, a federation of 39 independent member companies operating across all 50 states, the District of Columbia, and Puerto Rico. Each member company is financially responsible for its own products and, in most cases, has its own electronic claims payer ID, paper claims mailing address, and provider phone line. Because of this structure, there is no single Delta Dental payer ID that works everywhere. The correct payer ID for any given patient depends on which member company holds the group, which is normally determined by the state where the employer group is based, not by where the patient or the dental office is located.

This directory consolidates the payer IDs, paper claims addresses, and phone numbers published in Delta Dental's official member company contact sheet (DF03 rev. 10/24), cross-checked against state member company sites and clearinghouse payer lists. Delta Dental Insurance Company (DDIC) uses a universal payer ID, 94276, which many clearinghouses accept for any Delta entity as a fallback, but the company-specific ID is preferred. Payer IDs, PO boxes, and phone numbers change. Every entry below must be verified against the patient's current ID card or the live clearinghouse payer list before submitting a claim. Entries that could not be independently confirmed are flagged.

## How to find the right payer ID for a given patient

1. Read the patient's current ID card first. The card names the specific Delta Dental member company and shows the group number. The member company on the card, not the office's home state, determines where the claim goes.
2. The group's home state usually controls. The correct member company is normally the one in the state where the employer group is headquartered or where the plan was sold, which can differ from where the patient lives or is treated. A patient living in Nevada on a Texas employer group is a DDIC claim, not a Nevada-specific one.
3. Check the back of the card for the claims payer ID and claims mailing address. Many Delta cards print the payer ID and PO box directly on the reverse. When present, that printed value overrides any directory because it reflects the exact routing for that group.
4. Use Delta Dental's official Claims Submission Payer ID and Address Lookup Tool when the card is unclear. Select the member company from the dropdown to get the current address and payer ID.
5. Confirm in your clearinghouse payer list. Availity, DentalXChange, Change Healthcare, and Tesia each maintain their own Delta payer crosswalks; the same Delta entity can have a different routing code inside a given clearinghouse. When in doubt for a DDIC group, the universal 94276 is the documented fallback.
6. When the card and directory disagree, call the provider line for that member company and confirm before submitting.
