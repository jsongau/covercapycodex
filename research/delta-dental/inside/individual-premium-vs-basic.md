# Delta Dental Individual PPO: Premium vs Basic

**Last reviewed: June 2026**

## Summary

Delta Dental sells two individual (non-group) PPO tiers that share the same network and the same enroll-anytime structure but differ sharply in what they cover. The **PPO Individual Premium** plan (estimated starting near $73/month) is the full-coverage tier: it adds major services (crowns, root canals, implants, dentures), adult and child orthodontics, and a $2,000 annual maximum on top of the preventive and basic coverage. The **PPO Individual Basic** plan (estimated starting near $33/month) is the routine-care tier: it covers preventive care and a short list of basic services (fillings, simple extractions) at a lower coinsurance, caps benefits at $1,000 per year, and excludes major work, implants, dentures, periodontics, and orthodontics entirely.

Both tiers run on a **calendar year** (the annual maximum and deductible reset January 1), let the member visit any licensed dentist (with the best pricing in-network), and have **no waiting period for preventive care**. The most important thing for the pricecard toggle: Basic is not just "cheaper Premium." It is a structurally narrower plan that drops whole benefit categories, not just a tier with smaller numbers.

Two figures below carry a "(label: estimate)" note because published rates vary by age and ZIP code, and one benefit (basic coinsurance percentage) and several covered-service lines vary by the state certificate. Those variances are flagged in the table and notes.

## Decision guidance: who should pick which

**Pick Basic if:**
- You want low-cost protection mainly for routine, preventive care and the occasional filling.
- Your budget is the deciding factor (roughly $33/month versus $73/month is the largest single difference).
- You do not anticipate crowns, root canals, implants, dentures, gum treatment, or braces in the coverage year.
- You are comfortable with a $1,000 annual ceiling and 50% coinsurance on basic work.

**Pick Premium if:**
- You expect or want coverage for **major work**: crowns, root canals, bridges, dentures, or **implants** (all 50% after a 12-month wait).
- You or a dependent want **orthodontics** (adult or child, 50% up to a $1,500 lifetime maximum, 12-month wait, separate $50 ortho deductible).
- You want the **higher $2,000 annual maximum** and richer **80% basic coverage** (where the state certificate provides it).
- You value periodontal (gum) coverage, which Basic does not include at all.

The clean one-line rule for the hub: **Basic for routine and budget; Premium for ortho, implants, and major work.**

## State and benefit variation to flag

- **Premium basic coinsurance varies.** The DentalPlans.com Premium summary lists basic services at **80%**, while some Delta Dental state certificates publish basic at **50%**. Treat the basic percentage as state-dependent and verify against the specific state certificate before quoting a hard number.
- **Whitening and implants vary by state, and the California certificate is the key exclusion.** On the Premium plan, teeth whitening and night/mouth guards are shown at 80% as cosmetic services, but the **California Delta Dental certificate excludes teeth whitening** (and certain cosmetic items). Implant coverage on Premium is broadly available at 50% but is one of the lines most likely to differ or be limited by state, so confirm per certificate.
- **Missing-tooth clause: confirm by state.** Standard Delta Dental policies commonly apply a missing-tooth or pre-existing limitation to prosthodontics and implants (you generally cannot buy coverage for a tooth already missing before the policy began). Whether and how it applies depends on the state certificate, so this should be verified rather than asserted as "no missing-tooth clause."
- **Underwriting differs by state.** Premium and Basic are underwritten by Delta Dental Insurance Company in AL, DC, FL, GA, LA, MS, MT, NV, and UT, and by separate not-for-profit Delta Dental member companies in CA, PA, MD, NY, DE, and WV. Texas is sold as a DPO. This is why specifics shift state to state.

## Hub implications (the toggle)

For the "Inside Delta Dental" pricecard, build a two-state toggle (**Premium** | **Basic**) over a single card frame so the user reads the same rows in the same order and only the values swap. Lead the toggle with the two anchor numbers that move together: **premium price** (~$73 vs ~$33/mo, both labeled estimates) and **annual maximum** ($2,000 vs $1,000). Beneath those, render the coverage ladder (preventive, basic, major, ortho) as rows that visibly **gray out or read "Not covered"** on the Basic state for major, implants, periodontics, and orthodontics, so the toggle teaches the real difference rather than implying Basic is a discounted Premium.

Add a persistent footnote to the toggle: "Calendar-year maximum, resets Jan 1," "rates vary by age and ZIP," and "coverage and exclusions (including whitening and implants) vary by state, for example California excludes whitening." Surface the ortho detail (50%, $1,500 lifetime, separate $50 deductible, 12-month wait) only on the Premium state, and use the Premium toggle as the natural CTA bridge into the implants and orthodontics treatment guides.
