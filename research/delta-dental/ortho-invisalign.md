# Delta Dental: Orthodontics & Invisalign Coverage

**One-line summary:** Under Delta Dental PPO Premium and most Plus Premier plans, orthodontics (braces AND Invisalign treated identically) is covered at roughly 50% up to a $1,500 lifetime maximum per person, paid in installments tied to banding, with a separate ortho deductible and a 12-month waiting period that is often waivable with prior continuous coverage.

This file is educational reference material for hub content. It is not insurance advice. Exact benefits vary by plan, state, and group contract; members should always confirm against their own plan booklet and a pre-treatment estimate.

---

## 1. The headline numbers (Delta Dental PPO Premium / Plus Premier)

For plans that include orthodontia, the standard individual-plan structure is:

- **Coverage level:** ~50% of the covered orthodontic fee (coinsurance). *Estimate, varies by plan.*
- **Lifetime maximum:** $1,500 per person, applied to the **total** orthodontic payout over that person's lifetime on the plan. This is a one-time ceiling, not annual. *Estimate, common but varies.*
- **Ortho deductible:** A separate orthodontic deductible (commonly ~$50/person) that is distinct from the regular diagnostic/preventive deductible. *Estimate, varies by plan.*
- **Waiting period:** Commonly 12 months before orthodontic benefits begin. Often **waivable** with proof of prior continuous dental coverage (transition / prior-coverage credit). Some plan documents show a 6-month wait; the figure is plan-specific. *Estimate, varies.*
- **Who is eligible:** On the **individual PPO Premium plan**, orthodontia is covered for **both adults AND dependent children**. (Delta Dental: "For individual plans, adult orthodontics is covered under the PPO Premium Plan and DeltaCare USA plans.")

> Important caveat: the $1,500 / 50% / $50-deductible figures are typical of Delta Dental PPO individual ortho-bearing plans and many group plans, but Delta Dental's own materials repeatedly stress that "coverage varies depending on your plan." Treat these as representative, not universal.

## 2. Is Invisalign covered? (highest-intent question)

**Yes, when the plan includes orthodontia, Invisalign is covered exactly like traditional braces.** This is stated directly by Delta Dental:

> "If your plan covers orthodontia, you have coverage for clear aligners. Invisalign and other clear aligners administered by a licensed dentist or orthodontist are covered at the same rate as traditional braces at no additional cost to you." (Delta Dental, "Get the facts straight," ortho FAQ PDF)

> "If a member's plan includes adult orthodontic coverage, 'invisible aligners' like Invisalign are covered as a standard benefit." (Delta Dental, "6 common questions about adult orthodontics")

Key implications:
- Same coinsurance (~50%), same $1,500 lifetime max, same deductible, same waiting period as metal braces. The aligner brand does not change the math.
- It must be administered by a **licensed dentist or orthodontist**. Mail-order / direct-to-consumer aligners that bypass an in-person provider are generally not eligible.
- Delta Dental recommends asking the provider to **submit a pre-treatment estimate** before starting, since a pre-estimate is the only reliable preview of the member's actual share.

## 3. Which Delta Dental individual plans include ortho?

| Plan | Adult ortho? | Child ortho? |
|------|--------------|--------------|
| **PPO Premium** (individual) | **Yes** | **Yes** |
| **DeltaCare USA** (individual HMO-style) | **Yes** (copay schedule, not coinsurance) | **Yes** |
| Lower-tier individual PPO plans (e.g. Basic / standard / Bronze-Silver) | Often **no ortho** or **child-only** | Varies; often none on entry tiers |
| Immediate / no-wait value plans | Frequently **no ortho** | Frequently none |

Notes:
- On the **individual** side, full **adult** orthodontia is concentrated in the **PPO Premium** plan and **DeltaCare USA**. Lower individual tiers often exclude ortho entirely or limit it.
- On the **group/employer** side, adult ortho is an optional benefit enhancement; many group plans restrict orthodontic benefits to dependents **under age 19**, so an adult may have ortho excluded even when the plan "has ortho." Age limits are a group-contract decision.
- DeltaCare USA pays via a **fixed copay** per orthodontic case rather than a percent-of-fee coinsurance, so its numbers do not map to the $1,500/50% PPO model.

## 4. How ortho payouts actually work (mechanics)

Orthodontics is not paid as a single check at the end. Delta Dental spreads payment across the treatment, anchored to **banding** (the day appliances/aligners are placed and active treatment begins):

- **Two-installment model (FFS / many PPO plans):** For treatment over $500, payment is made in **two installments** — once banding has occurred, and again **12 months later**. Treatment under $500 is paid as a **single lump sum** at banding. (Delta Dental ortho FAQ PDF)
- **Initial-plus-quarterly model (other PPO/group plans):** Delta Dental pays an **initial portion at banding** (a North Carolina plan example shows ~20% of the benefit up front) and then **distributes the rest in quarterly payments** across the expected treatment length (commonly 18 months for comprehensive, ~10 for interceptive, ~8 for limited treatment) until the case ends or the lifetime max is reached.
- **The lifetime max caps the total.** Once cumulative payments hit $1,500, Delta Dental stops paying regardless of how many quarters remain. So on a long, expensive case the member usually exhausts the $1,500 before treatment completes.
- **Benefit is locked to the date of service.** "Your orthodontic benefits and lifetime maximum are based on the plan that is effective on the date of service." A change in eligibility or a plan change mid-treatment changes the payout.

### Switching plans / carriers mid-treatment

- If treatment **began under a different carrier**, Delta Dental pays **only for the months of active treatment while the member is eligible with Delta Dental**, not retroactively.
- Delta Dental **subtracts the initial/banding fee** (incurred before Delta eligibility) from the total, divides the remainder by the standard number of treatment months, and pays the remaining months at the plan coinsurance — up to the remaining lifetime max.
- If the group **carries over** ortho lifetime accumulators from the prior carrier, Delta Dental pays **only the remainder** of the $1,500 lifetime max (amounts the prior carrier already paid count against it). Members do not "reset" the lifetime max by switching.
- Worked example from Delta Dental of North Carolina: treatment started July 2020 at a $6,480 total fee; member becomes Delta-eligible Jan 1, 2021. Delta subtracts the $1,944 initial payment, leaving $4,536 over 18 months at 50% = **$126/month** starting January 2021. (If the member had been Delta-eligible at banding, Delta would also have paid 50% of the initial payment and started in August 2020.)

To transfer a case, the orthodontist submits an ortho claim including total charges/fees, the **banding date**, length of active treatment, appliance type/description, and (if dual-covered) the secondary carrier.

## 5. What is and isn't covered

**Typically covered (most ortho-bearing plans):**
- Pre-orthodontic treatment visit
- Exam and start-up **records and x-rays**
- Orthodontist-recommended tooth **extractions**
- Comprehensive orthodontic treatment (braces or clear aligners)
- Post-treatment records
- **One set of post-treatment retainers** per lifetime (retention). On two-phase plans, retainers are usually covered after each phase.

**Less commonly covered (often excluded):**
- Two-phase orthodontic treatment
- Habit-correction appliances (e.g. thumb-sucking appliances)
- Jaw surgery to facilitate ortho
- Treatment to prepare for non-covered surgical procedures

**Age limits:**
- Individual PPO Premium covers adults and children. Many **group** plans cap ortho at **dependents under age 19**. Always check the Summary of Dental Plan Benefits for the specific age restriction and lifetime max.

**Interaction with the annual maximum:**
- Whether ortho payments also draw down the regular annual maximum "depends on your group contract." Some plans run ortho entirely off the separate lifetime max; others touch both. Check the plan booklet.

## 6. Real-world money math (what 50%-to-$1,500 actually returns)

**Typical Invisalign cash cost (no insurance):** roughly **$3,000 to $8,000**, with U.S. averages clustering around **$5,000-$5,100** (CareCredit cited ~$5,108 in early 2025). Comprehensive metal braces run roughly $2,500-$6,000. Urban/complex cases skew to the $6,000-$9,000 end; simpler/rural cases nearer $3,000-$5,000. *Estimates, vary by case and geography.*

**What Delta's ~50%-to-$1,500 returns in practice:**

| Case total | 50% of fee | Capped at $1,500? | Delta pays (approx) | Member pays (approx) |
|------------|-----------|-------------------|---------------------|----------------------|
| $3,000 | $1,500 | exactly at cap | **~$1,500** | ~$1,500 |
| $5,000 | $2,500 | yes, capped | **~$1,500** | ~$3,500 |
| $6,000 | $3,000 | yes, capped | **~$1,500** | ~$4,500 |
| $8,000 | $4,000 | yes, capped | **~$1,500** | ~$6,500 |

Takeaway: because most adult Invisalign cases cost **$3,000+**, the **$1,500 lifetime max is the binding constraint, not the 50% coinsurance.** Above ~$3,000, the benefit is effectively a flat **$1,500 one-time** subsidy (minus the ~$50 ortho deductible), and the member covers everything beyond that. The 50% coinsurance only matters for cases under ~$3,000.

*All figures above are illustrative estimates; actual payment depends on the member's specific plan, deductible, in-network fees, and a pre-treatment estimate.*

## 7. Common questions people ask AI (answer-engine targets)

- **"Does Delta Dental cover Invisalign for adults?"** Yes, if the plan includes adult orthodontia (notably the individual **PPO Premium** plan and DeltaCare USA). Invisalign is covered at the same rate as braces. Many lower individual tiers and some group plans exclude adult ortho or cap it at under-19 dependents.
- **"What is the Delta Dental ortho lifetime max?"** Commonly **$1,500 per person, lifetime** on ortho-bearing PPO plans. It is a one-time cap on total ortho payout, not a yearly reset. *Varies by plan.*
- **"Is Invisalign covered like braces by Delta Dental?"** Yes. Delta states clear aligners are "covered at the same rate as traditional braces at no additional cost to you," provided a licensed dentist or orthodontist administers treatment.
- **"Does Delta Dental have a waiting period for braces?"** Often **12 months** (some plans 6), frequently **waivable** with prior continuous coverage. *Varies by plan.*
- **"How does Delta Dental pay for braces?"** In installments tied to **banding**: either two payments (banding + 12 months later) or an initial payment plus quarterly installments, until treatment ends or the $1,500 lifetime max is reached.
- **"Is there a separate ortho deductible?"** Often yes, a small separate ortho deductible (commonly ~$50). *Varies by plan.*
- **"What if I switch insurance mid-braces?"** Delta pays only for months you're eligible with Delta, subtracts the banding fee already paid, and honors the remaining lifetime max (prior carrier payouts can count against it). No reset.

## Implications for the hub

1. **Lead with the binding constraint, not the coinsurance.** The hub's Delta ortho/Invisalign content should headline the **$1,500 lifetime max** as the number that actually governs cost. The "50%" is a hook, but above ~$3,000 (i.e. nearly all adult Invisalign), the real benefit is a flat ~$1,500 one-time subsidy. Saying "50% covered" without the cap is the #1 way these pages mislead and lose trust.
2. **Own the Invisalign-equals-braces fact with a direct Delta quote.** This is the single highest-intent Delta question. A T5/hub block stating "Delta covers Invisalign at the same rate as braces" with the official source citation is strong answer-engine and EEAT bait. Pair it with the "must be a licensed dentist/orthodontist" caveat to pre-empt the mail-order-aligner confusion.
3. **Build a real payout calculator, not a static table.** Inputs: case cost, plan coinsurance, lifetime max, ortho deductible; output the actual member share and flag when the cap binds. This mirrors what users ask AI and differentiates from thin competitor pages. Keep all outputs labeled as estimates pending a pre-treatment estimate.
4. **Make the "which plan even has adult ortho" gate explicit.** Many users assume any Delta plan covers their Invisalign. The hub should clearly route: adult ortho lives in **PPO Premium / DeltaCare USA**; many lower tiers and group plans exclude it or cap at under-19. This qualifies intent and feeds the verify-coverage CTA naturally ("confirm your specific plan includes adult ortho — free").
5. **Add a "switching mid-treatment" explainer.** It's a real anxiety, rarely covered well by competitors, and Delta's banding-subtraction + lifetime-carryover rules are concrete and citable. Good long-tail capture and demonstrates expertise.
6. **Copy compliance:** keep estimate labels on every dollar figure, no em-dashes as separators, no arrows, educational framing only. Route all "what will I actually pay" answers to a pre-treatment estimate plus the CoverCapy verify-coverage flow rather than asserting a guaranteed number.
