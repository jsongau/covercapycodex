# Delta Dental for Seniors: Medicare Advantage, SCAN Health Plan, and Medi-Cal (Denti-Cal)

**One-line summary:** For most California and Washington seniors, Delta Dental coverage already comes bundled inside a SCAN Medicare Advantage plan (DeltaCare USA DHMO at no extra cost, with an optional Delta Dental Medicare Advantage PPO buy-up), and Medi-Cal Dental (Denti-Cal) is a separate state program that Delta Dental administers but whose benefits do not transfer to any private Delta plan; a standalone individual Delta PPO Premium only makes sense for seniors who lack both of those routes.

---

## 1. SCAN Health Plan: dental runs through Delta Dental

SCAN Health Plan is a not-for-profit Medicare Advantage organization. For 2026 it serves more than 400,000 Medicare beneficiaries across 33 counties in Arizona, California, Nevada, New Mexico, Texas, and Washington. That is the overall medical service area for SCAN-branded plans (including SCAN Desert Health Plan in Arizona and the state-specific SCAN entities).

Dental is a supplemental benefit layered on top of the medical plan. SCAN offers dental through two different partners depending on plan, state, and county:

- **Delta Dental** serves SCAN members in southern California and Washington.
- **DentaQuest** serves SCAN members in Arizona, California, Nevada, New Mexico, and Texas.

Both use the member's existing SCAN ID card. There is no separate dental ID card. This research note focuses on the Delta Dental side.

### What a SCAN member with Delta Dental actually has

All SCAN plans include the preventive dental services that Original Medicare covers. Most SCAN plans also bundle supplemental dental at no additional premium, and that is where Delta Dental comes in.

- **Base benefit (most members, no extra cost): DeltaCare USA, a Dental HMO (DHMO).** This is the default. The member is treated through the DeltaCare USA network and, like most DHMOs, works from an assigned in-network dentist with low, predictable copays, year-long coverage, no claim forms, and no annual dollar maximum. The member can change the assigned DeltaCare USA dentist at any time.
- **Optional upgrade (extra premium): Delta Dental Medicare Advantage PPO buy-up.** Members who want a wider choice of dentists can pay an additional monthly premium to switch into the Delta Dental Medicare Advantage PPO network instead of DeltaCare USA.

### How to use it before buying anything separate

The practical message for a 65+ SCAN member: you most likely already have dental coverage built into your Medicare Advantage plan. Before paying for a standalone individual dental policy, a member should:

1. Confirm whether their SCAN plan includes the dental benefit and whether it is Delta Dental or DentaQuest in their county.
2. If Delta Dental, confirm whether they are on the no-cost DeltaCare USA DHMO or have already added the PPO buy-up.
3. Use the Delta Dental "Find a Dentist" tool or call SCAN/Delta Dental customer service at 1-855-830-6583 (TTY 711) to check whether their preferred dentist is in network.
4. Only if the network does not fit their needs should they evaluate the PPO buy-up, and only after that consider a fully separate plan.

Buying a separate individual dental plan on top of SCAN dental usually means paying twice for overlapping coverage.

## 2. The Delta Dental Medicare Advantage PPO network (the buy-up)

The optional PPO buy-up moves a SCAN member from the DeltaCare USA DHMO into the Delta Dental Medicare Advantage PPO network. When using a dentist in that PPO network, the member gets:

- Discounted, pre-negotiated rates on plan-covered services (cost protection).
- Plan-covered services at pre-set copays.
- No annual dollar maximum.
- No claim forms to submit.

Important operational details:

- **SCAN does not auto-assign a PPO dentist.** The member must choose one from the Delta Dental Medicare Advantage PPO network using the Find a Dentist tool.
- **Out-of-network costs more.** Out-of-network dentists can charge their standard, non-discounted rates.
- **Enrollment in the buy-up is time-limited.** It is restricted to certain times and/or qualifying events during the year. To enroll, the member contacts SCAN Member Services (number on the back of the SCAN card) and submits the SCAN Dental Enrollment Form.

For 2026, Delta Dental lists SCAN Health Plan (CA, WA) among its Medicare Advantage PPO health-plan partners, alongside plans such as Aspire Health Plan, Central Health, Champion Health Plan (new for 2026), CHRISTUS Health Plan, Imperial Health Plan, Molina Healthcare, and Sharp Health Plan. From the dentist's side, services for these Medicare Advantage PPO patients are only covered if the office participates in Delta Dental's Medicare Advantage PPO network, which is a distinct network from commercial Delta Dental PPO.

## 3. Medi-Cal Dental (Denti-Cal): a separate state program

Medi-Cal Dental, historically branded Denti-Cal, is California's Medicaid dental program, run under the California Department of Health Care Services (DHCS). Delta Dental of California administers the fee-for-service portion of this program on behalf of DHCS: it processes treatment authorization requests and claims and enforces the rules and guidelines that DHCS sets. DHCS, not Delta Dental, owns the program and its benefit rules.

Key facts:

- **Eligibility is needs-based.** A member must first be Medi-Cal eligible, which the county department of social services determines based on income and family size. Once eligible, the member's scope of dental benefits is set by an aid code assigned at eligibility determination.
- **Members find care through the Medi-Cal Dental Find-A-Dentist directory** and member-facing resources at SmileCalifornia.org. The member help line is 1-800-322-6384.

### Clearing up the common confusion

Many seniors and caregivers assume that because Delta Dental administers Denti-Cal, a Denti-Cal patient somehow has a Delta Dental private plan, or that benefits from one carry over to the other. They do not. The distinction matters:

- **Denti-Cal is a public benefit program.** Its dentist network, covered services, authorizations, and limits are defined by DHCS and Medi-Cal rules, not by any commercial Delta Dental product.
- **Benefits do not transfer.** Being on Denti-Cal does not give a person access to Delta Dental's commercial PPO or DeltaCare USA networks, and a commercial Delta Dental plan does not give access to Denti-Cal providers or pricing.
- **Delta Dental here is the claims administrator, not the insurer of a private policy.** A Denti-Cal patient is a Medi-Cal beneficiary who happens to have Delta Dental of California processing the paperwork.

For a 65+ person, the relevant point is that Denti-Cal and a private Delta plan are mutually exclusive in practice: someone who qualifies for full-scope Medi-Cal Dental generally should use that benefit rather than pay out of pocket for a private Delta plan, and someone who does not qualify cannot rely on Denti-Cal at all.

## 4. General guidance: when a 65+ person should vs should not buy an individual Delta PPO Premium

Original Medicare (Parts A and B) does not include routine dental, so seniors who want predictable dental coverage need it from somewhere else: a Medicare Advantage plan's dental benefit, Medi-Cal Dental if eligible, or a standalone individual policy such as Delta Dental PPO Premium. The following is educational, not personalized advice; coverage, premiums, and waiting periods vary by plan and state, and a licensed agent or the carrier should confirm specifics.

**A standalone individual Delta PPO Premium is more likely to make sense when a senior:**

- Has Original Medicare with no Medicare Advantage dental benefit and is not Medi-Cal eligible.
- Wants the widest dentist choice and richer coverage for major work (crowns, dentures, denture repair, bridges, implants, root canals) that a DHMO copay schedule may not fully address.
- Is diligent about twice-yearly cleanings and exams and expects at least one moderate procedure per year, so premiums are likely offset by covered services.
- Can plan around waiting periods: major services on many individual plans are subject to roughly a 9 to 12 month waiting period before coverage applies.

**A standalone individual Delta PPO Premium is less likely to make sense when a senior:**

- Already has Delta Dental (or DentaQuest) dental bundled in a Medicare Advantage plan such as SCAN. Buying a separate plan usually means paying twice for overlapping coverage; the PPO buy-up inside the existing plan is often the cleaner upgrade path.
- Qualifies for full-scope Medi-Cal Dental (Denti-Cal), in which case the public program is the primary route and a private plan generally is not needed for covered services.
- Is in excellent dental health and realistically needs nothing beyond one annual cleaning. In that narrow case, a dental discount plan or paying cash can cost less than monthly premiums.
- Has major work scheduled within the next several months, since waiting periods can mean the plan does not pay for that work in time.

**Decision shortcut for the hub:** check the existing Medicare Advantage plan first, then check Medi-Cal eligibility, and only then price a standalone individual Delta PPO Premium. The standalone plan is the fallback, not the default.

## Implications for the hub /over-65 page

- **Lead with "you may already be covered."** The biggest senior misconception is that Original Medicare includes dental and the second biggest is that a standalone plan is the only option. The /over-65 page should open by routing the visitor: do you have a Medicare Advantage plan (like SCAN) with dental, are you Medi-Cal eligible, or neither. Only the "neither" path lands on an individual Delta PPO Premium recommendation.
- **Explain the DeltaCare USA DHMO vs PPO buy-up choice clearly.** Many SCAN members do not realize their no-cost dental is a DHMO with an assigned dentist, and that a wider dentist choice is available as a paid buy-up inside the same plan rather than as a brand-new policy. A simple two-column comparison (DeltaCare USA DHMO at no extra cost vs Delta Dental Medicare Advantage PPO buy-up) fits the boutique-concierge tone and prevents double-buying.
- **Add a dedicated Denti-Cal myth-buster block.** State plainly that Delta Dental administers Denti-Cal for the state but that Denti-Cal is a separate public program and its benefits do not transfer to any private Delta plan. This protects the brand from confused users and positions CoverCapy as the trustworthy concierge that tells people the truth even when it means not selling them a plan.
- **Frame the standalone Delta PPO Premium as the fallback, not the hero.** Use the should/should-not guidance above as a short checklist, and flag the waiting-period trap (roughly 9 to 12 months on major work) prominently so a senior with imminent major work is not steered into a plan that will not pay in time.
- **Keep it educational, with a soft handoff to verification.** Every claim should be framed as general information with a prompt to verify specifics with the plan or a licensed agent, which dovetails with the existing CoverCapy "verify my coverage, free" CTA pattern.
