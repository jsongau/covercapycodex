# Delta Dental: GEO/FAQ Question Mining + Keyword Clusters

**One-line summary:** A cited mining of 35+ real Delta Dental questions grouped by intent, with verified short answers, long-tail keyword clusters, and ready-to-ship FAQPage schema sets for the Delta hub, compare, and over-65 cluster pages.

> Conventions used here: no em-dash separators, no arrow glyphs. All figures labeled "(estimate)" are not authoritative and vary by state company and plan. Delta Dental is a federation of ~39 independent state companies, so almost every coverage and price detail varies by state and plan. Verify against the member's plan documents.

## 1. Question bank by intent (35+ real questions)

### A. Coverage ("does Delta cover X")
1. **Does Delta Dental cover implants?** Usually yes under major services at about 50% after any waiting period, deductible, and annual maximum, and only when deemed medically necessary to restore function. Varies by plan and state.
2. **Does Delta Dental cover crowns?** Typically 50% of the cost under major services, often limited to once per tooth in a 5 to 7 year window. Varies by plan.
3. **Does Delta Dental cover root canals?** Generally yes, often at about 80% as a basic service on PPO plans. Varies by plan.
4. **Does Delta Dental cover braces / orthodontics?** Only on plans that include an ortho rider, usually 50% up to a lifetime maximum (commonly $1,000 to $2,500), often child-only unless the plan includes adult ortho. Varies widely.
5. **Does Delta Dental cover Invisalign?** Yes when the plan includes adult ortho and the case meets clinical criteria; treated the same as braces against the ortho lifetime max. Varies by plan.
6. **Does Delta Dental cover wisdom teeth removal?** Usually yes, commonly 50% to 80% of cost depending on whether it is simple or surgical. (estimate) Varies by plan.
7. **Does Delta Dental cover dentures?** Yes, typically a major service at about 50% after the major-services waiting period. Varies by plan.
8. **Does Delta Dental cover deep cleaning (scaling and root planing)?** Often yes as a basic/periodontal service, with frequency limits (cleanings commonly capped at two per benefit year). Varies by plan.
9. **Does Delta Dental cover veneers?** Usually no when cosmetic; may be payable only when medically necessary due to fracture or decay, once per tooth per ~8 years. Mostly excluded.
10. **Does Delta Dental cover teeth whitening?** Generally no, it is cosmetic and excluded on most plans.
11. **Does Delta Dental cover night guards / occlusal guards?** Often yes, commonly once per ~3 year period on plans that include it. Varies by plan.
12. **Does Delta Dental cover sealants?** Usually yes for children, often first and second permanent molars, age 15 and under, once per tooth per ~3 years. Varies by plan.
13. **Does Delta Dental cover fillings?** Yes, basic service, commonly ~80% on PPO plans. Varies by plan.
14. **Does Delta Dental cover dental bridges?** Yes, major service at about 50%. Varies by plan.

### B. Cost / premium / annual maximum
15. **How much does Delta Dental cost per month?** Individual PPO plans commonly run roughly $20 to $60 per month; one 2026 state example listed individual at $38/mo, family at $130/mo. (estimate) Varies by state and plan.
16. **What is the Delta Dental annual maximum?** Commonly $1,000 to $2,000 per benefit year; the PPO Premium individual plan has moved to a $2,000 maximum in several states. Varies by plan.
17. **What is the Delta Dental deductible?** Often ~$50 per person and ~$150 per family per calendar year, usually waived for diagnostic and preventive care. Varies by plan.
18. **What does Delta Dental PPO Premium cost and cover?** Highest individual tier, larger annual maximum (often $2,000) and broader major coverage, higher premium than Basic. Varies by state.

### C. Waiting periods
19. **Does Delta Dental have a waiting period?** Often yes for basic and major services; major work (crowns, dentures, implants) commonly has a 6, 12, or 24 month wait, with 12 months typical.
20. **Is there a waiting period for crowns / major services?** Commonly 12 months on Platinum/Gold/Silver style tiers. Varies by plan.
21. **Does Delta Dental have a no-waiting-period plan?** Some plans offer immediate coverage across preventive, basic, and major. Availability varies by state.
22. **Can the waiting period be waived?** Sometimes, if you had comparable prior coverage that ended within ~30 to 60 days of the new plan's effective date.

### D. Network / find-a-dentist
23. **What is the difference between Delta Dental PPO and Premier?** PPO is the lower-cost network (dentists accept reduced PPO fees as payment in full); Premier is larger but with smaller savings; you save most with PPO.
24. **How do I find a Delta Dental dentist near me?** Use the state company's Find a Dentist tool, enter ZIP, and select the network; you can filter for "accepts new patients."
25. **Does my dentist take Delta Dental?** Check the directory or call the office; verify both the carrier and the specific network (PPO vs Premier), since participation differs.
26. **What happens if I see an out-of-network dentist?** You usually pay up front and submit a claim for reimbursement, and may owe balance billing above Delta's allowed amount.

### E. Ortho / implants (high-intent)
27. **What is the Delta Dental orthodontic lifetime maximum?** Commonly $1,000 to $2,500; PPO Premium and PPO plus Premier plans often list ~$1,500. Varies by plan.
28. **Does Delta Dental cover adult orthodontics?** Only on plans with adult ortho; individual adult ortho is generally limited to the PPO Premium and DeltaCare USA plans. Varies by state.
29. **Is there an age limit for ortho coverage?** Many plans limit ortho to under 19 unless the plan specifically includes adult ortho.
30. **How much does Delta Dental pay toward implants?** Typically 50% of the major-service allowed amount, capped by the annual maximum. Varies by plan.

### F. Seniors / Medicare
31. **Does Medicare cover dental, and where does Delta Dental fit?** Original Medicare and Medigap do not cover routine dental; Delta Dental sells standalone senior/individual plans and partners with some Medicare Advantage plans.
32. **What do Delta Dental Medicare Advantage dental benefits include?** Often preventive only (exams, cleanings, x-rays), frequently in-network only, with limited or no major coverage. Varies sharply by MA plan and state.
33. **Can a retiree over 65 buy a Delta Dental individual plan?** Yes, standalone individual/senior plans can be purchased year-round, no Medicare enrollment window required.

### G. Claims
34. **How do I file a Delta Dental claim?** In-network dentists submit for you and Delta pays them directly; out-of-network, you submit a signed claim form plus itemized receipt by mail or online.
35. **How do I check my Delta Dental claim status?** Log in to the member account online to see claims, eligibility, and benefits for you and dependents.
36. **Is there a deadline to file a claim?** Generally yes; claims received more than 12 months after treatment may not be paid.

### H. Calendar year / benefit period
37. **When does the Delta Dental annual maximum reset?** For calendar-year plans, the maximum and deductible reset January 1; once the max is hit, you pay 100% until reset.
38. **What is Carryover Max?** A feature on some plans that rolls unused annual maximum into the next year (like a dental savings account), but it requires at least one cleaning or exam each year or accrued carryover is lost. Plan-dependent.

### I. "Is Delta Dental good insurance"
39. **Is Delta Dental good insurance / worth it?** Generally strong value if you use preventive and basic care and have in-network providers nearby; less worthwhile with minimal needs or thin local networks. Largest US dentist network is a frequent pro.

### J. Cancellation
40. **How do I cancel my Delta Dental plan?** Submit a written request (email, fax, or mail) with name, DOB, address, policy number, reason, and termination date; many individual policies carry a 12-month commitment unless a qualifying event applies. Customer service for individual plans: 888-899-3734.

## 4. Competitor ranking notes and CoverCapy gaps to win

**What ranks now (observed in results):**
- Dental-practice blogs (Humble Memorial, Moores Chapel, Carolina Complete, Nassau Bay, etc.) dominate "does Delta Dental cover [procedure]" queries. They are thin, single-procedure posts that rank because they directly answer one PAA question.
- Delta's own state companies (deltadentalwa.com, deltadentalil.com, deltadental.com) own the definitional terms (annual maximum, waiting period, PPO vs Premier, Carryover Max).
- Third-party marketplaces (DentalPlans.com, dentalinsurance.com, SeniorLiving.org, budgetseniors.com) rank for plan-cost and senior intent.

**Gaps CoverCapy can win:**
1. **State/plan variance honesty.** Practice blogs state flat percentages as if national. A page that explicitly shows "varies by state company and plan, here is the typical range, here is how to verify" earns AI-answer trust and featured-snippet eligibility.
2. **"Verify before you book" angle.** None of the competitors connect the coverage question to an action. CoverCapy's verify-my-coverage CTA closes the loop the blogs leave open.
3. **PPO vs Premier vs DeltaCare in one comparison.** Delta explains PPO vs Premier but rarely lines up all three plus cost impact in a single scannable answer block.
4. **Procedure + waiting-period + annual-max combined.** Most ranking pages answer only the coverage percent and ignore that the waiting period and annual max gate real out-of-pocket. A combined answer is more useful and more snippet-worthy.
5. **Over-65 clarity.** SeniorLiving and budgetseniors rank, but a page that cleanly separates "Original Medicare = no dental," "MA dental = usually preventive only, in-network only," and "standalone Delta individual = year-round" can win the senior cluster.
6. **Find-a-dentist intent with local pairing.** CoverCapy's 6,400 local pages can capture "does [my dentist] take Delta Dental" and "Delta Dental PPO dentist in [city]" long-tail that Delta's generic tool does not target by city.
