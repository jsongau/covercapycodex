# Delta Dental PPO Premium: Limitations, Exclusions, Frequencies, and State Variation

**One-line summary:** Delta Dental PPO Premium is a single national marketing brand that is actually underwritten by different state companies, and the binding contract (the policy / Certificate / Evidence of Coverage) varies materially by state on waiting periods, the missing-tooth rule, frequency intervals, and even whether teeth whitening and implants are covered at all, so the national "80% whitening / 50% implants" summary directly conflicts with the filed contract in states like California.

> Scope note: This brief covers the individual and family Delta Dental PPO Premium product. Employer/group plans differ and are negotiated per contract. All percentages, intervals, and amounts below are read from the cited documents on the dates fetched (June 2026). The binding document is always the member's own state policy, not any summary.

---

## 1. The core problem: brand is national, contract is per-state

Delta Dental PPO is one national marketing brand, but it is underwritten by a patchwork of legally separate companies. Each files its own policy with its own state regulator, so limitations and exclusions are NOT uniform across states even under the same "PPO Premium" name. The official Delta Dental product page itself says basic, major, implant and ortho coverage carry a "waiting period (varies by state)" and that "specific benefit information may vary by state." [DDINS PPO product page]

### Underwriter-by-state map (official, current 2026 disclosure)

Delta Dental PPO plans are underwritten by:

- **Delta Dental Insurance Company** in: AL, DC, FL, GA, LA, MS, MT, NV, UT (DC policy form IENT-P-CORE-DC-R26)
- **Delta Dental of California** in: CA
- **Delta Dental of Pennsylvania** in: PA, MD
- **Delta Dental of New York, Inc.** in: NY
- **Delta Dental of Delaware, Inc.** in: DE
- **Delta Dental of West Virginia, Inc.** in: WV
- **Texas:** Delta Dental Insurance Company provides a Dental Provider Organization (DPO) plan, not a true PPO.

Individual PPO plans are sold in: **AL, CA, DE, FL, GA, LA, MD, MS, MT, NV, NY, PA, TX, UT, WV, and DC** (16 jurisdictions). Other states are served by separate regional Delta member companies (for example Oregon is Oregon Dental Service dba Delta Dental Plan of Oregon, a different legal entity with different contract language). [DDINS PPO product page; SmileWay footnote 2; DentalPlans.com Premium disclaimer]

Practical implication: a Delta Dental of Oregon policy and a Delta Dental of California policy can both be called "Delta Dental PPO" yet have different crown-replacement intervals (7 years in OR vs 5 years in CA) and opposite treatment of cosmetic services.

## 2. Frequency limits

### National marketing summary (DentalPlans.com / DDINS)
PPO Premium markets: Preventive 100%, Basic 80%, Major 50%, $2,000 calendar-year max, $50/$150 deductible. The summary states exams/cleanings/x-rays/sealants are covered but does not enumerate intervals; it directs the reader to the policy. [DentalPlans.com Premium; DDINS PPO product page]

### California (Delta Dental of California, official Certificate of Coverage, form XIAtBhi-CA-ENT-24)
- **Oral exams:** no more than twice per calendar year.
- **Cleanings (prophylaxis / periodontal maintenance / scaling for inflammation, any combination):** no more than twice per calendar year. Full-mouth debridement once per lifetime under conditions.
- **Bitewing x-rays:** once each calendar year.
- **Complete intraoral series and panoramic film:** each once every 60 months (5 years).
- **Crowns and Inlays/Onlays:** not more than once in any 60-month period (5 years) per tooth, with an exception for documented extensive structural loss.
- **Core buildup, post and core, crown repairs:** each once per 60 months.
- **Fixed bridge / denture (prosthodontic appliance):** replaced only after 60 months (5 years).
- **Fillings:** replacement of a filling by the same provider within 24 months is not separately covered.
- **Scaling and root planing:** once per quadrant per 24 months.
- **Sedative/protective restorations:** once per tooth per lifetime.

[Delta Dental of California Certificate of Coverage PDF]

### Oregon (Delta Dental Plan of Oregon, official 2025 benefit summary)
- **Exams:** once in any 6-month period.
- **Cleaning or periodontal maintenance:** once in any 6-month period, plus up to 2 additional periodontal maintenances per year for members with periodontal disease.
- **Bitewing x-rays:** once in any 12-month period.
- **Complete series or panoramic film:** once in any 5-year period.
- **Fluoride:** once in any 6-month period; standard to age 19, and for adults 19+ only with recent periodontal surgery or high decay risk.
- **Sealants:** limited to unrestored occlusal surfaces of **permanent molars**, one per tooth per 5-year period.
- **Crowns / cast restorations (incl. pontics):** once in any **7-year** period per tooth.
- **Bridge or denture:** once in any **7-year** period, and only if the tooth has not received a cast-restoration benefit in the prior 7 years.
- **Scaling and root planing:** once per quadrant per 2-year period.
- **Implants and implant removal:** once per lifetime per tooth space; crown over implant once per lifetime of the implant.
- **Night guard:** 100% once per 5-year period up to a $200 max; over-the-counter guards excluded.

[Delta Dental of Oregon 2025 PPO Plan benefit summary PDF]

**Flagged variation:** Crown/bridge/denture replacement interval is **5 years in California vs 7 years in Oregon**. Any hub statement of "once every X years" must be labeled per-state, not stated as a single national fact.

### SmileWay Wellness Benefits (the "up to 4 cleanings" path)
The four-cleanings-per-year figure comes from **SmileWay Wellness Benefits**, NOT the base PPO frequency. SmileWay is an add-on (called SmileWay Enhanced Benefits in Texas) offered on "many" Delta Dental PPO plans, mostly **employer-sponsored**, and it is "not included under all plans." It covers, at 100% per calendar/contract year: one periodontal scaling and root planing per quadrant, PLUS up to four procedures (any combination) from prophylaxis (D1110/D1120), periodontal maintenance (D4910), and scaling in presence of inflammation (D4346). Eligibility requires a diagnosed qualifying chronic condition (diabetes, heart disease, cancer, chronic kidney disease, stroke, etc.) and the member must opt in. It is subject to the plan's normal maximums and deductibles. [SmileWay Wellness Benefits page]

**Flag:** Marketing "up to 4 cleanings a year" is conditional (chronic condition + opt-in + plan must carry the benefit). The base individual PPO Premium frequency is **2 cleanings per calendar year** (per the CA Certificate). Do not present "4/yr" as a standard PPO Premium feature.

## 3. Waiting periods by state, and the prior-coverage waiver

- **National summary (DentalPlans.com):** Basic 6 months, Major 12 months, Orthodontics 12 months; Preventive none. [DentalPlans.com Premium]
- **Official DDINS product page:** Basic, Major, Implants/Dentures, and Ortho all carry "waiting period (varies by state)"; Preventive none. [DDINS PPO product page]
- **California (official Certificate):** Major Services for age 19 and older have a **6-month** waiting period. Basic preventive/diagnostic has none. **This 6-month major-service wait is WAIVED with proof of prior comparable coverage**, prorated one month per month of prior coverage. [Delta Dental of California Certificate of Coverage PDF]

**Flagged conflict:** The generic "12-month major waiting period" in third-party summaries is NOT accurate for California, where the filed Certificate sets a 6-month major-service wait (age 19+) that is fully waivable. The hub must say "waiting periods vary by state; in California the major-service wait is 6 months and is waived with proof of prior comparable coverage."

**The waiver rule (general):** Across Delta filings, the basic/major waiting period is waived (or prorated) when the enrollee provides proof of prior comparable dental coverage. This is the single most useful consumer fact and is state-contract-specific.

## 4. Missing-tooth clause: present or not, by state

- **California (official Certificate):** The CA individual policy does **not** apply a traditional "missing-tooth exclusion." Instead it conditions prosthetic coverage on timing: "The initial installation of a prosthodontic appliance is not a Benefit unless the prosthodontic appliance, bridge or denture is made necessary by natural, permanent teeth extraction occurring during a time the Enrollee was under a Delta Dental plan." In practice this is a functional missing-tooth limitation: a replacement for a tooth lost **before** coverage may not be a benefit. (Separately, implants themselves are excluded in this CA form. See Section 5.) [Delta Dental of California Certificate of Coverage PDF]
- **Other states / group plans:** Delta filings and dentist-facing summaries indicate a missing-tooth clause may or may not appear depending on the specific group or state contract. California has separately moved (via state law, AB 1048) to bar the missing-tooth clause in many **fully insured group** plans, which does not automatically govern individual policies the same way. **Estimate / unverified for individual product:** treatment of pre-coverage missing teeth on non-CA individual PPO Premium policies should be confirmed against each state's filed policy before being stated as fact. [Secondary: DayDream Dental; RealDentalCosts; general legal coverage]

**Flag:** Do not state a blanket "Delta has no missing-tooth clause." The CA individual Certificate contains a pre-coverage-extraction limitation that functions like one. Label any cross-state statement as state-dependent and estimate where not read from a filed policy.

## 5. Whitening / cosmetic: the headline conflict

**National marketing summary says whitening IS covered:**
- DDINS official PPO product page comparison table lists **Teeth whitening: 80%** for PPO Premium.
- DentalPlans.com Premium lists "Cosmetic Services: Teeth whitening and mouth/night guards 80%." [DDINS PPO product page; DentalPlans.com Premium]

**The filed contract says whitening is EXCLUDED:**
- The official **Delta Dental of California Certificate of Coverage** lists among exclusions for adults: "cosmetic surgery or procedures for purely cosmetic reasons, **including teeth whitening and veneers**." It also excludes Night Guards/Occlusal guards under exclusion (6). [Delta Dental of California Certificate of Coverage PDF]
- The official **Delta Dental of Oregon** summary excludes "Services for cosmetic reasons." [Delta Dental of Oregon 2025 benefit summary PDF]

**FLAGGED CONFLICT (high priority):** The national "80% whitening" marketing claim is contradicted by at least two state contracts (CA and OR) that exclude cosmetic services / whitening outright. The likely explanation is that whitening (and night guards) are added by rider in some states/forms and excluded in others, so the brand-level table reflects a best-case configuration, not the universal contract. The hub must NOT promise whitening coverage. State it as "available in some states; excluded as cosmetic in others (for example, the California and Oregon contracts exclude teeth whitening), check your state policy."

## 6. Implants: another state conflict

- **National summary:** Implants & dentures covered at **50%** under PPO Premium. [DDINS PPO product page; DentalPlans.com Premium]
- **California Certificate:** explicitly **excludes** "services for implants ... their removal or other associated procedures." [Delta Dental of California Certificate of Coverage PDF]
- **Oregon summary:** implants ARE covered (Class 3), once per lifetime per tooth space. [Delta Dental of Oregon 2025 benefit summary PDF]

**Flag:** Implant coverage is genuinely state-variable. The marketing "50% implants" is not true for the CA individual Certificate fetched. Present implants as "covered in some states (e.g., Oregon), excluded in others (e.g., the California contract); verify by state."

## 7. Alternate benefit / least-expensive-alternative-treatment (LEAT)

Both contracts contain an alternate-benefit clause:
- **California:** "If an Enrollee receives Optional Services, an alternate Benefit will be allowed, which means Delta Dental will base Benefits on the lower cost of the customary service or standard practice instead of on the higher cost of the Optional Service. The Enrollee will be responsible for the difference." Example given: porcelain/resin facings on posterior crowns, and downgrading to less costly metal. [Delta Dental of California Certificate of Coverage PDF]
- **Oregon:** "If a more expensive treatment than is functionally adequate is performed, Delta Dental Plan of Oregon will pay the applicable percentage of the maximum plan allowance for the least costly treatment." [Delta Dental of Oregon 2025 benefit summary PDF]

**Consumer impact:** Even where a service is "covered," reimbursement can be based on a cheaper alternative (e.g., composite downgraded to amalgam allowance, porcelain crown on a back tooth downgraded to metal). The member pays the difference. This belongs in the fine print on every cost example.

## 8. Annual maximum and deductible are CALENDAR YEAR

- **Annual maximum:** $2,000 per enrollee, **per calendar year** (PPO Premium); $1,000 for PPO Basic. Resets January 1. [DDINS PPO product page; DentalPlans.com Premium]
- **Deductible:** $50 per enrollee / $150 per family, **per calendar year**. Orthodontics has a separate $50 deductible and a $1,500 lifetime ortho maximum (DentalPlans.com figures). Preventive is exempt from the deductible. [DentalPlans.com Premium]
- The Oregon summary confirms preventive (Class 1) services do not apply to the calendar-year maximum and the deductible is waived for preventive. [Delta Dental of Oregon 2025 benefit summary PDF]

**Implication:** Because the max and deductible reset every January 1 (not on the enrollment anniversary), the "Smart Timing" / two-calendar-year sequencing point is valid: large treatment split across late December and early January can draw on two annual maximums. State plainly that this is a calendar-year, not policy-year, reset.

## 9. Common exclusions (representative, from CA and OR contracts)

Recurring across filed policies (CA Certificate and OR summary):
- Cosmetic procedures (whitening, veneers) where the contract excludes them.
- Experimental / investigational procedures and prescribed drugs.
- Services covered by workers' compensation or employers' liability, or available without cost from a government agency (Medicaid carve-out applies).
- Congenital/developmental malformations (cleft palate, jaw malformation, enamel hypoplasia, fluorosis, congenitally missing teeth), except certain newborn services.
- Treatment to stabilize teeth or restore structure lost to wear/erosion/abrasion; occlusal/night guards (CA); occlusal adjustments.
- Services started before the enrollee became eligible.
- Hospital/facility charges and extra fees for hospital treatment.
- Implants (in states where excluded, e.g., CA), maxillofacial prosthetics, indirect resin inlays/onlays.
- Oral hygiene instruction, plaque-control programs, dietary instruction, broken/missed appointments, x-ray duplication, infection-control/administrative charges.
- General anesthesia/IV sedation except with covered oral surgery or selected endo/perio surgery.
- Claims submitted more than 12 months after date of service (OR).

[Delta Dental of California Certificate of Coverage PDF; Delta Dental of Oregon 2025 benefit summary PDF]

## Implications for the hub fine-print / disclaimers

1. **Never state whitening or implants as covered without a state caveat.** The filed CA Certificate excludes both; the national table shows 80% whitening and 50% implants. Use: "Coverage for teeth whitening and implants varies by state. Some state contracts (for example California) exclude them. Check your state policy."
2. **Waiting periods must be labeled "varies by state."** Do not print "12-month major wait" as universal; in CA it is 6 months and waivable. Always pair with: "waived or prorated with proof of prior comparable coverage."
3. **Replacement intervals are state-specific.** Crowns/bridges/dentures: 5 years in CA, 7 years in OR. State intervals as "typically 5 to 7 years depending on your state policy," not a single number.
4. **Cleanings: base benefit is 2 per calendar year.** The "up to 4" figure is the SmileWay add-on, conditional on a diagnosed chronic condition, an opt-in, and the plan carrying the benefit. Do not present 4/yr as standard PPO Premium.
5. **Annual maximum and deductible reset on January 1 (calendar year).** This validates calendar-spanning treatment sequencing language. Say "calendar year," never "plan year."
6. **Alternate-benefit / LEAT clause applies even to covered services.** Add to every cost example: reimbursement may be based on the least costly clinically adequate alternative, and the member pays the difference (e.g., porcelain-to-metal downgrade on back teeth, composite-to-amalgam downgrade).
7. **Underwriter disclosure.** Because the policy is issued by a specific state company (Delta Dental of California, Delta Dental of Pennsylvania, etc.), the hub should reproduce the per-state underwriter line and a "binding document is your state policy" statement.
8. **Missing-tooth handling is state-dependent.** Avoid a blanket claim. The CA Certificate limits prosthetics for teeth extracted before coverage; treat non-CA individual policies as unverified (estimate) until each filed policy is read.
9. **Standard exclusions list** (experimental, cosmetic-where-excluded, workers' comp / government-covered, congenital malformations, oral hygiene instruction, missed appointments, hospital fees) should appear once in the hub fine print, with a note that the exact list is set by the state contract.
