# Delta Dental — In-Network Rates as a Perk
**One line:** Delta PPO dentists bill the contracted "maximum allowable charge," often 25 to 40 percent below their walk-in fee, and that lower rate helps even on services the plan does not pay for (whitening) and after the annual maximum is spent.

## Why this is a perk worth its own module
- PPO dentists agree to Delta's fee schedule. Members save up to ~40% off the dentist's regular fee on covered procedures (Delta sources).
- The discount persists when coverage runs out: after you hit the $2,000 calendar-year maximum, or when a cleaning falls outside the frequency limit, you still pay the network rate, not the full retail price.
- For NON-covered cosmetic work (whitening), the network discount is not guaranteed in every state, but a contracted dentist's rate is typically still below cash. Delta's separate "Patient Direct" discount product explicitly gives ~20% off everything including whitening; the PPO contracted fee often does too. Always confirm the office's contracted fee. Do not present whitening as a covered benefit.

## Framing rules
- Network rates "vary by ZIP and dentist"; cash figures are national estimates.
- On covered services the plan pays its coinsurance share ON TOP of the lower network rate.
- Whitening is cosmetic and usually not covered; the network rate still typically beats cash but is not guaranteed in every state.
