Ameritas PrimeStar Care Complete at a glance

Ameritas PrimeStar Care Complete is an individual PPO dental insurance plan from Ameritas Life Insurance Corp. costing about $60 per month with a $50 deductible and an annual maximum of $2,000 in year one, rising to $3,500 in year two and beyond.

It has no waiting periods on any category. In network: preventive care pays 100% from day one (and doesn't count against the annual maximum), basic care pays 80% in year one rising to 90% in year two, and major care — including crowns, root canals, dentures, oral surgery, and implants — pays 20% in year one rising to 50% in year two.

Implants are covered from day one at the major-work rates, with an implant-specific benefit of $1,000 in year one rising to $1,500 in year two and beyond.

It does not cover orthodontics or whitening. Out-of-network rates are lower (basic 60%/70%, major 10%/30%).

It is best for people who need treatment to start immediately and plan to keep the plan: every coverage number improves in year two. Verified June 12, 2026 against the Ameritas Summary of Dental Benefits and Coverage.

How long are the waiting periods on Ameritas PrimeStar Care Complete?

There are no waiting periods on this plan — none, on anything. The carrier's own benefits disclosure says it in one sentence: "There is no waiting period." Preventive, basic, major, even implants are eligible from day one. The part the "no waiting period" marketing whispers: year-one major work pays 20%, rising to 50% in year two.

That's not a trick; it's a different shape of the same actuarial protection a waiting period provides — and PrimeStar pairs it with a reward for staying: basic care starts at a strong 80% and rises to 90%; the annual maximum jumps from $2,000 to $3,500; the implant benefit grows from $1,000 to $1,500. If you need a crown now, 20% of something beats the 0% that Aetna, Guardian, or Mutual of Omaha pay during their 12-month major waits. And uniquely here, preventive care doesn't count against your annual maximum — the whole cap stays available for actual treatment.

Does Ameritas PrimeStar Complete cover implants?

Yes — from day one, with no waiting period. Implants pay at the major-work rates (20% year one, 50% year two+) under an implant-specific benefit of $1,000 in year one, rising to $1,500 in year two and beyond.

This is the only plan on the CoverCapy shelf with zero implant waiting period. The honest math on a $4,500 single implant: in year one the plan pays 20% — about $890, inside the $1,000 implant cap. In year two, 50% would suggest $2,225, but the $1,500 implant maximum is the ceiling. So the smart sequencing is: handle the extraction and any surgical prep in year one (covered at major rates, day one), and place the implant in year two on the better coinsurance and the bigger benefit.

For a one-and-done single-year payout, Mutual of Omaha reimburses more (~$2,225, no implant-specific cap) if you can wait 12 months; Humana Extend 5000 pays up to $2,000/year after just 6 months. Ameritas wins when the work needs to start now. Estimate your implant cost to see which ceiling your case needs.

Does Ameritas PrimeStar Complete cover crowns?

Yes — from day one, at 20% in year one and 50% from year two onward. No other plan on the shelf pays anything on a crown in the first months of a policy.

The worked math on a $1,400 crown in network: in year one, plan pays roughly $270 after deductible (20%); in year two, roughly $675 (50%) — and the year-two $3,500 maximum means a crown barely dents the budget. If the crown can safely wait until your second benefit year, waiting more than doubles your payout. If it can't, $270 still beats the $0 every 12-month-wait plan pays. Run your own crown estimate and have your dentist confirm urgency — that answer decides the timing, and the timing decides the money.

Does Ameritas PrimeStar Complete cover root canals?

Yes — day one, on the major schedule: 20% in year one, 50% from year two.

A $1,300 molar root canal in year one returns about $250; in year two about $625. Worth knowing: root canals rarely schedule themselves politely. If endodontic risk is why you're buying insurance, day-one eligibility — even at 20% — is the feature that matters, and this is the only plan on the shelf that has it. Meanwhile your fillings and simple extractions pay a strong 80% immediately.

Does Ameritas PrimeStar Complete cover braces or Invisalign?

No. Orthodontics are not covered on this tier, and neither is whitening.

For dependent orthodontics, Guardian Premier 2.0 is the only option on the shelf (50%, under 19, 12-month wait).

Can I use any dentist? In-network vs. out-of-network

PPO rules apply: any dentist is allowed, in-network Ameritas dentists carry negotiated fees. The fee difference compounds with this plan's percentage structure — 20% of a $950 negotiated crown fee plus a smaller patient share beats 20% of a $1,400 retail fee on both sides of the ledger.

In-network savings on Ameritas networks commonly run 25–50% below retail. Since the whole logic of this plan is multi-year sequencing, pick a dentist you'll keep for two benefit years: find a PPO dentist and verify they're in the Ameritas network before enrolling.

Frequently asked questions

Does Ameritas PrimeStar really have no waiting periods?

Yes — the carrier's benefits disclosure states it plainly: there is no waiting period on any category, including major work and implants. The trade-off is coinsurance: major work pays 20% in year one, rising to 50% from year two.

How does the plan grow in year two?

Four numbers improve at once: basic care rises from 80% to 90%, major work from 20% to 50%, the annual maximum from $2,000 to $3,500, and the implant benefit from $1,000 to $1,500. It's built to reward keeping the plan.

Why does my 'no waiting period' plan only pay 20% on major work?

Year-one major coinsurance is how this plan protects itself instead of making you wait. 20% now versus 0% during a competitor's 12-month wait is the real comparison — and in year two it rises to 50%.

Does it cover implants?

Yes, from day one at the major-work rates (20% year one, 50% year two+), under an implant-specific benefit of $1,000 in year one and $1,500 from year two. For a big implant year, compare Mutual of Omaha (no implant-specific cap) and Humana ($2,000/year) — they pay more per implant but make you wait 6–12 months.

Do cleanings count against the annual maximum?

No — preventive and diagnostic services are excluded from the cap on this plan, which preserves the full $2,000 (year one) or $3,500 (year two+) for actual treatment. That exclusion is rare and quietly valuable.

Who should pick Ameritas over Humana or Mutual of Omaha?

Someone who needs treatment to start now — filling, extraction, crown, root canal, even implant prep — rather than after a 6-or-12-month wait, and who plans to keep the plan into the stronger year-two schedule. If your case is one big implant and you can wait, the high-cap plans pay out more.
