Ameritas PrimeStar Care Complete at a glance

Ameritas PrimeStar Care Complete is an individual PPO dental insurance plan from Ameritas Life Insurance Corp. costing about $60 per month with a $50 deductible and an annual maximum of $2,000 in year one, rising to $2,500 in year two and beyond.

It has no waiting periods: preventive care pays 100% day one (with diagnostic services excluded from the annual cap), basic care pays 60% day one, and major care pays 20% in year one rising to 50% in year two.

Phase 1 implant services — extraction and bone graft — are eligible on day one; full implant placement is covered after a 12-month wait.

It does not cover orthodontics or whitening.

It is best for phased treatment plans: extract and graft now, place the implant or crown on year-two coinsurance and maximums. Verified June 12, 2026.

How long are the waiting periods on Ameritas PrimeStar Care Complete?

There are no waiting periods on this plan. Every category — preventive, basic, major, even Phase 1 implant surgery — is eligible from day one. This is the claim that "no waiting period" marketers (including plans Ameritas itself underwrites for other brands) lead with, so here is the part they whisper: year-one major work pays 20%, not 50%. The full 50% arrives in year two.

That's not a trick; it's a different shape of the same actuarial protection a waiting period provides. The question is which shape fits you. If you need a crown now, 20% of something is more than the 0% that Aetna, Guardian, or Mutual of Omaha pay during their 12-month major waits. If you can wait a year anyway, those plans' 50% may beat Ameritas' year-two rates only if their caps fit your case. Also unique here: diagnostic and preventive services don't count against your annual maximum, so the whole cap stays available for actual treatment.

Does Ameritas PrimeStar Complete cover implants?

Partially day one, fully after 12 months — and the split is the strategy. Phase 1 implant services (extraction and bone graft) are eligible immediately; the implant placement itself carries a 12-month wait.

This is the only plan on the CoverCapy shelf that covers Phase 1 from day one, which enables a sequencing play nobody else can match: enroll, extract and graft this month, let the site heal (which oral surgeons want anyway — typically 3–6 months), and place the implant after month 12 on year-two coinsurance (50%) and the year-two $2,500 maximum. The biology and the benefits calendar actually cooperate here.

For a single implant where you can wait 12 months but want a bigger ceiling, compare Mutual of Omaha ($5,000 cap); for the fastest full-implant path, Humana Extend 5000 covers placement after just 6 months. Estimate your implant cost to see which ceiling your case needs.

Does Ameritas PrimeStar Complete cover crowns?

Yes — from day one, at 20% in year one and 50% from year two onward. No other plan on the shelf pays anything on a crown in the first months of a policy.

The worked math on a $1,400 crown: in year one, plan pays roughly $270 after deductible (20%); in year two, roughly $675 (50%). If the crown can safely wait until your second benefit year, waiting doubles your payout. If it can't, $270 still beats the $0 every 12-month-wait plan pays. Run your own crown estimate and have your dentist confirm urgency — that answer decides the timing, and the timing decides the money.

Does Ameritas PrimeStar Complete cover root canals?

Yes — day one, on the major schedule: 20% in year one, 50% from year two.

A $1,300 molar root canal in year one returns about $250; in year two about $625. Worth knowing: root canals rarely schedule themselves politely. If endodontic risk is why you're buying insurance, day-one eligibility — even at 20% — is the feature that matters, and this is the only plan on the shelf that has it.

Does Ameritas PrimeStar Complete cover braces or Invisalign?

No. Orthodontics are not covered on this tier, and neither is whitening.

For dependent orthodontics, Guardian Premier 2.0 is the only option on the shelf (50%, under 19, 12-month wait).

Can I use any dentist? In-network vs. out-of-network

PPO rules apply: any dentist is allowed, in-network Ameritas dentists carry negotiated fees. The fee difference compounds with this plan's percentage structure — 20% of a $950 negotiated crown fee plus a smaller patient share beats 20% of a $1,400 retail fee on both sides of the ledger.

In-network savings on Ameritas networks commonly run 25–50% below retail. Since the whole logic of this plan is multi-year sequencing, pick a dentist you'll keep for two benefit years: find a PPO dentist and verify they're in the Ameritas network before enrolling.

Frequently asked questions

Does Ameritas PrimeStar really have no waiting periods?

Yes — every coverage category is eligible from day one, including major work and Phase 1 implant surgery. The trade-off is coinsurance: major work pays 20% in year one and 50% from year two. Full implant placement is the one exception, carrying a 12-month wait.

What is the Phase 1 implant benefit?

Extraction and bone grafting — the surgical site preparation for an implant — are eligible on day one. The implant placement itself is covered after 12 months. Since graft sites need months of healing anyway, the benefit calendar and the biology line up.

Why does my 'no waiting period' plan only pay 20%?

Year-one major coinsurance is how this plan protects itself instead of making you wait. 20% now versus 0% during a competitor's 12-month wait is the real comparison — and in year two it rises to 50%.

Do cleanings count against the annual maximum?

No — diagnostic and preventive services are excluded from the cap on this plan, which preserves the full $2,000 (year one) or $2,500 (year two+) for actual treatment. That exclusion is rare and quietly valuable.

What's the annual maximum?

$2,000 in year one, $2,500 in year two and beyond, with a $50 deductible. Preventive and diagnostic work doesn't reduce it.

Who should pick Ameritas over Humana or Mutual of Omaha?

Someone who needs treatment to start now — extraction, graft, crown, root canal — rather than after a 6-or-12-month wait, and who can structure bigger procedures into year two. If your case is one big implant and you can wait, the high-cap plans may pay out more.
