What is Ameritas PrimeStar Complete?

Ameritas PrimeStar Complete is a PPO dental plan from Ameritas, a long-established benefits carrier, built around one defining idea: major dental coverage that starts from day one. Instead of forcing you to wait six to twelve months before majors are covered, it pays a share immediately, a structure aimed squarely at people who need treatment now.

Most individual dental plans are designed for people who plan ahead. You buy coverage while your teeth are healthy, ride out a waiting period, and the major benefits are there when you eventually need them. That logic falls apart the moment a tooth fractures or a root canal becomes unavoidable this month. PrimeStar Complete is Ameritas's answer to that gap.

Ameritas is not a fringe player, it's a mutual-structured carrier with a large dental network and decades in the benefits business. That matters because the no-long-wait positioning isn't a gimmick from an unknown name; it's a deliberate product design from a carrier dentists already recognize. The tradeoff, which we'll be honest about throughout, is that immediate access comes at the price of a lower first-year reimbursement on major work.

Day-one coverage: why it matters

The standout reason to choose PrimeStar Complete is simple: it pays about 20% on major work from day one, with no waiting period to sit through. If you already need a crown or root canal and a traditional plan would make you wait six to twelve months, day-one coverage is the whole point, partial help now beats full help later.

Here's the honest tradeoff. A conventional plan might cover majors at 50%, but only after you've held it long enough to clear the waiting period. PrimeStar Complete flips that: year-one major reimbursement is lower, about 20% in year one (verify current terms), but it applies from day one with no waiting period. That percentage often increases in later years if you keep the plan, rewarding people who stay enrolled.

So the math is a timing decision, not a quality decision. If your tooth needs work today, 20% of a $1,400 root canal paid now is real money in your pocket that a "better" plan with a waiting period gives you exactly zero of for the next year. If you can comfortably wait, the calculus changes, and we cover that honestly below.

What does it cover?

Like most PPO dental plans, PrimeStar Complete is built in three tiers: preventive care (cleanings, exams), basic services (fillings, simple extractions), and major services (crowns, root canals, more complex work). The distinguishing feature is that major services are covered from day one at a lower first-year percentage that may rise in later plan years.

In general terms, here's how the tiers tend to work, always confirm specifics for your state and plan version:

Preventive care

Routine cleanings, exams, and basic diagnostics typically sit at the top of the coverage ladder and are generally available without a long wait. This is the foundation of any dental plan and where day-to-day value shows up.

Basic services

Fillings and straightforward procedures usually fall in the middle tier. Coverage levels and any short waiting rules vary by plan, so check the schedule of benefits rather than assuming.

Major services

This is where PrimeStar Complete earns its name. Crowns, root canals, and other major work are designed to be covered from day one at a year-one rate around 20% (verify), with the percentage often improving in subsequent years. The exact deductible, coinsurance, and annual maximum that apply to majors vary, treat any number here as positioning, not a quote.

Verify before enrolling. Coverage percentages, deductibles, and the annual maximum that caps what the plan pays per year all vary by state and plan. Confirm the current schedule of benefits before you enroll.

Who is it best for?

PrimeStar Complete is best for one specific person: someone who needs major dental work now and cannot afford to wait out a six-to-twelve-month waiting period. If that's you, day-one coverage is worth more than a higher reimbursement rate you can't access yet. If you can comfortably wait, a different plan likely pays you more.

Best for you if…

  • You already need a root canal, crown, or other major work
  • You can't wait six to twelve months for coverage to kick in
  • You'd rather have partial help now than full help later
  • You want a recognized carrier with a real PPO network
  • You plan to keep the plan, so later-year increases reward you

Not ideal if…

  • You want high first-year major reimbursement (e.g. 50%)
  • Your teeth are healthy and you can comfortably wait it out
  • You're buying purely for preventive care, not majors
  • You expect a large procedure where year-one 20% feels thin
  • You'd prefer to bank a waiting period now for richer benefits later

If you fall in the right-hand column, that's not a knock on PrimeStar, it just means a plan with a waiting period and a higher major percentage may serve you better. The point of an honest review is to point you to the plan that fits your timeline, not to sell you this one.

Ameritas PrimeStar vs other no-wait plans

PrimeStar Complete competes in a small field of plans built to cut or skip the waiting period on major work. The closest comparisons are fast-activating PPOs like UHC Primary Dental and shorter-wait options like Humana Extend 5000. The right choice comes down to how immediate your need is versus how much first-year reimbursement you want.

A few honest reference points as you shop:

  • UHC Primary Dental, known for fast activation and a strong preventive base, a natural alternative if you want quick access with a different benefit structure. See the UHC Primary Dental review
  • Humana Extend 5000, positioned around shorter waiting periods and a higher annual maximum, worth a look if you anticipate larger overall spend and can tolerate some wait.
  • Ameritas PrimeStar Complete, the most aggressive on day-one major access, with the tradeoff of a lower year-one percentage.

Don't pick on brand name alone, pick on the waiting-period and coinsurance combination that matches your situation. Compare PPO dental plans side by side to see how these stack up on the dimensions that actually affect your bill.

Using it for a root canal

For a root canal you need now, PrimeStar Complete's day-one major coverage does real work: even at around 20% in year one, that's a meaningful share of a $700 to $1,600 procedure paid from day one, far better than the 0% a waiting-period plan pays you this year. The smart move is to let the plan cover its slice, then finance the rest.

Run the honest math. A molar root canal might land around $1,400. A traditional plan that covers majors at 50% but enforces a waiting period pays you nothing for the next several months, you're stuck at full cash today. PrimeStar Complete paying ~20% from day one knocks a real chunk off immediately, and you cover the remainder however suits you best.

Frequently asked questions

Does Ameritas PrimeStar have a waiting period?

PrimeStar Complete is positioned around day-one coverage, meaning it's designed to avoid the long six-to-twelve-month waiting period many plans apply to major services. The tradeoff is a lower first-year reimbursement on majors. Waiting-period rules can vary by state and plan, so verify the current terms before enrolling.

How much does PrimeStar cover for major work in year one?

It typically reimburses major services at around 20% in the first plan year, with that percentage often increasing in later years if you keep the plan. Exact coinsurance, deductible, and annual maximum vary by state and plan version, so confirm the current numbers before relying on them.

Is Ameritas PrimeStar a PPO?

Yes. PrimeStar Complete is a PPO-style dental plan. You generally pay less by staying in the Ameritas network and keep the flexibility to see out-of-network dentists, usually at a higher cost. Network details vary by region, so confirm your dentist's participation.

Can I use it right away for a root canal?

This is exactly what it's built for, it's designed to pay toward major services like root canals and crowns from day one rather than after a long wait. First-year reimbursement on majors is lower, around 20%, but partial coverage from day one still beats paying full cash. Verify your specific plan terms first.

Is Ameritas PrimeStar Complete worth it?

It's most worth it for people who need major dental work now and can't wait out a traditional waiting period. If you can wait six to twelve months, a plan with higher first-year major reimbursement may save you more. The right answer depends on your timeline and current needs, compare options before deciding.
