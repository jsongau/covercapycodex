In short

What is Guardian Premier 2.0?

Guardian Premier 2.0 is an individual PPO from The Guardian Life Insurance Company of America, sold on the DentalGuard Preferred network. Preventive is 100% and basic work pays 85% from day one with no waiting period. Major work pays 60% in-network (50% out-of-network) after a 12-month wait, implants the same up to a $1,250 lifetime maximum, and child orthodontics for dependents under 19 pays 60% in-network after a 12-month wait, capped at $750 per year and a $1,500 lifetime maximum. The annual maximum is a flat $3,000. Adult orthodontics are not covered.

Why is the 85% day-one basic rate a big deal?

Fillings pay 85% from day one with no waiting period. A new patient with two fillings and a deep cleaning gets most of that bill paid in the first week. If your first year is fillings and cleanings rather than crowns, the plan covers a large share right away. See the glossary for how coinsurance and waiting periods interact.

How does the annual maximum work?

The annual maximum is a flat $3,000. That is the most the plan pays toward covered work in a benefit year, after which you cover the rest until the next year resets. If you expect to cancel and re-enroll, note the 12-month re-enrollment lockout below.

Does it cover braces?

Yes, for dependents under age 19, at 60% in-network (50% out-of-network) after a 12-month wait, capped at $750 per year and a $1,500 lifetime orthodontic maximum on Premier 2.0. Guardian treats clear aligners like Invisalign on the same terms as braces. Adult orthodontics are not covered on any Guardian individual plan.

Best for you if…

  • You need fillings now. Basic pays 85% from day one with no waiting period, which is unmatched here.
  • You have a child who may need braces. Dependents under 19 get 60% orthodontics in-network, one of the few plans on this shelf with kids' braces coverage.
  • You value carrier strength. Guardian holds an A++ AM Best rating, the highest available.

Skip it if…

  • You need major work soon. Crowns and root canals wait 12 months before the plan pays.
  • You are an adult who needs braces. Orthodontics are for dependents under 19 only.
  • You may need to cancel and re-enroll. A 12-month lockout applies after cancellation.

Good to know

The fine print that changes the bill

Exact wording varies by state. Read your own plan's exclusions section to confirm.

Missing tooth clause

Teeth that were missing before your effective date are not covered for replacement during the first period of coverage. This shapes any implant or bridge plan.

12-month major wait

Crowns, root canals, oral surgery, implants and child orthodontics all wait 12 months. The wait can be waived with proof of 12 months of prior comparable coverage.

Re-enrollment lockout

After you cancel, a 12-month wait applies before you can re-enroll. Do not cancel unless you have a replacement plan in place.

Crown restriction on some tiers

On some plan tiers, crowns are only covered when the tooth cannot be restored with a filling. Review the fine print before planning a crown.

FAQ

Common questions

Does Guardian Premier 2.0 cover braces for adults?
No. Orthodontic coverage applies only to dependents under age 19, at 60% in-network (50% out-of-network) after a 12-month waiting period, capped at $750 per year and a $1,500 lifetime maximum on Premier 2.0. Adult orthodontics are not covered on any Guardian individual plan. Compare ortho plans
Is Invisalign covered?
For dependents under 19, yes. Guardian treats clear aligners as orthodontics, covered on the same 60% in-network rate and 12-month wait as braces, subject to the $750 annual and $1,500 lifetime maximum on Premier 2.0. Adult Invisalign is not covered.
What is the waiting period on Guardian Premier 2.0?
Preventive and basic care have no waiting period; basic pays 85% from day one. Major work, implants and child orthodontics wait 12 months, paid at 60% in-network (50% out-of-network). The major wait can be waived with proof of 12 consecutive months of prior comparable coverage.
Does it cover implants?
Yes, at 60% in-network (50% out-of-network) after a 12-month wait, subject to a $1,250 lifetime maximum and the missing tooth clause. If implants are your priority, the higher-cap implant plans on the shelf are Humana Extend 5000 and Mutual of Omaha Dental Preferred. Compare implant plans
Why is the 85% day-one basic rate a big deal?
Fillings pay 85% from day one with no waiting period. If your first year is fillings and deep cleanings rather than crowns, you get a large share of the bill covered right away, instead of waiting six or twelve months as some plans require.
How much does Guardian Premier 2.0 cost per month?
Premier 2.0 runs about $70 per month. The exact figure depends on your state, ZIP, and age, so confirm your area-specific price on Guardian's official site.
Guardian Premier 2.0 vs Humana Extend 5000?
Guardian pays 85% of basic from day one and is one of the few plans here that cover child orthodontics, with a flat $3,000 maximum, though major work waits 12 months. Humana Extend 5000 carries a $5,000 maximum, covers implants, and bundles vision, but charges a higher premium. The right pick depends on whether you need fast basic and ortho, or a bigger maximum with implants. See the Humana breakdown
