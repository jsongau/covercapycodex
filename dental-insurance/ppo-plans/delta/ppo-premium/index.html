Delta Dental PPO Premium is a higher-premium individual PPO plan that pays 100% for preventive care from day one, 80% for basic work and 50% for major work, implants and braces after waiting periods, up to a $2,000 annual maximum. Preventive (cleanings, exams, X-rays) is covered immediately; basic care unlocks after 6 months and major, implant and orthodontic work after 12 months. Pricing is illustrative and varies by ZIP — this is an independent review, not a guarantee of coverage.

Best for you if…

  • You want implants or braces covered — rare on individual plans.
  • You can plan major work 6–12 months out.
  • Your dentist is in the Delta Dental PPO network.

Skip it if…

  • You need major work now — the 12-month wait means full price meanwhile.
  • You only want cleanings — a cheaper plan likely fits.
  • You expect a big case — $2,000 fills fast (see estimator).

Does Delta PPO Premium cover implants?

Yes — implants are covered at 50% coinsurance after a 12-month waiting period, up to the shared $2,000 annual maximum. Watch for a missing-tooth clause, which can exclude teeth lost before the policy started. See our dental implants coverage guide for how plans compare.

Does it cover braces (orthodontics)?

Yes — both adult and child orthodontics are covered at 50% after a 12-month wait, which is uncommon on individual plans. Braces and aligners usually draw on a separate orthodontic lifetime maximum, so verify that amount for your state before enrolling.

What's the catch on crowns?

The main catch is Delta's alternate-benefit (LEAT) downgrade: a porcelain or zirconia crown on a back tooth may be reimbursed only at the cheaper metal-crown rate, and you pay the difference. Ask for a pre-treatment estimate so you know your share before the work begins, and check unfamiliar terms in our dental insurance glossary.

Missing-tooth clause

May not pay to replace a tooth lost before the policy started — key for implants and bridges.

Frequency limits

Cleanings, X-rays and crowns are capped per period; extra visits are out of pocket.

$2,000 maximum

Mid-range cap. One implant case can use it up; you pay full price until the year resets.

State variations

Premium, coinsurance and availability differ by state — confirm for your ZIP.

Crown downgrade (alternate benefit)

Delta's alternate-benefit / LEAT rule may pay a porcelain or zirconia crown at the lower metal-crown rate — or a crown at the filling rate. You pay the difference. Ask for a pre-treatment estimate.

FAQ

Common questions

Does it cover implants?
Implants show 50% after a 12-month wait, but a missing-tooth clause may exclude teeth lost before the policy began. Confirm both on your state's document. Compare implant plans →
How long until major work is covered?
Major, implants and ortho: 12 months. Basic: 6 months. Preventive: day one. Waits can sometimes be reduced with proof of prior continuous coverage — ask the carrier.
What does it cost per month?
Premiums vary by state, ZIP and effective date, so we don't publish one national price. The ~$75/mo used in the example is for math only — enter your ZIP for an area price.
Is this an endorsement?
No. This is an independent review — breakdown and analysis, not endorsement. No carrier pays for placement.
How much does Delta PPO Premium cost per month?
There is no single national price — premiums vary by state, ZIP and effective date. The ~$75/mo figure on this page is illustrative, used only for the worked example; enter your ZIP for an area-specific price. We don't guarantee any rate or coverage.
Which dentists accept Delta PPO Premium?
You pay the least with dentists in the Delta Dental PPO network. Acceptance is product-specific, so verify your dentist for this exact plan using Delta's directory and a phone call, or run a free network check. Verify your dentist's network →
Delta PPO Premium vs Ameritas PrimeStar — which is better?
It depends on your timeline. Delta PPO Premium is the better fit if you want adult and child orthodontics and can wait out the 6- and 12-month periods for basic and major work. Ameritas PrimeStar is better if you need work soon, since it has no waiting periods. Neither is universally "best" — this is an independent comparison, not a guarantee of coverage.
