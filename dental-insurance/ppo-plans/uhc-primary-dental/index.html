In short

What is UHC Primary Dental?

UHC Primary Dental is an individual dental plan from UnitedHealthcare, underwritten by Golden Rule Insurance Company, for adults ages 18 to 64. It is built for routine care: preventive visits are covered 100% from day one, and basic work like fillings is covered 50% from day one with no waiting period, rising to 65% and then 80% the longer you stay on the plan. The annual maximum is $1,000 per calendar year, the deductible is $50, and coverage can start as soon as the next business day. Major work, implants, orthodontics, and hearing are not covered, and vision can be added separately. If you need crowns or implants, UnitedHealthcare's higher tiers cover them.

How fast does coverage start?

As soon as the next business day. Online applications can take effect the day after Golden Rule Insurance Company receives them, and you can request any future start date. This is among the fastest activation in the individual market. Both preventive and basic care are live from your effective date with no waiting period. See the glossary for how effective dates and waiting periods differ.

What does Primary Dental actually cover?

Two things, both from day one: preventive care at 100% (cleanings, exams, routine X-rays) and basic care at 50% (fillings, simple extractions), with the basic share rising to 65% after year one and 80% after year two. The $50 deductible applies to basic work only. Major work, implants, and orthodontics are not covered on this tier.

Can I upgrade to a higher maximum and more coverage?

Yes. Primary Dental is UnitedHealthcare's entry tier, focused on preventive and basic care. UnitedHealthcare also offers higher individual dental tiers with larger annual maximums (up to about $3,000) that add major work such as crowns, root canals, and dentures, plus implant and orthodontic benefits and a vision bundle. If your needs grow, you can step up to one of those tiers; this page focuses on Primary Dental.

Who stands behind it

Backed by the largest US health insurer

Primary Dental comes from UnitedHealthcare, the insurance arm of UnitedHealth Group, the largest US health insurer and, by revenue, the largest health care company in the country (over $400 billion in annual revenue, serving roughly 50 million members). The plan itself is underwritten by Golden Rule Insurance Company, a UnitedHealthcare company you may know as UnitedHealthOne.

Best for you if…

  • You want low-cost routine coverage for cleanings, exams, and the occasional filling, at about $30 a month.
  • You want to start fast. Coverage can begin as soon as the next business day, with preventive and basic care live from day one.
  • You'll stay on the plan a while. Basic coverage climbs from 50% to 80% the longer you keep it.

Skip it if…

  • You need major work. Crowns, root canals, dentures, and implants are not covered on Primary Dental.
  • You want a high annual maximum. The cap is $1,000 a year; a higher UnitedHealthcare tier or another carrier may fit better.
  • You need orthodontics or hearing. Neither is part of this plan.

The speed angle

Coverage that can start as soon as the day after you apply

Most individual dental plans go into effect on the first of the following month. Primary Dental can move faster. The Golden Rule policy language sets the effective date for an online application as the later of the date you request or the day after Golden Rule Insurance Company receives the application. You pick your own start date, so coverage can begin as soon as the day after your application is received.

Apply online and choose your start date. The application takes a few minutes; you select the effective date you want.
Golden Rule receives and accepts the application. The policy sets your effective date as the later of your requested date or the day after receipt.
Preventive and basic care are live from your effective date. There is no waiting period on either, so cleanings, exams, and fillings are covered right away.

The sourced policy language is "the day after receipt," not a guaranteed next business day. Activation timing is application-process information and is not printed on the official Primary Dental benefit page. Treat it as "as soon as the day after your application is received," and confirm specifics with the carrier.

How moving up works

UnitedHealthcare individual dental enrolls year-round, with no open-enrollment window, so you are not locked into a once-a-year change. Each plan is a separate Golden Rule policy with its own application. Moving up generally means applying for the higher plan as a new policy rather than toggling an upgrade in place, so the new plan's waiting periods and coverage year-counts generally start fresh from its new effective date. UnitedHealthcare does not publish a formal upgrade process, so confirm the specifics with the carrier before you switch. We frame this as "generally" because it follows from how the policies are structured, not from a quoted carrier upgrade rule.

Good to know

The fine print that changes the bill

Exact wording varies by state. Read your own plan's exclusions section to confirm.

No major coverage

Crowns, root canals, bridges, dentures, and implants are not covered. This plan is for preventive and basic care; for major work, step up to a higher UnitedHealthcare tier or another carrier.

Modest $1,000 maximum

The plan pays up to $1,000 per calendar year toward covered work. Preventive does not count against it, but basic care shares that cap. It resets every January 1.

Basic ramps over time

Basic care starts at 50% and reaches 80% only after two years on the plan. If you switch carriers often, you may never reach the higher rate.

State and age variations

Premium and availability differ by state, and premiums are age-banded, so older enrollees pay more. Eligibility runs ages 18 to 64. Confirm for your ZIP.

Frequency limits

Like most dental plans, covered care has frequency caps. The plan covers two routine exams and cleanings per calendar year and one bitewing X-ray series per calendar year, with a full-mouth or panoramic X-ray once every 36 months.

Missing-tooth clause

UnitedHealthcare individual dental plans carry a missing-tooth clause: replacing a tooth that was already missing before your policy started is generally not covered. This matters mainly on the higher tiers that cover bridges, dentures, or implants; Primary Dental does not cover that work at all.

Not sold in New York

UnitedHealthcare individual dental plans, including Primary Dental, are not available in New York. Availability also varies by state for some tiers, so confirm the plan is offered where you live.

FAQ

Common questions

Does UnitedHealthcare Primary Dental cover implants?
No. Primary Dental does not cover implants. Among UnitedHealthcare's individual dental plans, implants are covered by only one tier, Premier Plus, which also covers orthodontics for children. If implants are your goal, Premier Plus is the UnitedHealthcare option to compare. Read the implant guide
Does UHC Primary Dental cover crowns or implants?
No. Primary Dental covers preventive and basic care only. Crowns, root canals, bridges, dentures, implants, and orthodontics are not covered. If you need major work, UnitedHealthcare offers higher individual tiers that cover it, with larger annual maximums. Read the implant guide
What does Primary Dental cover?
Two things, both from day one with no waiting period: preventive care at 100% (cleanings, exams, routine X-rays) and basic care at 50% (fillings, simple extractions). The basic share rises to 65% after one year and 80% after two. The annual maximum is $1,000 per calendar year and the deductible is $50, which applies to basic work only.
How fast does coverage start?
As soon as the next business day. Online applications can take effect the day after Golden Rule Insurance Company receives them, and you can request any future start date. Both preventive and basic care are live from your effective date with no waiting period.
How much does UHC Primary Dental cost per month?
About $30 a month for many applicants, though the exact premium depends on your state, ZIP, and age, and there is no single national price. Eligibility is for adults ages 18 to 64, and premiums are age-banded, so older enrollees pay more. The ~$30/mo used in our examples is illustrative.
Is there a waiting period?
No. Preventive and basic care are both covered from day one with no waiting period. Because major work is not covered at all on this tier, there is no major waiting period to count down.
Can I upgrade to a higher maximum and more coverage?
Yes. Primary Dental is the entry tier. UnitedHealthcare offers higher individual tiers: Primary Preferred adds major work, the Premier tiers raise the maximum to between $1,500 and $3,000, and Premier Plus is the only one that covers implants and child orthodontics. UnitedHealthcare individual dental enrolls year-round with no open-enrollment window, but each plan is a separate Golden Rule policy with its own application, so moving up generally means applying for the higher plan as a new policy and its waiting periods generally start fresh. UnitedHealthcare does not publish a formal upgrade process, so confirm specifics with the carrier.
Which dentists accept the UHC dental network?
Primary Dental uses the UnitedHealthcare Dental network, one of the largest individual networks. You pay the least in-network, and in-network visits are paid directly to the dentist.
