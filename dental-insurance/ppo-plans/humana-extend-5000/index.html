Humana Extend 5000 at a glance

Humana Extend 5000 is an individual PPO dental insurance plan from Humana Inc. costing about $100 per month with a $5,000 annual maximum and a $50 deductible.

Preventive care pays 100% from day one. Basic care pays 50% after a 3-month wait. Major care and dental implants pay 50% after a 6-month wait — the shortest implant waiting period on CoverCapy's shelf, and one that cannot be waived.

Implant benefits are capped at $2,000 per year and $4,000 lifetime, subject to clinical review, typically one implant per tooth per five years.

Critical caveat: the plan's missing tooth clause may exclude coverage for replacing a tooth lost before the policy's effective date — verify this before enrolling if you already have a gap.

It also includes a named $200 teeth-whitening allowance after 3 months. It is best for implant cases needing the fastest covered path. Verified June 12, 2026.

How long are the waiting periods on Humana Extend 5000?

The ladder: preventive day one → basic at 3 months → major and implants at 6 months. Six months is the shortest major-work wait on the shelf — half of what Aetna, Guardian, and Mutual of Omaha ask for — and per Humana's plan documents, the implant waiting period cannot be waived, regardless of prior coverage.

For an implant patient the timeline is realistic rather than fast: consultations, imaging, and any preliminary work fill the first months anyway. Enroll, do the workup during the wait, and the 6-month gate opens around the time a properly sequenced case reaches surgery. Only Ameritas moves faster on the surgical front end — its Phase 1 (extraction and graft) is day-one eligible, a sensible pairing to consider if the tooth hasn't come out yet.

Does Humana Extend 5000 cover implants?

Yes — 50% after a 6-month wait, capped at $2,000 per year and $4,000 lifetime, with clinical review and typically one implant per tooth per five years. It is the fastest covered implant path on CoverCapy's shelf.

The caps shape the strategy. A $4,500 single implant at 50% suggests $2,225 — but the $2,000 annual implant cap trims that to $2,000 in one benefit year. The two-year play: place the implant body in year one (~$2,000 reimbursed), the abutment and crown in year two (drawing fresh annual implant benefit), approaching the $4,000 lifetime pool. For a one-and-done single-year payout, Mutual of Omaha reimburses more in that single year; for speed and staged or multiple implants, Humana's structure wins.

The missing tooth clause comes first. If the tooth was already missing before your policy's effective date, the plan may exclude its replacement entirely. Before enrolling for an existing gap: ask Humana in writing whether the missing tooth clause applies, and request a predetermination before scheduling surgery. This one clause decides whether the rest of this page matters for your case.

Does Humana Extend 5000 cover crowns?

Yes — 50% after the 6-month major waiting period.

A $1,400 crown after the wait returns about $675, and at a $5,000 annual cap, crowns coexist easily with bigger work in the same year. The 6-month gate is the differentiator: every other 50%-crown plan on the shelf makes you wait 12 months. Only Ameritas pays anything sooner (20% from day one). Estimate your crown cost against your dentist's fee schedule.

Does Humana Extend 5000 cover root canals?

Yes — on the major schedule at 50% after 6 months (confirm classification in your state's plan documents; some Humana plans schedule endodontics under basic at 3 months).

A $1,300 molar root canal post-wait returns roughly $625. If the classification lands at basic, the wait is just 3 months — a question worth one written inquiry to Humana, since it moves the eligible date by a quarter.

Does Humana Extend 5000 cover braces or Invisalign?

No. Orthodontics are not covered — adult or pediatric. What it has instead is rarer: a named $200 teeth-whitening allowance available after 3 months.

For child orthodontics, Guardian Premier 2.0 is the shelf's only option. The whitening allowance, meanwhile, is nearly unique — most PPOs exclude whitening as cosmetic without exception.

Can I use any dentist? In-network vs. out-of-network

PPO rules, implant stakes: in-network negotiated fees move four-figure numbers. A $4,500 retail implant might carry a ~$3,500 in-network fee — your 50% share drops by ~$500 before reimbursement even enters it. Out of network, the allowable-fee schedule plus balance-billing can erase most of what the plan pays.

Implant cases also involve multiple providers — surgeon, restorative dentist, sometimes a lab-heavy crown. Verify network status for each. Find PPO dentists near you, confirm who places and who restores, and get the predetermination in writing before any anesthesia is scheduled.

Frequently asked questions

Does Humana Extend 5000 cover implants?

Yes — 50% after a 6-month waiting period that cannot be waived, capped at $2,000 per year and $4,000 lifetime, subject to clinical review, typically one implant per tooth per five years. It's the fastest covered implant path among CoverCapy's six plans.

What is the missing tooth clause?

A provision that can exclude coverage for replacing teeth lost before your policy's effective date. If you already have a gap and implants are the goal, ask Humana in writing whether the clause applies to you before enrolling, and get a predetermination before scheduling surgery.

How long are the waiting periods?

Preventive: none. Basic care: 3 months. Major care and implants: 6 months. The 6-month major wait is half the industry-standard 12 months and the shortest on CoverCapy's shelf.

How does the $2,000/year implant cap work with a $4,500 implant?

The plan's 50% would suggest $2,225, but the annual implant cap trims year-one reimbursement to $2,000. Staging the case across two benefit years — body in year one, abutment and crown in year two — accesses fresh annual benefit and approaches the $4,000 lifetime pool.

Does it really cover teeth whitening?

Yes — a named $200 whitening allowance after a 3-month wait. Nearly every other PPO excludes whitening as cosmetic, making this one of the rarest named benefits on the shelf.

Humana Extend 5000 vs. Extend 2500?

The 5000 doubles the annual maximum ($5,000 vs $2,500) and carries the implant benefit structure that makes the plan worth its premium. If implants or major work are the reason you're here, the 5000 is the relevant tier; the 2500 suits lighter expected years.
