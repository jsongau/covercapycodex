Delta Dental, common questions

Is Delta Dental good dental insurance?
For most people, yes. Delta Dental has one of the largest dentist networks in the U.S., and its PPO Premium individual plan covers more than most: 100% preventive, 80% basic, 50% major, implants and adult orthodontics, with a $2,000 annual maximum. The main trade-off is timing: basic work waits about 6 months, and major, implants and orthodontics wait about 12 months, waivable with proof of prior coverage (varies by state). If you need a crown or filling immediately, a plan with day-one basic coverage may suit you better first.
Is Delta Dental PPO worth the higher premium?
It is worth it if you want broad coverage and dentist choice: adult orthodontics, implants, a $2,000 maximum and a large PPO network. It is less worth it if you only need cleanings and the occasional filling, where a cheaper day-one plan covers you sooner.
What is the downside of Delta Dental PPO Premium?
The waiting periods. Preventive care is covered on day one, but basic services wait about 6 months and major, implant and orthodontic services wait about 12 months, waivable with proof of prior comparable coverage. Prior continuous coverage can shorten these. Always verify the exact waits for your state before enrolling.
