Inside Delta Dental · for employers

Offer dental benefits employees actually value

Delta is the most widely offered dental carrier in the country, which is why nearly half of the Fortune 1000 use it. Here is how employer plans work, what a rich $5,000-max plan with orthodontia looks like, and an estimator for the monthly cost per employee.

What makes an employer Delta plan feel premium?
Four levers: a high annual maximum, an ortho rider, the D and P Maximum Waiver, and SmileWay. A rich plan pushes the maximum toward $5,000, adds orthodontia at 50% to a separate lifetime cap, keeps preventive care off the annual maximum so the full amount stays for restorative work, and layers SmileWay extra cleanings for members with chronic conditions, all on the PPO Plus Premier network. How to offer it

Set up a Delta plan in 6 steps

1. Pick your laneSmall business is roughly 2 to 50 enrolled employees; midsize to large is 100+.
2. Engage a broker or Delta salesBrokers can pull instant quotes for 2 to 50 employee groups.
3. Submit an RFPUse Delta's RFP Assistant; enter the state where benefits decisions are made to route to the right local Delta company.
4. Choose family and richnessPPO, PPO Plus Premier, or DeltaCare USA; set the maximum, deductible, ortho rider, D and P waiver, and SmileWay.
5. Set cost sharingDecide the employer contribution; the common split is the employer paying 70 to 80 percent (estimate).
6. OnboardDedicated implementation and account teams; many small-business plans have no waiting periods for most services.

Common questions

How much does Delta Dental cost an employer per employee?
As an estimate, employer-sponsored dental PPO premiums average about $62.75 per employee per month, and a dental DHMO averages about $22.75. Employers commonly pay 70 to 80 percent of the premium. A rich PPO with a high annual maximum and an orthodontia rider can run higher. Delta Dental does not publish group rate cards, so the exact figure comes from a quote tied to your group size, location and census.
What does a rich Delta Dental employer plan look like?
A rich plan has a high annual maximum toward the top of the $1,500 to $5,000 range, an orthodontia rider (typically 50% up to a separate lifetime maximum), the D and P Maximum Waiver so preventive visits do not draw down the annual maximum, SmileWay Wellness Benefits for chronic conditions, and PPO Plus Premier for the widest network.
Does Delta Dental offer a $5,000 annual maximum plan?
Yes, plans toward a $5,000 annual maximum are available, but they sit well above the standard market, where about 65 percent of dental PPOs carry a maximum of $1,500 or more. A $5,000 maximum and an orthodontia rider raise the premium, so pricing requires a quote.
Which big companies offer Delta Dental?
Delta Dental reports serving more than 148,000 groups and that nearly half of US Fortune 1000 companies offer Delta Dental, and that 1 in 3 Americans with dental insurance are covered by Delta. Confirmed examples include University of California employees and federal employees through the FEDVIP program. We do not name individual employers without a primary source.
How does an employer set up a Delta Dental plan?
Pick your group-size lane (small business is roughly 2 to 50 employees; midsize to large is 100+), engage a broker or contact Delta sales, submit a request for proposal through Delta's RFP Assistant, choose the plan family and richness, set the employer contribution, and onboard with a dedicated implementation team.
