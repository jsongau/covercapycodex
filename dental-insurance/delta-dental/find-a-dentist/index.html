Why the network matters

PPO vs Premier vs DeltaCare USA: who you can actually see

Delta Dental is one brand running three different networks, each with its own rule set. A dentist can be in one and not another, so the product on your card decides who is in-network for you.

PPO contracted offices accept the lowest agreed fees, so you save the most. Premier is a larger network where offices accept a higher agreed fee, so reach is wider but your share can be higher, and Premier-only offices may balance bill you on a PPO plan. DeltaCare USA is the HMO product, which generally assigns you to one in-network office and works on fixed copays. Matching the right network is the whole game, so read the exact product on your card first. See how HMO, PPO and Premier compare.

How to verify a dentist really takes your Delta plan

Four steps to confirm an office is in-network on your specific product, not just whether they take Delta in general.

  2. Search by that exact networkUse the Delta Dental Find a Dentist tool or CoverCapy and search by the exact network on your card. An office can be in Premier but not PPO, so searching the wrong tier returns offices you cannot use at in-network rates.
  3. Call the office and confirm the productPhone the office and ask whether they are in-network on your specific product, naming PPO, Premier or DeltaCare USA. Accepting Delta in general does not mean in-network on your tier.
  4. Ask about balance billing for Premier vs PPOPPO contracted offices accept the lowest fee, so you owe the least. Premier-only offices accept a higher fee and can bill you the difference, which raises your share. Confirm this before treatment, since terms vary by state.

Common questions

How do I find a dentist that takes Delta Dental near me?
To find a dentist who takes Delta Dental near you, use the Delta Dental Find a Dentist tool and select your exact plan, PPO, Premier or DeltaCare USA, because an office can be in one network and not another. Then confirm the office is in-network on your specific tier before booking. Is my dentist Delta PPO or Premier?
A dentist can be in the Delta Dental PPO network, the Premier network, both, or neither. PPO contracted offices accept the lowest agreed fees and give you the best savings. Premier offices accept a higher agreed fee, so the network is larger but your share can be higher. The only reliable way to know is to give the office the exact product name from your card and ask whether they are in-network on that specific tier, not just whether they take Delta.
Can I see any dentist with Delta PPO?
With a Delta Dental PPO plan you can usually visit any licensed dentist, but you save the most at PPO in-network offices. Out-of-network and Premier-only offices can balance bill you for the difference between their charge and the plan allowance, so your cost is higher. DeltaCare USA, the HMO product, is different and generally requires you to use an assigned in-network dentist. Confirm the office is in-network on your tier before you book.

Always reconfirm with the office directly, since network participation and plan terms can change.
What if my dentist is not in my Delta network?
If your dentist is out-of-network or Premier-only on a PPO plan, you can often still be seen, but your out-of-pocket cost is usually higher and Premier-only offices may balance bill. You can ask the office to file the claim, switch to a nearby in-network office for the best savings, or compare what an individual Premium plan would cover. Coverage and reimbursement vary by state and certificate, so confirm before treatment.
