Inside Delta Dental · federal employees

Delta Dental for federal employees

Through FEDVIP, Delta offers two PPO plans, Standard and High. Both cover preventive care fully in network, and the High Plan carries an unlimited in-network annual maximum, a benefit almost no private plan matches.

Does Delta Dental cover federal employees?
Yes, through FEDVIP, the Federal Employees Dental and Vision Insurance Program. Delta offers two PPO plans, Standard and High. Both cover preventive care at 100% in network. The High Plan adds richer major coverage, adult orthodontics, and an unlimited in-network annual maximum. Eligible: federal employees and annuitants including USPS, military retirees and gray-area reservists, and their families. You enroll through BENEFEDS during Open Season. Active-duty families use the separate TRICARE Dental Program, which is run by United Concordia, not Delta.
The Delta Dental FEDVIP High Plan has an unlimited in-network annual maximum, a benefit almost no private individual plan offers. Source: Delta Dental FEDVIP plan details, verified July 2026.

Who qualifies

Who is eligible for Delta FEDVIP?

Federal employees and annuitants, including the U.S. Postal Service. Retired uniformed service members and active duty within 60 days of retirement. Retired National Guard and Reservists, including gray-area retirees not yet receiving retired pay. And the eligible family members of all those groups.

Military note: FEDVIP is for military retirees and reservists, not active-duty families. Active-duty family dental is the TRICARE Dental Program, administered by United Concordia, a separate program from Delta.
Enroll

How to enroll in Delta FEDVIP

You enroll through BENEFEDS.gov or 1-877-888-FEDS, not through an agency HR system. Choose Delta Dental, then Standard or High. Most people enroll during Open Season, November to early December, with coverage effective January 1. If you are newly eligible or have a qualifying life event, you have a 60-day window; a retiring service member can enroll from 31 days before to 60 days after their retirement date.

Common questions

What is the difference between the FEDVIP Standard and High plans?
Both cover preventive care at 100% in network. The High Plan adds higher coverage for major work, includes adult orthodontics, and has an unlimited in-network annual maximum. The Standard Plan caps non-orthodontic annual benefits at $1,500 in network and $1,000 out of network.
Can military retirees get Delta Dental?
Yes, through FEDVIP. Retired uniformed service members, gray-area reservists, and their families can enroll in a Delta Dental FEDVIP plan. Active-duty families use the separate TRICARE Dental Program, which is administered by United Concordia, not Delta.
How do I enroll in Delta FEDVIP?
Enroll on BENEFEDS.gov or call 1-877-888-FEDS, choose Delta Dental, and pick the Standard or High plan. Enroll during Open Season for a January 1 start, or within 60 days if you are newly eligible or have a qualifying life event.
