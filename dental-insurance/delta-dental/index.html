What is Delta Dental, in one read?
Delta Dental is an association of 39 independent companies, the largest dental benefits carrier in the country by members covered, with roughly 78 to 89 million members across all 50 states, Washington DC and Puerto Rico. Its PPO network lists 112,000+ dentists across 278,000+ locations, and the broader Premier network is larger still. The major member companies hold an AM Best A (Excellent) financial strength rating. Delta is not publicly rated by S and P. Figures are approximate and aggregated from separate company filings.

Common questions

Is Delta Dental the largest dental network?
Yes, by members covered. Delta Dental is an association of 39 independent companies that together cover roughly 78 to 89 million people, the most of any U.S. dental carrier. Its PPO network lists more than 112,000 dentists across over 278,000 locations, and the broader Premier network is larger still. Some sources rank MetLife higher on raw provider count, but Delta leads on members and on combined PPO plus Premier reach, with a member company in all 50 states, Washington DC and Puerto Rico.
What is the difference between Delta Dental Premium and Basic?
Both are individual PPO plans on the same network. Premium runs about $75 a month, carries a $2,000 calendar-year maximum, and adds major work, implants and adult or child orthodontics at 50 percent. Basic runs about $33 a month, caps at $1,000 a year, and covers preventive at 100 percent plus basic care at 50 percent only, with no major work, implants or orthodontics. Basic is the lowest monthly cost option and is structurally narrower, not just a cheaper Premium. Figures are illustrative and vary by state, so verify your certificate before enrolling.
Which Delta am I, and how do I check eligibility?
Delta Dental is a federation of 39 regional companies, so your plan is run by a member company tied to your state or your employer, for example Delta Dental of California or Delta Dental of Pennsylvania. Check the carrier name and member ID on your card, then sign in to that company's member portal or the national site to confirm your benefits, network tier and any waiting periods. Dentists verify eligibility through provider tools using the payer ID printed on the card. Portals and payer IDs change, so confirm against the current card.
Does Delta cover implants and adult braces?
On the individual Premium plan, yes, typically at 50 percent for both implants and adult or child orthodontics, with orthodontics subject to a $1,500 lifetime maximum and a $50 ortho deductible, after a waiting period of about 12 months. The Basic plan does not cover implants or orthodontics at all. Coverage varies by state, since some certificates exclude implants or whitening, so confirm your specific plan documents before assuming a benefit applies.
Is Delta Dental good insurance?
It depends on what you value. Delta is financially strong, with major member companies rated A Excellent by AM Best, and it has the widest dentist acceptance in the country. Independent review platforms are more mixed, with Trustpilot around 1.9 out of 5 and recurring complaints about service wait times and 6 to 12 month waiting periods on basic, major and orthodontic work. Money named Delta Best for Braces, not best overall. A fair summary is solid and stable on the things that matter, more uneven on customer service.
Does Medicare include Delta dental?
Original Medicare does not include routine dental. Some Medicare Advantage plans add dental benefits administered by Delta Dental, for example SCAN Health Plan uses Delta Dental for its dental network in California and Washington. These plans often pair a DeltaCare base benefit with a PPO buy-up. Availability and the administering company vary by plan and state, so check the specific Medicare Advantage plan documents to confirm Delta is the dental administrator.
