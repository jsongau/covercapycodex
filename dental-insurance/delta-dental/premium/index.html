Beyond the dental coverage

Member savings the coverage table never shows

Delta members get access to savings programs alongside the dental benefit. These are discount programs, not insurance, but they are real money most plans never mention.

SmileWay® wellness

If you have certain chronic conditions, such as diabetes, heart disease or a pregnancy, you can get up to 4 cleanings a year at 100% instead of the usual two.

LASIK savings (QualSight)

Up to about 35% off the national average LASIK price at 800+ locations, with a free consultation. A savings program, not an insured benefit.

Hearing care (Amplifon)

Around 66% average savings on hearing aids across 6,000+ providers, plus complimentary follow-up care. A savings program, not an insured benefit.

Teledentistry

Virtual checkups and advice from a dentist by video or photo, useful for a quick read on whether something needs an in-person visit.

LifePerks discounts

Member discounts on oral care, fitness, groceries and meal delivery, travel, pet insurance, child care and more.

Calendar-year reset

The $2,000 maximum runs on a calendar year and resets every January 1, and coverage can start as soon as about 9 days out.

Savings programs are access-to-discount programs provided through third parties (QualSight, Amplifon and others), are not insurance, and are separate from your dental coverage. Availability varies by state and plan; confirm with Delta Dental.

The underrated perk

You pay the network rate, even when the plan does not

In-network dentists agree to Delta's contracted fee, the maximum allowable charge, which is often 25 to 40 percent below their walk-in price. You keep that lower rate even after you have used up your $2,000 annual maximum, or when a visit falls just outside a frequency limit. It even helps on cosmetic work the plan does not cover, such as whitening, because a contracted dentist's rate is typically lower than the cash price.

Good to know

Delta Dental PPO Premium FAQs

Does Premium cover adult Invisalign?
Yes. Orthodontics for adults and dependent children is covered at 50%, up to a $1,500 lifetime maximum with a separate $50 ortho deductible, after a 12‑month wait that is waivable with proof of prior coverage. Invisalign is treated the same as braces. Implants are also covered at 50%.
What are the waiting periods?
None for preventive (day one). Basic care waits about 6 months; major work, implants and orthodontics wait about 12 months. All waits are waivable with proof of prior comparable dental coverage (pro‑rated if shorter). Terms vary by state.
Is there a missing‑tooth clause?
No. This Delta Dental Premium policy has no missing‑tooth clause, so a tooth lost before your coverage started can still be benefited for an implant or bridge (confirm with a free pre‑treatment estimate, and check your state).
What's the annual maximum and deductible?
$2,000 per enrollee, per calendar year, resetting every January 1; $50 individual / $150 family deductible, also per calendar year. The $2,000 cap is higher than the $1,000 to $1,500 typical of individual plans.
How fast can coverage start?
You choose from set 1st or 15th-of-month effective dates, and the earliest effective date is roughly 9 days out. Preventive care is covered from your effective date with no waiting period.
Does Delta Dental include LASIK or hearing benefits?
As savings programs, not insurance. Delta members get discounted LASIK through QualSight and discounted hearing aids and care through Amplifon, plus teledentistry and LifePerks member discounts. These are access-to-savings programs, separate from your dental coverage.
Does Delta lower the price on services it does not cover, like whitening?
In-network dentists bill Delta's contracted fee, often 25 to 40 percent below their walk-in price. That lower rate applies to covered care and still helps even after you reach your $2,000 annual maximum. For cosmetic work like whitening that the plan does not cover, a contracted dentist's network rate is usually still lower than cash (for example, in-office whitening near $288 per arch on a published fee schedule versus $400 to $700 cash), though it is not guaranteed in every state, so confirm the office's fee before booking.
Where is this plan available?
Individual Delta Dental PPO is offered in AL, CA, DE, FL, GA, LA, MD, MS, MT, NV, NY, PA, TX, UT, WV and DC. Benefits and waits vary by state.
I'm 65+ on Medicare in California, should I buy this?
Maybe not first. If you're on a SCAN Health Plan in CA or WA, you likely already have a Delta Dental benefit through Medicare Advantage. See the SCAN Delta benefit
