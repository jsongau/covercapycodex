Key takeaways for business owners

  • Group dental is configured per company, not bought off a shelf. Premiums are quoted by your team's census, ZIP and plan design, so there is no honest flat national price.
  • Group plans waive the waiting periods that punish individual buyers. Humana and Guardian waive all waits at five or more enrolled; Delta, Beam, Principal and Ameritas commonly have none.

  • You decide the contribution. Pay all, part, or none. Humana, Ameritas, Principal and Beam allow a zero employer contribution with employees paying through pre-tax payroll.

✶ The basics

How group dental actually works

Individual dental plans have fixed, published prices. Group dental does not. You choose the plan design from a carrier menu, the annual maximum, the coinsurance, whether to add orthodontia and implants, and the carrier quotes a rate based on your company's census, ages, ZIP code, industry and how many people enroll. You are not shopping a shelf product, you are configuring a benefit. CoverCapy makes that feel like a concierge choosing for you.

  1. Tell us about your team. How many employees, where they are, and how generous you want to be. That drives the design.

  3. You choose your contribution. Pay all of it, part of it, or run it voluntary so employees pay through pre-tax payroll. Several carriers allow a zero employer contribution.
  4. Your team enrolls and gets routed to a dentist. Coverage activates and CoverCapy sends each newly covered employee to a vetted in-network dentist ready to see them.

✶ The money

Who pays what

Three moving parts decide the cost, and the answers to the questions owners worry about most are built into how a PPO works.

Employer contribution. You decide your share. Most carriers want at least half of the employee-only premium, but Humana, Ameritas, Principal and Beam allow a zero contribution, so you can offer it fully voluntary. Paying more usually unlocks lower participation requirements and waived waiting periods.

Employee payroll deduction. Whatever you do not cover, the employee pays through pre-tax payroll, so the real cost to them is lower than the sticker premium.

Dependents. Adding a spouse or children moves the employee to a higher rate tier, and that cost is almost always the employee's. This is the honest answer to "won't people add all their kids for free": they can add their kids, but they pay the family-tier rate to do it, and each covered person still has their own deductible and their own annual maximum. The plan is tiered and capped, not a free-for-all.

The copay is built in. On a PPO there is no flat per-visit fee. Cleanings are free on purpose because they prevent expensive crowns, but basic work costs the patient about 20 percent and major work about 50 percent after a deductible. That rising cost share, zero, then about 20, then about 50 percent, is the natural brake against overuse.

✶ Braces

Orthodontia: braces for the kids, and sometimes adults

Orthodontia is almost always a separate rider with its own lifetime maximum, commonly $1,000 to $2,500 per person, paid at 50 percent, and it does not draw on the annual maximum. Two things vary by carrier. First, child versus adult: many designs cover children only, but Humana, MetLife, Ameritas, Aetna, Guardian, Cigna and Beam on its higher tiers cover adults too when you elect it. Second, group-size gates: a few carriers restrict ortho by team size, while Humana offers it at just two enrolled. "I included braces for your kids" is one of the most resonant lines in the whole benefit, so lead with it.
