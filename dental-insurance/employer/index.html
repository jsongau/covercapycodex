✶ For the owner

Why this is smart business, not a handout

Benefits are table stakes at the companies your recruits are comparing you to. Dental is offered to about 70 percent of workers at large firms but only 30 percent at small ones (BLS, March 2025), so offering it is a genuine differentiator with the exact people weighing your offer against a bigger company. It is also the rare benefit employees actually use, every six months, so they feel it rather than forget it. And because group dental is quoted per company, you see real numbers for your actual team before you decide anything.

✶ The lever

Why good people leave, and what actually keeps them

Turnover is the most expensive line item nobody puts on a budget. Replacing one person runs roughly 50 to 200 percent of their salary once you count recruiting, lost productivity, and the months a new hire takes to get up to speed (Gallup). Nationally, voluntary turnover costs US employers about a trillion dollars a year, and Work Institute finds that roughly 63 percent of those exits were preventable. This is not a talent problem you have to accept, it is a retention problem you can get ahead of.

Here is the opening: employees who believe their employer genuinely cares about their wellbeing are about 69 percent less likely to job-hunt (Gallup), yet fewer than one in four workers actually feel that care. A real benefit, one your team uses and values, is one of the clearest ways to close that gap, and most of your competitors have not bothered.

✶ The honest math

Dental costs less than you think, and sometimes nothing

Run the numbers for a ten-person firm. A single preventable departure at modest pay lands somewhere between $12,000 and $28,000 once you count hiring, ramp-up, and lost output. A dental plan, by contrast, runs roughly $1,200 to $3,000 a year for a small team, and some voluntary arrangements cost the business nothing at all. Put plainly: the plan only has to help you keep a fraction of one person, one time, to more than pay for itself. Everything after that is margin, and morale.

You decide the contribution. Pay all of it, part of it, or none. Several carriers, including Humana, Ameritas, Principal and Beam, allow a zero employer contribution, so employees pay through pre-tax payroll and the coverage costs the business nothing. Paying more usually unlocks lower participation requirements and waived waiting periods, and your contribution is tax deductible.
