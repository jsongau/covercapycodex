Plan design

  • Annual max: $1,000 / $1,500 / $2,000 + Maximum Carry-Over (never expires)
  • Takeover: Carry-In up to ~$1,000 of unused max from a prior carrier
  • Network: Dental Complete ~143,000 dentists (or the smaller, cheaper Dental Prime)
  • Orthodontia: standard child-only to age 18, 50%, $1,000–$1,500 lifetime

Why it pays off

The value for your team

Best for: The owner in an Anthem state who wants a recognizable Blue brand at a lower premium and does not need a high ceiling.

Strongest angle: Anthem pitches carryover plus network boost as "$2,500-max-equivalent at up to 15% lower premium," and does not require bundling Anthem medical.

For a 10-person firm, voluntary turnover quietly costs roughly $12,000 to $28,000 a year (employees × ~13% turnover × salary × ~25% replacement cost). This plan runs about $1,200 a year for the team, so it only has to nudge retention slightly to pay for itself. Employees with dental coverage are about 17% more likely to plan to stay (MetLife survey data; association, not a guaranteed causal effect).

Honest caveat. A genuine weakness: preventive services count against the annual maximum on Anthem designs (many competitors exclude them). Maxes top at $2,000; no $5,000. Available in ~14 states only. Premiums quoted per company.
