# Benefit Maxing — Mega Nav Panel Strategy

## The problem
The four panels each carry one clear verb except this one.
- Insurance = buy
- Find a Dentist = find
- Guides = learn
- Benefit Maxing = ??? (people read "maxing" as hype or jargon)

It needs a verb of its own. The honest one is **plan** (or **time**). The promise: help people use the dental benefits they already pay for, before those benefits reset and disappear. That is money-oriented and true. PPO plans run on a yearly limit that does not roll over, so unused benefits are simply lost. We help people not lose them.

## The promise (honest framing)
Do NOT say "save $X" or "guaranteed savings." Say what is actually true:
- Your plan has a yearly limit that resets and does not carry over.
- We help you plan care so you actually use what you are paying for.
- We help you space treatment across two plan years when that fits your dentist's plan.

## Eyebrow options
- USE YOUR PLAN
- BEFORE IT RESETS
- MAKE YOUR PLAN WORK

## Headline options
- Use the benefits you already pay for
- Don't let your plan year reset on you
- Plan your care, use your whole benefit

## Supporting line options
- Your plan resets every year and unused benefits don't carry over. We help you time care so you use what you've paid for.
- A short read on annual limits, plan-year timing, and how to make a dentist visit count.

## Framing the two ideas (no dollar claims)
- **Time treatment across plan years:** "If you need more than one visit, your dentist can sometimes start now and finish after your plan resets, so each year's limit covers part of the work. Ask your office what fits." Never promise it works for everyone.
- **Use your full annual maximum:** "Most plans cap what they pay each year. Anything unused is gone when the year ends. A quick check tells you what's left."

## Lead / featured item
Lead with the **Smart Timing Protocol**, not emergencies. It's the everyday, calm, money-positive case. Emergencies are a secondary link.

## Patient-first tone
Calm, plain, no countdowns, no pressure. We inform, the dentist decides.
