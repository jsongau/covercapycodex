Quick answer

Can you finance a root canal monthly?

Yes, you can finance a root canal monthly. Realistic routes are in-office payment plans, health-specific cards like CareCredit or Sunbit, and true 0% APR plans for those who qualify. Apply your dental insurance first so you finance only the remaining balance. Pick a true 0% APR plan over a deferred-interest offer, which charges back-interest if unpaid in time.

The short answer

Can you finance a root canal?

Yes, and you rarely need to pay it all upfront. A root canal can be split into monthly payments through 0% APR healthcare cards (CareCredit, Sunbit), no-credit-check plans, interest-free in-house office plans, dental savings plans, an FSA/HSA, or a personal loan. The cheapest result comes from stacking a PPO plan on top, so you finance a smaller balance.

Monthly math

What you'll actually pay per month

On a typical $1,200 molar root canal, a PPO plan covering 70% leaves a ~$360 balance, about $30 per month over 12 months at 0% APR. Without coverage, the full $1,200 over 12 months at 0% is roughly $100 per month. Exact numbers depend on your plan, tooth, and provider.

Ranked options

Your financing options, ranked

From cheapest to most expensive way to cover the bill: a PPO plan that pays 50% to 80%, a 0% APR healthcare card, an interest-free in-house plan, a dental savings plan, FSA/HSA pre-tax dollars, and finally a personal loan for larger cases.

1. PPO plan that pays toward it

  • Usually the cheapest route overall
  • Covers 50% to 80% after deductible and waiting period
  • Often pays back its own premium on one procedure
  • Compare PPO plans

2. CareCredit / Sunbit (0% APR)

  • Promotional 0% for 6 to 24 months at participating offices
  • Sunbit approves broad credit profiles, sometimes no money down
  • Clear the balance before the promo ends to avoid deferred interest

3. In-house office payment plan

  • Pay the office directly in installments
  • Frequently interest-free and the most flexible
  • Underused because front desks rarely volunteer it, just ask

4. No-credit-check plans

  • Sunbit and several in-house programs approve without a hard pull
  • Ideal if credit is a barrier
  • Terms and approval vary by provider

5. Dental savings plan

  • Roughly $100 to $200 per year membership
  • Cuts prices 10% to 60% with no waiting period
  • Useful when you cannot wait out an insurance waiting period

6. FSA / HSA and 7. Personal loan

  • Root canals are FSA/HSA eligible, effectively 20% to 35% off
  • Pre-tax dollars depend on your tax bracket
  • A fixed-rate loan suits root canal + crown or multiple teeth
  • A loan can beat a deferred-interest balance you cannot clear in time
