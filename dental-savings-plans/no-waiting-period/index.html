Dental savings plans have no waiting period and activate within about one to four days, so you can use the discount on crowns, root canals, and implants immediately. Questions

No waiting period FAQ

Do dental savings plans have a waiting period?+
No. Dental savings plans have no waiting period. They activate within roughly one to four days of enrolling, and you can use the discount on major work like crowns, root canals, and implants immediately. This is one of their biggest advantages over insurance.
Why does dental insurance have a waiting period?+
Insurers use waiting periods, often 6 to 12 months for major work, to discourage people from buying a policy only when they already need expensive treatment. A dental savings plan is not insurance, so it has no waiting period, but it also never pays a share, you pay the discounted fee yourself.
Can I get coverage that works immediately for a crown?+
A dental savings plan is the most common way to save on a crown right away, with no waiting period and no annual maximum. Some no-waiting-period insurance plans also exist, usually at a higher premium. Confirm your dentist participates before enrolling in either.
How fast does a savings plan activate?+
Most activate within about one to four days, and some are usable the same day. You then show your membership and pay the discounted fee at the visit.

One thing worth checking first

A savings plan discount comes out of the dentist's pocket

A dental savings plan lowers your price because the dentist agrees to a smaller fee. No insurance carrier pays them the difference. That discount is the office doing you a favor at its own expense, which is part of why some premium and cosmetic dentists do not take these plans.

Individual dental insurance works differently. The carrier shares the cost of care, so you pay a smaller portion and the dentist is still paid a reimbursement on top of what you owe. When you can plan ahead and time bigger treatment to a year where your coverage is high and any waiting period has cleared, that shared cost often means you pay less and the office is paid more fairly for the same work.

A savings plan still has its place. It is ready right away with no waiting period, and it has no annual maximum, which helps when you need care now or your treatment plan runs past what a policy would cap. If your timing is flexible, it is worth seeing what an individual PPO plan would cover before you decide.
