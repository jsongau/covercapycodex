Can you use a dental savings plan with insurance?

Short answer: yes. A dental savings plan is not insurance, so it can run alongside a PPO. The two are not billed together, but you can choose whichever gives the better price on a given procedure, which is especially useful once your insurance runs out for the year.

The honest verdict

You cannot combine a savings plan and insurance on the same claim, but you can hold both and use whichever helps more. The clearest win is after you hit your annual maximum: insurance stops paying, and the savings plan keeps discounting with no ceiling. A savings plan also covers gaps insurance leaves, like cosmetic work, and bridges a new plan's waiting period. If your insurance covers everything you need within the cap, you may not need both.

When stacking pays off

Three situations where both help

You maxed out your plan

A big year of dental work exhausts the annual maximum. The savings plan discounts everything after that.

You want cosmetic work

Insurance excludes veneers and whitening entirely. The savings plan discounts them while your PPO handles the rest.

Your new plan is still waiting

A new PPO will not pay for major work yet. The savings plan bridges that waiting period.

When you may not need both

Keep it simple if you can

Light, covered needs

If your insurance covers your cleanings and the occasional filling within the annual maximum, a savings plan may add little. There is no need to pay for both.

Heavy or excluded needs

If you expect major work, cosmetic care, or a year that will exceed the cap, holding a savings plan alongside insurance can save real money.

Using a savings plan with insurance FAQ

Can you use a dental savings plan with insurance?+
Yes. A savings plan is not insurance, so you can hold both. You cannot apply them to the same procedure at once, but you can use whichever gives the lower price. The clearest benefit is after you hit your insurance annual maximum, when the savings plan keeps discounting with no ceiling.
Can I combine a savings plan and insurance on the same procedure?+
No. They are not billed together. For each treatment you choose one or the other, whichever gives the better price. Many people use insurance for covered work and the savings plan for the rest.
When should I stack a savings plan on top of insurance?+
When you expect to exceed your annual maximum, when you want cosmetic work insurance excludes, or when a new plan's waiting period leaves you exposed on major work. In those cases the savings plan fills the gap.
Is it worth having both a savings plan and insurance?+
It depends on your needs. For light, well-covered needs within the annual maximum, probably not. For heavy, cosmetic, or over-the-cap needs, holding both can save real money. Confirm your dentist accepts both before relying on the combination.

One thing worth checking first

A savings plan discount comes out of the dentist's pocket

A dental savings plan lowers your price because the dentist agrees to a smaller fee. No insurance carrier pays them the difference. That discount is the office doing you a favor at its own expense, which is part of why some premium and cosmetic dentists do not take these plans.

Individual dental insurance works differently. The carrier shares the cost of care, so you pay a smaller portion and the dentist is still paid a reimbursement on top of what you owe. When you can plan ahead and time bigger treatment to a year where your coverage is high and any waiting period has cleared, that shared cost often means you pay less and the office is paid more fairly for the same work.

A savings plan still has its place. It is ready right away with no waiting period, and it has no annual maximum, which helps when you need care now or your treatment plan runs past what a policy would cap. If your timing is flexible, it is worth seeing what an individual PPO plan would cover before you decide.
