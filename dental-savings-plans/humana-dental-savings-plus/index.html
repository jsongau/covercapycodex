The honest verdict

Humana Dental Savings Plus fits an uninsured person who wants immediate savings on both routine and major work with no annual cap, and who values the bundled prescription, vision, and hearing discounts. If you mainly need preventive cleanings, a PPO that covers them at 100 percent, including Humana's own insurance plans, will usually serve you better. Confirm your dentist participates first.

What it is

A discount plan with extras

1

You pay the discounted fee

You pay a membership fee, then pay the dentist a discounted price at the visit. No claims, no reimbursement, no annual maximum, no waiting periods, no deductible.

2

Bundled discounts

It adds discount programs for prescriptions, with 10 to 85 percent off at many pharmacies, plus vision and hearing. These are discount programs, not insurance.

3

Activates fast

It activates in about a day, with no exclusion for problems you already have, and works on major procedures immediately.

4

Not the same as Humana insurance

It is separate from Humana's insurance plans, Extend, Preventive Value, Complete, and Loyalty Plus, which actually pay claims. Do not confuse the discount plan with those.

Pricing

What it costs

Retail through DentalPlans.com

About 185 dollars a year for an individual and about 220 dollars a year for a family, plus a one time 20 dollar processing fee. A 30 day refund window applies, with state variations.

On Humana's own site

The price varies by state and age through a plan finder, so there is no single national number. Lower advertised figures appear on some third party sites and could not be verified, so confirm the current price at checkout.

Family member definition: cardholder, spouse, and dependents up to age 18, or up to 25 if a full time student.

vs a PPO

When it beats a PPO, and when it does not

Humana Dental Savings Plus wins when

  • You are uninsured and need care now, with no waiting period.
  • You face major work that would blow past a PPO annual maximum.
  • You want a low monthly cost plus bundled prescription, vision, and hearing discounts.
  • You have a pre-existing issue a PPO would delay.

A PPO wins when

  • You mainly need preventive care, which insurance often covers at 100 percent.
  • You want the insurer to actually pay a share of claims.
  • You need coverage that meets minimum creditable coverage rules.

Questions

Humana Dental Savings Plus FAQ

Is Humana Dental Savings Plus insurance?+
No. It is a dental discount plan, not insurance, and it does not meet minimum creditable coverage requirements. You pay a membership fee, then pay a discounted price directly to participating dentists. It is separate from Humana's insurance products like Humana Extend, Preventive Value, Complete, and Loyalty Plus.
Who runs Humana Dental Savings Plus?+
The dentist network and fee schedule are run on Careington's network, branded HMNA for Humana. Depending on the sales channel, the named discount plan organization can differ, for example New Benefits, Ltd. on DentalPlans.com. It carries the Humana name but runs on the Careington network.
How much does Humana Dental Savings Plus cost?+
At retail through DentalPlans.com it is about 185 dollars a year for an individual and about 220 dollars a year for a family, plus a one time 20 dollar processing fee. On Humana's own site the price varies by state and age. Confirm the current price before enrolling.
How much can I save?+
The plan advertises 20 to 40 percent off most services in most instances, with a flat 20 percent at specialists. There are no waiting periods, no deductibles, and no annual maximum, and it activates in about a day.
What extras come with it?+
It bundles discount programs for prescriptions, with savings of 10 to 85 percent at many pharmacies, plus vision and hearing discounts. These extras are discount programs, not insurance.

One thing worth checking first

A savings plan discount comes out of the dentist's pocket

A dental savings plan lowers your price because the dentist agrees to a smaller fee. No insurance carrier pays them the difference. That discount is the office doing you a favor at its own expense, which is part of why some premium and cosmetic dentists do not take these plans.

Individual dental insurance works differently. The carrier shares the cost of care, so you pay a smaller portion and the dentist is still paid a reimbursement on top of what you owe. When you can plan ahead and time bigger treatment to a year where your coverage is high and any waiting period has cleared, that shared cost often means you pay less and the office is paid more fairly for the same work.

A savings plan still has its place. It is ready right away with no waiting period, and it has no annual maximum, which helps when you need care now or your treatment plan runs past what a policy would cap. If your timing is flexible, it is worth seeing what an individual PPO plan would cover before you decide.
