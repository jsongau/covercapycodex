How dental discount cards work

A dental discount card, also called a dental savings plan, is simpler than insurance. There are no claims and no reimbursements. You pay a yearly fee, and in return you pay a lower, pre-negotiated price at participating dentists.

The honest verdict

Here is the whole model in one line: you pay an annual membership fee, show your membership at a participating dentist, and pay their discounted fee directly at the visit. There is no insurer paying a share, no claim to file, no deductible, no annual maximum, and no waiting period. It is a membership that unlocks a lower price, not coverage that pays your bill.

Step by step

The four steps

1

You join and pay a yearly fee

Membership commonly costs $100 to $200 a year for an individual. It activates within about one to four days.

2

You get a membership and a fee schedule

The plan gives you access to a network of dentists who have agreed to discounted, pre-negotiated fees.

3

You visit a participating dentist

You show your membership. The office charges the discounted fee schedule rather than its usual rate.

4

You pay the discounted fee directly

You pay the office at the visit. There is no claim to file and no reimbursement to wait for.

The key differences

How it differs from insurance

No claims

Nothing to file. You simply pay the lower price at the chair.

No annual cap or deductible

Use it as much as you want. There is no maximum and nothing to meet first.

No waiting period

Major work is discounted immediately, including problems you already have.

The tradeoff: a discount card never pays your bill, it only lowers it. To weigh that against insurance, see savings plan vs PPO.

Who runs them

The companies behind the cards

Networks and administrators

The dentist networks come from names like Aetna, Cigna, UnitedHealthcare, Humana, and Delta Dental. The memberships are often administered by discount plan organizations such as Careington or New Benefits, sometimes behind a carrier brand.

Why dentists take part

Offices are paid in full at the visit with no claims, and they give a smaller discount off their own fee than a PPO would demand. It also brings them new patients. The discount is the cost of filling the chair.

Questions

How discount cards work FAQ

How do dental discount cards work?+
You pay an annual membership fee, then pay a lower, pre-negotiated price directly to participating dentists at the visit. There are no claims, no reimbursements, no deductible, no annual maximum, and no waiting period. It is a membership that unlocks a discounted price, not insurance that pays your bill.
Do you pay the dentist directly with a discount card?+
Yes. You pay the discounted fee to the dental office at the time of service. The plan does not pay the dentist and does not reimburse you, which is why there is no paperwork.
How fast does a dental discount card start working?+
Most activate within about one to four days of enrolling, and some are usable the same day. You then show your membership and pay the discounted price.
Are dental discount cards the same as insurance?+
No. Insurance pays a share of covered procedures after a deductible, up to an annual maximum, and often makes you wait for major work. A discount card pays nothing, it only lowers the price, but it has no cap and no waiting period.
