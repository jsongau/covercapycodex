Dental coverage after a job loss

Losing a job usually means losing dental benefits too, often right when a delayed cleaning or a nagging tooth needs attention. A dental savings plan is a fast, low-cost way to stay covered while you sort out what is next.

The honest verdict

If you just lost dental benefits, a savings plan is often the quickest and cheapest bridge. It activates within days, has no waiting period, no annual cap, and typically costs $100 to $200 a year, usually far less than COBRA continuation of a dental plan. When you land new coverage, you can switch or stack. If you expect only routine cleanings and can get a subsidized plan soon, weigh a PPO too.

The gap is real

Dental is the first benefit to disappear

1

It ends with the job

Employer dental usually stops at the end of your last month. There is rarely a replacement waiting.

2

COBRA is pricey

Continuing an employer dental plan through COBRA means paying the full premium yourself, often more than a savings plan costs for a year.

3

New plans make you wait

A brand-new PPO can impose a 6 to 12 month wait on major work, exactly when you may need it.

Why a savings plan bridges well

Fast, cheap, and no waiting

Covered within days

Most savings plans activate in about one to four days, with no waiting period, so you can keep a cleaning appointment or handle an urgent problem without a gap.

Switch or stack later

A savings plan is not insurance, so when you start a new job with dental benefits, you can drop it or keep it alongside your new plan for savings once you hit the annual maximum.

Questions

Coverage after job loss FAQ

What is the fastest way to get dental coverage after losing my job?+
A dental savings plan is usually the fastest option. It activates within about one to four days, has no waiting period, and costs $100 to $200 a year. You pay a discounted fee at the dentist rather than a share being paid for you.
Is a dental savings plan cheaper than COBRA?+
Often, yes. COBRA continuation means paying the full dental premium yourself, which can exceed a savings plan's whole yearly cost. A savings plan is not insurance, though, so it discounts care rather than paying claims.
Can I use a savings plan while I look for a new job?+
Yes. It works immediately and can be canceled or kept later. Many people use it as a short bridge, then switch to a new employer plan or stack the two once the insurance annual maximum is reached.
Will a new PPO make me wait for major work?+
A brand-new PPO commonly imposes a 6 to 12 month waiting period on major procedures. A savings plan has no waiting period, which is why it works well as an immediate bridge.
