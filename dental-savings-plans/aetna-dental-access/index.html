The honest verdict

Aetna Dental Access is worth it when you need real dental work soon, especially major or cosmetic work a PPO would delay, cap, or exclude. It is usually not the best choice if you are healthy and only need cleanings, since a PPO that covers preventive care at 100 percent tends to win there. What it is

A membership, not a policy

1

You pay the dentist directly

At the visit you pay the full discounted price. The plan never reimburses you and never pays the office, which is why there are no claims and no paperwork.

2

No insurance rules

No deductible to meet first. No annual maximum that cuts you off. No waiting period before major work. No exclusion for problems you already have.

3

It discounts everything

Cleanings, fillings, crowns, root canals, implants, orthodontics, and cosmetic work like veneers and whitening are all discounted, including procedures insurance will not touch.

4

It is not insurance

It does not pay for anything and does not meet minimum creditable coverage. If your dentist does not participate, it does nothing there, which is why verifying your dentist matters.

Who actually runs it

Two layers, one network

A point most consumer guides skip. Knowing this is a trust signal, not fine print.

1
The network: Aetna Dental AccessThe directory of participating dentists and their discounted fees is administered by Aetna Life Insurance Company, part of CVS Health.
2
The membership: a separate companyThe plan you buy is run by a discount plan organization, either New Benefits, Ltd. through DentalPlans.com or Careington International through Careington and 1Dental.

Pricing

What it costs

Individual and family

At the two retailers we verified, an individual membership runs about 155 to 175 dollars a year and a family membership about 210 dollars a year, plus a one time processing fee of around 20 dollars at some sellers. Lower priced tiers are advertised through some Careington channels.

One major procedure pays for it

A single crown saves about 551 dollars and a molar root canal about 744 dollars, several times the annual membership. The membership math is strongest for anyone facing major work, and tightest for someone who only needs cleanings.

Prices vary by seller and promotion. Not sold in Vermont or Washington through some channels. Confirm the current price at checkout.

vs a PPO

When it beats a PPO, and when it does not

Aetna Dental Access wins when

  • You need major work now and cannot wait out a PPO waiting period.
  • You already used up your PPO annual maximum and still need care.
  • You want cosmetic work a PPO will not cover.
  • You are on Medicare or recently lost coverage and want savings immediately.

A PPO wins when

  • You are healthy and mostly need routine cleanings, which a PPO often covers at 100 percent.
  • You need a large covered procedure that fits under the annual maximum.
  • Your employer pays part of the premium.

Questions

Aetna Dental Access FAQ

Is Aetna Dental Access insurance?+
No. It is a dental savings plan, not insurance. You pay one annual membership fee, then pay a discounted price directly to participating dentists at the time of your visit. There are no claims, no deductibles, no annual maximum, and no waiting periods, and it does not meet minimum creditable coverage requirements.
How much does Aetna Dental Access cost?+
At major retailers, an individual membership runs roughly 155 to 175 dollars a year and a family membership about 210 dollars a year, plus a one time processing fee of around 20 dollars at some sellers. Lower priced tiers are advertised through some Careington channels. Prices vary by seller and promotion, so confirm the current price at checkout.
Who actually runs Aetna Dental Access?+
It has two layers. The dentist network and discounted fee schedule are administered by Aetna Life Insurance Company. The membership you buy is operated by a separate discount plan organization, either New Benefits, Ltd. through DentalPlans.com or Careington International through Careington and 1Dental. In short, Aetna provides the network and a separate company runs the membership.
Does it cover implants and cosmetic work?+
Yes. It discounts major and cosmetic procedures that insurance often excludes or caps, including implants, orthodontics, veneers, and whitening, with no waiting period and no annual maximum. The percentage saved is usually smaller on orthodontics than on routine care.
Do dentists accept Aetna Dental Access?+
The network is one of the largest discount dental networks in the country, so participating dentists are common in most metro areas. The discount only applies at participating offices, so confirm your specific dentist participates before you enroll. CoverCapy can help verify a dentist near you.

One thing worth checking first

A savings plan discount comes out of the dentist's pocket

A dental savings plan lowers your price because the dentist agrees to a smaller fee. No insurance carrier pays them the difference. That discount is the office doing you a favor at its own expense, which is part of why some premium and cosmetic dentists do not take these plans.

Individual dental insurance works differently. The carrier shares the cost of care, so you pay a smaller portion and the dentist is still paid a reimbursement on top of what you owe. When you can plan ahead and time bigger treatment to a year where your coverage is high and any waiting period has cleared, that shared cost often means you pay less and the office is paid more fairly for the same work.

A savings plan still has its place. It is ready right away with no waiting period, and it has no annual maximum, which helps when you need care now or your treatment plan runs past what a policy would cap. If your timing is flexible, it is worth seeing what an individual PPO plan would cover before you decide.
