Dental savings plans for the self-employed

When you work for yourself, there is no group dental plan behind you. A savings plan is a simple, low-cost way to keep dental care affordable without buying a full individual insurance policy.

The honest verdict

For freelancers, gig workers, and business owners, a savings plan is a practical, low-commitment option. It costs $100 to $200 a year, works immediately with no waiting period, has no annual cap, and requires no employer. If you expect a lot of covered routine care or want an insurer to share big costs, an individual PPO is worth comparing, and you can stack a savings plan on top later.

You are on your own

About 27 percent of US adults have no dental insurance, and the self-employed are heavily represented, because there is no group plan to join.

A standalone dental policy can run $240 to $600 a year with waiting periods and an annual cap. A savings plan is usually a fraction of that.

Why it fits

Low cost, no waiting, no cap

Predictable and cheap

One low yearly fee, then a known discounted price at the dentist. For a healthy freelancer who mainly wants cleanings and the occasional filling handled affordably, that predictability is the appeal.

Ready when work is not steady

Income can be uneven when you are self-employed. A savings plan keeps dental costs down without a large premium commitment, and it works the moment you need it.

Questions

Self-employed dental FAQ

What are the dental options for self-employed people?+
The main options are an individual dental insurance policy or a dental savings plan. Insurance pays a share but has premiums, waiting periods, and an annual cap. A savings plan is a lower-cost membership that discounts care immediately with no cap, and you can use both together.
Is a dental savings plan good for freelancers?+
It fits many freelancers well, because it is inexpensive, works immediately, requires no employer, and can be canceled or stacked as your situation changes. The tradeoff is that it discounts care rather than paying for it.
How much does a self-employed dental savings plan cost?+
Individual memberships commonly cost $100 to $200 a year, often far less than an individual insurance premium. Prices vary by plan and seller, so confirm the current price at checkout.
Can I switch to insurance later?+
Yes. Dental coverage has no medical underwriting, so you can add an individual PPO whenever it makes sense, and even keep the savings plan alongside it to keep discounting once you hit the annual maximum.

One thing worth checking first

A savings plan discount comes out of the dentist's pocket

A dental savings plan lowers your price because the dentist agrees to a smaller fee. No insurance carrier pays them the difference. That discount is the office doing you a favor at its own expense, which is part of why some premium and cosmetic dentists do not take these plans.

Individual dental insurance works differently. The carrier shares the cost of care, so you pay a smaller portion and the dentist is still paid a reimbursement on top of what you owe. When you can plan ahead and time bigger treatment to a year where your coverage is high and any waiting period has cleared, that shared cost often means you pay less and the office is paid more fairly for the same work.

A savings plan still has its place. It is ready right away with no waiting period, and it has no annual maximum, which helps when you need care now or your treatment plan runs past what a policy would cap. If your timing is flexible, it is worth seeing what an individual PPO plan would cover before you decide.
