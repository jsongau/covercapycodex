Dental savings plans for dentures

Dentures are a major, often urgent expense, and insurance tends to cap and delay them. A dental savings plan discounts full and partial dentures immediately, with no waiting period and no annual maximum.

The honest verdict

For dentures, a savings plan is a practical way to cut the cost right away. A full upper denture near $1,954 can drop to about $1,123 on one published schedule, applied immediately with no cap. A brand-new PPO usually makes you wait and then pays only a share up to the annual maximum, so a savings plan frequently wins in year one, especially for someone who needs the work soon.

Questions

Dentures and savings plans FAQ

Do dental savings plans cover dentures?+
Savings plans discount dentures rather than cover them. The discount applies immediately with no waiting period and no annual maximum. For example, a full upper denture near $1,954 can drop to about $1,123 on one published schedule.
Is a savings plan or insurance better for dentures?+
If you need dentures soon and have no active, no-wait coverage, a savings plan usually wins, because a new PPO makes you wait and then pays only a share up to the annual cap. If you already have active insurance with room under the maximum, the PPO can be better.
Does Medicare cover dentures?+
No. Original Medicare does not cover dentures. Many seniors use a dental savings plan to discount them, since there is no waiting period, no underwriting, and no annual cap.
How much can I save on dentures?+
On one published schedule, a full upper denture drops from about $1,954 to $1,123, a saving of roughly $831. A full set saves more. Exact savings vary by plan, dentist, and region.
