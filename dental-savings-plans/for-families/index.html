Dental savings plans for families

A busy household rarely needs the same care at the same time: a child in braces, a parent with a crown, everyone due for cleanings. A family savings plan covers all of it under one membership, with no annual maximum to ration.

The honest verdict

A family savings plan fits households with varied, ongoing needs. There is one low yearly fee, no annual maximum per person, no waiting period, and immediate discounts on everything from cleanings to braces to a parent's crown. If your family mostly needs routine cleanings and little else, a PPO that covers preventive at 100 percent may cost less, so compare both.

Why families feel the cap

Insurance rations, a savings plan does not

1

Per-person maximums

Each family member has a separate annual cap, commonly $1,000 to $2,000. One child's ortho or one parent's crown can exhaust it, leaving the rest at full price.

2

Waiting periods

New coverage often makes everyone wait 6 to 12 months for major work. A savings plan works within days for the whole household.

3

One membership, everyone covered

A family savings plan discounts care for the cardholder, spouse, and dependents, with no cap to plan around and no claims to file.

A household example

What a busy year can look like

The needs

Two kids due for cleanings, one child starting braces, and a parent who cracked a molar and needs a crown. On insurance, the braces and crown alone could blow past two separate annual maximums.

With a savings plan

Every item is discounted immediately under one membership. The crown drops from about $1,500 to roughly $949, the braces get a flat discount with no lifetime cap, and the cleanings are discounted too, with nothing rationed.

Questions

Family savings plans FAQ

Are dental savings plans good for families?+
They can be, especially for households with varied, ongoing needs. One membership covers the cardholder, spouse, and dependents with no annual maximum and no waiting period, so a busy year of braces, crowns, and cleanings is not rationed by per-person caps.
Who counts as a family member on a savings plan?+
Definitions vary by plan, but commonly the cardholder, spouse, and dependents up to age 18, or up to 25 if a full-time student. Delta Dental Patient Direct is enrolled per household. Check the specific plan before enrolling.
Is a family savings plan cheaper than family insurance?+
The yearly fee is usually lower, and there is no per-person cap. But cheaper to join is not always cheaper overall. A family that only needs cleanings may do better on a PPO that covers preventive at 100 percent.
Can one family plan cover braces for more than one child?+
Yes. A savings plan discounts orthodontics with no lifetime cap, so the same membership can be reused for each child in braces, which insurance cannot match.
