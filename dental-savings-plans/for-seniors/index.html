Dental savings plans for seniors

Original Medicare leaves routine dental out, so many retirees pay full price for cleanings, crowns, and dentures. A dental savings plan closes that gap with immediate discounts, no waiting period, and no annual cap.

The honest verdict

For a senior without dental coverage, a savings plan is often a sensible, low cost safety net. There is no underwriting, no waiting period, and no annual maximum, so you can enroll with existing problems and save right away on major work. If you mainly need cleanings and want an insurer to share the cost, a standalone PPO may serve you better. Confirm your dentist participates before choosing.

The Medicare gap

Why so many seniors have no dental coverage

1

Original Medicare excludes it

Parts A and B do not cover routine dental. Cleanings, fillings, dentures, and root canals are on you.

2

Advantage dental is limited

Some Medicare Advantage plans add dental, but caps are low and networks vary, so gaps remain common.

3

Group coverage ends at retirement

Employer dental usually stops when you retire, often right when you need more care, not less.

About 47 percent of adults aged 65 to 80 have no dental coverage, a gap a savings plan is well suited to fill.

Questions

Seniors and savings plans FAQ

Does Medicare cover dental?+
Original Medicare, Parts A and B, does not cover routine dental care like cleanings, fillings, dentures, or root canals. Some Medicare Advantage plans include limited dental, but many seniors have no dental coverage, which is why dental savings plans are popular in this group.
Are dental savings plans good for seniors?+
They can be a strong fit. There is no medical underwriting, no waiting period, and no annual maximum, so a senior can enroll with existing dental problems and save immediately on crowns, dentures, implants, and cleanings. The tradeoff is that a savings plan discounts care rather than paying for it.
What is the best plan for a retiree with no coverage?+
It depends on your needs. If you expect major work, a savings plan with a broad network and no cap often saves the most. If you mainly need cleanings and want an insurer to share the cost, a standalone PPO may fit better. Confirm your dentist participates before choosing either.
Can seniors use a savings plan for dentures?+
Yes. Savings plans discount dentures with no waiting period and no annual maximum. For example, a full upper denture around 1,954 dollars can drop to roughly 1,123 dollars on one published schedule, though fees vary by dentist and region.
