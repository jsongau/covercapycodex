Dental savings plans for braces

Braces are a big, planned expense, and insurance treats them with a strict lifetime cap. A dental savings plan takes a different approach: a flat discount with no cap and no waiting period, usable across every child in the family.

The honest verdict

For orthodontics, the savings-plan advantage is immediacy and no cap, not a huge percentage. Braces run $3,000 to $7,000 and clear aligners $3,500 to $8,000. Discounts on ortho are modest, often 15 to 31 percent, but there is no lifetime maximum and no waiting period, and the same membership can be reused for multiple children. A PPO with a high ortho lifetime maximum can win for a single child, so weigh both.

Why insurance falls short

The ortho lifetime cap

A once-in-a-lifetime benefit

PPO orthodontic coverage is capped by a lifetime maximum, commonly $1,000 to $2,500 per person at 50 percent. On a $6,000 case, a family still pays roughly $3,000 to $4,500 out of pocket, and once that lifetime benefit is used, it is gone.

A flat discount instead

A savings plan applies a flat discount with no lifetime cap. On a $6,000 case at 20 percent off, that is about $1,200 saved, immediately, with no waiting period, and the same membership discounts the next child too.

Questions

Braces and savings plans FAQ

Do dental savings plans cover braces?+
Savings plans discount braces rather than cover them. The discount, often 15 to 31 percent, applies immediately with no waiting period and no lifetime maximum, and the same membership can be reused for multiple children.
Is a savings plan or insurance better for braces?+
It depends. Insurance ortho is capped by a lifetime maximum, commonly $1,000 to $2,500 at 50 percent, then it is gone. A savings plan gives a smaller percentage but no cap and no wait, and works across a whole family. For a single child, a PPO with a high ortho maximum can win.
How much can I save on braces with a savings plan?+
On a $6,000 case at around 20 percent off, that is roughly $1,200, applied immediately. Exact savings vary by plan, provider, and region, and clear aligners are usually priced separately from metal braces.
Can I use one plan for more than one child?+
Yes. A family membership discounts orthodontics for each covered child with no lifetime cap, which is a key advantage over insurance for families with several kids in braces.
