The honest verdict

CignaPlus Savings fits someone uninsured who needs care now or faces big or cosmetic work a PPO would cap or exclude, especially if they value the bundled vision, LASIK, and hearing discounts. Cigna itself notes that a discount does not mean free, so for routine preventive care a PPO that covers cleanings at 100 percent usually wins. Confirm your dentist participates before enrolling.

What it is

A discount program, plainly

1

You pay the discounted fee

You pay the reduced fee in full directly to the dentist, based on Cigna's contracted fee schedule. The program never reimburses you.

2

None of the insurance limits

No claim forms, deductibles, waiting periods, age limits, frequency limits, annual maximums, or pre-existing exclusions, and no referrals for specialists.

3

Vision, LASIK, and hearing

Included at no extra cost as bundled discount programs, with savings on exams, frames, contacts, LASIK, and hearing aids.

4

It is not insurance

Cigna also sells true dental insurance separately. The discount program does not pay claims, and Cigna warns a discount does not mean free.

What it costs

Individual and family

Through an authorized seller it lists at about 185 dollars a year for an individual and about 220 dollars for a family, with promotional pricing closer to 166 and 198 dollars, plus a 20 dollar processing fee.

Prices vary by channel

Different sellers and promo codes show different tiers, and the final price often appears only at enrollment. Treat any single figure as a starting point and confirm at checkout.

Family eligibility: spouse, dependent children up to age 18, and full time students up to age 25.

vs a PPO

When it beats a PPO, and when it does not

CignaPlus Savings wins when

  • You are uninsured and want immediate access, with no waiting period.
  • You face major or cosmetic work that a PPO would cap or exclude.
  • You have already exhausted a PPO annual maximum and want to stack savings.
  • You value the bundled vision, LASIK, and hearing discounts.

A PPO wins when

  • You mainly need preventive care insurance covers at 100 percent.
  • The in network PPO rate after premium and coinsurance is lower than the discount price.
  • You want real reimbursement and protection against a catastrophic year.

Questions

CignaPlus Savings FAQ

Is CignaPlus Savings insurance?+
No. It is a dental discount program, not insurance. Members pay an annual fee and then pay the discounted fee in full directly to participating dentists, based on Cigna's contracted fee schedule. There are no claims, deductibles, waiting periods, or annual maximums.
How much does CignaPlus Savings cost?+
Through an authorized seller it lists at about 185 dollars a year for an individual and about 220 dollars for a family, with promotional pricing closer to 166 and 198 dollars, plus a 20 dollar processing fee. Prices vary by channel and promotion, so confirm the current price at enrollment.
How much can I save?+
Cigna states discounts average about 34 percent off most commonly performed services. There are no claim forms, deductibles, waiting periods, age limits, frequency limits, annual maximums, or exclusions for pre-existing conditions, and no referrals for specialty care.
What network does it use?+
It runs on Cigna's own discount network, Cigna Dental Network Access, with more than 110,000 participating provider locations nationwide. The discount only applies at participating providers, so confirm your dentist participates.
What extras are included?+
At no extra cost it includes discount savings on vision, LASIK, and hearing, each a discount program rather than insurance.

One thing worth checking first

A savings plan discount comes out of the dentist's pocket

A dental savings plan lowers your price because the dentist agrees to a smaller fee. No insurance carrier pays them the difference. That discount is the office doing you a favor at its own expense, which is part of why some premium and cosmetic dentists do not take these plans.

Individual dental insurance works differently. The carrier shares the cost of care, so you pay a smaller portion and the dentist is still paid a reimbursement on top of what you owe. When you can plan ahead and time bigger treatment to a year where your coverage is high and any waiting period has cleared, that shared cost often means you pay less and the office is paid more fairly for the same work.

A savings plan still has its place. It is ready right away with no waiting period, and it has no annual maximum, which helps when you need care now or your treatment plan runs past what a policy would cap. If your timing is flexible, it is worth seeing what an individual PPO plan would cover before you decide.
