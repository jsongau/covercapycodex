Dental savings plans for deep cleaning

A deep cleaning, known clinically as scaling and root planing, treats gum disease and is billed by the quarter of the mouth. Across a full mouth it adds up, and a dental savings plan discounts it immediately with no annual cap.

The honest verdict

For deep cleaning, a savings plan discounts periodontal care right away, commonly around 40 percent. Scaling and root planing near $311 per quadrant can drop to about $179, and a full-mouth treatment across four quadrants can otherwise brush against a PPO annual maximum, especially when gum care recurs. If you mainly need routine cleanings, though, a PPO that covers preventive at 100 percent may still be the better value.

Deep cleaning vs routine

Know which you need

A deep cleaning

Scaling and root planing treats active gum disease below the gumline, billed per quadrant. A savings plan discounts it with no cap, and gum care often recurs, which the no-cap structure suits.

A routine cleaning

A standard cleaning is preventive and much cheaper. If routine cleanings are all you need, a PPO that covers preventive at 100 percent can beat a discount plan, so weigh both.

Questions

Deep cleaning and savings plans FAQ

How much does a deep cleaning cost with a savings plan?+
Scaling and root planing near $311 per quadrant can drop to about $179 with a savings plan, roughly 40 percent off, applied immediately with no annual cap. A full mouth is typically four quadrants, so the total scales accordingly.
What is the difference between a deep cleaning and a regular cleaning?+
A regular cleaning is preventive and removes plaque above the gumline. A deep cleaning, or scaling and root planing, treats active gum disease below the gumline and is billed per quadrant, so it costs much more. A savings plan discounts both.
Is a savings plan good for gum disease treatment?+
It can be, because periodontal care often recurs and a savings plan has no annual maximum to ration it. The discount applies immediately with no waiting period. For someone who only needs routine cleanings, a PPO may still be the better value.
Does insurance cover deep cleaning?+
Insurance often covers periodontal scaling and root planing partially, subject to a deductible, coinsurance, and the annual maximum, and sometimes a waiting period. A savings plan instead discounts it immediately with no cap, but pays nothing itself.
