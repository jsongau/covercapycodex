Why patients pick PPO

A PPO lets a patient keep a wider choice of dentist, including out of network options, while in network visits use negotiated rates. That freedom is why it remains the default for most people who choose their own dental plan.

The coverage gap

Why so many people end up without dental cover

The gap is rarely a choice. It is the byproduct of how dental coverage is structured.

1

Dental is decoupled from medical

Only about 1.2 percent of commercial dental benefits are integrated into medical policies (NADP). So a marketplace medical plan, a new job, or a move can leave a person with full medical and no dental.

2

Medicare excludes routine dental

Original Medicare does not cover routine dental, and about 31 percent of Medicare recipients lack dental coverage (ADA HPI). Medicare Advantage dental enrollment actually fell 11.4 percent in 2024 (NADP).

3

Life events end employer plans

Changing jobs, retiring, turning 26 and aging off a parent plan, or going self employed all end an employer dental benefit, often with no obvious replacement waiting.

4

Cost is shifting to the individual

About 51 percent of commercial group dental is now voluntary, employee paid (NADP), and self insured plans rose to about 46 percent. The financial weight of dental is moving onto the person, not the employer.

The moments that send people looking for cover

Dental demand is event driven. These are the points where coverage lapses and a real need appears at the same time.

Where the market is heading

The market is moving toward the individual

No waiting period plans

Carriers are expanding direct to consumer plans where a higher premium buys shorter or no waiting periods, lowering the friction of buying cover on your own.

Regulators are following

New York removed waiting periods on most adult services for individual marketplace dental plans effective January 1, 2025, an early signal of where standalone dental is heading.

Voluntary and self insured rising

Voluntary, employee paid plans now exceed half of commercial group dental, and self insured arrangements rose to about 46 percent, reversing a long decline.

Questions

Dental insurance market FAQ

How many Americans have dental insurance?+
About 284 million Americans, around 83 percent of the population, had some form of dental benefit at the end of 2024, according to the National Association of Dental Plans. That was a 2.3 percent decline from the prior year, the first drop after a record high near 290 million in 2023.
What percent of Americans have no dental insurance?+
Population wide, about 13 percent have no dental coverage per NADP 2024 data. Among adults specifically, the ADA Health Policy Institute estimates about 27 percent, roughly 72 million people, lack dental insurance. The two figures differ because one counts all ages and the other counts only adults.
How big is the US dental insurance market?+
Analysts estimate the US dental insurance market at roughly 95 billion to 100 billion dollars in 2025 and 2026, with mid to high single digit annual growth projected into the next decade. These are analyst estimates and vary by methodology, so CoverCapy presents them as a range.
Do most people have PPO or DHMO dental plans?+
PPO plans dominate. Dental PPO products make up about 89 percent of commercial dental enrollment, a share unchanged year over year per NADP 2024 data. DHMO plans are a smaller segment, though some analysts project DHMO to grow faster from a lower base.
Why do so many people lack dental insurance?+
Dental is usually separate from medical coverage, so it is easy to lose. Original Medicare excludes routine dental, job changes and retirement end employer plans, and adults age off a parent plan at 26. Over half of commercial group dental is now voluntary, meaning cost increasingly falls on the individual.
How is the dental insurance market changing in 2026?+
Coverage dipped in 2024 for the first time after a record, individual and voluntary plans are taking a larger share, and carriers are expanding no waiting period, direct to consumer plans. New York removed waiting periods on most adult marketplace dental plans in 2025, a signal of where the market is heading.
Does CoverCapy sell dental insurance?+
No. CoverCapy is a concierge layer over the PPO dental market. It helps you compare PPO plans, then find a PPO or Capy Accredited dentist who treatment plans around your in network rates so your coverage is used effectively. CoverCapy does not underwrite or issue insurance policies.
