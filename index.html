PPO dental insurance, answered.

Straight answers to the questions patients ask us most about PPO dental insurance, networks, and what they will actually pay.

What is PPO dental insurance?

A PPO (Preferred Provider Organization) dental plan lets you see any dentist, but you pay less when you use one in the plan's network. You are not locked to a single office and do not need referrals, which makes PPO plans the most flexible common type of dental coverage.

Does a dentist who accepts my insurance mean they are in my PPO network?

Not always. A dentist can bill your carrier without being in your exact PPO network. In-network dentists agree to discounted rates, so confirming the specific network, not just that they take your insurance, is what protects you from surprise costs.

What is a waiting period on a PPO dental plan?

A waiting period is the time after your plan starts before certain services are covered, often a few months for major work like crowns or implants. Some plans waive the wait for preventive care, and a few cover major services sooner.

What is an annual maximum?

The annual maximum is the most your plan will pay toward your care in a plan year. Once you reach it, you pay the rest yourself, so the maximum matters most when you expect major treatment.

How do I estimate what I will actually pay for dental treatment?

Your cost depends on the dentist's fee, the PPO allowed amount, your plan's coverage percentage and deductible, and your remaining annual maximum. CoverCapy helps you line these up before you book.

I'm a University of California student. Do I already have dental insurance?

Students enrolled in UC SHIP generally receive dental coverage through a Delta Dental PPO plan. Using a Delta Dental PPO dentist can help reduce out-of-pocket costs through contracted network rates. CoverCapy helps students understand the network and find nearby options.

Is CoverCapy an insurance company or a broker?

Neither. CoverCapy is a concierge dental network, not a carrier or a broker. We explain how PPO plans, costs, and provider networks work, help you compare options, and confirm with the office whether a dentist accepts your exact PPO network. Plans are purchased directly from the carrier.

Is CoverCapy free to use?

Yes. Searching dentists, understanding your coverage, and estimating costs on CoverCapy are free. If you choose to buy a plan, it is purchased from the carrier, not from CoverCapy.
