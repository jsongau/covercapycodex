Crown cost by material

Material is the single biggest driver. These are 2026 US ranges without insurance.

Crown typeTypical cost (per tooth)Why
Porcelain-fused-to-metal (PFM)$800 to $2,000Usually the most affordable tooth-colored crown; metal core under a porcelain shell.

Ranges, not quotes. Geography, the dentist, and add-ons such as a build-up or root canal move the final number. A crown after a root canal or core build-up costs more because those are separate procedures.

What you are paying for

What drives the price

Material

Gold and premium all-ceramic cost more than PFM. The metal content and the lab artistry are the biggest swings.

Chair time and lab work

Two or more visits, impressions or digital scans, and a lab fabricating a precise restoration all sit inside the fee.

Add-ons

A core build-up, post, root canal, or gum work before the crown are billed separately and can add hundreds to the total.

Tooth and network

Front esthetic crowns cost more, and an in-network dentist is reimbursed at a lower allowed amount than out-of-network.

With vs without a PPO

What insurance actually pays

A crown is a major service. Most PPO plans pay a share after your deductible and any waiting period, capped by your annual maximum.

Without a PPO

$800 to $2,500+

You pay the full fee, plus any build-up or root canal. A dental savings plan or financing is often the only offset.

With a PPO

~$400 to $900

At 50% to 60% on major work, a $1,400 crown often nets roughly $400 to $900 out of pocket after the deductible, until your annual maximum is used.

On the CoverCapy shelf, Delta Dental PPO Premium pays 50% on major work after a 12-month wait under a $2,000 annual maximum, Guardian Premier pays 60% in-network after a 12-month wait under a $3,000 maximum, and NCD Complete by MetLife pays on a graduated major schedule from day one under a $10,000 maximum. Crowns are covered only when medically necessary, not when purely cosmetic. Compare PPO plans for crown coverage.

Timing and payment

Waiting periods and how to pay

Because crowns are a major service, many PPO plans apply a waiting period of 6 to 12 months before they pay toward one. Some carriers pay from day one but ramp the percentage over the first years. If a crown is already recommended, choose a fast-activating plan, then time elective work around the wait.

If you need the crown now, you can spread the cost. Options include 0% APR healthcare cards such as CareCredit and Sunbit over 6 to 24 months, in-house office payment plans, dental savings plans, your FSA or HSA dollars, a personal loan, or a PPO plan that pays a share. No-credit and low-down options exist for many patients.

Questions

Dental crown cost FAQ

How much does a dental crown cost in 2026?+
A dental crown typically costs about $800 to $2,500 per tooth in 2026, with a national average near $1,000 to $1,500. Porcelain-fused-to-metal sits at the lower end, while zirconia, all-ceramic and gold crowns cost more. A crown that follows a root canal or build-up raises the total.
Does dental insurance cover crowns?+
Most PPO dental plans cover crowns as a major service, paying about 50% to 60% of the allowed amount after your deductible and any waiting period, but only when the crown is medically necessary rather than cosmetic. One crown can use most of a $1,000 to $2,000 annual maximum, so limits vary by plan.
What is the cheapest type of crown?+
Porcelain-fused-to-metal crowns are usually the cheapest at roughly $800 to $2,000. They balance strength with a tooth-colored look. All-ceramic, zirconia and gold crowns cost more, but last longer or look more natural depending on the tooth.
How much is a crown with insurance?+
With a PPO that pays 50% to 60% on major services, a typical crown often nets roughly $400 to $900 out of pocket after the deductible, until your annual maximum is used. In-network rates lower the bill further, so verifying coverage at the office matters.
Is there a waiting period for crown coverage?+
Often yes. Because crowns are a major service, many PPO plans apply a waiting period of 6 to 12 months before they pay toward one. Some carriers pay from day one but ramp the percentage over the first years, so a fast-activating plan helps if you need a crown soon.
Can I finance a dental crown?+
Yes. You can finance a crown through 0% healthcare cards such as CareCredit or Sunbit over 6 to 24 months, in-house office payment plans, dental savings plans, an FSA or HSA, or a personal loan. No-credit and low-down options exist for many patients.
