Mega Nav Prototype

Click to open. Click again to enter.

Hover any top-level item to peek the panel. Click it once to lock it open, and the label morphs into "Open [Section]" so a second click takes you to the page. Scroll down: the global nav stays sticky and condenses, and the Delta Dental sub-nav stacks right beneath it, both pinned, never overlapping.

Try it: click "Insurance" to reveal the panel, then click it again to enter. Or use the sub-nav above to jump, anchors clear both sticky bars.

Plans

This block exists to demonstrate scrolling and the stacked sticky behavior. As you scroll, watch the global navigation condense from 84px to 62px while the Delta Dental sub-nav re-pins directly below it using the live --nav-h custom property updated by a ResizeObserver. The active sub-nav tab updates as each section scrolls into view.

Curated, real links live inside each mega panel rather than dumping the full sitemap. Large clusters, the 24-term glossary, all carriers, the 11 emergency pages, collapse to a single "View all" pointing at their hub.

Eligibility

Anchor jumps land with the section heading fully visible, never tucked behind the two sticky bars, thanks to scroll-margin-top: calc(var(--nav-h) + var(--subnav-h) + 14px). The combined offset stays correct even after the header condenses, because both heights are measured live and written back to CSS variables.

Networks

The mega panels reveal with an ease-out-expo curtain (clip-path) and a gentle column stagger that reads as deliberate and considered rather than springy. Exit is faster, with no stagger. All of this respects prefers-reduced-motion, collapsing to a simple fade.

Over 65

On mobile, the dropdowns are replaced by a slide-in drawer with accordion sections, a ZIP search, body-scroll-lock, focus trap, and return-focus to the menu button on close. Resize this window below 1080px to try it.
