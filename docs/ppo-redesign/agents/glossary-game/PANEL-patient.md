# PANEL-patient.md — Real Person Reaction

**Who I am:** A normal patient on the test panel. Not an insurance person. I got hit with a surprise dental bill once (thought my crown was "80 percent covered," paid way more than I expected), and ever since, insurance words make my eyes glaze. I am reading this on my phone, one-handed, probably while half-watching TV. I bounce fast.

I read the three memos (calculator, scenario simulator, progression/XP). Here is my gut reaction, blunt.

---

## THE ONE THING THAT WOULD ACTUALLY STOP ME

The "Your Real Out-of-Pocket" slider (Widget 1) is the only idea here that fixes MY exact problem. The whole memo is built around the thing that burned me: 80 percent coverage does not mean I pay 20 percent. When the memo says "you expect $240, the widget shows $280," that is literally my bad day on a screen. If you show me that in five seconds with a slider I drag, I will stop scrolling. That is not homework. That is "wait, THAT is why my bill was wrong?"

Build that one. If you only ship one thing, ship that.

---

## WHAT WORKS (would make me learn, not bounce)

- **The slider with the bar that resizes.** I get it instantly. Drag the cost up, watch the colors move, see the number. No reading required. This is the format my brain accepts.
- **The "what if she enrolled today" replay** in the cracked-molar scenario. Changing ONE thing and watching the $1,200 bill vanish is the kind of "oh" moment I would actually screenshot and send to my sister. That is the whole point.
- **The annual maximum bucket draining to zero.** I did not even know the cap was on what THEY pay, not what I pay. Watching it run dry and flip to full price taught me something real in one tap.
- **No fake celebration when something bad happens.** The "Maxed out" dry stamp instead of confetti felt respectful. Confetti when I just learned I am broke would have made me distrust the whole thing.

---

## WHAT FEELS LIKE HOMEWORK OR A GIMMICK (cut or shrink)

- **The XP / Crowns / badges system (UX05) is the weakest for me, and I would not finish it.** "You know 7 of 23 terms" with a progress ring is fine for like two seconds, but 23 terms is a LOT. The second I see a 23-step trail, my brain says "this is a course," and I close the tab. I came to understand ONE bill, not earn a "Capy Crowns Scholar" medallion. Badges named "No More Surprises" and "The Fine Print" are clever but I do not care about badges for money stuff. I care about not getting screwed again.
- **Cut the "Coverage Confidence meter" framing if it ever feels like a score I am being graded on.** I do not want to feel quizzed on my own money. The memo says it is "not money to avoid feeling like a sales pitch," but a meter filling up over multiple scenarios still feels like a video game grind I will quit.
- **The quiz-first thing in Widget 3 ("Which tier covers a crown?") is a coin flip between great and annoying.** If I guess wrong and it goes "Most users guess Basic, but it is Major," that is a good gotcha. But if every single widget makes me guess before it shows me anything, I will feel tested and tired. Use the quiz ONCE, on the most surprising term (crown = Major), not everywhere.

---

## WHERE I GET CONFUSED OR BORED

- **Too many sliders at once.** Widget 1 has Slider A, B, C, plus a toggle. On my phone that is four things to fiddle with before I see the answer. Default them all to a real example (the crown, $50 deductible, 80 percent) so I see the "$280 not $240" punchline BEFORE I touch anything. Let me explore after. Do not make me build the scenario myself first.
- **"Effective coverage 76.7 percent."** A decimal percent makes my eyes glaze. Just say "the plan really covered about 77 percent, not 80." Round it. Decimals feel like a spreadsheet, not a person talking to me.
- **The calendar-year vs plan-year toggle (Scenario 2).** Honestly this is the moment I would bounce. Two kinds of "year" is one nuance too many for a phone. I do not know which one I even have. If you keep it, tell me how to FIND OUT which one I have, or I am just more confused than when I started.
- **"Predetermination," "downgrade," "UCR," "allowed amount," "balance billing."** These words in the badge clusters mean nothing to me. If the glossary itself does not translate them in plain words in the first sentence, I am lost. "UCR" especially. No idea.

---

## JARGON THAT STILL TRIPS ME

- **Coinsurance vs copay.** I still mix these up and the memos assume I know the difference. The slider says "coinsurance (plan pays)" which actually helped, keep that parenthetical everywhere.
- **"Effective date" vs "enrollment date."** The scenario memo says the gap matters, and I believe it, but I would need that spelled out like I am five: "you signed up Monday, you are not actually covered until [date]." Show me the literal gap.
- **"Waiting period."** The cracked-molar story is the best teacher of this I have seen. Keep the story. The word alone never landed for me before.

---

## DOES THE GAMIFICATION FEEL RESPECTFUL OR CHILDISH?

Mixed. The sliders and scenarios feel respectful, because the "game" is just seeing real dollars move. That is not childish, that is useful. The badge/XP/Crowns layer leans childish for a money-and-health topic. "Capy Crowns Scholar" and medallions feel like a language-learning app, and I do not want my dental bill gamified like that. The memo keeps SAYING "boutique, adult, calm," but a 23-term collectible trail is not calm, it is a completionist grind. Trust the sliders. Drop the trophy case.

---

## WHAT WOULD EARN MY TRUST (and make me click "compare plans")

1. **Show me the ugly number honestly.** The fact that this tool shows me I pay MORE than I thought, instead of selling me a rosy picture, is the single biggest trust signal. The memos already do this (the $280, the maxed-out stamp, "do not imply 100 percent on major work"). Keep that honesty front and center. That is what makes me believe CoverCapy is on my side and not just another insurance upsell.
2. **Do not ask for my email until I have gotten value.** The 18/23 email nudge is fine in theory, but if a save-progress bar pops up before I have learned anything, I am out. Let me get the "aha" first, for free, no signup.
3. **Tell me what to do next in plain English.** After the crown scenario shows me I overpaid, the right CTA is not "earn a badge," it is "here is how to not let this happen to you, compare plans." Connect the lesson directly to the fix.
4. **Round numbers, plain words, real procedures I recognize** (cleaning, filling, crown, root canal). The memos already anchor on these, good.

---

## MOBILE REALITY (I am on my phone)

- **Three-plus sliders stacked on a phone is a thumb nightmare.** Tiny range thumbs are hard to grab. Prefer the snap-buttons (50/80/100) over free sliders wherever possible, they are far easier to tap one-handed.
- **The stacked bar splitting into three colors is great on mobile** as long as the labels do not overlap. On a narrow screen, gold/teal/mint segments with tiny text on top will collide. Put the labels below or in a legend.
- **The 12-month timeline with a sweeping playhead worries me on a small screen.** A horizontal 12-month track is wide. On my phone it either shrinks to where I cannot read the month labels, or it scrolls sideways and I miss half the animation. Test this hard on a real phone before committing.
- **The URL-bitmask progress (?p=) is invisible to me and that is fine,** but if I share a link with my sister and it is full of "?p=3a9f2," she will think it is spam or a tracking link. People are suspicious of ugly URLs now.
- **Keep each interaction to ONE screen, no scrolling to see the answer.** If I drag a slider at the top and the answer is below the fold, I will not connect them.

---

## 7-BULLET SUMMARY

- **WORKS:** The "Your Real Out-of-Pocket" slider is the killer app. It fixes my exact surprise-bill problem (80 percent does not mean I pay 20 percent) in five seconds with no reading. Ship this even if you ship nothing else.
- **WORKS:** The "what if she enrolled earlier" replay and the draining annual-max bucket create genuine "oh" moments I would screenshot. Cause-and-effect I caused myself sticks better than any definition.
- **CUT / SHRINK:** The 23-term XP / Crowns / badge trail (UX05) feels like a course and a completionist grind. I will not finish it. Keep a quiet "you have seen 3 terms" at most, drop the medallions and "Scholar" capstone for a money topic.
- **CONFUSING:** Default the sliders to a real example so I see the punchline before I touch anything, round "76.7 percent" to "about 77 percent," and reconsider the calendar-vs-plan-year toggle (one nuance too many for a phone, and I do not know which I have).
- **JARGON:** Coinsurance vs copay, effective date vs enrollment date, and UCR / predetermination / balance billing still lose me. Translate in the first sentence, keep the "(plan pays)" parentheticals, and show the literal sign-up-to-covered date gap.
- **TRUST:** Showing me I pay MORE than I expected, honestly, is the biggest trust signal. Do not ask for my email before I get a free aha. Then connect the lesson straight to "compare plans," not to a badge.
- **MOBILE:** Prefer tap snap-buttons over fiddly sliders, keep answers above the fold on one screen, and stress-test that wide 12-month timeline on a real phone before building it.
