✶ The plain-English shelf

The terms that decide your bill.

Annual maximum
The most a dental plan will pay toward your care in one year. Once you reach it, you cover the rest yourself.
Waiting period
The number of months you must hold a plan before a tier of dental care becomes covered.
Deductible
The amount you pay out of pocket each year before your dental plan begins sharing the cost of care.
In-network
A dentist who has agreed to a plan's discounted PPO fees, so your share of the bill is lower.
Coinsurance
The share of a covered procedure you pay after the deductible, such as 50% of the cost of a crown.
Major services
Higher-cost dental work such as crowns, bridges, dentures and implants, usually the last tier to be covered.
Missing-tooth clause
A rule that excludes coverage for replacing teeth you lost before the plan started.
Out-of-pocket cost
The portion of a dental bill you pay yourself after the plan has paid its share.
Effective date
The day your dental coverage actually begins after you enroll in a plan.
Balance billing
When a dentist bills you for the difference between their fee and the plan's allowed amount.
