## Network & underwriter

- **Network:** Delta Dental PPO — marketed as one of the largest dental networks in the nation. You get the lowest out-of-pocket cost with an in-network PPO dentist. You may visit **any licensed dentist**, in or out of network, but out-of-network visits can cost more and expose you to balance billing.
- **Underwriter / who is financially responsible:** "Delta Dental" is a national association of independent member companies. Per Delta's own disclosure, PPO plans are **underwritten by Delta Dental Insurance Company** in AL, DC, FL, GA, LA, MS, MT, NV and UT, and by **not-for-profit dental service companies** in others — e.g., Delta Dental of California (CA); Delta Dental of Pennsylvania (PA, MD); Delta Dental of New York, Inc. (NY); Delta Dental of Delaware, Inc. (DE); Delta Dental of West Virginia, Inc. (WV). **In Texas, it is offered as a dental provider organization (DPO) plan, not a PPO.** This is why plan terms vary by state — the legal entity and filed policy differ.
- **Where it's offered (individual PPO):** Delta lists individual PPO availability in **AL, CA, DE, FL, GA, LA, MD, MS, MT, NV, NY, PA, TX, UT, WV and DC.** If your state isn't listed, Delta directs you to other individual plans (e.g., DeltaCare USA).

### How to verify a dentist for *this exact product*
1. Use Delta Dental's online **Find a Dentist** tool.
2. **Select the "Delta Dental PPO" network specifically** (not Delta Dental Premier or another network) — a dentist can be in Premier but not PPO, which changes your cost.
3. Confirm directly with the dental office that they are **in-network for Delta Dental PPO** as of your start date.
4. Match the dentist's network status to the **exact policy** you're quoted for your ZIP, since networks and the underwriting company differ by state.

## Major limitations (read before you rely on coverage)

These are the provisions most likely to surprise members. They are **standard Delta practice** but **the exact wording is set by the policy filed in your state** — confirm in your booklet.

### 1. Missing-tooth handling (implants, bridges, dentures)
A **missing-tooth clause** is a common dental-insurance provision: if a tooth was already missing **before** your coverage began, the plan won't pay to replace it (implant, bridge, or partial). **Delta's practice here is not uniform:**
- Some Delta plans/states apply a **missing-tooth clause** (no benefit for pre-existing missing teeth).
- Some Delta plans instead use a **"Missing Tooth Inclusion,"** which *does* cover replacement of teeth lost before coverage for eligible members (often age 16+) when restorative work is covered.
- A few Delta companies (e.g., certain state plans) state they have **no** missing-tooth exclusion.

**Bottom line:** Whether your pre-existing gap is covered is **[VARIES BY STATE / BY POLICY]**. Do not assume an implant or bridge for an already-missing tooth is covered. Get it confirmed in writing.

### 2. Crown alternate-benefit / LEAT downgrade (detailed — confirmed Delta practice)
Delta plans use an **alternate-benefit / Least Expensive Alternative Treatment (LEAT)** provision. Under LEAT, when more than one clinically accepted treatment can correct a problem, the plan **bases its payment on the least expensive adequate option** — *even if you and your dentist choose a different, more expensive treatment.* You can still get the treatment you want; the plan just pays less, and you cover the gap.

How this commonly hits **crowns**:
- **Material downgrade on back teeth.** If you get a **porcelain or zirconia (tooth-colored)** crown on a **molar/back tooth**, the plan may pay only at the **rate for a metal or porcelain-fused-to-metal (PFM) crown**, treating the esthetic upgrade as your choice to pay for. Example (illustrative): a $1,800 zirconia crown vs. an $1,200 PFM allowance — the plan applies your 50% to the **$1,200**, not the $1,800, so it pays ~$600 and you pay the rest, including the full upgrade difference.
- **Crown downgraded to a filling.** If the plan's adjudication system judges that a **large filling** would have adequately restored the tooth, it may pay the crown at the **filling benefit level** and you owe the difference.
- **Composite-to-amalgam downgrade on fillings** is the same mechanic at the basic tier: a white (composite) filling on a back tooth may be paid at the silver (amalgam) rate.

This is a **cost-containment feature, not a denial of care** — but it is the single most common reason a Delta crown costs more out-of-pocket than members expect. (See ADA LEAT reference in Sources.)

### 3. Maximum Allowable Charge (MAC) / reimbursement basis
Delta PPO plans calculate payment against a **maximum contract allowance / Maximum Allowable Charge (MAC)** — a fee schedule, not the dentist's billed fee.
- **In-network PPO dentists** agree to Delta's PPO fees, so you generally aren't balance-billed above the allowance.
- **Out-of-network dentists** are reimbursed only up to the plan's allowed amount; if their fee is higher, **you may owe the difference (balance billing).** Example (illustrative): dentist bills $120, contract allowance is $100 → Delta calculates its share and yours on **$100**, and out-of-network you may also owe the $20 gap.
- Some employer/group versions are explicitly labeled **"PPO (MAC)"** plans, where out-of-network is paid on the PPO schedule (lowest reimbursement). The reimbursement basis for **non-Delta dentists varies by plan** — confirm yours.

### 4. Frequency limits
Even covered services have **frequency caps**, e.g.:
- Cleanings/exams typically **twice per benefit year** (some plans allow more with medical need). **[UNVERIFIED exact frequency — confirm per policy]**
- Bitewing/full X-ray sets limited by interval.
- Crowns/major restorations on the same tooth limited to once every several years (commonly ~5 years). **[UNVERIFIED exact interval — confirm per policy]**
- Orthodontics may carry a **lifetime maximum** separate from the annual maximum. **[UNVERIFIED — confirm per policy]**

### 5. Downgrades / LEAT generally + state variation
- LEAT applies **across categories**, not just crowns (bridges vs. partials, composite vs. amalgam, etc.).
- The **annual maximum ($2,000)** caps total plan payment per person per year; you pay 100% of costs after it's reached.
- **State variation is the headline caveat:** the underwriting company, exact coinsurance, waiting periods, whitening coverage, missing-tooth treatment, and frequency rules **all differ by state and policy version.** A summary like this can describe the *shape* of the plan but cannot substitute for your filed policy.

## Who it fits / who should keep comparing

**May fit you if:**
- You want **strong preventive coverage (100%, no wait)** plus a real benefit toward **crowns, root canals, implants, dentures, and orthodontics**.
- You can use an **in-network Delta Dental PPO dentist** (lowest cost) and value the large network.
- You're planning **ahead** for major or ortho work and can sit through the ~12-month waiting period, or you have prior coverage that may shorten it.
- You want a **higher annual maximum ($2,000)** than entry-level plans.

**Keep comparing if:**
- You need **major work soon** — waiting periods (~6/12 months) may not clear in time.
- You're replacing a **pre-existing missing tooth** — missing-tooth handling is uncertain and state-dependent.
- You strongly prefer **tooth-colored crowns on back teeth** — LEAT downgrades can raise your out-of-pocket meaningfully.
- Your preferred dentist is **out of network** — MAC/balance-billing exposure can erode the value.
- You're highly **premium-sensitive** — PPO Premium is Delta's higher-premium tier; PPO Basic or DeltaCare USA may cost less (with less coverage).

## Illustrative total-cost example

**Assumptions (all illustrative, in-network, after waiting periods cleared, second plan year):**
- Premium: ~$75/mo × 12 = **$900/yr** (placeholder; your real premium varies by age/ZIP)
- Deductible: **$50** (applies to basic/major, not preventive)
- Services in the year:
  - 2 cleanings + 2 exams + X-rays → **preventive, plan pays 100%** → your cost ~$0
  - 1 filling, allowed amount ~$200 → **basic 80%** → plan pays ~$160, you pay ~$40
  - 1 crown (PFM allowance ~$1,200, *after any LEAT downgrade*) → **major 50%** → plan pays ~$600, you pay ~$600

**Your estimated annual out-of-pocket:**
| Item | Your cost |
|---|---|
| Premiums (12 mo) | ~$900 |
| Deductible | ~$50 |
| Filling coinsurance (20%) | ~$40 |
| Crown coinsurance (50% of allowed) | ~$600 |
| **Total (illustrative)** | **~$1,590** |

Plan paid roughly **$760** toward services, well under the $2,000 annual maximum.

**Caveats:** If your crown is **zirconia on a back tooth**, the LEAT downgrade means your 50% applies to the lower PFM/metal allowance and you **also** pay the full upgrade difference — pushing your out-of-pocket higher than shown. If your dentist is **out of network**, add potential balance billing above the MAC. Numbers above are for illustration only and are **not** a quote.

## How to get your EXACT cost

1. **Get the official policy for your ZIP.** Request the **Policy and Benefit Details / plan booklet** for the exact PPO Premium product sold in your state. Verify deductible, annual maximum, coinsurance, waiting periods, frequency limits, missing-tooth handling, and whitening — these are the items that vary.
2. **Ask your dentist for an itemized treatment plan with CDT codes.**
3. **Request a pre-treatment estimate (pre-determination) from Delta Dental** *before* any work begins. Delta will return, in writing, what it expects to allow and pay — including any **LEAT downgrade** and any **frequency/missing-tooth** limitation applied to your case.
4. **Compare the two documents.** The dentist's itemized estimate plus Delta's written pre-determination together give you your true expected out-of-pocket. Get both **before** treatment starts.
5. **Confirm network status** of your dentist for the **Delta Dental PPO** network specifically, as of your coverage start date.

A pre-treatment estimate is the only reliable way to know your real cost. Verbal quotes and summaries (including this document) are not binding.
