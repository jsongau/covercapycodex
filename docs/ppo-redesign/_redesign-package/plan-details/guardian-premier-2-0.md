# Guardian Premier 2.0 — Plan Reference

**One-line summary:** An individual/family PPO dental plan from Guardian that pays 100% for preventive care, an unusually strong **85% for basic services from day one**, 50% for major work (after a 12-month wait), child-only orthodontics, and teeth whitening — all built around the Guardian PPO / DentalGuard Preferred network and underwritten by The Guardian Life Insurance Company of America.

**Last reviewed:** 2026-06-21

**Verification status:** PARTIALLY VERIFIED — illustrative reference only, with one **material conflict to resolve before relying on this plan** (see box below).
Guardian's plan family, network, underwriter, day-one preventive/basic structure, $3,000 annual maximum, $50 deductible, 1st-of-month effective-date rule, and child-only orthodontics are corroborated against Guardian's official pages and a licensed Guardian marketplace listing (see Sources). However, **CoverCapy is an independent marketplace and does not sell, administer, or confirm this coverage.** Every figure here is **illustrative** until matched to a current, official Guardian plan policy/booklet for **your specific state and ZIP code.** Premiums, coinsurance, waiting periods, frequency limits, and even which services are covered **vary by state, by plan version, and by underwriting filing.** Items we could not confirm against a primary Guardian source are flagged **[UNVERIFIED]**, **[VARIES BY STATE]**, or **[CONFLICT — VERIFY]** inline.

> **⚠️ Plan-name / coverage conflict — verify before relying on this.** Our CoverCapy database describes **"Premier 2.0"** with **Major at 50%**, **implants NOT covered**, and **child orthodontics at 50%**. The closest publicly documented Guardian product — marketed as **"Guardian Advantage Premier 2.0"** — shows **Major (crowns/root canals) at 60% with a 12-month wait, implants COVERED at 60% ($1,250 lifetime max), and child orthodontics at 60% in-network ($750 annual / $1,500 lifetime ortho max)**, plus whitening at 50% ($500 max). These may be **different tiers or versions of the same family, or our record may be a state/group variant.** Treat the coverage figures in this document as the **as-stored CoverCapy baseline**, and **confirm the exact plan name, tier, and percentages against the official Schedule of Benefits for your state before enrolling.** Where the public source differs, it is noted inline.

> **Year-one vs. later years:** This plan is unusually front-loaded. In your **first 6 months** you have full **preventive (100%)** and **basic (85%)** access immediately. **Whitening** unlocks at ~6 months; **major** work and **child orthodontics** unlock at **12 months.** From the **second year on**, all covered categories are available subject to the annual maximum, separate ortho/whitening maximums, and frequency limits.

## Network & underwriter

- **Network:** **Guardian PPO / DentalGuard Preferred.** Guardian markets a large national PPO network (it cites well over 100,000 providers nationwide; some materials cite ~130,000+). You get the lowest out-of-pocket cost with an in-network dentist. You may visit **any licensed dentist**, in or out of network — Guardian PPO plans **do not require a primary dentist or referrals** — but out-of-network visits can cost more and expose you to balance billing. Note there is also a narrower **"DentalGuard Preferred Select"** network on some plans; confirm which network your plan uses.
- **Underwriter / who is financially responsible:** Coverage is **underwritten and issued by The Guardian Life Insurance Company of America (Guardian), New York, NY.** DentalGuard Insurance is a Guardian product. The underwriting entity and the exact filed policy can differ by state, which is why plan terms vary.

### How to verify a dentist for *this exact product*
1. Use Guardian's **Find a Dentist** tool at **guardiananytime.com** (the provider-finder app) or the directory on **guardianlife.com**.
2. **Select the correct network** — choose **"PPO: DentalGuard Preferred"** (and confirm whether your plan uses the full network or **DentalGuard Preferred Select**, which is narrower). A dentist can be in one Guardian network but not another, which changes your cost.
3. Confirm directly with the dental office that they are **in-network for your specific Guardian PPO network** as of your coverage start date.
4. Match the dentist's network status to the **exact policy** quoted for your ZIP, since networks and filed terms differ by state.
5. If unsure, call Guardian member services (the number on guardianlife.com / your ID card) to confirm the dentist and the exact plan.

## Major limitations (read before you rely on coverage)

These are the provisions most likely to surprise members. They reflect **standard Guardian practice**, but **the exact wording is set by the policy filed in your state** — confirm in your Schedule of Benefits.

### 1. No implant coverage (per our record)
Our CoverCapy record lists **implants as NOT COVERED** on Premier 2.0. **[CONFLICT — VERIFY]** The publicly documented "Advantage Premier 2.0" lists implants **covered at 60% with a $1,250 lifetime maximum.** If implant coverage matters to you, do **not** assume it is included — get the implant provision confirmed in writing for your exact plan and state before enrolling.

### 2. Orthodontics is child-only, with a lifetime maximum
- **Dependents age 19 and under only.** Comprehensive orthodontic treatment is covered for covered dependents when the **active appliance is first placed before age 19**. **Adult orthodontics is NOT covered.**
- **Orthodontia carries a separate LIFETIME maximum** distinct from the $3,000 annual maximum. Public "Advantage Premier 2.0" materials cite a **$750 ortho benefit-year maximum** and a **$1,500 ortho lifetime maximum** (with in-network ortho at 60%). **[VERIFY]** Our baseline lists child ortho at 50%; the exact percentage, annual cap, and lifetime cap **must be confirmed against your state's Schedule of Benefits.** A typical individual-PPO ortho lifetime max in this family is on the order of **$1,000–$1,500** — treat any specific figure as illustrative until verified.

### 3. 12-month waiting period on major work and child ortho
Crowns, root canals, dentures, and child orthodontics are **not available until month 12.** If you need major work soon, this plan will not pay for it in year one. (Some states cap the maximum wait — MN ≤9 months, WA ≤6 months — **[VARIES BY STATE]**.)

### 4. Missing-tooth clause
Guardian plans commonly include a **missing-tooth provision**: expenses to **replace teeth that were lost or extracted before the policy effective date are not covered** (may vary by state and plan). This most often affects bridges, partials, and dentures (and implants where covered). **[VERIFY per state]** — do not assume a pre-existing gap will be covered.

### 5. Frequency limits
Even covered services have **frequency caps**, e.g.:
- Cleanings/exams typically **2× per benefit year**. **[Confirm exact frequency per policy]**
- Bitewing / full X-ray sets limited by interval. **[UNVERIFIED exact interval]**
- Major restorations (e.g., crowns) on the same tooth limited to once every several years (commonly ~5–7 years). **[UNVERIFIED exact interval — confirm per policy]**

### 6. Out-of-network reimbursement basis
Reimbursement is based on **the lower of the dentist's billed fee or Guardian's negotiated/allowed fee.** In-network dentists accept Guardian's fee schedule, so you generally aren't balance-billed. **Out-of-network dentists** can bill you the difference between their fee and Guardian's allowance (**balance billing**), raising your out-of-pocket.

### 7. State variation (the headline caveat)
The underwriting entity, exact coinsurance, waiting periods, whitening coverage, implant treatment, ortho caps, and frequency rules **all differ by state and plan version.** Maximum-waiting-period caps in MN (≤9 mo) and WA (≤6 mo) are concrete examples. A summary like this describes the *shape* of the plan but cannot substitute for your filed Schedule of Benefits.

## Who it fits / who should keep comparing

**May fit you if:**
- You have a **child or teen (≤19) who needs or may need braces** and you can clear the 12-month ortho wait — this is a child-ortho-capable plan.
- You want **immediate, strong everyday coverage**: 100% preventive **and 85% basic from day one** (fillings/extractions covered right away — a standout feature).
- You'd use the **whitening benefit** (50% after a short 6-month wait).
- You can use an **in-network Guardian PPO / DentalGuard Preferred dentist** and value a large network.
- You want a **higher annual maximum ($3,000)** than entry-level plans.

**Keep comparing if:**
- You **need dental implants** — our record shows them **not covered** (and even the public variant caps them at a $1,250 lifetime), so confirm before relying on it.
- You **need adult orthodontics** — **not covered** (child-only).
- You **need major work within the first 12 months** — the major-services wait won't clear in time.
- Your preferred dentist is **out of network** — balance-billing exposure can erode the value.
- You're **highly premium-sensitive** — a lower-tier Guardian plan (e.g., Core/Achiever family) may cost less with less coverage.

## Illustrative total-cost example

**Assumptions (all illustrative, in-network, second plan year so all waits are cleared):**
- Premium: ~$70/mo × 12 = **$840/yr** (placeholder; your real premium varies by age/ZIP/tier)
- Deductible: **$50** (applies to basic/major, typically waived for in-network preventive)
- Services in the year:
  - 2 cleanings + 2 exams + X-rays → **preventive, plan pays 100%** → your cost ~$0
  - 2 fillings, allowed amount ~$200 each = ~$400 → **basic 85%** → plan pays ~$340, you pay ~$60
  - 1 crown, allowed amount ~$1,200 → **major 50%** → plan pays ~$600, you pay ~$600

**Your estimated annual out-of-pocket:**
| Item | Your cost |
|---|---|
| Premiums (12 mo) | ~$840 |
| Deductible | ~$50 |
| Filling coinsurance (15% of ~$400) | ~$60 |
| Crown coinsurance (50% of ~$1,200) | ~$600 |
| **Total (illustrative)** | **~$1,550** |

Plan paid roughly **$940** toward services, well under the $3,000 annual maximum. The **85% basic** coinsurance is what makes the everyday-care portion cheap here relative to typical 80%/50% plans.

**Caveats:** If your **major** coinsurance is actually **60%** (per the public "Advantage Premier 2.0" listing) rather than 50%, your crown cost drops to ~$480 — another reason to confirm the exact percentages. If your dentist is **out of network**, add potential balance billing above Guardian's allowance. Child orthodontics would add its own coinsurance up to the **separate ortho lifetime maximum**, not shown here. Numbers above are for illustration only and are **not** a quote.

## How to get your EXACT cost

1. **Get the official policy for your ZIP.** Request the **Schedule of Benefits / plan booklet** for the exact Guardian plan and tier sold in your state. Verify the **plan name** (Premier 2.0 vs. Advantage Premier 2.0), deductible, annual maximum, **major and ortho percentages**, **whether implants are covered**, waiting periods, frequency limits, missing-tooth handling, and the **ortho annual + lifetime maximums** — these are the items that vary and that our record flags as conflicting.
2. **Ask your dentist for an itemized treatment plan with CDT codes.**
3. **Request a pre-treatment estimate (pre-determination) from Guardian** *before* any work begins. Guardian will return, in writing, what it expects to allow and pay — including any **frequency, missing-tooth, or ortho-cap** limitation applied to your case.
4. **Compare the two documents.** The dentist's itemized estimate plus Guardian's written pre-determination together give you your true expected out-of-pocket. Get both **before** treatment starts.
5. **Confirm network status** of your dentist for your **specific Guardian PPO / DentalGuard Preferred** network, as of your coverage start date.

A pre-treatment estimate is the only reliable way to know your real cost. Verbal quotes and summaries (including this document) are not binding.
