Is this the right plan

Who Delta PPO Premium may fit — and who should keep comparing

Premium plans only pay off if you actually reach the major-work benefits. This split is scenario-based; none of it is a guarantee until your exact policy is verified.

May fit you if…

  • You want one plan that reaches across preventive, basic, major, implants and braces rather than preventive-only cover.
  • You can plan ahead — your crown, implant or ortho work is 6–12 months out, not this week.
  • You value the Delta Dental PPO network's size and your dentist already participates.
  • You're comfortable with a higher premium in exchange for adult orthodontic coverage, which many plans exclude.

Keep comparing if…

  • You need major work now — the 12-month major/implant wait means you'd pay full price in the meantime.
  • You only want cleanings and exams — a cheaper preventive plan likely serves you for less.
  • Your treatment plan is large — a $2,000 maximum can be consumed by a single crown-plus-implant case.
  • Your dentist is out of the Delta PPO network — out-of-network costs can erase the premium difference.

Waiting periods

When each benefit actually unlocks

Your policy effective date (when premiums and preventive cover begin) is not the same as category eligibility (when the plan will pay toward a crown or implant). Both matter for timing treatment.

Effective date
1st of the month after you enroll. Preventive care is available immediately — cleanings, exams and routine x-rays at 100% in-network.
+6 months
Basic services unlock. Fillings and simple extractions become eligible at 80%.
+12 months
Major, implant and orthodontic services unlock at 50%. Treatment started before this date is generally not reimbursed retroactively.
Waiting periods can sometimes be waived with proof of prior continuous coverage. Whether that applies to you depends on the state filing and your enrollment record — ask the carrier directly before assuming a wait is gone.

Network

The network is named "Delta Dental PPO" — verify it twice

This plan reimburses at its best rates only when you see a dentist in the Delta Dental PPO network. Delta also operates a broader "Premier" network that pays differently, and the same office can be in one and not the other. A carrier name on a directory is a starting point, not a guarantee for your exact plan.

Before you book anything major, confirm participation in two places: search the carrier's own provider tool for your specific plan, and call the office to confirm they take that exact Delta product and are accepting new patients. Out-of-network care under a PPO can leave you with balance bills the premium was supposed to protect against.

Major limitations

The fine print that changes the bill

These are the categories most likely to surprise patients. Exact wording varies by state filing — read your own plan's exclusions section.

Missing-tooth clause

Many PPO plans won't pay to replace a tooth that was already missing before the policy started. Confirm whether this plan has one before counting on implant or bridge coverage.

Frequency limits

Cleanings, x-rays and major restorations are usually limited (e.g. two cleanings a year, one crown per tooth every 5–7 years). Extra visits are paid out of pocket.

Downgrade (LEAT)

Plans may reimburse the cost of the "least expensive alternative treatment" — e.g. a silver-colored filling rate on a white filling — leaving you the difference.

Work in progress

Treatment started before your effective date — or before a category's waiting period ends — is typically not covered. Timing the start date matters.

Annual maximum

At $2,000, the cap is mid-range. One implant case can exhaust it, after which you pay full price until the benefit year resets.

State variations

Premium, coinsurance and even plan availability differ by state. A figure that's right in one state may not apply to your ZIP — always confirm locally.

FAQ

Common questions about Delta PPO Premium

Does Delta PPO Premium cover implants?
The shelf snapshot shows implants at 50% coinsurance after a 12-month waiting period, but a missing-tooth clause may exclude teeth lost before the policy began. Confirm both the coinsurance and the missing-tooth language in your state's official document before relying on implant coverage. Compare implant plans →
How long is the waiting period for major work?
Major services, implants and orthodontics show a 12-month wait; basic services show 6 months; preventive care starts on the effective date. Waits can sometimes be reduced with proof of prior continuous coverage — ask the carrier.
What does it actually cost per month?
Premiums vary by state, ZIP and effective date, so we don't publish a single national price. The illustrative figure used in the cost example (~$75/mo) is for math only. Enter your ZIP above for area-specific pricing and availability.
Is my dentist in the Delta PPO network?
Possibly — but verify it for this exact product. Delta runs both PPO and Premier networks, which reimburse differently. Check the carrier's provider tool and call the office. Search Delta dentists →
Is this a CoverCapy endorsement of the plan?
