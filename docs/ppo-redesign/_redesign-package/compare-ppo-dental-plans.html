FAQ

Comparing PPO dental plans

How do I compare PPO dental plans?
Compare on the four levers that actually move cost: what each category covers (preventive, basic, major, implants, ortho), how long you wait before the plan pays, how much it pays (coinsurance), and the annual maximum. Use the table above to sort by premium or annual max and filter to plans that match your need, then pin two to four to see them head to head. Finish by verifying your exact plan free for your ZIP.
Which PPO dental plan has the highest annual maximum?
Of the eight plans we track, MetLife NCD Complete shows the highest annual maximum at ~$10,000, though it's still gathering reviews so its non-preventive coverage is under review. Among fully-reviewed plans, Mutual of Omaha Dental Preferred and Humana Extend 5000 both list a $5,000 maximum. A higher cap only helps if you actually have enough covered treatment to use it — sort by "Annual max ↓" above to rank all eight.
Which PPO dental plans cover implants?
Among the plans here, Ameritas PrimeStar Care Complete (20% day one), Delta Dental PPO Premium (50% after a 12-month wait), Mutual of Omaha Dental Preferred (50% after 12 months) and Humana Extend 5000 (50% after 6 months) list implant coverage. Use the "Covers implants" filter above to show only those, and note implant benefits are usually subject to a missing-tooth clause — verify your specific policy. See the implant guide for detail.
