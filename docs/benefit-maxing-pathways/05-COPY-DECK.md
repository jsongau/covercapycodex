## PATHWAY A: CARE NOW

**Eyebrow:** If it cannot wait

**Heading:** Start care now, and keep the monthly cost flat.

**Body:**
When treatment cannot wait, the plan does not need to cover everything. It needs to pay for the care that should happen now and give you an in-network rate on the rest. A low-cost plan with a small annual maximum does exactly that.

Take UHC Primary Dental as the example. For about 30 dollars a month it covers your cleaning in full and pays about half of a filling from day one, with no waiting period on basic care. You use the roughly 1,000 dollar maximum on the maintenance that is overdue, and for anything larger you move straight to monthly payments, sometimes at 0% APR for eligible patients.
*Plan figures are illustrative and modeled. Confirm terms with the carrier.*

This plan does not cover crowns or root canals. That is the trade for speed and a low premium. If you need a crown right now and want the plan to pay part of it, there is a better fit below.

**Bridge sub-section heading:** Need major work now and want coverage on it?

**Bridge body:**
Ameritas PrimeStar covers major work from day one with no waiting period. A root canal is covered at about 80 percent, a crown at about 20 percent in the first year, against a roughly 2,000 dollar maximum. For about 60 dollars a month, it pays something on the crown immediately, where the cheaper plan pays nothing. You still finance whatever is left, at 0% APR where available.
*Illustrative, modeled figures. Confirm with the carrier.*

## PATHWAY B: STRATEGIZE

**Eyebrow:** If you can plan ahead

**Heading:** A little patience, a much larger maximum.

**Body:**
When you have real work ahead, several crowns or a full treatment plan, the size of your annual maximum decides how much insurance you actually collect. A 1,000 dollar maximum is gone after one crown. A 5,000 dollar maximum carries a whole plan.

Humana Extend 5000 is the example. For about 100 dollars a month it gives you a roughly 5,000 dollar maximum, and it covers crowns and root canals at about half after a six-month wait. Activate it now, use the first months on preventive care, and let the waiting period run. After six months the major work begins with 5,000 dollars of coverage behind it, and you finance only the copay that remains, at 0% APR where available.
*Plan figures are illustrative and modeled. Confirm terms with the carrier.*

The waiting period is not a flaw here. It is the price of a maximum five times larger. For someone who can wait, it is worth thousands.

**Amplifier sub-section heading:** Work larger than one year of coverage?

**Amplifier body:**
A treatment plan bigger than a single year's maximum can often be phased across the reset. Stage one bills against this year's maximum, stage two against next year's. Two maximums, one course of care. Ask your dentist whether your plan can be sequenced this way.
