# 01: The Two Pathways
### The full logic of each path, the decision tree, when each wins
*Plan specs in file 03. Copy in file 05. Routing in file 06.*

---

## The pivot question

One question sorts every visitor: can the treatment wait.

- If it cannot wait, the goal is to start care now and keep the monthly cost manageable. That is Pathway A.
- If it can wait a few months, the goal is to put the largest possible maximum behind the work. That is Pathway B.

A second factor refines it: how much work is needed. A single filling does not justify a 100 dollar a month plan with a six-month wait. A mouth full of crowns does.

---

## Pathway A: Care now

**Who it is for**
Someone with pain, a broken tooth, an overdue filling, or no patience for a waiting period. Often they do not yet know the full scope, they just know they need a dentist soon.

**The move**
1. Get a low-cost plan with a small annual maximum that pays from day one. Headline: UHC Primary Dental, about 1,000 dollars maximum, cleaning in full, fillings at about half with no wait.
2. Use the plan on what it covers now. A cleaning, the fillings, the exam and x-rays.
3. For anything the small plan does not cover, or any larger treatment, go straight to 0% financing for eligible patients. The balance after insurance becomes a monthly payment.

**Why it works**
The small plan is not trying to cover a crown. It covers the maintenance that should happen now and gives an in-network rate on everything else. Financing carries the rest so care starts immediately instead of waiting for a richer plan to mature.

**The honest limit, stated on the page**
UHC Primary Dental does not cover crowns or root canals. So a crown on this pathway is financed, not reimbursed by the plan. That is the trade. Speed now, less insurance on major work. If the person needs major work now and wants the plan to pay part of it, the page offers the bridge plan below.

**Bridge plan for urgent major work: Ameritas PrimeStar**
About a 2,000 dollar maximum, and it covers major work from day one with no waiting period: a root canal at about 80 percent, a crown at about 20 percent in the first year. For the person who needs a crown now and refuses to wait six months, Ameritas pays something immediately while UHC pays nothing on it. Offer Ameritas as the "urgent and I want coverage on the crown" option inside Pathway A.

**Where it ends**
Estimate cost, then 0% financing for the balance, then find a dentist who can see you soon.

## Pathway B: Strategize

**Who it is for**
Someone with a real treatment plan ahead. Multiple crowns, a root canal and a crown on the same tooth, several restorations, the kind of work where the bill runs into the thousands. Crucially, they can wait a few months before the major work begins.

**The move**
1. Get a high-maximum plan now. Headline: Humana Extend 5000, a 5,000 dollar annual maximum, crowns and root canals covered at about half after a six-month wait, fillings at about 80 percent after three months.
2. Use the first months on preventive care, which is covered immediately, and let the waiting period run.
3. After the six-month wait, do the major work with a 5,000 dollar maximum behind it. A plan that pays half of several crowns up to 5,000 dollars covers far more than a 1,000 dollar plan ever could.
4. Finance only the remaining copay at 0%, so even the strategic path keeps the monthly cost flat.

**Why it works**
Six months of patience changes the math. A 1,000 dollar maximum is spent after one crown. A 5,000 dollar maximum carries a full treatment plan. The waiting period is the price of admission, and for someone who can wait, it is worth thousands.

**The two-maximum amplifier**
If the treatment plan is larger than even 5,000 dollars of coverage, phase it across the plan-year reset. Stage one bills against this year's maximum, stage two against next year's. Two maximums, one course of care. This pairs naturally with Pathway B because the person is already planning ahead.

**Where it ends**
Estimate cost across the higher-maximum plan, see the plan pay a large share, then 0% financing for the copay, then find a dentist who will sequence the work.

## The decision tree (for the hero and the audit handoff)

```
Need care now, cannot wait?
  -> YES
       Mostly maintenance (cleaning, fillings)?  -> UHC Primary Dental + 0% finance the rest   (Pathway A)
       Need a crown or root canal now?           -> Ameritas PrimeStar + 0% finance the rest    (Pathway A bridge)
  -> NO, I can wait a few months
       A lot of major work ahead?                -> Humana Extend 5000, wait the 6-month major wait, finance the copay  (Pathway B)
       Treatment larger than one year's maximum? -> Humana Extend 5000 + phase across two plan years   (Pathway B amplifier)
```

The cost estimator already compares all of these plans on real numbers, so the hub's job is to frame the choice and route into the estimator with the right plan in mind.

---

## What both pathways share

- Both start by estimating the number on the cost estimator.
- Both end at 0% financing for the balance or copay, so the monthly cost stays flat either way.
- Both finish at a dentist in the network.
- Both explain insurance terms with the glossary hover so the reader actually understands annual maximum, waiting period, coinsurance, and copay while reading.
