Quick answer

The fastest-starting individual dental plans turn coverage on within a day to a week, and the no-waiting-period plans, Ameritas PrimeStar Care Complete and Mutual of Omaha Dental Preferred, cover basic and major work from day one, though major work is paid at a reduced first-year rate that ramps up. Plans with longer 6-to-12-month waits, like Aetna Dental Direct and Delta Dental PPO Premium, usually let you waive those waits with proof of recent prior coverage. For the highest ceiling, NCD Complete by MetLife carries a $10,000 calendar-year maximum; for implants on a high maximum, Humana Extend 5000 and Mutual of Omaha Dental Preferred carry $5,000 maximums.

How fast it turns on

Activation speed

"Activation" is when coverage starts, not how fast a claim pays. Immediate-coverage options have become more common across the individual market in 2026, and the shelf splits into next-day, within-a-week, and first-of-the-month starts.

Next day
Ameritas PrimeStar can begin as soon as the next day; same-day access refers to the electronic policy and ID card, not the coverage start.
Day after
UnitedHealthcare Primary Dental can start as soon as the day after the application is received.
About 5 days
Delta Dental PPO Premium lists an earliest effective date about five days out; rules vary by Delta affiliate.
About a week
Humana Extend 5000 is typically active in about a week, with the card arriving shortly after.
1st of next month
Aetna Dental Direct, Guardian Premier 2.0 and NCD Complete by MetLife start on the first of the month after enrollment.
Varies
Mutual of Omaha does not publish a fixed effective-date rule, so confirm the start date at enrollment.

Read it as a start date, not a finish line. A fast start gets you a member ID quickly, but waiting periods still decide when each category of work is actually covered. The two are separate, and the next section is the one that usually matters more.

When work is covered

Waiting periods

Most plans fall into three buckets: preventive is almost always covered from day one, basic work waits zero to six months, and major work waits six to twelve. The exceptions are worth knowing.

No waiting period at all

Ameritas PrimeStar Care Complete and Mutual of Omaha Dental Preferred cover every category from day one, including major work. The trade is a reduced first-year major rate that ramps up in year two, rather than a wall you wait behind.

Day-one basic work

UnitedHealthcare Primary Dental, Guardian Premier 2.0, Ameritas and Mutual of Omaha all cover basic work like fillings without a wait. Guardian pays the highest day-one basic rate on the shelf at 85% in-network.

Waivable waits

Aetna Dental Direct waives its 6-month and 12-month waits if everyone enrolled had dental coverage within the past 90 days. Delta Dental and Humana waive most waits with proof of recent comparable coverage. Humana's implant wait, however, can never be waived.

State exceptions

Waits vary by state. Guardian caps applicable waits at six months in Washington and nine in Minnesota. New York removed waiting periods on most adult dental services for individual stand-alone marketplace plans, effective January 1, 2025, so local rules can differ from a plan's national terms.

If you are coming off an employer dental plan, the waiver rules are your friend. A plan with a 6-month or 12-month major wait can effectively start at zero if your prior coverage was recent enough, which is why Aetna and Delta suit people switching from a workplace plan.

The part people underestimate

Major work and implants

A short waiting period does not mean a generous first-year payout. Several no-wait plans pay major work at a low rate that climbs over the first two or three years, so when you do the work matters as much as whether it is covered.

Graduated major rates

NCD Complete by MetLife pays major at about 10% in year one, rising to 50% in year two and 60% after. Ameritas and Mutual of Omaha pay about 20% in year one rising to 50% in year two. Humana pays 50% in year one rising to 60%. Timing a crown or implant for year two often doubles what the plan pays.

Implant coverage and caps

Guardian covers implants after a 12-month wait with a $1,250 lifetime cap. Mutual of Omaha carries a $3,000 lifetime implant maximum. Humana caps implants at $2,000 a year and $4,000 lifetime. MetLife allows up to $3,000 per calendar year within its $10,000 maximum. Delta covers implants under its shared $2,000 maximum, and Ameritas covers them from day one, though that implant sub-cap is drawn from the annual maximum.

Two plans do not cover implants at all: UnitedHealthcare Primary Dental and Aetna Dental Direct. If an implant is on your horizon, start from the plans that carry a dedicated implant benefit and a high enough ceiling to use it. Delta and Guardian also apply a missing-tooth clause, so a tooth lost before the policy began may not qualify.

The yearly ceiling

Annual maximum

The annual maximum is the most a plan pays in a year; you cover everything above it. Across the individual market, maximums typically run $1,000 to $2,000, and about 73% of PPO members now carry a maximum of $1,500 or more, up from 67% the prior year.

The shelf, low to high

UnitedHealthcare $1,000, Aetna $1,250, Delta and Ameritas around $2,000 (Ameritas rises to $3,500 in year two), Guardian $3,000, and Humana and Mutual of Omaha $5,000. NCD Complete by MetLife sits well above the rest at $10,000.

What stretches a maximum

On several plans, preventive care does not count toward the maximum, so cleanings and exams do not eat into the ceiling you are saving for major work. Rollover of unused maximum and this preventive credit are the notable design trends the American Dental Association points to.

For a single implant or a crown, a modest maximum can still work if you stage the case across two calendar years and draw on two annual maximums. For multiple implants or a full-arch case, start from the high-ceiling plans so the maximum, not your timing, sets the pace.

Questions

Carrier Watch FAQ

Which dental plans have no waiting period?+
On the CoverCapy shelf, Ameritas PrimeStar Care Complete and Mutual of Omaha Dental Preferred have no waiting period on any category, including major work, which is paid at a reduced first-year rate that rises in year two. UnitedHealthcare Primary Dental and Guardian Premier 2.0 have no wait on preventive or basic.
How fast can dental insurance start?+
It varies by carrier. Ameritas PrimeStar coverage can begin as soon as the next day, and UnitedHealthcare Primary Dental can start as soon as the day after the application is received. Delta Dental PPO Premium lists an earliest effective date about five days out. Several carriers start on the first of the month after enrollment.
Which dental plan has the highest annual maximum?+
NCD Complete by MetLife has the highest annual maximum on the CoverCapy shelf at $10,000 per calendar year. Humana Extend 5000 and Mutual of Omaha Dental Preferred each offer a $5,000 calendar-year maximum, and Guardian Premier 2.0 carries a flat $3,000 maximum.
Which dental plans cover implants?+
Several do. Ameritas PrimeStar covers implants from day one at the major rate, NCD Complete by MetLife and Humana Extend 5000 cover them on a graduated schedule, Mutual of Omaha covers them with a $3,000 lifetime implant maximum, and Delta Dental and Guardian cover them after a 12-month wait. UnitedHealthcare Primary Dental and Aetna Dental Direct do not.
Can dental insurance waiting periods be waived?+
Often, yes. Aetna Dental Direct waives its 6-month and 12-month waits if everyone enrolled had dental coverage within the past 90 days. Delta Dental and Humana waive most waits with proof of recent prior comparable coverage. Humana's implant wait can never be waived, and waiver rules vary by carrier and state.
What is the cheapest dental plan on the CoverCapy shelf?+
UnitedHealthcare Primary Dental is the lowest-premium plan at an estimated $30 a month, in line with the roughly $27 to $30 average for individual dental. It covers cleanings at 100% and fillings at 50% from day one but does not cover major work, implants or orthodontics, and is for adults ages 64 and under.
Which dental plans cover orthodontics?+
Among featured plans, Delta Dental PPO Premium covers adults and children at 50% after a 12-month wait, and Guardian Premier 2.0 covers children under 19 after a 12-month wait. Most individual dental plans exclude adult orthodontics, so Delta's adult ortho benefit is unusual.
How is the Carrier Watch ranked?+
It is not a paid ranking. Every plan fact, premium, annual maximum, waiting period and coverage percentage comes from CoverCapy's single source of truth for each plan, verified against the carrier's own brochure and dated. We show estimates as estimates and tell you who each plan suits, rather than crowning a single winner.
