# CoverCapy Writing Style and Anti AI Copy Rules

## Purpose

Use this file as a binding writing standard for every CoverCapy page, component, CTA, link, modal, FAQ, prompt, and content revision.

The goal is not to sound polished for the sake of sounding polished. The goal is to sound clear, human, informed, calm, and specific.

CoverCapy copy should read like a knowledgeable dental office operator explaining something important to a patient in ordinary American English. It should not read like a corporate brochure, an AI generated landing page, a startup pitch, or an insurance company disclaimer.

When this file conflicts with a generic copywriting habit, follow this file.

---

# 1. Nonnegotiable Rules

## 1.1 Never use arrows in visible copy

Do not use any of these in CTA text, links, headings, cards, breadcrumbs, or body copy:

- `->`
- `→`
- `➜`
- `⟶`
- Decorative arrow characters after links
- Arrow based process labels

Wrong:

- Compare PPO Plans →
- Find a Dentist ->
- Coverage → Cost → Dentist
- Learn More ➜

Correct:

- Compare PPO plans
- Find a PPO dentist
- Compare coverage, estimate costs, and find a dentist
- See how it works

A functional chevron may be used inside a dropdown control or carousel control when it is necessary for interface behavior. Do not attach one decoratively to CTA or link text.

## 1.2 Avoid dashes in sentences when normal wording works

Do not use em dashes as sentence separators.

Do not use hyphens to create dramatic pauses.

Do not automatically hyphenate phrases when a natural open phrase is clear.

Wrong:

- Dental insurance can be confusing — but it does not have to be.
- We help patients compare plans - before they book.
- Built from dental-office experience.
- A patient-first approach to care.
- Find an in-network dentist — without calling around.

Correct:

- Dental insurance can be confusing. CoverCapy helps make it easier to understand.
- Compare plans before you book.
- Built from dental office experience.
- A patient first approach to dental coverage.
- Find a dentist in your PPO network without calling every office.

### Approved exceptions

Use a hyphen only when it is grammatically necessary, legally established, part of a proper name, part of a URL, or required for search clarity.

Examples that may sometimes remain:

- in-network
- out-of-network
- patient-first
- self-funded
- employer-sponsored
- ZIP-based
- URLs and file names

Even with approved exceptions, rewrite the sentence without the hyphen when the result sounds natural.

Preferred:

- a dentist in your network

Instead of:

- an in-network dentist

Preferred:

- dental office operations

Instead of:

- dental-office operations

## 1.3 Never use the word “chair” in marketing copy

The word `chair` is banned in patient facing and dentist facing brand copy.

This includes:

- dental chair
- chair time
- chairside
- before you sit in the chair
- on your side of the dental chair
- valuable chair time
- filling the chair
- empty chairs

Why: it sounds clinical, transactional, and overused. It also makes dental anxiety copy feel forced.

Use the real meaning instead.

Wrong:

- Spend less chair time explaining insurance.
- Patients feel calmer before they sit in the chair.
- Better use of chair time.

Correct:

- Spend less appointment time explaining insurance.
- Patients feel calmer when they understand the cost before their visit.
- Start the appointment with clearer insurance information.
- Give the care team more time to focus on treatment.

## 1.4 Do not write visible keyword stuffing

SEO language must sound like real language.

Do not publish:

- rows of repeated keywords
- awkward city and service combinations
- visible keyword banks
- meta keyword sections
- headings created only to repeat a phrase
- the same exact keyword in every paragraph
- location names inserted where a person would not naturally say them

Use the main search term clearly in the title, opening copy, one useful heading, supporting details, FAQ content, internal links, metadata, and structured data.

SEO should strengthen the page without making the page sound written for a crawler.

## 1.5 Do not invent trust

Never fabricate:

- testimonials
- patient quotes
- dentist quotes
- statistics
- ratings
- review counts
- partnerships
- accepted insurance plans
- dentist availability
- nearby offices
- cost savings
- exact treatment prices
- awards
- certifications
- response times
- guarantees

Use factual language and label estimates as estimates.

# 2. The CoverCapy Voice

## 2.1 Core tone

CoverCapy should sound:

- clear
- calm
- sharp
- warm
- knowledgeable
- patient centered
- direct
- trustworthy
- specific
- slightly playful only when the context supports it

The copy should feel confident without pretending to know more than it knows.

## 2.2 The human reference point

Write like an experienced dental front office operator who has handled:

- insurance verification
- PPO eligibility
- treatment estimates
- claims questions
- scheduling
- financing conversations
- network confusion
- patient concerns about cost

That experience should show through specific explanations, not through self important claims.

Good:

> Dental offices often need the exact PPO network name before they can confirm participation. The carrier name alone may not be enough.

Weak:

> Our experts leverage deep industry knowledge to help you navigate the complex dental insurance ecosystem.

## 2.3 Patient first, not anti dentist or anti insurer

CoverCapy may explain confusing systems, common gaps, and expensive mistakes.

Do not attack dentists, insurance carriers, brokers, or dental teams.

Wrong:

- Insurance companies make everything confusing on purpose.
- Dentists do not want you to know this.
- The system is designed to trick patients.
- Stop letting dental offices overcharge you.

Correct:

- PPO plans can be difficult to compare because the network, annual maximum, waiting period, and covered services all matter.
- A treatment estimate may change after the office confirms eligibility and reviews the procedure.
- The same procedure can cost very different amounts depending on the plan, network, office fee, and timing.

## 2.4 Insider knowledge must be specific

The word `insider` is useful only when the copy immediately explains what the knowledge is.

Good:

> Built from years of dental office operations, including eligibility checks, treatment estimates, claims, and patient scheduling.

Weak:

> Get the insider scoop on dental care.

Good:

> Learn what experienced PPO members check before choosing a plan.

Weak:

> Unlock insider secrets that transform your dental journey.

## 2.5 Premium is a design standard, not empty copy

The page may feel premium, refined, editorial, or concierge level through its design and precision.

Do not keep calling the service `premium`, `luxury`, or `exclusive` in public copy unless the statement has a real purpose.

Show quality through:

- exact wording
- strong information hierarchy
- useful comparisons
- calm pacing
- restrained claims
- thoughtful details

Do not announce quality with empty adjectives.

# 3. Sentence Style

## 3.1 Use ordinary American English

Prefer the words a patient or dental office would actually use.

Use:

- dentist
- dental office
- PPO plan
- insurance network
- annual maximum
- waiting period
- treatment estimate
- monthly payment
- office fee
- covered service
- verify
- compare
- check
- estimate
- find
- call
- book

Avoid replacing simple words with corporate language.

Wrong:

- facilitate
- utilize
- leverage
- optimize the patient journey
- activate a care pathway
- deliver a frictionless experience

Correct:

- help
- use
- compare
- check
- find
- explain
- call the office
- start with your plan

## 3.2 Keep one main idea in each sentence

Use short and medium length sentences.

Break up sentences that contain several promises, qualifications, and lists.

Wrong:

> CoverCapy helps patients navigate dental insurance, compare PPO plans, understand treatment estimates, discover providers, unlock financing, and make informed decisions with confidence.

Correct:

> Compare PPO plans in plain language. Estimate what treatment may cost. Then find a dentist who works with your network.

## 3.3 Avoid synthetic three part rhythm

Do not force every sentence into a three item pattern.

Triplets are acceptable when the three items are genuinely useful, such as:

- annual maximum, deductible, and waiting period
- coverage, cost, and dentist search

Do not turn every section into:

- simple, seamless, and stress free
- clear, confident, and convenient
- compare, connect, and thrive

## 3.4 Avoid empty contrast formulas

Do not rely on these AI sounding patterns:

- It is not X. It is Y.
- More than X. It is Y.
- Not just X, but Y.
- From X to Y.
- Whether you are X or Y.
- In a world where...
- When it comes to...
- At the end of the day...
- Now more than ever...
- The future of...
- This is where X comes in.

Rewrite with a direct statement.

Wrong:

> CoverCapy is not just a directory. It is your partner in the dental journey.

Correct:

> CoverCapy connects PPO plan guidance, treatment cost estimates, and dentist search tools in one place.

## 3.5 Do not stack fragments for drama

Wrong:

> Your plan. Your dentist. Your smile. Finally connected.

Correct:

> Connect your PPO plan with a dentist who works with your network.

Fragments may be used sparingly in a headline. They should not become the rhythm of an entire page.

## 3.6 Use contractions when they sound natural

Natural:

- you do not
- you can
- you are
- we help
- it may
- what it costs

Contractions such as `you’re`, `we’ll`, and `don’t` are acceptable when they make the sentence sound more conversational. Do not force them into legal, financial, or estimate disclosures.

## 3.7 Remove filler introductions

Delete openings such as:

- It is important to note that
- It is worth mentioning that
- In order to
- As you may already know
- The fact of the matter is
- At CoverCapy, we believe
- Our mission is to
- We are proud to
- We understand that

Start with the useful fact.

Wrong:

> It is important to note that annual maximums can affect your dental costs.

Correct:

> Your annual maximum limits how much the plan may pay during the benefit year.

`Transparent` should point to something visible, such as the estimate assumptions or ranking method.

`Discover` should usually become `find`, `compare`, `see`, or `read`.

`Journey` should almost always be removed.

- a clearer way to compare PPO plans, estimate treatment costs, and find a dentist
- a patient focused guide to dental coverage and care

Avoid:

- revolutionary dental ecosystem
- seamless dental care platform
- all in one oral wellness solution
- trusted partner for your smile journey
- next generation dental marketplace

## 5.2 Useful origin language

Preferred:

> CoverCapy was created from years of firsthand experience in dental office operations.

Preferred:

> The guidance is informed by eligibility checks, insurance verification, treatment estimates, claims follow up, scheduling, and patient conversations.

Preferred:

> Patients are often expected to understand network rules, annual maximums, waiting periods, and treatment estimates without anyone clearly explaining how the pieces connect.

Avoid:

> We are passionate about empowering patients through innovative dental solutions.

## 5.3 Cost language

Use:

- estimate
- estimated cost
- approximate price
- likely range
- may pay
- may cover
- based on the information entered
- final cost depends on the office, plan, eligibility, procedure, and treatment needs

Do not use:

- exact cost
- guaranteed savings
- you will pay
- the plan will cover
- save thousands
- lowest price
- no surprise bill

Preferred:

> Estimate what a crown may cost with insurance, without insurance, or with monthly financing.

Preferred:

> The result is an educational estimate, not a quote from a dental office or insurance carrier.

## 5.4 Network language

Preferred:

- dentist in your PPO network
- dentist who may participate in your network
- confirm insurance acceptance with the office
- verify the exact network before treatment
- carrier name and network name
- participation may vary by location and provider

Avoid:

- guaranteed in network
- accepts every plan from this carrier
- your insurance is confirmed
- exact network match, unless actually verified

## 5.5 Patient anxiety language

Keep it calm and practical.

Preferred:

> Dental care feels less overwhelming when the cost, coverage, and next step are easier to understand.

Preferred:

> Check the plan and likely cost before you book.

Avoid:

- conquer your dental fear
- eliminate dental anxiety
- a stress free dental journey
- do not let fear hold you back
- take the first brave step

Do not exploit pain, urgency, shame, or fear.

# 6. CTA and Link Rules

## 6.1 CTA structure

Use a direct verb and a clear object.

Strong CTA examples:

- Compare PPO plans
- Estimate treatment costs
- Find a PPO dentist
- Check insurance acceptance
- Verify my coverage
- See monthly payments
- View dentist profile
- Book appointment
- Call office
- Compare plan details
- Check waiting periods
- See covered services
- Read the cost guide
- View nearby dentists
- Claim profile
- Apply for accreditation

## 6.2 CTA length

Aim for two to five words.

Longer CTA text is acceptable only when the action genuinely needs more context.

Good:

- Find a dentist who takes my plan

Less useful:

- Click here to begin exploring all of the dental care options available to you

## 6.3 CTA formatting

- Use sentence case.
- Do not end with punctuation.
- Do not add an arrow.
- Do not add a decorative chevron.
- Do not write CTA text in all caps.
- Do not use vague motivational language.
- Keep the CTA aligned with the destination page.
- Do not promise an action that the button does not complete.

Wrong:

- GET STARTED NOW →
- Unlock My Benefits
- Begin My Journey
- Explore More
- Learn More
- Take Control Today
- Get Peace of Mind
- Discover Dentists

Correct:

- Compare PPO plans
- Check my coverage
- Find a PPO dentist
- Estimate my cost
- View plan details
- See financing options

## 6.4 Text link rules

Link text should name the destination or answer.

Wrong:

- Click here
- Read more
- Learn more
- Go here
- See this
- More information →

Correct:

- Compare PPO dental plans
- Read the crown cost guide
- See how annual maximums work
- Find dentists in Orange County
- Estimate dental financing payments

## 6.5 Do not make every line a CTA

A page should not feel like a wall of commands.

Use one primary CTA per decision point. A secondary CTA may support a clearly different next step.

Do not place three or four equal buttons next to each other unless the interface is an intentional selection tool.

# 7. Headlines, Eyebrows, and Supporting Copy

## 7.1 Headlines

A good CoverCapy headline should communicate one useful idea.

Preferred styles:

- Compare PPO dental plans without guessing
- Estimate what dental treatment may cost
- Find a dentist who works with your PPO network
- Already have dental insurance? Use it with a better plan
- The annual maximum is only one part of a PPO plan
- A $3,000 dental bill may look very different with the right plan and timing

Avoid vague slogans:

- Dental care reimagined
- Your smile, elevated
- The future of oral wellness
- Confidence starts here
- Better care. Better life.
- Your journey begins today

## 7.2 Eyebrows

Eyebrows should classify the section or create useful context.

Good:

- Inside dental insurance
- PPO plan comparison
- Treatment cost estimator
- Dental office insight
- Before you choose a plan
- What patients often miss
- For dental practices

Avoid decorative labels with no meaning:

- A better tomorrow
- Your next chapter
- The CoverCapy difference
- Designed for you
- Innovation meets care

## 7.3 Supporting copy

Keep hero supporting copy to about one or two short sentences.

The copy should answer:

1. What does this page help the visitor do?
2. Why does that matter?
3. What can the visitor do next?

Do not put every product, benefit, proof point, and keyword into the hero.

# 8. SEO Without AI Sounding Copy

## 8.1 Use exact nouns

Search engines and readers both benefit from clear nouns.

Use:

- PPO dental plan
- dental insurance
- annual maximum
- deductible
- waiting period
- in network dentist
- dental crown cost
- root canal cost
- dental financing
- monthly payment
- Orange County dentist
- Fountain Valley dentist

Do not replace useful nouns with vague terms such as:

- solution
- experience
- platform
- path
- offering
- ecosystem

## 8.2 Use local details naturally

Useful local copy may mention:

- city
- county
- neighborhood
- ZIP code
- nearby landmark
- university
- airport
- shopping center
- travel corridor
- nearby city

Do not force all local entities into one paragraph.

Good:

> This Fountain Valley page includes PPO dentists near Mile Square Regional Park, the 405, and nearby parts of Huntington Beach and Westminster.

Bad:

> Find the best Fountain Valley Orange County California PPO dentist near me for dental insurance, crowns, implants, and emergency dentistry.

## 8.3 Write answers that can be cited

For AI search visibility, use direct factual answers.

Preferred structure:

- give the answer first
- explain the important condition
- provide an example
- identify what must be verified
- link to the relevant tool or deeper guide

Example:

> A higher annual maximum does not automatically make a PPO dental plan better. The waiting period, network, deductible, covered percentage, and negotiated fee can change the real value of the plan. Compare those details before choosing based on the maximum alone.

## 8.4 Do not add FAQ filler

FAQ answers should usually be two or three useful sentences.

Do not repeat the question in the first sentence.

Do not hedge every line with `generally`, `typically`, `often`, and `may` unless the uncertainty is real.

Use precise qualification where needed.

# 9. Words Claude Commonly Misuses

## 9.1 “Providing” and “offering”

Do not begin paragraphs with:

- Providing patients with...
- Offering a comprehensive range...
- We offer...
- Our platform provides...

State the action.

Wrong:

> CoverCapy provides patients with access to helpful dental insurance resources.

Correct:

> Compare PPO plans, estimate treatment costs, and find dentists by location and network.

## 9.2 “Ensuring”

Do not claim that CoverCapy ensures an outcome it cannot control.

Wrong:

> Ensuring every patient receives affordable care.

Correct:

> Help patients compare the plan, network, likely cost, and dentist before they schedule.

## 9.3 “Trusted”

Do not call a person, practice, or resource trusted without evidence.

Wrong:

> Find a trusted dentist near you.

Better:

> Compare nearby dentists by rating, reviews, services, location, and PPO information.

## 9.4 “Expert”

Do not use `expert` as a self awarded label.

Show expertise through useful specifics.

Wrong:

> Get expert guidance.

Better:

> Compare the annual maximum, deductible, waiting period, network, and major service coverage.

## 9.5 “Personalized”

Only use it when the output actually changes based on user information.

Wrong:

> Receive personalized dental guidance.

Correct when true:

> See results based on your ZIP code, PPO network, treatment, and estimated office fee.

# 11. Content Accuracy Rules

## 11.1 CoverCapy identity

Do not describe CoverCapy as:

- an insurance carrier
- a licensed insurance broker, unless that becomes legally true and is documented
- a dental office
- a dentist
- a clinical provider
- a replacement for professional advice

Preferred:

> CoverCapy is a patient first dental insurance concierge and PPO dentist network.

Preferred:

> CoverCapy offers educational tools and dentist discovery resources. Insurance participation, benefits, and treatment costs should be confirmed with the dental office and carrier.

## 11.2 Cost estimates

All treatment estimates must remain approximate.

State the assumptions clearly.

Do not turn an estimate into a quote.

## 11.3 Dentist and network information

Dentist participation can change.

The office and carrier should confirm the exact network.

Do not imply that a carrier name alone proves network participation.

## 11.4 Medical language

Do not diagnose.

Do not recommend a procedure as medically necessary.

Do not promise outcomes.

Direct urgent clinical concerns to a licensed dental professional or emergency service when appropriate.

# 14. Fast Copy Formula

When Claude becomes too wordy, use this order:

1. State what the visitor can do.
2. Name the specific factors that matter.
3. Explain one important limitation.
4. Give one clear next step.

Example:

> Compare PPO dental plans by annual maximum, deductible, waiting period, network, and major service coverage. A larger maximum does not always mean the plan will cost less overall. Review the full plan details before choosing. Compare PPO plans.
