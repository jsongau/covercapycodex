The Doctor Behind The Practice

Meet Dr. David Abri, DDS

Dr. David Abri is a general, cosmetic, implant, oral facial pain, and TMJ dentist, a graduate of the USC School of Dentistry, and the lead doctor and founder of Abri Dental. He has practiced in California for more than two decades and has lived in West Los Angeles most of his life.

His working philosophy is short: take pride in the work, run the practice with honesty and integrity. Patients describe him as gentle and compassionate, which matters in the dentistry he focuses on. Cosmetic cases, implant cases, and chronic jaw pain all need a doctor who listens before drilling.

After USC, he stacked up certifications in advanced restorative dentistry, including training in newer implant and cosmetic techniques. That restorative base is why he plans whole cases instead of single procedures: a veneer plan accounts for the bite, an implant plan accounts for the neighbors and the gums, and a TMJ workup accounts for everything at once.

A dentist who understands restorations, implants, cosmetics, and the jaw joint can plan a whole mouth, not just a single tooth.

Outside the operatory he is active in organized dentistry: a member of the Los Angeles Dental Society, with legislative leadership and past board service at the Iranian American Dental Association (IADA). He founded the Abri Dental group, today ten offices across California, and personally leads the Beverly Hills and Burbank locations.

Restorative Depth

Dental implants in Burbank and Beverly Hills

Implant dentistry is one of Dr. Abri's core trainings, and the practice backs it with a rare in house surgical bench: two oral and maxillofacial surgeons and a board certified periodontist.

An implant is a small titanium post that replaces a missing tooth root, restored with a crown that works like a natural tooth. Done well, it is the closest dentistry gets to growing the tooth back. Done poorly, it fails in the bone or wrecks the bite. The difference is planning, and planning is the house specialty.

Single tooth implants

One missing tooth replaced without grinding down healthy neighbors for a bridge. Planned by Dr. Abri, placed with surgical support, restored to match.

Multiple and full arch

From several missing teeth to complete arch restoration, including All on 4 style cases with Dr. Dom Medina, an oral surgeon trained at UCLA Harbor Medical Center, with IV sedation available.

Implant site preparation

Gum and bone health decide whether an implant can succeed. Periodontist Dr. Sara Tanavoli handles grafting, recession, and tissue work in house.

Every implant plan here starts with honest answers: whether you are a candidate today, what site preparation might come first, what your PPO will actually contribute, and the real timeline from consult to final crown.

The Differentiator

TMJ and oral facial pain

This is what separates Dr. Abri from most Los Angeles dentists. Oral facial pain and TMJ treatment are named pillars of his practice and his formal training history, not marketing garnish.

The temporomandibular joints connect the lower jaw to the skull. When they get inflamed, displaced, or overloaded by a bad bite or grinding, the symptoms spread: clicking and popping, locking, morning soreness, ear pain, tension headaches, pain when chewing. Many patients bounce between physicians, ENTs, and dentists for years because the problem sits in the seam between specialties.

Jaw pain lives in the seam between medicine and dentistry. Dr. Abri practices in that seam.

A workup here looks at the whole system: joint sounds and range of motion, muscle tenderness, bite relationships, grinding wear, and the dental history that often started the cascade. Treatment planning is conservative first: bite evaluation and adjustment, custom oral appliances, habit management, and restorative coordination so the bite supports the joints instead of fighting them.

Where CoverCapy Comes In

PPO insurance and treatment planning

Most of the dentistry on this page, implants, crowns, Invisalign, TMJ appliances, lives in the part of a PPO plan where the fine print decides everything: annual maximums, waiting periods, and what counts as a major procedure.

A PPO plan lets you choose your dentist and shares costs on a fee schedule. Preventive care is covered generously, basic procedures at a middle tier, and major work like crowns and implants at a lower percentage, all capped by an annual maximum. Many plans also apply waiting periods before major work. Different plans fit different situations: some activate fast with little or no waiting, others trade a higher monthly cost for stronger major coverage. The right answer depends on the treatment you actually need.

That is the conversation to have before booking major treatment, and it is the problem CoverCapy was built to solve.
