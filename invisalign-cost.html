Invisalign typically costs about $3,000 to $8,000 in 2026, with express and lite cases at the low end and full comprehensive treatment around $5,000 to $8,000. The honest part

Does insurance cover Invisalign?

Sometimes. Orthodontics is a separate benefit with its own lifetime maximum, and the adult question is where plans differ most.

Separate lifetime maximum

Ortho usually has its own lifetime cap, often $1,000 to $2,500 (some premium plans to ~$3,500), separate from your annual maximum and not refreshed each year.

Often 25% to 50%

When a plan covers ortho, it typically pays about 25% to 50% of the cost, and Invisalign is usually covered on the same terms as metal braces.

Adults are often excluded

About 86% of plans limit full ortho benefits to patients under 18. Adult coverage is growing but still less common, so verify before you assume.

On the CoverCapy shelf, Delta Dental PPO Premium is unusual in covering adults and children at 50% after a 12-month wait, up to a $1,500 lifetime orthodontic maximum, and treats Invisalign the same as braces. Guardian Premier covers child orthodontics only, dependents under 19, up to a $1,500 lifetime maximum, and does not cover adult orthodontics. NCD Complete by MetLife does not cover orthodontics. Coverage varies by carrier, plan and state. Compare PPO plans for orthodontic coverage.

How to pay

HSA, FSA and financing

Invisalign is eligible for an HSA, FSA, HRA or limited-purpose FSA. You can apply pre-tax dollars to copays, your deductible, or the balance above a plan's lifetime orthodontic maximum, which meaningfully lowers the real cost. If your benefit year is ending, spending the funds before they lapse can be worth timing your start around.

To spread what remains, most orthodontic offices offer in-house monthly plans with a down payment. CareCredit, accepted by many providers, offers deferred-interest promotions over 6 to 24 months or fixed-APR terms up to 60 months for larger cases. A PPO ortho benefit that pays a share lowers the amount you finance in the first place.

Invisalign cost FAQ

How much does Invisalign cost in 2026?+
Invisalign typically costs about $3,000 to $8,000 in 2026. Express and Lite cases for minor correction sit at the low end, often $1,800 to $4,500, while full comprehensive treatment runs roughly $5,000 to $8,000, with most adults averaging near $5,700. Case complexity is the main driver.
Is Invisalign covered by insurance?+
Sometimes. When a PPO plan covers orthodontics, it usually pays about 25% to 50% of the cost up to a lifetime orthodontic maximum, often $1,000 to $2,500. Many plans limit full ortho benefits to dependents under 18, so adult coverage is less common, though it is growing.
Does Invisalign cost more than braces?+
Not always. For mild to moderate cases, Invisalign and metal braces often cost about the same. In 2026, braces average roughly $3,500 to $5,500, while Invisalign runs about $4,500 to $8,500, with the higher end reflecting complex full treatment.
Is there a lifetime maximum for orthodontics?+
Yes. Orthodontics usually has its own lifetime maximum, separate from your annual maximum, commonly $1,000 to $2,500, with some premium plans up to about $3,500. It is a once-per-person cap, not an amount that refreshes each year.
Can I use HSA or FSA for Invisalign?+
Yes. Invisalign is eligible for an HSA, FSA, HRA or limited-purpose FSA. You can apply pre-tax dollars to copays, the deductible, or the balance above your plan's lifetime orthodontic maximum, which lowers the real cost of treatment.
