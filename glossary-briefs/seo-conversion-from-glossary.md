## 3. Placement Psychology

### Where CTAs should appear within a term entry

**Anatomy of a term entry:**
1. Term headline (H2 or H3)
2. Plain-language definition (2-4 sentences)
3. "Why it matters" callout (1-2 sentences tying the term to a real buying decision)
4. Example (optional, for complex terms)
5. **CTA placement zone**

**Placement rules:**

**High-intent terms** (waiting period, in-network, PPO, deductible, annual maximum): CTA appears immediately after "Why it matters" and ALSO as a sticky nudge at scroll depth. These readers are close to a purchase decision.

**Mid-intent terms** (coinsurance, EOB, coordination of benefits): CTA appears only at the bottom of the full term entry, after the example. Readers need the full context before acting.

**Low-intent terms** (COBRA, qualifying life event, prior authorization): No inline CTA. These readers are in education mode. Instead, a soft micro-conversion CTA ("Save this comparison for later") is more appropriate.

**Rule of thumb:** Place a hard CTA only when the "Why it matters" block ends with a question the reader is actively asking. If the term is definitional or administrative, defer to a micro-conversion.

**Visual treatment:** CTAs should appear as a teal-bordered card (using `--teal-night` border, `--cream-card` background) that reads as a natural continuation of the entry rather than an ad unit. One button, one supporting line of copy. No icons other than a right-pointing arrow.

## 4. Page-Level Conversion Blocks

Place 2-3 larger blocks between term clusters to break up the reading flow and capture readers who are skimming rather than reading every term.

### Block 1: After Cluster A (Cost terms) — Plan Comparison Block

**Placement:** Between cost/pricing terms and network terms.

**Headline:** "The numbers are only half the story."
**Subhead:** "The other half is whether your dentist accepts the plan. CoverCapy shows you both."
**Primary CTA:** "Compare plans for my zip code" → /compare-ppo-dental-plans
**Secondary CTA:** "Find dentists near me" → /find-my-dentist
**Visual treatment:** Full-width block, `--teal-night` background, `--mint` accent on primary button. Cream text. Clean. No stock photos.

---

### Block 2: After Cluster C (Timing terms) — Match Quiz Block

**Placement:** Between timing/eligibility terms and benefits terms.

**Headline:** "Three questions. Your best plan match."
**Subhead:** "Tell us when you need coverage, what treatments you expect this year, and your monthly budget. We surface the plans that fit."
**Primary CTA:** "Take the match quiz" → /compare-ppo-dental-plans (quiz anchor)
**No secondary CTA** — keep this block single-focus.
**Visual treatment:** `--cream` background, centered layout, `--teal-700` headline. Feels like a concierge invitation, not a lead capture form.

---

### Block 3: After Cluster E (Plan management terms) — Dentist Search Block

**Placement:** At the end of the glossary, after all term clusters.

**Headline:** "You have done the research. Now find your dentist."

**Primary CTA:** "Search dentists near me" → /find-my-dentist

## 5. Micro-Conversions

For readers who are not ready to compare plans or search dentists, offer lower-commitment actions that keep them in the funnel.

### Micro-conversion 1: Save Plan Comparison

**Trigger:** Appears on all Cluster A (cost) and Cluster D (benefits) term entries.
**Action:** Opens a lightweight email capture modal — "Send yourself a plan comparison link."
**Copy:** "Not ready to decide today? Send yourself a link and come back when you are."
**Why it works:** Captures the email, creates a return visit, and signals plan-comparison intent without requiring immediate commitment.
**Form:** Single email field. One checkbox: "Also send me tips on getting the most from a PPO plan." No phone number, no name required.

---

### Micro-conversion 2: Plan Alert Subscription

**Trigger:** Appears on Cluster C (timing) terms, especially "open enrollment" and "qualifying life event."
**Action:** "Get an alert when open enrollment opens in your state."
**Copy:** "Enrollment windows move fast. Let us notify you when yours opens."
**Why it works:** Captures timing-sensitive buyers before they go elsewhere. The alert creates a high-intent reactivation touch.
**Form:** Email + zip code. No name. The zip code also enables geo-targeted dentist recommendations in the follow-up email.

---

### Micro-conversion 3: The 3-Question Match Quiz

**Trigger:** Available as a persistent element in the right-rail sidebar (desktop) or as an inline card after Block 2.
**Action:** A 3-question wizard that surfaces a recommended plan.
  - Q1: "When do you need coverage to start?" (This month / Next 1-3 months / Just exploring)
  - Q2: "What treatments are you expecting this year?" (Checkboxes: cleanings, fillings, crowns, root canals, orthodontia, implants)
  - Q3: "What is your monthly budget for a dental plan?" (Under $30 / $30-$60 / $60-$100 / Flexible)
**Output:** A soft recommendation ("Based on your answers, a mid-tier PPO may fit you well. Here are 3 options.") with a link to /compare-ppo-dental-plans pre-filtered.
**Why it works:** Transforms a passive reader into an active participant. Quiz completion rates tend to be 3 to 5 times higher than direct CTA clicks on informational pages.

## 6. Exit-Intent Strategy

**Trigger conditions:** Cursor moves toward browser chrome (desktop) OR user has scrolled to 80 percent depth and then scrolls back up rapidly (mobile).

**What to show:** A concierge-tone overlay, not a panic popup.

**Overlay design:** Centered modal, `--cream-card` background, `--teal-night` border. No countdown timer. No fake urgency.

**Headline (3 variants for A/B testing):**
- **A:** "Before you go: see if your dentist is already in our network."
- **B:** "One search before you go. Find a covered dentist near you."
- **C:** "Your plan comparison is one click away. No account needed."

**Body copy:** "CoverCapy shows you PPO plans that fit your dentist and your budget. Free to use, no signup required."

**Primary CTA:** "Find dentists near me" → /find-my-dentist  
**Secondary CTA (text link):** "Compare plans instead" → /compare-ppo-dental-plans  
**Dismiss link:** "I will come back later" (sets a 7-day cookie to suppress the modal)

**Tone rule:** The overlay must feel like a helpful concierge offering one more option, not a gate blocking the user from leaving. Never ask for an email in the exit modal. That ship has sailed.

## 7. Scroll Depth Triggers

Inline CTAs should be earned, not immediate. Readers who see a CTA too early bounce faster than readers who see none.

| Scroll depth | What triggers | Rationale |
|-------------|--------------|-----------|
| 0-20% | Nothing additional | Reader is orienting. Respect the silence. |
| 25% | Right-rail quiz card appears (desktop only) | Early enough to catch skimmers, late enough to avoid feeling pushy |
| 40% | First inline term CTA activates (Cluster A or B terms) | Reader has demonstrated interest in at least one term cluster |
| 60% | Page-level Block 1 (Plan Comparison Block) activates | Mid-page readers are engaged. This is the optimal conversion zone. |
| 75% | Page-level Block 2 (Match Quiz Block) activates | Deep readers are evaluating. A quiz CTA fits the methodical reader mindset. |
| 85% | Sticky bottom bar appears (both desktop and mobile) | Near-exit readers need a low-friction last call |
| 90-100% | Page-level Block 3 (Dentist Search Block) is visible | Terminal zone. Reader has consumed the full page. This is a high-intent signal. |

**Implementation note:** Scroll depth triggers should use IntersectionObserver on anchor elements within the page, not raw scroll event listeners. This avoids performance jank on long glossary pages.

## 8. Mobile CTA Behavior

Mobile readers scroll faster, have smaller patience windows, and are more likely to be mid-decision (looking something up while talking to a dentist's office). Design accordingly.

### Sticky Bottom Bar (mobile)

**Activates at:** 40% scroll depth  
**Height:** 56px fixed bottom  
**Content:** Single CTA only. Rotate based on scroll zone:
- Scroll 40-70%: "Find dentists near me" → /find-my-dentist
- Scroll 70-100%: "Compare PPO plans" → /compare-ppo-dental-plans

**Design:** `--teal-night` background. `--mint` text. Full-width button. No secondary option. No dismiss button on mobile (it auto-hides if the user taps the page content area).

**Do not show:** The sticky bar should not appear while a term entry accordion is open. It competes with the content and degrades reading experience.

### Inline Cards (mobile)

Replace the right-rail sidebar with inline cards inserted between term entries at:
- After the 3rd term in any cluster (not the 1st or 2nd — give the reader time to settle)
- After "Why it matters" blocks on high-intent terms only

**Card design:** Full-width, `--cream-card` background, `--teal-night` 1px border, single button, 12px padding. Reads like a natural content block, not an ad unit.

**Suppress duplicate cards:** If the reader has already tapped any CTA on the page, do not show inline cards for the rest of the session. One converted micro-action is enough. Do not pile on.

### No pop-ups on mobile

Exit-intent overlays do not work well on mobile (no cursor movement to detect). Replace with a bottom sheet that slides up after 90% scroll depth and 3 seconds of inactivity. Same copy as the desktop exit-intent overlay. Dismiss with a swipe down.

## 9. A/B Test Recommendations

Three specific, high-value tests to run on the glossary page.

### Test 1: CTA Placement Position Within Term Entries

**Hypothesis:** Placing the CTA after "Why it matters" (mid-entry) outperforms placing it at the bottom of the full term entry (post-example).

**Control:** CTA at the bottom of the term entry, after the example block.  
**Variant:** CTA immediately after "Why it matters," before the example block.

**Primary metric:** Click-through rate on inline CTAs  
**Secondary metric:** Scroll depth of users who click (to understand if they skipped the example or not)  
**Segment:** High-intent term clusters only (Clusters A and B)  
**Duration:** 3 weeks minimum, 1,000 unique visitors per variant  

**Expected finding:** Mid-entry placement will outperform bottom placement on cost and network terms because the reader's intent is highest immediately after understanding why the term matters. Example blocks are for comprehension, not motivation.

---

### Test 2: Primary CTA Copy: Action-Led vs. Outcome-Led

**Hypothesis:** Outcome-led copy ("See plans with no waiting period") converts better than action-led copy ("Compare plans now") because it names a specific benefit rather than describing a task.

**Control (action-led):**
- "Compare PPO plans"
- "Search dentists"
- "Find my plan"

**Variant (outcome-led):**
- "See plans with no waiting period" (on timing cluster)
- "Find dentists who accept your insurance" (on network cluster)
- "See which plan covers your next crown" (on benefits cluster)

**Primary metric:** Click-through rate  
**Secondary metric:** Conversion rate on /compare-ppo-dental-plans after arriving from the glossary  
**Segment:** All term clusters, rotated by cluster type  
**Duration:** 4 weeks, segmented by cluster to isolate which cluster sees the biggest lift

**Expected finding:** Outcome-led copy will outperform on high-specificity terms (waiting period, in-network, major services) but may underperform on broad terms (PPO, premium) where action-led copy is clearer.

---

### Test 3: Exit-Intent Overlay vs. No Exit-Intent Overlay

**Hypothesis:** An exit-intent overlay with a dentist search CTA reduces bounce rate and increases sessions-per-visit without meaningfully increasing frustration signals (rage clicks, immediate dismissals).

**Control:** No exit-intent overlay. Reader leaves freely.  
**Variant:** Exit-intent overlay with "Find dentists near me" primary CTA and "Compare plans instead" secondary text link.

**Primary metric:** Glossary-to-/find-my-dentist conversion rate  
**Secondary metric:** Rage click rate on the overlay (a frustration proxy)  
**Guard metric:** Glossary page bounce rate must not increase in the variant (this would indicate the overlay is backfiring)  
**Duration:** 3 weeks, desktop traffic only (mobile uses the bottom-sheet variant)

**Expected finding:** The overlay will lift /find-my-dentist arrivals by 8-15% on desktop. If rage click rate on the overlay exceeds 12%, the headline copy needs softening before scaling.

---

## Implementation Priority Order

1. **Highest priority:** Intent-matched inline CTAs on Cluster B (network terms) and Cluster C (timing terms). These readers are actively evaluating. High conversion ceiling.
2. **Second priority:** The 3-question match quiz as a micro-conversion. Captures the methodical readers who will not click a button without first feeling understood.
3. **Third priority:** Mobile sticky bottom bar. Mobile glossary traffic often comes from voice search on dental terms. Capture that intent at the moment it is highest.
4. **Fourth priority:** Page-level Block 1 (Plan Comparison Block) between cost and network clusters. This is the highest-visibility placement for passive readers.
5. **Defer:** Exit-intent overlay. Build last. Test cleanly. Do not assume it will work until Test 3 results are in.
