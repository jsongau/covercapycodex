## 6. H1 Recommendation

**Recommended H1:**
```
Every Dental Insurance Term Worth Knowing
```

**Why it should differ from the title tag:**

The title tag (`Dental Insurance Glossary | CoverCapy`) exists to rank and display in SERPs. The H1 exists to welcome the reader who already clicked. It should feel like the opening line of a good article, not a category label.

"Every Dental Insurance Term Worth Knowing" does three things the title tag cannot:
- "Every" signals completeness without sounding clinical
- "Worth Knowing" adds editorial judgment — CoverCapy as curator, not just a data dump
- It naturally seeds "dental insurance term" as a long-tail phrase distinct from "glossary"

H1 and title tag should share primary keyword intent but not be identical. Google treats them as separate signals; using both gives you two distinct phrase-match surfaces.

## 7. H2 Structure for the Page

These H2s organize the page into scannable sections that serve both readers and Google's passage-ranking algorithm.

```
H2: What Is a Dental Insurance Glossary? (And Why You Need One)
```
*Intro section.* Answers the implicit search intent for informational readers. Sets editorial authority early. Targets "what is dental insurance" adjacent queries.

---

```
H2: A-Z Dental Insurance Terms
```
*Main terms section.* Simple, scannable. If terms are broken into alphabetical subgroups, use H3s for each letter anchor (A, B, C, etc.). Keep H2 clean for crawling.

---

```
H2: PPO Plans vs. Other Dental Insurance Types
```
*Contextual explainer section.* Surfaces the "ppo dental terms" keyword cluster naturally within editorial content, not just a definition list. Positions CoverCapy as an advisor.

---

```

```
*Priority terms section (waiting period, annual maximum, deductible, coinsurance).* Human-interest framing. "Your bill" is concrete and anxiety-resolving — fits the concierge brand voice. Catches readers who are mid-crisis, not just researching.

---

```

```
*FAQ section.* Targets PAA (People Also Ask) boxes. Should include 4-6 questions in FAQ schema. Example questions: "What does PPO mean in dental insurance?", "What is a waiting period for dental insurance?", "What is an annual maximum in dental insurance?"

```

```
*CTA section.* H2 as conversion moment. "Ready to use your knowledge" rewards the reader's time on page. Ties glossary content to the product (dentist finder). "PPO dentist near you" is a high-intent local keyword.

## 8. Keyword Density Guidelines

These are target-range guidelines for natural, non-penalized keyword presence across a ~1,500-2,000 word glossary page.

### "PPO" (and "PPO dental" / "PPO plan" / "PPO network")
- **Target density:** 0.6-0.9% of total word count
- **At 1,800 words:** approximately 11-16 mentions
- **Placement:** H1 (optional), H2 (2 instances), intro paragraph (1), definition entries (naturally distributed), CTA section (1)
- **Watch for:** "PPO" appearing in every definition entry regardless of relevance. Only use it where it genuinely applies.

### "Dental insurance" (head term)
- **Target density:** 1.0-1.5% of total word count
- **At 1,800 words:** approximately 18-27 mentions
- **Placement:** Title, H1, meta, intro, multiple definition entries, FAQ section, CTA
- **This is the primary keyword and can tolerate higher frequency** because it is the topic itself, not a modifier. Google expects density proportional to topical relevance.
- **Avoid:** Starting 3 or more consecutive sentences with "Dental insurance." Vary sentence structure around the keyword.

### "Waiting period"
- **Target density:** 0.3-0.5% of total word count
- **At 1,800 words:** approximately 5-9 mentions
- **Placement:** Meta description (1), "Terms That Affect Your Bill" section (2-3), FAQ (1), glossary entry itself (1-2)
- **Do not force it into sections where it does not belong.** It has one dedicated glossary entry; additional mentions should be contextual (e.g., "many PPO plans have a 12-month waiting period for major services").

### General over-optimization signals to avoid
- Three or more exact-match keyword phrases in a single paragraph
- The same phrase appearing in every H2
- Keyword in alt text of an image that has no relevance to the term
- Keyword in the URL slug AND in every section header (URL slug is sufficient for one-time placement)

### Natural variation phrases (use interchangeably to avoid repetition)
| Primary phrase | Acceptable variations |
|---|---|
| dental insurance | dental plan, your dental coverage, dental benefits |
| PPO | preferred provider organization, PPO network, in-network PPO |
| waiting period | benefit waiting period, coverage waiting period, plan waiting period |
