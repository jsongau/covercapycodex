## 4. Missing Entities — Gaps vs Competitors

The following dental insurance entities appear consistently on competitor glossary pages (Delta Dental, Ameritas, NADP, Cigna, eAssist) but are absent or underrepresented from CoverCapy's current context. Adding them closes topical authority gaps.

**High-priority missing entities:**

1. **Benefit Period** — the 12-month window that governs deductible resets and annual maximums. Distinct from "plan year" (which may not be calendar year). Competitors define both.

2. **Rollover Benefits** — newer plan feature where unused annual maximum (up to a threshold) carries forward. Becoming a standard differentiator in plan comparison content. Not present in most glossaries yet — CoverCapy can own this term.

3. **Non-covered Service (NCS)** — procedures the plan excludes entirely (e.g., cosmetic whitening, sleep apnea devices). Patients confuse "not covered at 100%" with "not covered at all." High patient confusion signal.

4. **Table of Allowances** — fee schedule used by indemnity plans and some out-of-network PPO reimbursements. Competitor glossaries include it; CoverCapy should define it briefly to cover indemnity plan context.

5. **Dual Coverage / Birthday Rule** — coordination of benefits rule determining which plan is primary when a patient has two dental plans. Families with two working parents frequently search this. High-volume missed opportunity.

6. **Carve-out** — when a dental plan excludes specific procedures that are instead covered under a medical plan (e.g., sleep apnea, TMJ). Creates billing confusion; competitors address it.

7. **Dependent Age Limit** — the age at which a child is no longer covered as a dependent (typically 26 under ACA for health, but dental varies). A common parent-concern search.

8. **Frequency Limitation** — how often an insurer covers a procedure (e.g., cleanings twice per year, full-mouth X-rays every 3 years). Patients experience claim denials on this and search for explanations.

9. **Least Expensive Professionally Acceptable Alternative (LEPAA)** — when an insurer covers only the least expensive treatment option (e.g., amalgam filling instead of composite). A major patient grievance that generates search volume. Only advanced glossaries define it.

10. **Orthodontic Lifetime Maximum** — separate from the annual maximum; applies to braces and Invisalign. Frequently searched by parents. Delta Dental's Premier plan includes $1,500 lifetime orthodontic max — mentioning this is both useful and a natural carrier reference.

**Medium-priority missing entities:**
- Assignment of Benefits (AOB) — patient directs insurer to pay dentist directly
- Eligible Expense — what the carrier deems payable
- Claim Form (ADA Dental Claim Form) — the standard form dentists submit
- Exclusions vs Limitations — the distinction patients confuse
- Managed Care — umbrella term patients encounter in employer plan language

## 5. Related Search Integration — Natural Incorporation Strategy

The goal is to answer related searches within the glossary copy without forcing keyword repetition. Each related search is a question Google has identified users asking — answer it directly in the prose.

**Strategy: Answer-first structure per entry**

Each glossary term entry should follow this pattern:
1. One-sentence plain-English answer to the most common related search
2. The formal definition
3. A practical example with dollar amounts
4. A natural CTA connecting to CoverCapy's service

**Implementation examples:**

For "Annual Maximum" entry — integrate related search "what happens when you hit your dental annual maximum":
> Once you reach your annual maximum, your plan stops paying for the rest of the benefit year. For most PPO plans, that limit is between $1,000 and $2,000 — a threshold set 40 years ago that has not kept pace with the cost of crowns or bridges. If you are approaching your maximum mid-year, timing matters: scheduling major work in January resets the clock.

For "Waiting Period" entry — integrate related search "dental insurance no waiting period for major work":
> Most PPO plans impose a six-month to one-year waiting period before covering crowns, bridges, or dentures. If you need major work now, look for plans that waive waiting periods — or verify whether your prior coverage qualifies for a waiver (no coverage gap longer than 63 days typically required).

For "In-Network / Out-of-Network" entry — integrate related search "can I see any dentist with PPO insurance":
> Yes. With a PPO plan, you can see any licensed dentist — in-network or out-of-network. You pay less when you stay in-network because your carrier has pre-negotiated rates with those dentists. Out-of-network, your carrier pays based on its UCR rate for your area, and the dentist can bill you for the difference.

**Copy rules for integration:**
- Lead with the question (implicit or explicit), then answer it in the first clause
- Never repeat a phrase more than twice per entry
- Use the exact related search phrase once, naturally, within the first 100 words of the entry
- Do not chain related searches back-to-back — intersperse with original explanatory prose
- Avoid lists of keywords disguised as sentences; write for a reader, not a crawler

## 6. Knowledge Graph Alignment — Making CoverCapy a Recognized Entity

Google's knowledge graph identifies organizations via structured data, external citations, and consistent name/URL/description signals across the web. CoverCapy is currently below the threshold for entity recognition as a PPO dental insurance platform. The following steps build that recognition.

### 6a. Organization Schema (immediate — on-page)

Every page on covercapy.com should include an `Organization` schema block in the `<head>`. The glossary page in particular should include:

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "Organization",
  "name": "CoverCapy",
  "url": "https://covercapy.com",
  "description": "CoverCapy is a PPO dental insurance concierge platform helping patients find in-network dentists and verify insurance coverage.",
  "knowsAbout": [
    "PPO dental insurance",
    "dental insurance verification",
    "Delta Dental PPO",
    "dental insurance annual maximum",
    "in-network dentists"
  ],
  "sameAs": [
    "https://twitter.com/covercapy",
    "https://www.linkedin.com/company/covercapy"
  ]
}
```

The `knowsAbout` array is the key signal — it explicitly tells Google what entities CoverCapy is authoritative about.

### 6b. DefinedTerm + DefinedTermSet Schema (glossary page)

The glossary page should use `DefinedTermSet` + `DefinedTerm` schema so Google can surface definitions in rich results:

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "DefinedTermSet",
  "name": "Dental Insurance Glossary",
  "description": "Plain-English definitions of PPO dental insurance terms, from deductibles to waiting periods.",
  "url": "https://covercapy.com/dental-insurance-glossary/",
  "hasDefinedTerm": [
    {
      "@type": "DefinedTerm",
      "name": "Dental PPO",
      "description": "A Preferred Provider Organization dental plan that lets you see any licensed dentist, with reduced costs when you visit an in-network provider.",
      "inDefinedTermSet": "https://covercapy.com/dental-insurance-glossary/"
    }
  ]
}
```

### 6c. Wikipedia / Wikidata Presence Strategy

CoverCapy does not yet have a Wikipedia article. Building one requires:
1. Demonstrable notability: at minimum 3 independent, reliable, secondary sources that mention CoverCapy by name (not press releases)
2. Press coverage in outlets like TechCrunch, Forbes, Modern Dentistry, or dental trade publications
3. A Wikidata entry can be created without Wikipedia notability requirements — this is a faster path

**Priority action:** Pursue 2–3 editorial placements in dental industry publications (Dental Economics, Dentistry Today, or ADA News) that describe CoverCapy as a PPO concierge platform. These citations create the external validation Google's knowledge graph requires.

### 6d. Citation Strategy

Google assigns entity authority through citation networks. For PPO dental insurance authority:

- **Cite the ADA** when referencing CDT codes — "as defined by the American Dental Association"
- **Cite NADP** for industry statistics — "according to the National Association of Dental Plans"
- **Link to Delta Dental's glossary** on a non-competing definition (e.g., CDT codes), establishing CoverCapy in the same citation neighborhood as Delta Dental
- **Get cited by dental offices**: encourage T5 dentist profile pages to be referenced by the practices they feature — each external link to covercapy.com from a dental practice website is a co-citation signal

### 6e. Consistent NAP + Entity Signals

Across all pages and any third-party listings:
- Always use "CoverCapy" (capital C, capital C, no space)
- Consistent description: "PPO dental insurance concierge — find a dentist and verify your coverage"
- Register on Google Business Profile as an "Insurance Agency" or "Financial Service"
- Submit covercapy.com to dental industry directories (NADP member directory, ADA vendor marketplace)

## 7. Topical Authority Score

**Current CoverCapy PPO Dental Insurance Topical Authority: 3.5 / 10**

### Scoring rationale:

| Signal | Score | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Depth of glossary content | 2/10 | Glossary page not yet indexed; no definitions published |
| Backlink profile (dental topic) | 3/10 | Thin; no authoritative dental publications link to covercapy.com |
| Entity recognition (knowledge graph) | 2/10 | No Wikipedia presence; no Wikidata entry; schema incomplete |
| Content breadth (pillar + spoke) | 5/10 | Landing pages exist (/no-waiting-period/, /self-employed/, /between-jobs/); core pillar thin |
| Internal link architecture | 4/10 | SEO pages exist but glossary is not yet linked from them |
| Schema implementation | 4/10 | T5 pages have LocalBusiness schema; no Organization or DefinedTermSet |
| Named entity mentions in copy | 3/10 | Carriers mentioned in modals but not in crawlable glossary copy |
| Competitor gap | 4/10 | Delta Dental, Cigna, Humana, and Ameritas all have indexed glossaries with 50–200 terms |

### The 3 biggest gaps to address:

**Gap 1: No published, indexed glossary content (highest impact)**
Competitor glossaries from Delta Dental, Ameritas, and NADP have 50–200 defined terms, indexed and linking internally. CoverCapy's glossary page returns empty. This single gap is the largest authority deficit. A 30-term glossary published and linked from every T5 dentist page would move topical authority from 3.5 to approximately 5.5 within 90 days of indexing.

**Gap 2: Zero external citations from dental industry sources**
Google's Helpful Content system evaluates whether a site is the source that content creators in a niche cite. No dental publication, dental billing service, or dental practice management platform currently cites covercapy.com. Three to five editorial placements in Dental Economics, Dentistry Today, or ADA News would establish CoverCapy as a citable reference in the PPO insurance space — the prerequisite for moving from a content site to an authority entity.

**Gap 3: No Organization or DefinedTerm schema across the site**
CoverCapy's T5 pages have `LocalBusiness` schema for dentists, but CoverCapy itself has no `Organization` schema, no `knowsAbout` declarations, and no `DefinedTermSet` on the glossary page. These are the fastest implementation wins — two schema blocks added to the homepage and glossary page respectively, achievable in a single generator update. Google uses `knowsAbout` to map an organization to topic clusters in its knowledge graph. Without it, CoverCapy is invisible as a topical entity even if the content itself is excellent.
