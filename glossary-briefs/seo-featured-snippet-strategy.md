## 2. OPTIMAL DEFINITION STRUCTURE

### The Winning Sentence Formula

Google's definition box algorithm extracts the **first complete, standalone sentence** that answers the query directly. The formula that consistently wins:

```
[Term] is [article] [category noun] that [function/action], [qualifying clause that adds real-world context].
```

**Examples using this formula:**

- **PPO:** "A PPO (Preferred Provider Organization) is a type of dental insurance plan that gives you access to a network of pre-negotiated dentists, letting you see any licensed provider while paying less when you stay in-network."
- **Waiting period:** "A dental insurance waiting period is the length of time you must be enrolled before your plan covers a specific category of care, typically ranging from 0 months for preventive cleanings to 12 months for major work like crowns."
- **Annual maximum:** "An annual maximum is the highest dollar amount your dental insurance plan will pay toward covered procedures within a single benefit year, after which all remaining costs become your responsibility."

### Rules for the opening sentence

1. Lead with the exact term as Google spelled it in the query — do not rephrase (e.g., use "PPO" not "Preferred Provider Organization" in the H2).
2. Use "is" or "are" as the connector verb — never "refers to," "can be defined as," or "means."
3. Include the category noun immediately ("a type of dental insurance plan," "a policy provision," "the dollar limit").
4. Add a concrete consequence or real-world implication in the same sentence or the next one.
5. Keep the opening sentence to 30–45 words. Google rarely extracts sentences over 50 words into a definition box.

### H2 / Entry Heading Format

Use the exact query phrase as the heading:

```html
<h2>What is a PPO in dental insurance?</h2>
```

Not:
```html
<h2>PPO Plans Explained</h2>
```

This matches the "what is" query pattern that triggers definition boxes most reliably.

## 3. LENGTH GUIDELINES

Google featured snippet extraction is governed by visual space in the SERP, not word count per se. Target the ranges below for the passage that will be extracted:

| Snippet type | Ideal length for extraction | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Definition (paragraph) box | **40–55 words** for the extractable passage | Google clips at ~58 words. Write a tight 2-sentence block. |
| Bullet list | **4–7 bullets**, each 8–12 words | More than 7 items causes Google to add "More items..." which reduces CTR |
| Table | **2–5 columns, up to 8 rows** visible | Tables over 5 columns collapse; keep to: Term, Definition, Typical Range |
| Ordered list (how-to) | **5–8 numbered steps** | Step length: 6–10 words each |

### Full entry structure (beyond the snippet)

After the snippet-optimized opening block, each glossary entry should continue with:
- **Why it matters to you** — 1 paragraph, 60–100 words, patient-focused
- **CoverCapy angle** — 1–2 sentences connecting the term to CoverCapy's value proposition
- **Quick stat or example** — a number, percentage, or dollar figure (increases E-E-A-T signal)
- **Related terms** — 3–5 crosslinks to other glossary entries

Total entry length target: **350–500 words** per term.

## 5. TEN COPY TEMPLATES FOR SNIPPET EXTRACTION

These are reusable sentence structures. Swap in the specific term where indicated. Each template is optimized for the definition box format.

**Template 1 — Standard Definition**
> "[TERM] is [ARTICLE + CATEGORY NOUN] that [FUNCTION], [QUALIFYING CLAUSE]."

Example: "A deductible is the fixed dollar amount you pay out of pocket before your dental insurance begins covering a percentage of your treatment costs."

---

**Template 2 — "The [X] in dental insurance"**
> "The [TERM] in dental insurance [IS/REFERS TO] [DIRECT OBJECT], which means [PATIENT IMPLICATION]."

Example: "The annual maximum in dental insurance is the highest total benefit your plan will pay in a calendar year, which means any care beyond that limit comes directly out of your pocket."

---

**Template 3 — Patient-action framing**
> "When your dental insurance has a [TERM], it means [PLAIN ENGLISH EXPLANATION]."

Example: "When your dental insurance has a waiting period, it means you must be enrolled for a set number of months before the plan will pay for certain categories of care."

---

**Template 4 — Comparison anchoring**
> "[TERM] is [DEFINITION] — different from [RELATED TERM], which [CONTRAST]."

Example: "Coinsurance is the percentage of a dental bill you pay after your deductible is met — different from a copay, which is a flat fee per visit regardless of the total cost."

---

**Template 5 — "Think of it as" analogy**
> "Think of the [TERM] as [ANALOGY]: [EXPLANATION IN PATIENT TERMS]."

Example: "Think of the allowed amount as the price ceiling your insurer set for a procedure: your plan pays its percentage of that ceiling, not of what the dentist actually charges."

---

**Template 6 — If/then consequence**
> "If your dental plan includes a [TERM], [CONSEQUENCE FOR PATIENT] — [ADDITIONAL CONTEXT]."

Example: "If your dental plan includes a missing tooth clause, any tooth lost before your coverage start date will not be covered for replacement — even if you need a bridge or implant today."

---

**Template 7 — Number-lead definition**
> "[TERM] typically [NUMBER RANGE], [EXPLANATION OF WHAT DRIVES VARIATION]."

Example: "Dental insurance waiting periods typically range from zero to twelve months, with preventive care usually available on day one and major procedures requiring the longest wait."

---

**Template 8 — Outcome-first framing**
> "To avoid [NEGATIVE OUTCOME], understand your plan's [TERM]: [SHORT DEFINITION]."

Example: "To avoid surprise bills, understand your plan's balance billing rules: balance billing is when an out-of-network dentist charges you the gap between their fee and what your insurer pays."

---

**Template 9 — One-sentence + one-sentence elaboration (two-sentence block)**
> "[TERM] is [CORE DEFINITION]. [SECOND SENTENCE: example, typical range, or CoverCapy context]."

Example: "CDT codes are the five-character procedure codes — starting with the letter D — that dentists use to describe treatments on insurance claims. When you see 'D1110' on your Explanation of Benefits, that code means a routine adult cleaning was billed."

---

**Template 10 — CoverCapy-branded definition**
> "At CoverCapy, [TERM] is one of the first things we check [PATIENT BENEFIT STATEMENT]."

Use sparingly — only where the term directly maps to CoverCapy's verification service (PPO, effective date, day-1 activation, in-network dentist).

Example: "At CoverCapy, the effective date is one of the first things we verify on your behalf, so you know exactly which day your coverage is active before you book your appointment."

## 7. SCHEMA MARKUP REQUIREMENTS

### Primary schema for glossary entries: `DefinedTerm`

Google formally recognizes `DefinedTerm` within a `DefinedTermSet` for glossary pages. This is the single highest-leverage schema for this use case.

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "DefinedTermSet",
  "name": "Dental Insurance Glossary",
  "url": "https://covercapy.com/dental-insurance-glossary/",
  "hasDefinedTerm": [
    {
      "@type": "DefinedTerm",
      "name": "PPO",
      "description": "A PPO (Preferred Provider Organization) is a type of dental insurance plan that gives you access to a network of pre-negotiated dentists, letting you see any licensed provider while paying less when you stay in-network.",
      "inDefinedTermSet": "https://covercapy.com/dental-insurance-glossary/"
    },
    {
      "@type": "DefinedTerm",
      "name": "Waiting Period",
      "description": "A dental insurance waiting period is the length of time you must be enrolled before your plan covers a specific category of care, typically ranging from 0 months for preventive cleanings to 12 months for major work.",
      "inDefinedTermSet": "https://covercapy.com/dental-insurance-glossary/"
    }
    // ... repeat for each term
  ]
}
```

### Secondary schema: `FAQPage`

Add `FAQPage` schema for any term where the entry includes a Q&A block. This unlocks the FAQ accordion in SERPs (expands directly in search results).

High-priority FAQ schema targets (because users phrase these as questions):
- "Does dental insurance cover implants?"
- "What is a waiting period in dental insurance?"
- "Can my dentist balance bill me?"
- "What is the missing tooth clause?"
- "When does my dental insurance become effective?"

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "FAQPage",
  "mainEntity": [
    {
      "@type": "Question",
      "name": "Does dental insurance cover implants?",
      "acceptedAnswer": {
        "@type": "Answer",
        "text": "Most dental PPO plans do not cover implants by default, or cover them at 50% after a 12-month waiting period if the plan includes implant benefits. Plans like Guardian and Cigna offer implant riders. CoverCapy verifies your specific plan's implant coverage before you book."
      }
    }
  ]
}
```

### Tertiary schema: `BreadcrumbList`

Required on the glossary page and on any per-term sub-pages:

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "BreadcrumbList",
  "itemListElement": [
    {"@type": "ListItem", "position": 1, "name": "Home", "item": "https://covercapy.com/"},
    {"@type": "ListItem", "position": 2, "name": "Dental Insurance Glossary", "item": "https://covercapy.com/dental-insurance-glossary/"}
  ]
}
```

### Technical requirements for all schema on this page

1. Place all schema in a single `<script type="application/ld+json">` block in the `<head>` — do not split across multiple script tags unless required for readability.
2. Validate using Google's Rich Results Test before deploying: https://search.google.com/test/rich-results
3. `description` field in `DefinedTerm` must be 40–55 words for optimal extraction — the same text used in the visible HTML definition block.
4. Do not use `sameAs` on `DefinedTerm` unless linking to an authoritative external definition (e.g., ADA.org for CDT codes).
5. `FAQPage` and `DefinedTerm` can coexist on the same page without conflict.

## 10. COPY RULES FOR ALL GLOSSARY CONTENT

Aligned with CoverCapy design system constraints from CLAUDE.md:

- No em-dashes in copy — use commas, colons, or sentence breaks
- No roman numerals in lists — use 1, 2, 3
- No countdown timers or urgency manipulation in copy
- Practice names in `<em>` when appearing in examples (modal context — not applicable here)
- CSS tokens must be used for any inline styling: `--teal-700` for links, `--mint` for accent elements
- Font: headlines in Fraunces, body/definitions in Inter Tight
- Tone: concierge-informed, not clinical; "you" not "members" or "insureds"
