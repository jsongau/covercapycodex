# CoverCapy Link Building Strategy: Dental Insurance Content Hub

## Overview

CoverCapy's content hub — including the dental insurance glossary, PPO comparison page, and gap-coverage guides — sits in a category where links are genuinely hard to earn and easy to waste budget chasing. This document maps the realistic link landscape, identifies 20 specific targets, and outlines the PR angles most likely to generate editorial coverage rather than paid placements.

The core asset that separates CoverCapy from every other dental insurance site is a single sentence: no carrier pays for a higher score. That independence is the leverage point for every outreach angle below.

---

## Why Dental Insurance Content Earns Links

Dental insurance is chronically underserved in personal finance media. Most people understand health insurance basics but cannot explain the difference between a PPO deductible and an annual maximum. Writers covering benefits, HR, open enrollment, and consumer finance regularly need to explain these concepts and have almost no trustworthy, readable sources to cite. A well-built dental insurance glossary is the kind of page that earns passive links for years because it solves a recurring editorial problem.

"Dental insurance between jobs" content earns links from an entirely different source: career, layoff, and job transition content. COBRA, ACA marketplace timing, and gap coverage are perennial topics for personal finance bloggers, HR publications, and outplacement services. A guide that is accurate, scannable, and neutral in tone will be cited whenever a writer covers job loss or career transitions.

The comparison page earns links primarily from "best dental insurance" and open enrollment roundups, where writers prefer to cite independent third parties rather than the carriers themselves.

## 20 Specific Link Targets and Outreach Angles

### Personal Finance and Benefits Publications

**1. NerdWallet (nerdwallet.com)**
Target their dental insurance explainer articles and "best dental insurance" reviews. Angle: CoverCapy's glossary defines terms like "waiting period," "missing tooth clause," and "PPO vs HMO" in plain language that NerdWallet writers currently lack a clean citation source for. Pitch the glossary as a reference, not CoverCapy as a product.

**2. Investopedia (investopedia.com)**
Their dental insurance topic cluster is thin. Angle: pitch CoverCapy's "dental insurance between jobs" guide as a primary source for their COBRA and gap coverage articles. Investopedia writers respond to data and specificity, so lead with the 63-day election window detail and the ACA marketplace SEP timing.

**3. The Balance Money (thebalancemoney.com)**
Covers benefits literacy for general consumers. Angle: the PPO comparison page methodology, specifically the independence claim, is a strong pitch for their "how to choose dental insurance" content. Frame it as the Wirecutter model applied to dental plans.

**4. Kiplinger Personal Finance (kiplinger.com)**
Skews older, retirement-focused readership. Angle: Medicare does not cover dental, which means this audience has acute dental insurance anxiety. A CoverCapy glossary link fits naturally in their Medicare supplement and retirement benefits coverage.

**5. Clark Howard (clark.com)**
Consumer advocacy site with strong reader trust. Angle: the independent scoring model aligns directly with Clark Howard's "no hidden fees, no kickbacks" brand positioning. A direct outreach to their editorial team framing CoverCapy as "the only dental search where no carrier pays for placement" is a natural fit.

### State-Specific and Local Targets

**17. State Insurance Commissioner Websites**
Many state insurance department websites link to independent consumer resources. Angle: submit CoverCapy's glossary and comparison methodology to state insurance departments in California, Texas, and Florida as a consumer education resource. These links are high-authority and low-competition.

**18. Local Business Journals (bizjournals.com network)**
The American City Business Journals network covers local business news. Angle: each market CoverCapy covers (Los Angeles, Houston, Phoenix, etc.) has a local business journal. A data pitch, such as "which PPO networks have the most in-network dentists in [city]," earns local coverage and a link.

### HARO and PR Angles

**19. HARO / Connectively Queries**
Monitor queries in the personal finance, health, and HR categories. Respond to any query touching dental insurance, open enrollment, COBRA, gap coverage, or PPO networks. The independence claim is the lead every time: "CoverCapy is the only dental search platform where no carrier pays for a higher ranking." A single sentence that is verifiably true and genuinely newsworthy cuts through HARO noise.

**20. Healthcare.gov and Benefits.gov Partner Links**
Federal consumer information sites periodically update their resource lists. CoverCapy's glossary, if submitted through the appropriate agency contact channels, can earn a link from healthcare.gov's dental coverage explainer pages. This is a long-game target, but the authority value is significant.

## How to Leverage the Independence Claim

The "no carrier pays for a higher score" positioning is the single most important differentiator for link building. Here is how to deploy it across outreach:

**In cold email subject lines:** "The only dental insurance comparison where no carrier buys placement"

**In HARO pitches:** Open every response with the independence claim before any other credential. Journalists covering consumer advocacy and insurance are acutely aware of how most comparison sites work. Calling it out directly earns credibility immediately.

**In contributed content pitches:** Frame CoverCapy as applying the Wirecutter / Consumer Reports model to dental insurance. Both of those brands are universally understood by editors as shorthand for "genuinely independent." The comparison is accurate and resonant.

**For editorial link targets:** When pitching the glossary or comparison page as a citation resource, the independence claim answers the unspoken question every editor has: "why would I send my readers to a site that might be steering them toward a carrier that paid for the recommendation?" Answering that question before it is asked is the difference between a reply and no response.

## Content Formats That Attract Links Passively

Beyond active outreach, certain content formats earn links without pitching:

- **Glossary pages with clear definitions** are cited by writers who need to define a term without derailing their article. The glossary must be scannable and linkable at the individual term level.
- **Data tables** comparing annual maximums, waiting periods, and network sizes across major PPO carriers get embedded in roundups and cited as primary sources.
- **Timeline graphics** for "dental insurance between jobs" decisions (COBRA election window, ACA marketplace SEP, when to expect coverage to lapse) are embedded in blog posts and HR guides regularly.
- **FAQ schema content** surfaces in Google's featured snippets, which increases brand visibility and prompts writers who see CoverCapy in search results to investigate the site as a source.
