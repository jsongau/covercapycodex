# 02 — Company History and Corporate Structure

Who actually stands behind Aetna Dental Access. This is both a trust section and a genuine content gap — most consumers have no idea that the "Aetna" card is really run by a separate discount-plan company.

---

## The two-layer structure (the single most important fact)

Aetna Dental Access is not one company selling one product. It is a **network** owned by one company and a **membership** sold and administered by others.

**Layer 1 — the network: Aetna Dental Access.**
The directory of participating dentists and their discounted fee schedules is administered by **Aetna Life Insurance Company (ALIC)**. Aetna contracts the dentists and sets the discounted fees. Aetna is part of **CVS Health**, which completed its roughly $70 billion acquisition of Aetna on **November 28, 2018**. Aetna itself was founded in **1853** in Hartford, Connecticut.

**Layer 2 — the membership: run by a discount plan organization, not Aetna.**
The membership you buy is operated by a separate **discount plan organization (DPO)**. The disclosure language is explicit: *"The discount program provides access to the Aetna Dental Access network. This network is administered by Aetna Life Insurance Company (ALIC). Neither ALIC nor any of its affiliates offers or administers the discount program."*

**Crucially, the administrator depends on who sold it to you:**
- Bought through **DentalPlans.com** → the DPO is **New Benefits, Ltd.** (Dallas, TX).
- Bought through **Careington / 1Dental** → the DPO is **Careington International Corporation** (Frisco, TX).

For the page, translate this to one plain line: **Aetna provides the dentist network; a separate company runs the membership and bills you.** That is legitimate and standard for the category — but saying it openly is exactly the kind of transparency competitors avoid.

---

## The companies behind the curtain

**Aetna (the network owner).** Founded 1853, Hartford, CT. One of the oldest and largest health insurers in the United States. Now a subsidiary of CVS Health following the 2018 acquisition. Aetna's role in this product is limited to administering the network and the fee schedules.

**Careington International Corporation (a primary DPO and the category's wholesale engine).** Founded in **1979** in Texas by two dentists; headquartered in Frisco, TX; roughly 400 employees and around 15 million members across its products. Became a licensed Texas dental HMO in 1985 and incorporated as Careington International in 1996. Careington is unusually important because it is not only a brand in its own right (Careington 500, POS series) but also the **administrator behind many other carriers' branded discount plans** — including some Humana discount offerings. When you understand Careington, you understand most of the category.

**New Benefits, Ltd. (the DPO behind the DentalPlans.com path).** A Dallas-based discount-program administrator. It is the named DPO when Aetna Dental Access is purchased through DentalPlans.com.

**DentalPlans.com (the dominant marketplace).** Founded in **1999** by George Michaelides and Josh Babyak; a pioneer of direct-to-consumer comparison shopping for dental savings plans. **Acquired by Internet Brands in July 2017** (joining its health vertical alongside Henry Schein One). It now markets 25–30+ savings and insurance plans and has served over a million customers. It is CoverCapy's closest competitor as a *discovery and comparison surface* — worth studying for UX, and worth out-trusting on honesty.

## Aetna Dental Access vs Vital Savings by Aetna

A common point of confusion the page should pre-empt:

- **Aetna Dental Access** is the **network plus fee schedule** (administered by ALIC). It is the backbone.
- **Vital Savings by Aetna** is a **branded membership product** that *rides on top of* the Aetna Dental Access network. (Aetna sells a version of Vital Savings through Costco — the warehouse-club connection is real.)

They are not competitors. Vital Savings is one packaged product; "Aetna Dental Access" as sold by DentalPlans.com or Careington is another. **Both run on the same underlying network.** Different label, same dentists and same discounted fees.

## Market positioning and history of the category

Aetna Dental Access is positioned as an **affordable alternative to dental insurance**, leaning on three selling points repeated across every retailer: no annual limits, no waiting periods, no claims. The broader dental-savings-plan category emerged in the 1980s–1990s (Careington's roots go back to 1979) and was brought to the consumer mainstream by DentalPlans.com in 1999 as an online comparison marketplace. The category has always lived in the gap left by traditional dental insurance — low annual maximums, long waiting periods, and cosmetic exclusions.
