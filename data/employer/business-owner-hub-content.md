## The hub in one line
"Insure your team's smiles. Get cover today, see a dentist tomorrow." A concierge that helps a business owner put high-ceiling PPO dental on their whole team in days, with no waiting, and routes those freshly-covered employees to a vetted CoverCapy dentist.

## Why this hub exists (the strategic logic)
CoverCapy gets paid by dentists who buy accreditation and Platinum Elite status. Dentists pay for that because CoverCapy sends them insured PPO patients. The fastest way to manufacture insured PPO patients in volume is not one consumer at a time, it is one employer at a time: a single business owner enrolling a team of 8, 20, 50 people creates 8, 20, 50 covered patients in one stroke, all needing a first dentist. This hub turns one owner's decision into a roomful of patients, then hands that room to accredited offices. It is the flywheel: businesses insured in masses, dentists competing to receive them, accreditation revenue paid to be first in line.

## Who we are talking to
The owner of a small or mid-size business (2 to 100 employees) who wants to offer real dental, often for the first time, without becoming a benefits expert. They feel three things: pride (I take care of my people), pragmatism (it has to be easy and not break the bank), and a little fear (am I doing this right). The concierge answers all three.

## The emotional hook (lead with this, not with carriers)
Nobody wakes up wanting "a Guardian DentalGuard Preferred PPO." They wake up wanting to tell their team: "I got you covered. Your cleanings are free, there's no waiting, and yes, braces for your kids are included." Sell that sentence. The plan is the proof, not the product.

Headline options:
- "Give your team something to smile about."
- "Cover your whole team's smiles. In days, not months."
- "The benefit your people actually feel. Free cleanings, no waiting, braces included."

Sub-headline:
- "CoverCapy helps you put high-ceiling PPO dental on your entire team, then connects each employee with a vetted dentist ready to see them. You contributed to their smile. They work happy."

## The three promises (the page's spine)
1. High ceilings, real coverage. Not a thin discount card. Plans that reach a $5,000 annual maximum (Humana, Beam Ultra, MetLife) with major work, implants, and orthodontia for kids and adults. (Honor the $5,000 guardrail: only Humana, Beam, MetLife carry that banner. See employer-plan-comparison.md.)
2. No waiting. Your team can use it now. Group plans waive the waits that punish individual buyers. Humana and Guardian waive all waits at 5+ enrolled; Delta, Beam, Principal and Ameritas commonly have none; switching carriers brings takeover credit so nobody loses ground.
3. Set up like a concierge, not a chore. Tell us your team and what you want to spend, and we configure it. Beam can be live in about five days; most groups in two weeks.

## How it works (employer flow, 4 steps)
1. Tell us about your team: how many employees, where, and how generous you want to be.
2. We match you to a plan design (concierge-picked from Humana, Beam, MetLife, Delta, Guardian, Ameritas, Principal, Aetna, Cigna, UHC, Mutual of Omaha) and show you a clean comparison, not a 40-page brochure.
3. You choose your contribution. Pay all of it, part of it, or run it voluntary (employees pay via pre-tax payroll). Several carriers (Humana, Ameritas, Principal, Beam) allow $0 employer contribution, so even a cash-tight owner can offer it.
4. Your team enrolls, coverage activates, and CoverCapy routes each newly-covered employee to a vetted, accredited dentist ready to see them. Get cover today, see a dentist tomorrow.

## Answering the owner's real questions (FAQ copy)
- "Won't people just add all their kids for free?" No. Adding a spouse or children moves the employee to a higher rate tier that the employee pays, and each covered person still has their own deductible and their own annual maximum. The plan is tiered and capped by design.
- "Is there a copay so people don't overuse it?" Yes, built in. Cleanings are free on purpose (they prevent expensive problems), but basic work runs about 20% to the patient and major work about 50%, after a deductible. That cost-share is the natural brake.
- "Can my team really use it right away?" On group plans, usually yes. Many carriers waive waiting periods entirely (Humana and Guardian at 5+ enrolled), and switching from an old carrier brings takeover credit.
- "I only have 3 employees. Am I too small?" No. Principal and Humana and Beam write groups down to 2 enrolled.
- "What about braces?" Orthodontia is a separate benefit with its own lifetime maximum (commonly $1,000 to $2,500), usually 50%, and several carriers cover adults too, not just kids.

## Plan personalities (concierge shorthand for matching owners)
- Want the biggest, no-waiting, braces-for-the-family plan: Humana group (hero).
- Want modern and fast, live in a week: Beam (Ultra for the $5,000 ceiling).
- Want a blue-chip name your team trusts: MetLife or Delta.
- Want a plan that rewards good habits and banks unused benefit: Guardian, Ameritas, or Principal (rollover/carryover).
- Tiny team (2 to 3 people): Principal.
- Already on UHC or Aetna medical and want to bundle: UHC or Aetna.

## The dentist pitch (this is where CoverCapy gets paid)
Headline for the dentist-facing page: "We don't just send you patients. We send you insured patients, by the busload."

The pitch:
- "CoverCapy is getting businesses to insure their employees on high-maximum PPO plans, then routing those newly-covered patients to a first dentist. Be that dentist."
- The value: every employer we sign is a cohort of patients with active PPO benefits, high annual maximums, and no existing dentist relationship. Accredited offices are first in line to receive them. Platinum Elite offices get the widest routing radius and top placement in a county.
- The math that sells accreditation: one local employer with 30 covered employees is 30 households shopping for a dentist at once, many with kids needing ortho and adults with deferred major work and a fresh annual maximum to spend. That is a patient-acquisition event no single Google ad can match, and CoverCapy hands it to accredited members.
- The status play: "Do you want to be the office that gets the new Beam and Humana groups in your zip code, or watch them go to the practice down the street that accredited first?" Scarcity is real because routing favors membership tier and is radius-limited by county.
- The close: accreditation ($285/mo founder) puts you in the routing pool; Platinum Elite ($1,499/mo, county-limited) makes you the default first dentist for newly-insured employees in your area.

## Guardrails (do not break)
- Never publish a group premium as a fixed fact. Group rates are quote-only; show "configured to your team" and route to intake.
- Never attach "$5,000 maximum" to any carrier outside Humana, Beam, MetLife.
- Never claim Delta pairs $5,000 with orthodontia. Its $5,000 plan is individual and excludes ortho and implants.
- Never state employer-mechanic percentages (participation, contribution) for Aetna, or any number for Mutual of Omaha group, as hard facts. They are MEDIUM/unpublished.
- Keep the luxury concierge tone. No healthcare-portal coldness, no insurance-comparison-site clutter, no countdown timers, no em-dashes.
