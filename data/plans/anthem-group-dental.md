## Positioning and notes
Anthem is the Blue-brand employer option in the ~14 states it serves (including New York as Empire). Its real differentiator is not a high ceiling but its Maximum Carry-Over plus Network Boost plus Carry-In takeover credit: a member who uses the plan modestly banks unused maximum year over year, and a switching group can carry in up to ~$1,000 from its old carrier. Anthem markets this as getting "$2,500-max-equivalent" coverage at up to 15% lower premium. Group plans commonly waive waiting periods, write down to 2 employees, and don't require bundling Anthem medical.

Honest caveats for the hub: published group maxes top out around $2,000 (a $3,000 tier is referenced but unconfirmed), there is no $5,000 Anthem plan, ortho is standard child-only, and a genuine weakness is that diagnostic and preventive services count against the annual maximum on Anthem designs (many competitors exclude them). So Anthem is the "smart carryover Blue, recognizable brand, group-friendly" play, not a maximum-coverage standout.

Dentist value angle: a recognizable Blue brand and a large Dental Complete network (~143K dentists); carryover encourages ongoing preventive visits, and the Network Boost specifically rewards staying in-network (accredited) dentists.
