## Positioning and notes
Cigna is the clean, ACA-standard small-group option with a recognizable national network (~149,000 dentists in the Total Cigna DPPO, with a built-in deeper-discount Advantage tier) and straightforward small-business rules: 2-99 marketing band, 50% employer contribution / 50% participation, 12-month rate guarantee, guaranteed issue. Its two-tier network design quietly steers patients to the deepest-discount dentists.

The honest caveat: Cigna's published group maxes are modest ($750-$2,000), base tiers often drop ortho and implants, and there's no published $5,000 group plan. So Cigna is the "trusted brand, sensible mid-tier, predictable rules" choice rather than a maximum-coverage standout.

Cost-sharing is standard group mechanics (employer funds >=50% of employee premium, employees pay the rest via payroll, dependents add cost, chair-side cost is coinsurance + deductible). Age or composite rating depending on whether multiple designs are offered.

Dentist value angle: large recognizable network and an Advantage tier that rewards in-network depth; ortho/implant patients depend on the employer electing the richer tier.
