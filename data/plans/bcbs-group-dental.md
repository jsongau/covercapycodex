## Positioning and notes
"Blue Cross dental" is one of the highest-volume dental searches in the country, but it is not one product. CoverCapy can own that SEO honestly by being the page that explains the federation: ~33 independent Blue licensees, each with its own dental plans, linked nationally through the National Dental GRID/GRID+ (about 490,000 access points in all 50 states). That explainer is genuinely useful and routes a searcher to the right state Blue, which is exactly the concierge move our brand wants.

For the employer hub, the practical Blues are HCSC (BCBS of TX/IL and three more states), CareFirst (DC/MD/VA), Florida Blue, Highmark (PA/WV/DE/NY), and Regence/Premera (Pacific NW), plus Anthem/Empire in its own file. Across all of them the pattern holds: maxes of $750-$2,000 (no $5,000), 100/80/50 coinsurance, premiums quote-only (except Highmark, which publishes rate cards), and big variation on the two things buyers care about most, implants and adult orthodontia. Florida Blue and Premera are the standouts for adult ortho; most others cap ortho at children-19 and exclude implants on base plans.

Dentist value angle: the Blue brands carry enormous trust and the GRID+ network is huge, so "we take Blue Cross" is something most offices can already say. CoverCapy's value is mapping which Blue, which network, and routing the covered employee correctly.
