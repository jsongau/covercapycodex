## Positioning and notes
Guardian's distinctive employer hook is Maximum Rollover: an employee who uses the plan but stays modest banks the unused annual maximum into a personal account, so a $1,500 or $2,000 plan quietly grows a cushion for the year they need a crown or implant. Combine that with no waiting periods at 5+ employees, prior-coverage takeover credit, and a clean ~2-week setup, and Guardian is the "smart, rewards-good-behavior" employer plan. Adult-and-child orthodontia is available on designs that elect it (e.g., Premier PPO 2000) at 50% up to $1,500 lifetime.

Who it is for on CoverCapy: the owner who likes the idea of a benefit that rewards employees for preventive care and accrues value rather than resetting to zero — a behaviorally smart, mid-tier premium choice.

Cost-sharing is standard group mechanics (employer funds a share, employee pays via payroll, dependents add cost, chair-side cost is coinsurance + deductible). Guardian is census-rated; exact contribution/participation set per group.

Dentist value angle: rollover means patients arrive with a growing pool of available benefit, and no-wait at 5+ means they can proceed with treatment immediately at an accredited office.
