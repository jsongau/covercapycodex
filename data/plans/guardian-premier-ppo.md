## Positioning and notes
Guardian Premier 2.0 is the day-one fillings play. Its signature feature is 85% on basic (fillings and simple extractions) from day one with no waiting period, the highest day-one basic payout on the CoverCapy shelf. It pairs that with a flat $3,000 annual maximum, a top-rated A++ AM Best carrier with 160+ years of history, and a deep DentalGuard Preferred network. Whitening is included after a 6-month wait (roughly $500/yr allowance), and it is one of the few individual plans here that covers child orthodontics, dependents under 19 at 60% in-network up to a $1,500 lifetime maximum.

It is best for a household whose near-term needs are cavities, cleanings and possibly a child's braces. It is a poor fit for anyone needing a crown, root canal or implant soon, because major work waits a full 12 months, and for adults who want braces, since ortho is child-only. Buyers should also note the 12-month re-enrollment lockout after cancellation, so it should not be cancelled without a replacement plan in place. Implants are covered but capped at a modest $1,250 lifetime, so implant-focused buyers do better on higher-cap plans.

Compared with the shelf: against Delta Dental PPO Premium, Guardian wins on day-one basic (85% vs 80% after a 6-month wait) and annual maximum ($3,000 vs $2,000), but Delta covers adult orthodontics where Guardian is child-only. Against Humana Extend 5000 and Mutual of Omaha Dental Preferred, those plans carry higher $5,000 maximums and stronger implant coverage, so Guardian trades raw implant capacity for fast basic coverage and kids' braces.
