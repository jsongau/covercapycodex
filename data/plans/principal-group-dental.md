## Positioning and notes
Principal is the genuine small-group champion: employer-paid groups start at just 2 enrolled employees (25 in Massachusetts), with a clear participation matrix that scales sensibly. Pair that with no waiting periods on filed plans, deductibles waived when satisfied with a prior carrier, the annual maximum refreshed the moment a group takes over, and ortho takeover credit, and Principal is the cleanest "switch from your old carrier without anyone losing ground" story on our shelf. Its Maximum Accumulation rollover lets a modest $2,000 plan bank unused benefit (file a claim each year or lose it).

Who it is for on CoverCapy: the very small business (literally 2-3 people, like a startup, a clinic, a boutique) that most carriers treat as too small to bother with. Principal will write them, with local sales/account support.

Cost-sharing is standard, 4-tier composite, quote-rated. The honest ceiling note: published maxes top at $2,000, so Principal is the small-group-access and clean-takeover play rather than the $5,000-max play.

Dentist value angle: takeover credit + no waits means patients can begin treatment immediately at an accredited office the moment their group goes live; rollover encourages ongoing preventive visits.
