## Positioning and notes
Humana Group Dental is the strongest "maximum coverage, minimum friction" employer story on our shelf for one reason: for any employer-sponsored group of 5 or more enrolled employees, Humana waives ALL waiting periods on basic, major and orthodontia. New hires can get a crown or start braces the month they enroll. Layer on the employer-selectable annual maximum (from $1,000 all the way to Unlimited, so a true $5,000+ design is easy to build) and the 30% Extended Annual Maximum that keeps paying after the ceiling is hit, and an owner can hand employees a benefit that feels closer to a concierge plan than a budget rider.

Who it is for on CoverCapy: the business owner who wants to say "I got my team real coverage — no waiting, high ceiling, braces for the kids included" without locking into a fixed mass-market design. Because Humana imposes no dental contribution requirement, even a cash-tight owner can offer it fully voluntary (employees pay via payroll) and still place the group at 2+ enrolled.

How employee cost-sharing works here (and generally): the employer picks the design and pays an agreed share; the employee pays the rest via pre-tax payroll deduction. Adding a spouse or kids moves the employee to a higher rate tier, and that dependent cost is usually the employee's. At the dentist, the patient's "copay" is the coinsurance (0% preventive, ~20% basic, ~50% major) plus the deductible — not a flat per-visit fee. Above the annual max the patient normally pays 100%, but on Humana they pay only 70% thanks to the 30% extended-max feature.

Dentist value angle: a Humana 5+ group means patients arriving with immediately usable major and ortho benefits and a high (or unlimited) ceiling — exactly the patients an accredited CoverCapy office wants routed to it first.
