## Positioning and notes
UnitedHealthcare is the bundling-and-scale option: a household-name carrier with 25M dental members, a 119,000+ provider PPO, and strong incentives for owners who already use (or would consider) UHC medical — including a one-time credit up to $5,000 for adding dental plus another line, and 2026 Level Funded dental+vision that auto-enrolls alongside Level Funded medical. The MaxMultiplier enhancers let unused annual maximum roll forward, softening the modest $1,500-ish base ceiling.

The honest caveats: UHC group dental is a configurable framework, not a fixed product, and its published ceilings are lower than the $5,000-with-ortho story — ortho is a rider gated at 10+ employees/8+ enrolled, implants require the FlexAppeal Enhanced buy-up, and a $5,000 max is not a published small-group tier. So UHC is the "bundle it with our medical, lean on the brand" play rather than the maximum-coverage play.

Cost-sharing is standard (employer funds >=50% of employee cost, employees pay the rest via payroll, dependents add cost, chair-side cost is coinsurance + deductible). Composite or age-banded rating, quoted per group.

Dentist value angle: a large, recognizable network and a big covered-member base; bundling-driven enrollment can push volume, though ortho/implant patients depend on the employer buying those riders.
