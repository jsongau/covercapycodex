## Positioning and notes
Aetna (a CVS Health company) is the big-network, flexible-design option. Its dental PPO runs one of the largest provider networks in the country, group plans carry no waiting periods (replaced by a fair late-entrant rule for people who skip their enrollment window), and richer designs like the 'Gold' Passive PPO pay an unusually generous 80% on major work and implants with adult-and-child ortho to $2,000 lifetime. The CVS connection adds brand familiarity for employees.

The honest caveat: published commercial maxes top out around $2,500; the only Unlimited-in-network ceiling is the federal FEDVIP plan, not a small-group product. So Aetna is the "huge network, generous coinsurance on richer designs" play rather than the published-$5,000 play.

Cost-sharing is standard group mechanics, quote-rated. Several employer-mechanic specifics (participation %, contribution %, min size) come from secondary sources and should be reconfirmed with a current Aetna underwriting guide before they appear as hard facts on a page.

Dentist value angle: very large network (most offices participate), and richer designs paying 80% on major/implants mean strong reimbursement for accepted treatment.
