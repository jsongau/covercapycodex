## Positioning and notes
Delta Dental's employer pitch is reach and recognition: it runs the largest dentist network in the country (Premier reaches 154,000+ dentists across 330,000+ locations, roughly three of every four US dentists), group plans carry no waiting periods at any group size, and clear aligners are included at no extra cost. For a business owner whose employees are spread across many ZIP codes, "almost any dentist already takes Delta" is the strongest single line.

The honest ceiling caveat: Delta's headline $5,000 plan is an individual product that drops orthodontia and implants. Group ortho-bearing designs realistically run a $2,000-$3,000 annual max plus a separate $1,000-$2,000 lifetime ortho benefit; the To Go carryover can stretch the effective ceiling over time. So Delta is the "everyone's dentist takes it, no waiting" play, not the "$5,000-with-braces" play (for that, see Humana group, Beam Ultra, MetLife Platinum).

Cost-sharing is standard: employer funds a share, employees pay the rest via payroll, dependents add cost, chair-side cost is coinsurance plus deductible. Group plans are pooled/community-rated and quoted per group.

Dentist value angle: most offices already participate in Delta Premier/PPO, so routing Delta-covered employees to an accredited CoverCapy office is frictionless — the office is very likely already in-network.
