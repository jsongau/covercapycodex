## Positioning and notes
Physicians Mutual is the heavily TV-advertised individual dental brand, and its marketing leans on two true-but-easily-misread claims: no deductible and no annual maximum. The reality to communicate honestly is the structure underneath: on three of its four plans (Economy, Standard, Preferred) it pays a fixed dollar amount per procedure rather than a percentage, so the real reimbursement on a crown or denture is capped by a schedule, not by an annual maximum. Only the top Premier plan pays a true 70% coinsurance. Preventive and basic are covered immediately, but major work carries a 12-month wait.

Who it is for: adults and seniors (guaranteed acceptance at 18+) who want simple, no-deductible coverage and value the brand, and who understand the fixed-schedule trade-off. It is not a high-reimbursement plan for big-ticket work on the lower tiers.

Honest framing for any CoverCapy page: present the no-deductible / no-annual-max headline, then immediately clarify the fixed-dollar schedule and the 12-month major wait, so the page is accurate and trustworthy (accuracy is the moat). Implants and orthodontia are not marketed as covered and should not be claimed without confirming the state schedule.

Dentist value angle: runs on the large Ameritas Classic PPO Network; patients have immediate preventive/basic coverage, but offices should set expectations that the lower plans reimburse major work on a fixed schedule.
