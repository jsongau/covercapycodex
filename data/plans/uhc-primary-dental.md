## Positioning and notes
UHC Primary Dental is the lowest-premium plan on the shelf, around $30 a month, built deliberately as an entry tier. It buys two things from day one with no waiting period: preventive care at 100% and basic care like fillings at 50%, with the basic share climbing to 65% after the first year and 80% after the second. It does not cover major work, implants, or orthodontics at all, and the annual maximum is a modest $1,000. Its other strengths are speed and trust: coverage can start as soon as the next business day, it runs on one of the largest individual dental networks, and it is underwritten by Golden Rule Insurance Company with an AM Best A+ rating. It is the right pick for someone who mainly wants cleanings covered, wants basic work eligible immediately, is age 64 or under, and is bridging a gap after losing employer dental benefits. Anyone needing crowns, root canals, dentures, implants, or braces should look at UnitedHealthcare's higher individual tiers (maximums up to about $3,000) or another carrier.
