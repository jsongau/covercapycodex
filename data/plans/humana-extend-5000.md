## Positioning and notes
Humana Extend 5000 is a high-maximum individual PPO that bundles dental, vision and hearing on a single plan (hearing everywhere except New York). Its $5,000 annual maximum is the joint-highest on our shelf alongside Mutual of Omaha Dental Preferred. Edge: preventive 100% day one, basic 80% (after a 90-day wait), and major plus implants paying 50% after a 6-month wait then rising to 60% in year two, so timing a larger case for the second year stretches the maximum further. It adds a $200 per calendar year whitening allowance as a small perk. Weakness: at about $100/mo it is on the higher side for individual dental, and it does not cover orthodontics.

Who it is for: someone who wants dental, vision and hearing together, expects major work or an implant, and values a high ceiling with a relatively fast path to major reimbursement.

Versus MetLife NCD Complete for multiple implants: NCD has double the annual maximum ($10,000 vs $5,000) and a higher dedicated implant allowance for people doing two or more implants, but Humana pays 50% on major after only a 6-month wait (better early-year reimbursement than NCD's ~10% year-one major). For a single implant with patience, the bigger NCD ceiling and implant allowance can win; for a faster major start plus the vision and hearing bundle, Humana wins. Watch the Humana implant sub-maximum ($2,000/yr, $4,000 lifetime) when planning two or more implants, since that cap is lower than NCD's dedicated implant allowance.

Versus Mutual of Omaha Dental Preferred (same $5,000 max): Humana costs more and applies waits, but pays 50% on major from month six and bundles vision and hearing. Mutual of Omaha is cheaper with no waits and 80% day-one basic, but only 20% on major in year one.
